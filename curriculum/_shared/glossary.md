# Glossary

Sanskrit terms used across the pilot modules, in IAST. Add new entries when a new term appears in any lesson. Sort alphabetically by IAST spelling within each section.

Each entry: **IAST** (Devanāgarī, alternate spellings) — literal meaning. *One-sentence functional definition as used in this curriculum.*

## Pañcabhūta — Five Elements (Module 2)

- **ākāśa** (आकाश; also akasha) — "space, ether." *The element of empty space that gives all other things room to exist.*
- **ap** / **jala** (अप्/जल; also apas) — "water." *The cohesive, fluid element; in modern terms, the liquid state of matter.*
- **bhūta** (भूत) — "that which has become; element." *In *pañcabhūta*, refers to gross observable elements (mahābhūta).*
- **mahābhūta** (महाभूत) — "great element." *Synonym for *bhūta* in this context; the five are earth, water, fire, air, space.*
- **pañcabhūta** (पञ्चभूत; also panchabhuta) — "five great elements." *The framework organising matter and phenomena into earth, water, fire, air, and space.*
- **pṛthvī** (पृथ्वी; also prithvi) — "earth." *The solid, supportive element; in modern terms, the solid state of matter.*
- **tanmātra** (तन्मात्र) — "subtle essence." *In Sāṅkhya, the subtle perceptual quality (sound, touch, form, taste, smell) associated with each *bhūta*. Senior-band only.*
- **tejas** (तेजस्; also agni — अग्नि) — "fire, brilliance." *The transformative element associated with heat and light; in modern terms, energy.*
- **vāyu** (वायु; also vayu) — "wind, air." *The mobile element; in modern terms, the gaseous state of matter.*

## Bindu Paddhati — Dot Addition (Module 5)

- **bindu** (बिन्दु; also bindu) — "dot, point." *In Bindu Paddhati, a placeholder dot drawn above a digit to record a carry.*
- **Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa** (एकाधिकेन पूर्वेण) — "by one more than the previous." *Tirthaji sūtra used in mental multiplication and division.*
- **gaṇita** (गणित) — "calculation, mathematics." *Generic Sanskrit term for mathematics.*
- **Nikhilam** (निखिलम्) — "all from nine and the last from ten." *Tirthaji sūtra used in complement-based subtraction and multiplication.*
- **paddhati** (पद्धति) — "method, path, technique." *In *Bindu Paddhati*, "the dot method."*
- **śuddha** (शुद्ध; also shuddha) — "pure, clean." *Tirthaji sūtra ("Śuddha" or "Śuddhi") used for column addition.*
- **sūtra** (सूत्र) — "thread, formula, aphorism." *In Vedic mathematics, a short mnemonic rule (16 main sūtras in Tirthaji's corpus).*
- **Ūrdhva-tiryagbhyāṁ** (ऊर्ध्व-तिर्यग्भ्याम्) — "vertically and crosswise." *Tirthaji sūtra used for general multiplication.*

## Nikhilam — Subtraction by Complements (Module 9)

- **daśa** / **daśataḥ** (दश / दशतः) — "ten / from ten." *The base referred to in the Nikhilam sūtra's final clause "the last from ten."*
- **nava** / **navataḥ** (नव / नवतः) — "nine / from nine." *The digit referred to in the Nikhilam sūtra's first clause "all from nine."*
- **Nikhilaṁ Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ** (निखिलं नवतश्चरमं दशतः) — "all from nine and the last from ten." *Full form of the Nikhilam sūtra; Tirthaji's sūtra #2. Gives the digit-wise complement of a number to the next power of ten.*
- **pūraka** (पूरक) — "that which completes; complement." *The number that, added to a given number, completes it to a base (10, 100, 1000...).*
- **vyavakalana** (व्यवकलन) — "subtraction; taking away." *Generic Sanskrit term for subtraction; used in classical Indian mathematical texts.*

(Note: *Nikhilam* and *sūtra* are first defined in the Bindu Paddhati section above and reused here.)

## Eleven Multiplication (Module 7)

- **Anurūpyeṇa** (अनुरूप्येण; also Ānurūpyeṇa) — "proportionally." *Tirthaji sub-sūtra used to extend the ×11 pattern to ×12, ×13, …, ×19 by scaling the middle-add step.*
- **Pariśiṣṭa** (परिशिष्ट) — "appendix, supplement." *In Tirthaji's claim, an Atharva-Veda Pariśiṣṭa text from which he says his 16 sūtras come — a claim no other Sanskritist has corroborated.*
- **sub-sūtra** (उपसूत्र, upasūtra) — "secondary aphorism." *In Tirthaji's corpus, 13 sub-sūtras complement the 16 main sūtras.*
- **Tirthaji** (तीर्थजी) — name. *Bharati Krishna Tirthaji (1884–1960), Śaṅkarācārya of Govardhana Maṭha (Puri), author of* Vedic Mathematics *(1965, posthumous).*

## Moon Phases and Tithi (Module 6)

- **adhika māsa** (अधिक मास) — "extra month." *Intercalary lunar month inserted approximately every 32.5 months to keep the lunisolar calendar aligned with the solar year.*
- **amāvasyā** (अमावस्या) — "dwelling-together (of Sun and Moon)." *New-moon day; the Moon is conjunct the Sun and not visible.*
- **karaṇa** (करण) — "half-tithi." *Each tithi is divided into two karaṇas; one of the five limbs of the pañcāṅga.*
- **kṛṣṇa pakṣa** (कृष्ण पक्ष; also krishna paksha) — "dark fortnight." *The waning half of the lunar month, from pūrṇimā to amāvasyā.*
- **māsa** (मास) — "month." *Lunar month, named after the nakṣatra in which the full moon occurs.*
- **nakṣatra** (नक्षत्र) — "lunar mansion, star group." *One of 27 segments of the ecliptic, ~13°20' each; used to locate the Moon's position.*
- **pakṣa** (पक्ष) — "wing, side, fortnight." *Half of a lunar month; either the waxing (*śukla*) or waning (*kṛṣṇa*) fortnight.*
- **pañcāṅga** (पञ्चाङ्ग; also panchanga) — "five-limbed." *The traditional Indian almanac with five components: tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa.*
- **pūrṇimā** (पूर्णिमा; also purnima) — "fullness." *Full-moon day; Moon directly opposite the Sun and fully illuminated as seen from Earth.*
- **śukla pakṣa** (शुक्ल पक्ष; also shukla paksha) — "bright fortnight." *The waxing half of the lunar month, from amāvasyā to pūrṇimā.*
- **Sūrya-siddhānta** (सूर्य-सिद्धान्त) — "treatise on the Sun." *Sanskrit astronomical text (compiled c. 5th–10th c. CE) describing planetary motion, tithi computation, and eclipse prediction.*
- **synodic month** — "lunation." *The ~29.53-day period between successive new moons; the basis of the lunar calendar.*
- **tithi** (तिथि) — "lunar day." *1/30 of a synodic month; the period during which the Moon's elongation from the Sun increases by 12°. A tithi can span more or less than 24 hours.*
- **vāra** (वार) — "weekday." *One of seven days named after the Sun, Moon, and five visible planets; one limb of the pañcāṅga.*
- **yoga** (योग) — "joining." *In pañcāṅga context, one of 27 divisions based on the combined longitudes of Sun and Moon (distinct from yoga as in Patañjali).*

## Doṣa Framework (Module 4)

- **agni** (अग्नि) — "fire." *In Ayurveda, the digestive/metabolic fire (*jaṭharāgni*); a key concept linked to *pitta*.*
- **ahāra** (आहार) — "food, intake." *What you eat; one of the three classical pillars of health.*
- **anila** (अनिल) — "wind." *Classical synonym for *vāta*.*
- **doṣa** (दोष; also dosha) — "that which can become faulty; humour." *In Ayurveda, one of three biological functional principles — *vāta, pitta, kapha* — that govern bodily processes. In balance, they sustain health; out of balance, they cause disease.*
- **kapha** (कफ; also shleshman, ślēṣman) — "phlegm, that which holds together." *The structural/lubricating *doṣa*, built from water (*ap*) and earth (*pṛthvī*). Governs stability, immunity, mass, and lubrication.*
- **madhura, amla, lavaṇa, kaṭu, tikta, kaṣāya** (मधुर, अम्ल, लवण, कटु, तिक्त, कषाय) — "sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, astringent." *The six tastes (*ṣaḍ-rasa*) classified in Ayurvedic dietetics.*
- **pitta** (पित्त) — "that which heats/digests." *The transformative *doṣa*, built from fire (*tejas*) and water (*ap*). Governs digestion, metabolism, vision, and intellect.*
- **prakṛti** (प्रकृति; also prakriti) — "nature, original constitution." *In Ayurvedic individual physiology, the proportion of *vāta : pitta : kapha* a person is born with — their constitutional baseline. Distinct from the broader Sāṅkhya use of the same word.*
- **ṣaḍ-rasa** (षड्रस) — "six tastes." *The six-taste system used to balance the *doṣa*s through food.*
- **tridoṣa** (त्रिदोष) — "three doṣas." *Collective term for *vāta, pitta, kapha*.*
- **vāta** (वात; also vata) — "that which moves, wind." *The motion *doṣa*, built from space (*ākāśa*) and air (*vāyu*). Governs movement, breath, circulation, nerve impulses, and elimination.*
- **vikṛti** (विकृति; also vikriti) — "deviation, modification." *The current imbalanced state, as opposed to *prakṛti*. Ayurvedic practice tries to bring *vikṛti* back to *prakṛti*.*

## Auṣadhi — Herbs (Module 10)

- **adraka** (अद्रक; also adrak) — "ginger." *Fresh rhizome of *Zingiber officinale*; warming, used as a digestive aid.*
- **auṣadhi** (औषधि; also oshadhi, aushadhi) — "herb; plant used as medicine." *In Vedic and Ayurvedic literature, any plant valued for healing or nourishment.*
- **Auṣadhi-sūkta** (औषधिसूक्त) — "hymn to the herbs." *Ṛgveda 10.97, a hymn addressing the medicinal plants as benevolent beings.*
- **bhāvana** (भावन) — "preparation, infusion." *Process of soaking a powder in a liquid to potentiate it; used here only as background context.*
- **haridrā** (हरिद्रा; also haldī, haldi) — "the yellow one." *Rhizome of *Curcuma longa*; central kitchen and Ayurvedic herb.*
- **kaṭu** (कटु) — "pungent." *One of the six tastes (*ṣaḍ-rasa*); the dominant taste of ginger, ajwain, and (partly) mint.*
- **nimba** (निम्ब; also neem) — "neem." *Azadirachta indica*; intensely bitter (*tikta*) tree of South Asia.*
- **pudīnā** (पुदीना) — "mint." *Mentha species (commonly *Mentha spicata* in Indian gardens); cooling.*
- **rasāyana** (रसायन) — "elixir, rejuvenation." *Branch of *aṣṭāṅga-āyurveda* focused on long-term tonics; many *rasāyana* formulations use herbs studied in this module. Senior band only.*
- **sūnṭhī** (सूण्ठी; also shunthi, sunthi) — "dried ginger." *The dried rhizome; in Ayurveda its properties shift from the fresh form (*adraka*).*
- **tikta** (तिक्त) — "bitter." *One of the six tastes; the defining taste of neem.*
- **tulasī** (तुलसी; also tulsi, tulasi) — "the incomparable one." *Ocimum tenuiflorum*, "holy basil"; an aromatic Lamiaceae herb common in Indian gardens.*
- **yavānī** (यवानी; also ajwain, ajavāyana) — "barley-like." *Ayurvedic Sanskrit name for the seeds of *Trachyspermum ammi* — the ajwain or carom seed.*

## Movement of the Sun (Module 12)

- **ayana** (अयन) — "course, path, movement." *The Sun's half-year course either northward (*uttarāyaṇa*) or southward (*dakṣiṇāyana*) along the horizon.*
- **ayanāṁśa** (अयनांश) — "precessional portion." *The accumulated angular difference (~24° today) between the *sāyana* (tropical) and *nirayana* (sidereal) zodiacs, growing ~50″ per year due to axial precession.*
- **dakṣiṇāyana** (दक्षिणायन; also dakshinayana) — "southward course." *The half-year from summer solstice (~21 June) to winter solstice (~21 December) when the Sun's noon altitude falls in the Northern Hemisphere.*
- **gaṇita** (गणित) — "calculation, mathematics." *In Indian astronomy, the computational branch — number-crunching the sky; complement of *gola*.*
- **gola** (गोल) — "sphere, globe." *In Indian astronomy, the branch concerned with spherical geometry of the celestial sphere; complement of *gaṇita* (computation).*
- **krānti-vṛtta** (क्रान्ति-वृत्त) — "ecliptic." *The apparent annual path of the Sun across the celestial sphere; tilted ~23.5° from the celestial equator.*
- **Makara Saṁkrānti** (मकर संक्रान्ति) — "entry into Makara (Capricorn)." *The saṁkrānti around 14 January when the Sun enters the rāśi of *Makara*; traditionally marks the start of *uttarāyaṇa* in the *nirayana* (sidereal) frame.*
- **nirayana** (निरयन; "without-ayana / sidereal") — *Reference frame fixed to the stars; the basis of traditional Indian *jyotiṣa* calendars.*
- **rāśi** (राशि) — "heap, sign of the zodiac." *One of 12 thirty-degree segments of the ecliptic; the Sun crosses one rāśi per ~30 days.*
- **sāyana** (सायन; "with-ayana / tropical") — *Reference frame fixed to the equinoxes; the basis of the modern astronomical zodiac and the seasons.*
- **saṁkrānti** (संक्रान्ति) — "transit, crossing." *The moment the Sun moves from one rāśi to the next; ~12 saṁkrāntis per year.*
- **Sūrya** (सूर्य) — "the Sun." *The luminary whose apparent annual motion this module tracks.*
- **uttarāyaṇa** (उत्तरायण) — "northward course." *The half-year from winter solstice (~21 December) to summer solstice (~21 June) when the Sun's noon altitude rises in the Northern Hemisphere.*
- **viṣuva** (विषुव) — "equal, equinox." *One of the two days a year when day and night are equal; the Sun crosses the celestial equator.*

## Magic Squares — Bhadragaṇita (Module 14)

- **bhadra** (भद्र) — "auspicious, prosperous." *In *Bhadragaṇita*, the term names the auspicious-square; magic squares were associated with protective and auspicious uses.*
- **Bhadragaṇita** (भद्रगणित; also Bhadra-gaṇita) — "mathematics of auspicious squares." *Title of Book 14 of Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita's *Gaṇita-kaumudī* (1356 CE); the most extensive pre-modern Indian treatment of magic squares.*
- **caturasra** (चतुरस्र) — "four-sided, square." *Generic Sanskrit term for a square or quadrilateral figure.*
- **Gaṇita-kaumudī** (गणितकौमुदी) — "moonlight of mathematics." *1356 CE treatise by Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita covering arithmetic, algebra, geometry, combinatorics, and magic squares.*
- **Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita** (नारायण पण्डित) — 14th-century mathematician. *Author of the *Gaṇita-kaumudī* (1356 CE); systematised methods for constructing magic squares of any order.*
- **samānaṅka** (समानङ्क) — "equal-number, equal-sum." *Property defining a magic square: all rows, columns, and main diagonals share a common sum (the magic constant).*
- **turagagati** (तुरगगति) — "horse-step, knight-move." *Nārāyaṇa's name for the stair-step / knight-like placement method used for constructing odd-order magic squares.*
- **yantra** (यन्त्र) — "instrument, diagram." *Geometric diagram, including numbered grids; magic squares were sometimes used as *yantra*s in contemplative or protective contexts.*

## Yoga and the Body (Module 8)

- **āsana** (आसन; also asana) — "seat, posture." *A held physical posture used in yoga practice.*
- **adho mukha śvānāsana** (अधो मुख श्वानासन) — "downward-facing dog posture." *Common standing/inversion pose.*
- **bhujaṅgāsana** (भुजङ्गासन; also bhujangasana) — "cobra posture." *Prone backbend used in Sūrya Namaskāra.*
- **dhyāna** (ध्यान; also dhyana) — "meditation, sustained attention." *In Patañjali's Yoga Sūtra, the seventh of eight limbs.*
- **prāṇa** (प्राण; also prana) — "breath, vital energy." *Used in yoga to refer to breath and the broader concept of life-energy.*
- **prāṇāyāma** (प्राणायाम; also pranayama) — "control of breath." *The fourth limb of Patañjali's eight; breath-regulation practice.*
- **Sūrya Namaskāra** (सूर्य नमस्कार; also Surya Namaskar) — "sun salutation." *Sequence of 12 postures performed in flow.*
- **tāḍāsana** (ताडासन; also tadasana) — "mountain posture." *Foundational standing pose.*
- **vṛkṣāsana** (वृक्षासन; also vrikshasana) — "tree posture." *Single-leg balance pose appropriate for all age bands.*
- **yoga** (योग) — "union, yoke." *From Patañjali: cessation of mental fluctuations (*citta-vṛtti-nirodha*). In modern practice, a discipline integrating posture, breath, and attention.*
