# Glossary

Sanskrit terms used across the pilot modules, in IAST. Add new entries when a new term appears in any lesson. Sort alphabetically by IAST spelling within each section.

Each entry: **IAST** (Devanāgarī, alternate spellings) — literal meaning. *One-sentence functional definition as used in this curriculum.*

## Pañcabhūta — Five Elements (Module 2)

- **ākāśa** (आकाश; also akasha) — "space, ether." *The element of empty space that gives all other things room to exist.*
- **ap** / **jala** (अप्/जल; also apas) — "water." *The cohesive, fluid element; in modern terms, the liquid state of matter.*
- **bhūta** (भूत) — "that which has become; element." *In *pañcabhūta*, refers to gross observable elements (mahābhūta).*
- **mahābhūta** (महाभूत) — "great element." *Synonym for *bhūta* in this context; the five are earth, water, fire, air, space.*
- **pañcabhūta** (पञ्चभूत; also panchabhuta) — "five great elements." *The framework organising matter and phenomena into earth, water, fire, air, and space.*
- **pṛthvī** (पृथ्वी; also prithvi) — "earth." *The solid, supportive element; in modern terms, the solid state of matter.*
- **tanmātra** (तन्मात्र) — "subtle essence." *In Sāṅkhya, the subtle perceptual quality (sound, touch, form, taste, smell) associated with each *bhūta*. Senior-band only.*
- **tejas** (तेजस्; also agni — अग्नि) — "fire, brilliance." *The transformative element associated with heat and light; in modern terms, energy.*
- **vāyu** (वायु; also vayu) — "wind, air." *The mobile element; in modern terms, the gaseous state of matter.*

## Bindu Paddhati — Dot Addition (Module 5)

- **bindu** (बिन्दु; also bindu) — "dot, point." *In Bindu Paddhati, a placeholder dot drawn above a digit to record a carry.*
- **Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa** (एकाधिकेन पूर्वेण) — "by one more than the previous." *Tirthaji sūtra used in mental multiplication and division.*
- **gaṇita** (गणित) — "calculation, mathematics." *Generic Sanskrit term for mathematics.*
- **Nikhilam** (निखिलम्) — "all from nine and the last from ten." *Tirthaji sūtra used in complement-based subtraction and multiplication.*
- **paddhati** (पद्धति) — "method, path, technique." *In *Bindu Paddhati*, "the dot method."*
- **śuddha** (शुद्ध; also shuddha) — "pure, clean." *Tirthaji sūtra ("Śuddha" or "Śuddhi") used for column addition.*
- **sūtra** (सूत्र) — "thread, formula, aphorism." *In Vedic mathematics, a short mnemonic rule (16 main sūtras in Tirthaji's corpus).*
- **Ūrdhva-tiryagbhyāṁ** (ऊर्ध्व-तिर्यग्भ्याम्) — "vertically and crosswise." *Tirthaji sūtra used for general multiplication.*

## Yoga and the Body (Module 8)

- **āsana** (आसन; also asana) — "seat, posture." *A held physical posture used in yoga practice.*
- **adho mukha śvānāsana** (अधो मुख श्वानासन) — "downward-facing dog posture." *Common standing/inversion pose.*
- **bhujaṅgāsana** (भुजङ्गासन; also bhujangasana) — "cobra posture." *Prone backbend used in Sūrya Namaskāra.*
- **dhyāna** (ध्यान; also dhyana) — "meditation, sustained attention." *In Patañjali's Yoga Sūtra, the seventh of eight limbs.*
- **prāṇa** (प्राण; also prana) — "breath, vital energy." *Used in yoga to refer to breath and the broader concept of life-energy.*
- **prāṇāyāma** (प्राणायाम; also pranayama) — "control of breath." *The fourth limb of Patañjali's eight; breath-regulation practice.*
- **Sūrya Namaskāra** (सूर्य नमस्कार; also Surya Namaskar) — "sun salutation." *Sequence of 12 postures performed in flow.*
- **tāḍāsana** (ताडासन; also tadasana) — "mountain posture." *Foundational standing pose.*
- **vṛkṣāsana** (वृक्षासन; also vrikshasana) — "tree posture." *Single-leg balance pose appropriate for all age bands.*
- **yoga** (योग) — "union, yoke." *From Patañjali: cessation of mental fluctuations (*citta-vṛtti-nirodha*). In modern practice, a discipline integrating posture, breath, and attention.*
