# Parent Guide — Module 1 (Primary): Five Gifts from India

**Your child's age:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 30 min

## What your child is learning

Five things India "gave to the world" — concepts and practices that we use or hear about every day:

1. **Zero** — the number we count with.
2. **Yoga** — moving with breath.
3. **Ayurveda** — old Indian wellness.
4. **Vedic Math** — fast counting tricks.
5. **Indian classical music** — rāga.

The whole module is intentionally light and story-led. No paper tests. By the end, your child can name all five and tell a 30-second story about their favourite one.

## What to expect

- A daily chant: *"Zero! Yoga! Ayurveda! Vedic Math! Music! Five gifts from India to the whole wide world!"*
- Drawing every day.
- A Day 10 show-and-tell project (a poster, drawing, or 30-second story).

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Ask "what was today's gift?" at dinner.
- Sing the chant with them.
- Notice these gifts in real life: count something big together (zero), do tree pose, recognise turmeric as an Ayurvedic herb, listen to one rāga (any classical Indian recording).

**Not helpful:**
- Quizzing them on Sanskrit terms (there aren't many at this age).
- Doing the project for them.

## What success looks like

By Day 10 your child can:
- Name 3+ of the 5 gifts.
- Point to one in their own life.
- Tell a 30-second story about their favourite gift.

That's it. Anything more is wonderful but not expected.
