# Day 7 — Sky Watchers (Indian Astronomy)

**Module:** Five Gifts (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children hear the Aryabhata story and try the "spinning earth" demo.

## Materials

- A ball (any size) with a small mark on it
- Open space

## Flow

### Settle + chant + song (5 min)

### Story (10 min)

*"About 1500 years ago, a young man in India named Aryabhata had a BIG idea."*

*"In his time, everyone thought the stars went round and round the earth — that's why the sun rises and sets, why stars move."*

*"Aryabhata thought: 'What if the stars stand still — and WE spin?'"*

*"He was right! He was 1000 years before anyone in Europe figured this out."*

*"He wrote a book called the Aryabhaṭīya. He was only 23 years old."*

### Demo (10 min)

- Hold the ball. Put a sticker or mark on it.
- Slowly spin the ball.
- *"If you were standing on this little mark, what would you see? You'd see the room going round and round. But it's not. YOU are spinning."*

- Stand the children up. Slowly spin in place 3 times. *"Look around. Did the room move, or did you?"*

### Drawing (3 min)

Quick: draw the earth as a ball, with you standing on it.

### Show + chant (2 min)

## Homework

- Tonight, look up at the stars. Notice that the stars seem to move. They don't. WE'RE moving.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Why don't we feel the earth spinning? (Because we're moving WITH it.)
- **Tier down:** Just remember: Aryabhata said WE spin, not the sky.
