# Day 6 — The Math Story

**Module:** Five Gifts (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children hear a story about Indian mathematicians and try one fast trick.

## Materials

- Whiteboard

## Flow

### Settle + chant + song (5 min)

### Story (10 min)

*"In old India, there were mathematicians who could do amazing tricks with numbers in their HEAD. No paper. No calculator. Just their mind."*

*"One of them was a man named Bhāskara who lived about 900 years ago. He wrote a math book — but he wrote it for his DAUGHTER! Her name was Līlāvatī. He filled the book with little puzzles and stories."*

*"Here's one of Bhāskara's puzzles in modern words:"*

> *A flock of 24 swans flew over a lake. 12 dived in to swim, 6 flew east, and the rest flew to the west. How many flew west?*

(Answer: 24 − 12 − 6 = 6)

### Try a fast trick (12 min)

Teach: **Multiplying by 11.**

Take any 2-digit number: **23.**
- Split: 2 _ 3
- Add the digits: 2+3 = 5
- Put the sum in the middle: **253**
- So 23 × 11 = **253**!

Try together: 41 × 11? 52 × 11? 63 × 11?

(For carries — 78 × 11 = 7(7+8)8 = 7(15)8 = 858 — slightly trickier, skip if too hard.)

### Show + chant (3 min)

## Homework

- Try 5 multiplication by 11 problems with a parent.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try the trick with carries (78 × 11 = 858).
- **Tier down:** Just remember: "multiplying by 11 has a quick trick."
