## Source for the ×11 trick

- **Tirthaji, B. K.** *Vedic Mathematics* (1965), Motilal Banarsidass. Sūtra 1, *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa*. The book was published posthumously; Tirthaji died in 1960.
- **SutraGanita corpus**: `/Users/sree/Projects/SutraGanita/content/` contains the *Microsoft Word - sutras1.pdf* reference (the 16-sūtra list) and *Vedic_Maths.pdf* (ISKCON Desire Tree) with practice examples suitable for primary teachers to pre-read.

## Why we don't teach the full method at Primary

The full ×11 trick requires:
- Confident 2-digit-number reading (fine — most Grade 3+ children have this).
- Single-digit addition fluency (most have this).
- Carry handling (Grade 3 has it; Grade 4–5 is solid).

We teach the no-carry version completely. We *hint* at the carry case on Day 5 (the "spill-over"), and we save the algebraic proof for Middle band.

## For families who ask

The ×11 trick is a mental-math technique taught in Indian schools and presented in Tirthaji's *Vedic Mathematics* (1965). Whether the technique is genuinely ancient ("Vedic") or a 20th-century synthesis is academically disputed — Senior band (Grade 9–12) tackles this honest framing. At Primary band we just teach the technique enthusiastically.
