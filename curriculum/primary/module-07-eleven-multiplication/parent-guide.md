# Parent Guide — Module 7: The Eleven-Friend Trick

**Your child's age:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 30 min

## What your child is learning

A mental-math trick for multiplying any small 2-digit number by 11. The story version:

> Every 2-digit number has an "eleven friend." When they meet, the number grows by adding itself in the middle.
>
> 12 meets its eleven friend → **1 (1+2) 2 = 132**.
>
> 34 meets its eleven friend → **3 (3+4) 4 = 374**.

The chant: **"Split! Add! Drop!"**
1. **Split** the digits with a gap.
2. **Add** the two digits.
3. **Drop** the sum in the middle.

By end of module your child can do this in under 3 seconds, mentally, for any 2-digit number where the digit-sum is less than 10.

At Day 5 they get a "peek" at what happens when the digit-sum is 10 or more (the "spill-over" — e.g. 73 × 11 = 803). They don't have to do this case fully yet — the carry case is Middle-band material.

At Day 6 they meet the Sanskrit name ***Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa*** (lit. "by one more than the previous") — said once, no quizzing on it. It's the older-kids' name for what they're doing.

## What to expect this fortnight

**A daily chant:**
> "Split! the number. Add! the digits. Drop! the sum in the middle. That's the eleven-friend trick!"

**Games at home:**
- The "Eleven Friend" card game (Day 1).
- Quick-fire ×11 with a parent or sibling. Call out a 2-digit number; your child shouts back the answer.

**A creative project (Days 7–10):** Your child will design their own "Eleven Friend" game — cards, board, dice, jumping — and play classmates' games on Day 10.

## How to help — and how NOT to help

**Helpful:**
- 5-minute drill before bed. Call out a 2-digit number; your child says the ×11 product. Aim for under 3 seconds per number.
- Try the trick yourself. Notice how fast it is once you see the pattern.
- For their project: provide materials (cards, paper) and let *them* design the rules.

**Not helpful:**
- Insisting they do "long multiplication" because that's how you learned. Both methods work; the trick is faster mentally.
- Designing their game for them.
- Quizzing them on the Sanskrit name. It's a one-line cultural anchor, not a vocabulary test.

## A note on "Vedic mathematics"

You may have heard the term "Vedic mathematics." The honest framing we use:

- The ×11 trick is a real, useful Indian mental-arithmetic technique.
- It appears in a book called *Vedic Mathematics* by Bharati Krishna Tirthaji, published in 1965 (five years after Tirthaji's death).
- Whether the trick is genuinely *ancient* Vedic mathematics or a 20th-century synthesis is academically disputed. Scholars (S. G. Dani 1993) note there's no surviving manuscript supporting the ancient claim.

By Senior band (Grade 9–12) your child will read this debate and form their own view. At Primary band we just teach the technique enthusiastically.

## What success looks like

By the end of the module, your child can:
- Compute any 2-digit × 11 (no-carry) mentally in under 3 seconds.
- Recite "Split, Add, Drop."
- Recognise the Sanskrit name *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* when they hear it.
- Show classmates a game they designed.
