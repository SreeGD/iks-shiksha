# Module 7 (Primary) — 10-Day Lesson Plan

## At a glance (30-min sessions)

| Day | Title | Activity | Output |
|-----|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Meet Your Eleven-Friend | Story + first 6 examples | First answers on slate |
| 2 | Split, Add, Drop chant | Chant + practice | Confident chant |
| 3 | Friend Game (no-carry) | Card-pair game | First wins |
| 4 | Speed Day | Pair quick-fire | Personal best |
| 5 | What if the middle is too big? | Hint at carry case | Awe |
| 6 | Meet the Sanskrit Name | *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* intro | New word |
| 7 | Make-a-Game Day | Design own game | Sketch + first prototype |
| 8 | Peek at 3-digit ×11 | Teacher demo + watch | Awe |
| 9 | Project Day | Build the game | Game ready to play |
| 10 | Game Showcase | Play each other's games | Stickers |

## Daily structure (30 min)

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 4 min | Settle + the "Split, Add, Drop" chant |
| 6 min | Story / new game introduction |
| 15 min | Game / activity |
| 5 min | Sharing / reset |

## The daily chant

> "**Split!** the number. **Add!** the digits. **Drop!** the sum in the middle. That's the eleven-friend trick!"

Sing it / chorus it with claps. By Day 3 it should be effortless.

## Pacing notes

- Games > worksheets at this band. If a worksheet feels slow, drop it and add another game round.
- Day 5 (the carry-case hint) is a "wow" moment — keep it short and mysterious.
- Day 6 names the Sanskrit term ONCE. Don't drill it.
- Day 8 (peek at 3-digit ×11) is teacher-led demo only. Children watch and gasp; they don't do it themselves.
- Days 7–10 build to the game showcase. Confirm groups by Day 6.

## What children leave with

- Instant recall of any 2-digit × 11 (with digit-sum < 10).
- The "Split, Add, Drop" chant in their bones.
- One Sanskrit word: *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* (they don't need to spell it; they recognise it).
- One game they made and one they played.
- Curiosity about the carry case and 3-digit ×11 for next year.
