# Teacher Notes — Module 7 (Primary)

## Posture

Mental ×11 (no-carry) is the single skill we want every child to take home. Everything else — the chant, the game, the Sanskrit name, the peek at 3-digit — is scaffolding around this one win.

Children should leave the module able to:
- See **12** and say **132** in one breath.
- See **34** and say **374** in one breath.
- Recognise that something different happens for **73 × 11** (without necessarily solving it).

## Common things to handle

1. **A child says "What about 73 × 11? You said 7 + 3 but that's 10."** Honour the curiosity. *"Big question — that's why older kids' brains get to do more. We'll see a hint on Day 5."*
2. **A child can't yet add single digits fluently.** The trick won't work until single-digit addition is automatic. Spend 5 extra minutes per class on "make 10" pair recall.
3. **A child knows the trick already.** Make them the "demo kid" — they show the next problem to the class. Refocus their advantage as social, not arithmetic.
4. **A child says "1 + 9 is 10, you can't write 10 in the middle!"** Brilliant — that's the carry case. Day 5 covers it; until then, hand-pick examples that avoid it.

## Safety

- No physical risk in this module.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Grade 5 children may be ready for the carry case (87 × 11 = 957). Pull them aside on Day 5 for the full procedure.
- **Tier down:** Grade 3 children still building single-digit addition need more chant time and slower examples. Skip Day 8's 3-digit demo if the no-carry case isn't solid.

## Sanskrit pronunciation guide for teachers

*Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* = eh-KAA-dhi-keh-na POOR-veh-na.

Say it once on Day 6. Don't drill it. The win is that children **recognise** the word when they hear it again in Middle band.

## Honest framing (for parent questions)

If a parent asks "Is this real Vedic mathematics?" — answer:

> *"The trick is real and useful. It comes from Tirthaji's book* Vedic Mathematics, *published in 1965. Whether the trick is genuinely ancient or a 20th-century synthesis is debated by scholars. Senior band tackles that debate directly. We teach the technique honestly."*

Don't oversell antiquity. Don't dismiss it either.

## Project oversight

- Days 7–10: children design an "Eleven Friend" game in pairs.
- The game just needs to USE the ×11 trick somewhere (e.g. cards: turn over a 2-digit card; first to call its ×11 wins the card).
- Materials limit: only what's in the classroom. Don't expect families to provide game supplies.
- Mixed-age project pairs work well; pair a fluent child with one still building.

## If you only have 6 days

- Day 1: Meet Your Eleven-Friend
- Day 2: Chant + Friend Game
- Day 3: Speed Day
- Day 4: Hint at carry case + Sanskrit name
- Day 5: Project Day
- Day 6: Game Showcase

Skip the dedicated 3-digit demo.
