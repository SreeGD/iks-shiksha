# Day 8 — Tirthaji and Historical Context

**Module:** ×11 Multiplication — Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Explain who Tirthaji was and why scholars debate the "Vedic" attribution.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *sūtra* (IAST: sūtra; lit. "aphorism / formula")

- The trick comes from a book by Bharati Krishna Tirthaji, who lived about 100 years ago.
- He named it after old Sanskrit ideas. Today, scholars are not sure if the methods are really thousands of years old. But the *trick still works* — and that's what matters for us!

Show a photo (or drawing) of Tirthaji. Children put a thumbs-up if they like the trick.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Mental Math Sprint Cards* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
