# Movement of the Sun

**Band:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Sessions:** 10 × 30 min · **Sanskrit:** Sūrya-gati — Uttarāyaṇa and Dakshiṇāyana

## Overview

Earth's axial tilt produces an apparent annual path of the Sun along the ecliptic. Indian astronomy names the northward course *Uttarāyaṇa* and southward course *Dakshiṇāyana*, with 12 *Rāśi* (zodiacal divisions) and *saṁkrānti* moments when the Sun crosses between them. Sāyana (tropical) vs nirayana (sidereal) reference frames are introduced for senior students.

## Key terms (this module)

- *Sūrya* — the Sun
- *Uttarāyaṇa* — northward solar course (Dec 21 – June 21)
- *Dakṣiṇāyana* — southward solar course (June 21 – Dec 21)
- *saṁkrānti* — Sun's crossing from one Rāśi to the next
- *rāśi* — 1/12th of the ecliptic; zodiacal sign
- *sāyana* — tropical zodiac (linked to seasons)
- *nirayana* — sidereal zodiac (linked to fixed stars)

## 10-day map

- **Day 1** — The Sun's Yearly Path: Observe that sunrise direction changes; introduce the ecliptic.
- **Day 2** — Axial Tilt and the Seasons: Demonstrate with torch+globe why we have summer and winter.
- **Day 3** — Uttarāyaṇa and Dakshiṇāyana: Identify the dates and direction of each.
- **Day 4** — Equinoxes — Viṣuva: Identify spring and autumn equinoxes (day = night).
- **Day 5** — Twelve Rāśis: Name the 12 rāśis in Sanskrit and pair with Greek zodiac names.
- **Day 6** — Saṁkrānti Moments: Identify Makara, Karka, and other saṁkrāntis on the calendar.
- **Day 7** — Sāyana vs Nirayana: Distinguish tropical and sidereal zodiacs; introduce ayanāṁśa.
- **Day 8** — Precession of the Equinoxes: Explain why Indian and Western zodiac dates differ today.
- **Day 9** — Cross-Cultural Solar Calendars: Compare with Egyptian, Roman, Gregorian.
- **Day 10** — Final Share and Assessment: Project share + summative quiz.
