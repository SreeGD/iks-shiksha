# Module 12 — Movement of the Sun (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Sūrya-gati* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

Solar year, solstices, equinoxes, and the 12 rāśis.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 12's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

*"Where did the sun rise today? Where will it set?"* Point. *"Did you know the sunrise direction CHANGES through the year? Today we learn why."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**The Sun moves on a big yearly path:**

- **Uttarāyaṇa (उत्तरायण)** — northward course — Dec to June — days get longer
- **Dakṣiṇāyana (दक्षिणायन)** — southward course — June to Dec — days get shorter

Two special days:
- **Solstice** — sun is at its farthest north (June) or south (Dec)
- **Equinox** — day = night (March 21, Sept 22)

12 zodiac signs (rāśis) for 12 months: chant them — Meṣa, Vṛṣabha, Mithuna, Karkaṭa, Siṁha, Kanyā, Tulā, Vṛścika, Dhanus, Makara, Kumbha, Mīna.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

In a sunny window: mark today's sunrise direction. Compare with a marked sunrise direction from 3 months ago (or imagine). Show the chant: *"Uttarāyaṇa! Dakṣiṇāyana!"*

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Tell the story of how the Sun visits 12 houses (rāśis) each year, taking one month at each — like a friend visiting 12 cousins.

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

Each child marks 4 special days on a year-circle (Mar 21, Jun 21, Sep 22, Dec 21) and labels them. Decorate.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child says one thing they learned about the Sun. Cheer.
