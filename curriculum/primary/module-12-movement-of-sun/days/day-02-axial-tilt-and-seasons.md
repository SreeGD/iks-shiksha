# Day 2 — Axial Tilt and the Seasons

**Module:** Movement of the Sun (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Demonstrate with torch+globe why we have summer and winter.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *Uttarāyaṇa* (IAST: Uttarāyaṇa; lit. "northward solar course (Dec 21 – June 21)")

- Why do we have seasons?

Demo: hold a ball (Earth) tilted to one side. Shine a torch (Sun) at it. Move the ball around the torch.
- Sometimes the TOP of the ball gets more light → summer.
- Sometimes the BOTTOM gets more → other hemisphere's summer.

*"Earth is TILTED. That's why we have seasons!"* Children draw a tilted Earth with the Sun.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sunrise Direction Tracking* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
