# Day 1 — The Sun's Yearly Path

**Module:** Movement of the Sun (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Observe that sunrise direction changes; introduce the ecliptic.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *Sūrya* (IAST: Sūrya; lit. "the Sun")

- The Sun rises in different places through the year!

Stand at a window facing east. Mark where the Sun comes up TODAY. (Just outside the window — point with arms.)

In summer, the Sun rises a bit more to the LEFT. In winter, more to the RIGHT (in northern places).

Children draw a window with the Sun in 3 spots: summer (left), middle (spring/autumn), winter (right). One drawing.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sunrise Direction Tracking* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
