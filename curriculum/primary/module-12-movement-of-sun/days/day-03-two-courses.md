# Day 3 — Uttarāyaṇa and Dakshiṇāyana

**Module:** Movement of the Sun (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Identify the dates and direction of each.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *Dakṣiṇāyana* (IAST: Dakṣiṇāyana; lit. "southward solar course (June 21 – Dec 21)")

- The Sun has two journeys each year:
  - **Uttarāyaṇa** ("Going North"): Dec to June — days get longer in the north.
  - **Dakṣiṇāyana** ("Going South"): June to Dec — days get shorter in the north.

Children stand with hands held high. Drift to the right (north). *"That's Uttarāyaṇa!"* Drift to the left (south). *"That's Dakṣiṇāyana!"*

Each child draws an arrow on a calendar showing today's direction.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sunrise Direction Tracking* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
