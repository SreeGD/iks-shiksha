# Day 4 — Equinoxes — Viṣuva

**Module:** Movement of the Sun (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Identify spring and autumn equinoxes (day = night).

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *saṁkrānti* (IAST: saṁkrānti; lit. "Sun's crossing from one Rāśi to the next")

- Two special days each year: day and night are EXACTLY the same length!

These are called **equinoxes** (or *viṣuva*).
- **Spring equinox** (around March 21): days getting longer.
- **Autumn equinox** (around September 22): days getting shorter.

Children stand still with arms balanced equally. *"Day equals night!"*

Draw two suns on a year-circle, one in March, one in September. Label "equinox."

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sunrise Direction Tracking* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
