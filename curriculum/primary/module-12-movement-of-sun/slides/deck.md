# Movement of the Sun
## Solar year, solstices, equinoxes, and the 12 Rāśis
### Primary band — Grades 3–5


---
## What this module is about


Earth's axial tilt produces an apparent annual path of the Sun along the ecliptic. Indian astronomy names the northward course *Uttarāyaṇa* and southward course *Dakshiṇāyana*, with 12 *Rāśi* (zodiacal divisions) and *saṁkrānti* moments when the Sun crosses between them. Sāyana (tropical) vs nirayana (sidereal) reference frames are introduced for senior students.

## Key Sanskrit terms


- *Sūrya* — the Sun

- *Uttarāyaṇa* — northward solar course (Dec 21 – June 21)

- *Dakṣiṇāyana* — southward solar course (June 21 – Dec 21)

- *saṁkrānti* — Sun's crossing from one Rāśi to the next

- *rāśi* — 1/12th of the ecliptic; zodiacal sign

- *sāyana* — tropical zodiac (linked to seasons)

- *nirayana* — sidereal zodiac (linked to fixed stars)

## 10-day arc


---
## Day 1 — The Sun's Yearly Path

Observe that sunrise direction changes; introduce the ecliptic.


---
## Day 2 — Axial Tilt and the Seasons

Demonstrate with torch+globe why we have summer and winter.


---
## Day 3 — Uttarāyaṇa and Dakshiṇāyana

Identify the dates and direction of each.


---
## Day 4 — Equinoxes — Viṣuva

Identify spring and autumn equinoxes (day = night).


---
## Day 5 — Twelve Rāśis

Name the 12 rāśis in Sanskrit and pair with Greek zodiac names.


---
## Day 6 — Saṁkrānti Moments

Identify Makara, Karka, and other saṁkrāntis on the calendar.


---
## Day 7 — Sāyana vs Nirayana

Distinguish tropical and sidereal zodiacs; introduce ayanāṁśa.


---
## Day 8 — Precession of the Equinoxes

Explain why Indian and Western zodiac dates differ today.


---
## Day 9 — Cross-Cultural Solar Calendars

Compare with Egyptian, Roman, Gregorian.


---
## Day 10 — Final Share and Assessment

Project share + summative quiz.

## Activity


**Sunrise Direction Tracking**


Over 5 days, students note the compass bearing of sunrise each morning (from the same window). They graph the bearing and predict how it will change in 3 months.

---
## Project


**Personal Solar Calendar**


Make a one-page solar calendar for the current year: mark the two solstices, two equinoxes, all 12 saṁkrānti dates, plus your own birthday with its rāśi.
