# Module 11 — The Multiply-Near-Ten Trick (Towards Nikhilam)

**Age band:** Primary · **Grades:** 3–5 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 30 min

## What children will learn

1. The deficits from 10: 1 → 9, 2 → 8, 3 → 7, 4 → 6, 5 → 5, 6 → 4, 7 → 3, 8 → 2, 9 → 1. Instantly.
2. A finger trick (the "hand multiplication" trick) for multiplying any two digits from 6 to 9.
3. The idea that you can multiply two "near 10" numbers by combining the deficits.
4. A peek at the full Nikhilam method (*Nikhilam*) as a "trick the older kids will learn."
5. A drawing project: design a poster or comic that teaches the finger trick to a younger child.

## How this module sounds

- Lots of dot patterns. Finger play. Chanting.
- No long worksheets. Children draw, count, talk, chant.
- Sanskrit term *Nikhilam* introduced once on Day 6 as "the older-grade name for this trick."

## Prerequisites

- Comfortable with multiplication facts up to 5 × 5.
- Can count fingers up and down with both hands.
- Can write single digits clearly.

## Materials

- Class wall-poster: "Deficits from 10" (1↔9, 2↔8, 3↔7, 4↔6, 5↔5).
- A4 drawing paper, crayons, coloured pencils.
- Counters (beads or buttons) — 10 per child.
- Dice (one per pair).
- Finger-trick demo card (teacher prepared, see Day 3).

## Day-by-day

| Day | Title | What we do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Make the Twin | Card game: find the "twin" that makes 10 with each card |
| 2 | Twins Chant | Memorise the 9 deficit pairs |
| 3 | The Finger Trick | Hand-multiplication for 6-9 × 6-9 |
| 4 | Why the Trick Works (drawing) | Dot patterns showing 7 × 8 = 56 |
| 5 | Pairs Race | Speed game with the finger trick |
| 6 | Meet Nikhilam | Brief demo by teacher of 97 × 96 |
| 7 | Design a Game | Children design a Make-the-Twin game |
| 8 | Project Day 1 | Start a poster or comic of the finger trick |
| 9 | Project Day 2 | Finish + decorate |
| 10 | Showcase | Stick posters on wall; each child explains theirs in 1 minute |

## Project

Children design a **poster or 4-panel comic** that teaches the finger-multiplication trick (for 6–9 × 6–9) to a younger child. They share it on Day 10.

See `assessments/project-brief.md`.

## Sources

The finger-multiplication trick is a folk method taught widely in Indian schools and globally. The Sanskrit name *Nikhilam* and the full multiplication procedure come from Bharati Krishna Tirthaji's *Vedic Mathematics* (1965). For older grades' deeper treatment, see the Middle-band version of this module.
