# Module 11 — Multiplication Near a Power of 10 (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Nikhilam* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

Fast multiplication when numbers are near 10, 100, or 1000.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 11's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

Write 9 × 8 on the board. *"Quick answer? 72."* Now write 99 × 98. *"That's harder. Today: a trick."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**For numbers JUST BELOW 100, use this trick:**

99 × 98:
- 99 is 100 − 1 (deficit 1)
- 98 is 100 − 2 (deficit 2)
- Cross-subtract: 99 − 2 = 97. (Same as 98 − 1 = 97.)
- Multiply deficits: 1 × 2 = 2.
- Answer: 9702!

Chant: *"Cross-subtract, multiply deficits!"*

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Practice in chorus: 9 × 8, 8 × 7, 7 × 6 (single digit deficits from 10). Then 99 × 98, 98 × 97 (deficits from 100).

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Tell the story of an old number-trick from India that lets you multiply BIG numbers if they are close to 100.

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

Worksheet: 8 problems of NN × NN where both numbers are 91-99. Pairs check.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child does one near-100 problem aloud. Cheer.
