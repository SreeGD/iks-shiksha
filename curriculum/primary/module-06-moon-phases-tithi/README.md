# Module 6 — Moon Phases & Tithi

**Age band:** Primary · **Grades:** 3–5 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 30 min (≈ 2 weeks)

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, children will be able to:

1. **Recognise** the Moon as something they see in the sky, and describe at least four different shapes it takes through a month.
2. **Name** *amāvasyā* (new moon) and *pūrṇimā* (full moon) and say what shape each one is.
3. **Tell** the story: the Sun lights up the Moon, and we see different bits as it travels.
4. **Keep** a 10-night Moon-shape diary, drawing what they see each night.
5. **Say** four of the 15 tithi names — *pratipadā, pañcamī, aṣṭamī, pūrṇimā* — as the "anchor" tithis.

## NEP 2020 alignment

- **§4.6** — Integration of Indian Knowledge Systems with early science.
- **§4.23** — Experiential learning through nightly Moon observation and torch-and-ball play.
- **§4.27** — Local context: children link the Moon to festivals their families celebrate.

## What we don't teach yet

Reserved for Middle and Senior bands:
- The 12° elongation rule
- *Kṣaya* and *vṛddhi* tithis
- *Yoga, karaṇa, nakṣatra* (mentioned only in passing if children ask)
- *Adhika māsa*
- Eclipse geometry (mentioned with safety only)
- Cross-cultural calendar comparison

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with night/day.
- Comfort with drawing simple shapes.
- No Sanskrit knowledge assumed.

## Key vocabulary (one new word per day, repeated)

- *Moon* (English — even Primary children learn the English word fresh too)
- *Sun*
- *amāvasyā* — new moon
- *pūrṇimā* — full moon
- *pakṣa* — fortnight (introduced softly: "the growing half" and "the shrinking half")
- *tithi* — a "moon day"
- Four anchor tithi names: *pratipadā, pañcamī, aṣṭamī, pūrṇimā*

## Materials checklist (whole module)

- A torch (flashlight)
- A small white ball (a table-tennis ball or styrofoam ball)
- A darkened classroom for 10 minutes
- A printed Moon-shape sticker sheet (8 stickers per child — see Day 5)
- Drawing paper + crayons
- Project supplies (poster paper, glue sticks)

## Day-by-day overview

| Day | Title | Focus |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 1 | The Moon Story | Story + torch-and-ball demo |
| 2 | The Two Halves | New moon and full moon; pakṣa idea |
| 3 | The Moon's House Visits | The story of the Moon visiting nakṣatra "houses" (folktale framing) |
| 4 | Drawing the Moon | Each child sketches 4 phases |
| 5 | The Four Tithi Friends | Pratipadā, pañcamī, aṣṭamī, pūrṇimā |
| 6 | Festivals and the Moon | When does Holī happen? When Dīwālī? |
| 7 | Safety + the Moon Goes Dark | Eclipse safety; "the Sun must never be looked at" |
| 8 | Moon Project — Make Your Own Calendar | Sticker calendar |
| 9 | Moon Project — Decorate and Present | Posters with phases |
| 10 | Show and Tell | Each child shows their Moon calendar and says one phase name |

## Project (one line)

> Each child makes a "Moon calendar" sticker poster of 10 nights, with the Moon shape and the date for each night. On Day 10 they show it and name two phases.

## A note for teachers

This module is observational and joyful. We don't compute anything. We don't test on Sanskrit spelling. The point is that a child leaves this module knowing the Moon changes shape, why (the Sun lights it up), and that there are special names for the new-moon and full-moon nights.
