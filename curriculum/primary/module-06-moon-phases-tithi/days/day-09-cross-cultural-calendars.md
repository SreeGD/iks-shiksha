# Day 9 — Cross-cultural Calendars

**Module:** Moon Phases and Tithi (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Compare with Hijri (Islamic), Chinese, and Hebrew lunar calendars.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *candra* (IAST: candra; lit. "Moon")

- People all over the world watch the Moon, but each tradition counts months a bit differently.
- Some use only Moon (Islamic Hijri). Some use Sun + Moon (Indian, Chinese, Hebrew). Some use only Sun (modern Gregorian — like the school year).

Show 4 calendar pictures and chant: *"India and China — Moon plus Sun! Islam — Moon alone! Modern world — Sun alone!"*

Each child colours a small "moon flag" with the calendar they think they use most.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *10-Day Moon Observation Diary* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
