# Module 6 — Moon Phases and Tithi (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Candra-pakṣa, Tithi* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

The moon's monthly cycle and the Indian lunar calendar.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 6's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

Show a chart of moon phases. *"Did you see the moon last night? Was it round, half, or thin? Today we learn why it changes shape!"*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**The moon changes shape because it goes around Earth.**

Two halves of the moon-month:
- **Śukla pakṣa** (शुक्ल पक्ष) — bright half — moon grows from tiny to full
- **Kṛṣṇa pakṣa** (कृष्ण पक्ष) — dark half — moon shrinks from full to tiny

Each day in each half has a number (tithi 1 to 15). Sing-chant the numbers.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Use a lamp (Sun), a tennis ball (moon), and a child's head (Earth) — walk the moon around Earth, watching which side is lit. Children take turns being Moon.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Tell the moon-grandmother story: the moon visits 30 friends each month (tithis), splitting her time between bright friends (śukla pakṣa) and shadow friends (kṛṣṇa pakṣa).

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

Each child draws today's moon and the moon for tomorrow (predicted). Compare with classmates. Send home with a prompt to observe tonight.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child says one thing they learned about the moon. Cheer.
