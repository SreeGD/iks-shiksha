# Module 6 (Primary) — 10-Day Lesson Plan

## At a glance

| Day | Session title | Lesson file | What happens |
|-----|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | The Moon Story | `days/day-01-moon-story.md` | Story + torch-and-ball demo |
| 2 | The Two Halves | `days/day-02-two-halves.md` | New moon, full moon, growing/shrinking |
| 3 | The Moon's House Visits | `days/day-03-house-visits.md` | Folktale framing of the Moon's monthly journey |
| 4 | Drawing the Moon | `days/day-04-drawing-the-moon.md` | Each child draws 4 phases |
| 5 | The Four Tithi Friends | `days/day-05-four-tithis.md` | Naming pratipadā, pañcamī, aṣṭamī, pūrṇimā |
| 6 | Festivals and the Moon | `days/day-06-festivals.md` | Holī, Dīwālī, Karva Cauth — when do they happen? |
| 7 | Safety + the Moon Goes Dark | `days/day-07-safety.md` | Eclipse safety + brief "sometimes the Moon goes dark" |
| 8 | Make Your Moon Calendar | `days/day-08-make-calendar.md` | Sticker calendar project |
| 9 | Decorate and Practise | `days/day-09-decorate.md` | Project finishing + practising "show and tell" |
| 10 | Show and Tell | `days/day-10-show-and-tell.md` | Each child presents |

## Pacing notes

- Sessions are 30 minutes, not 45.
- Day 7's safety lesson is the most important non-fun moment. Be firm, then move on.
- Project work fits in Days 8 and 9. Day 10 is presentation only.

## Running theme

Open every class with: **"Did you see the Moon last night? What shape was it?"** Two children share.

## What children leave with

- A 10-night moon diary (with simple sketches)
- A sticker-Moon calendar poster
- Two Sanskrit words they say correctly: *amāvasyā* and *pūrṇimā*
- A torch-and-ball memory of why the Moon changes shape
- The habit of looking up
