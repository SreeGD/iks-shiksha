# Moon Phases and Tithi
## The Moon's monthly cycle and the Indian lunar calendar
### Primary band — Grades 3–5


---
## What this module is about


The Moon's apparent change of shape across ~29.5 days is the synodic month. Indian astronomy divides this into 30 *tithi*s (lunar days), grouped into a waxing fortnight (*śukla pakṣa*) and waning fortnight (*kṛṣṇa pakṣa*). This module connects observation, lunar geometry, calendar construction, and the panchanga framework.

## Key Sanskrit terms


- *candra* — Moon

- *pakṣa* — fortnight (half-month)

- *śukla pakṣa* — waxing fortnight (new → full)

- *kṛṣṇa pakṣa* — waning fortnight (full → new)

- *tithi* — lunar day (1/30 of synodic month)

- *amāvasyā* — new moon

- *pūrṇimā* — full moon

- *pañcāṅga* — five-limbed Indian calendar (tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa)

## 10-day arc


---
## Day 1 — The Moon and Its Shapes

Observe and name the four basic phases (new, first quarter, full, last quarter).


---
## Day 2 — Two Fortnights — Pakṣa

Distinguish śukla pakṣa from kṛṣṇa pakṣa and name today's.


---
## Day 3 — Fifteen Tithis per Pakṣa

Recite the 15 tithi names (pratipadā, dvitīyā, ...) and explain why a tithi isn't exactly a day.


---
## Day 4 — Building a Tithi Calendar

Construct a tithi calendar for the current month using observation and computation.


---
## Day 5 — Lunar Months and Festivals

Map the 12 lunar months (caitra, vaiśākha, ...) to major festivals.


---
## Day 6 — Panchanga — Five Limbs

Identify the five components: tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa.


---
## Day 7 — Why Eclipses Happen

Explain solar/lunar eclipses using orbital plane geometry (5° tilt of Moon's orbit).


---
## Day 8 — Modern Lunar Science

Phases as Sun's illumination of Moon; mention libration, tides.


---
## Day 9 — Cross-cultural Calendars

Compare with Hijri (Islamic), Chinese, and Hebrew lunar calendars.


---
## Day 10 — Synthesis and Quiz

Final quiz and 10-day moon-diary share.

---
## Activity


**10-Day Moon Observation Diary**


Each evening for 10 days, students sketch the moon shape they see, note the time, the tithi name, and one feeling/observation. On Day 10 they compare sketches with the actual calendar.

---
## Project


**Class Tithi Calendar for the Current Month**


Each student picks 3 dates in the current calendar month. They compute (or look up) the tithi, identify the pakṣa, and produce a visual class calendar with all entries combined.
