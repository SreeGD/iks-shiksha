# Activity 01 — Sort It (Day 6)

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 20 min within Day 6

## Goal

Children sort everyday objects into the five element trays in pairs.

## Materials

- 5 large baskets / trays, each labelled with element name + picture
- ~30 small objects (mix of children's bring-from-home + teacher-supplied)
- Sticker reward chart

## Set-up

- 5 trays arranged in a circle on the floor.
- Each pair gets 5 objects.

## Children's instructions

> Sort with your partner.
>
> 1. Look at your object.
> 2. Talk: "Is this mostly Pṛthvī? Ap? Tejas? Vāyu? Ākāśa?"
> 3. Put it on the right tray.
> 4. Say WHY out loud. "This is mostly ____ because ____."
> 5. Next object.
>
> When all 5 are sorted, walk around and look at other pairs' trays.

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Pair pointing at the object, talking, agreeing | "Look — this is solid, so it's Pṛthvī!" |
| Needs help | One child doing all the placing | Silence; one-word answers |

## Safety

- No sharp objects (teacher pre-checks).
- No food objects (allergies).

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find an object that fits 3 elements.
- **Tier down:** Sort only 3 objects. Partner helps.

## Clean-up

- Children take their objects back.
- Teacher resets trays.
