# Teacher Notes — Module 2 Panchabhūta (Primary)

## Tone for Primary

Story first. Definitions later. Drawing always.

At this age, children learn by *touching* and *naming* — not by understanding abstract frameworks. The Sāṅkhya creation sequence and the *tanmātra* mapping (which Middle band gets) are **completely out of scope** here. Keep it concrete.

## Five names, that's the win

If by Day 10 every child can say the five names (Sanskrit + English) and point to one example of each, the module has succeeded. Everything else is bonus.

## Common things to handle

1. **A child says "fire is dangerous, I don't like it."** Validate. *"Yes, fire is powerful. We respect it. The candle on the table is fire being safe."*
2. **A child asks "where does space come from?"** Honest answer: *"Nobody is fully sure. The old Indian thinkers said it was the first thing to appear. Modern scientists are still working it out."*
3. **A child says their mum says yoga/Sanskrit is religious.** Acknowledge respectfully. *"Some families pray with these words. We're learning them as names for things we can see."*

## Safety

- No actual fire in the classroom. Candles unlit; warmth from sunlight or a tube light only.
- Element walk (Day 7) — accompanied by adult OR done on school grounds at break.
- No tasting samples (no allergy risk).

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Some Grade 5 children will be ready for the "quality count" idea (earth = 5, water = 4...). Introduce it as a counting game.
- **Tier down:** If a child can't remember all five names by Day 5, focus on three (*pṛthvī, ap, vāyu*). The other two can come in revision.

## Project oversight

- Day 8: every child commits to ONE element and ONE format. Don't allow project changes after Day 9 starts.
- Day 10: 30-second show-and-tell. If a child is shy, they can sit next to you while you describe their project for them.

## If you only have 6 days

- Day 1: Story + Earth, Water
- Day 2: Fire, Air
- Day 3: Space + Song
- Day 4: Sort It
- Day 5: One project session
- Day 6: Show + Tell

Skip the imbalance walk in compression.
