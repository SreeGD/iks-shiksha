# Module 2 (Primary) — 10-Day Lesson Plan

## At a glance (35-min sessions)

| Day | Title | Activity | Output |
|-----|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Five Friends Story | Story + meet sample objects | Drawing of one element |
| 2 | Earth and Water | Touch + sort | Drawing |
| 3 | Fire and Air | Feel warmth + blow on hand | Drawing |
| 4 | Space — the Quiet Friend | Notice the gap | Drawing |
| 5 | Five-Element Song | Sing the names | Recall check |
| 6 | Sort It | Big sorting activity | Group portrait of trays |
| 7 | Find an Imbalance | Walk + draw | Map drawing |
| 8 | Project Day 1 | Start your element project | Project draft |
| 9 | Project Day 2 | Finish + practise telling | Project ready |
| 10 | Show + Tell | Each child shows for 30 sec | Final project + sticker |

## Daily structure (35 min)

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 5 min | Settle + the daily chant (the five names) |
| 10 min | Story or new content |
| 15 min | Hands-on / drawing |
| 5 min | Show what you made (1–2 children) |

## The daily chant

Same chorus every day:

> *pṛthvī, ap, tejas, vāyu, ākāśa*
> Earth, water, fire, air, space!

Say it 3 times — soft, normal, loud. Add a clap on each name.

## Pacing notes

- 35 minutes is short — protect it. Don't let the story creep into the activity time.
- The hands-on time is the most important. Drawing IS the learning at this band.
- Skip Day 5 if your class is moving fast; the song can be a warm-up on other days.

## What children leave with

- The five names sung as a chant.
- One drawing per element in their drawing folder.
- One project they can show to a younger sibling.
- The habit of asking "what kind of thing is this?" when they look at an object.
