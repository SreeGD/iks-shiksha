# Day 7 — Find an Imbalance

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min (10 min outside)

## Goal

Children walk around the school grounds and find one place where an element is "too much" or "missing." They draw and tell the class about it.

## Materials

- Drawing paper + clipboards (or hard surfaces)
- Crayons
- Adult supervisor accompanying the walk

## The flow

### Settle + chant + song (5 min)

Daily chant + song.

### Story — what is imbalance? (5 min)

- *"Sometimes one of our five friends is too much, or one is missing. When that happens, the others suffer."*
- Examples (with simple drawings on the board):
  - Too much Vāyu = a strong wind storm. Trees fall.
  - Too little Ap = no rain. Plants die.
  - Too much Pṛthvī of the wrong kind = a pile of plastic waste. Smelly.
  - Too noisy Ākāśa = traffic horn outside the school. We can't sleep or study.

### The walk (10 min)

- Walk around the school grounds in a single line. Adult at the front and back.
- Children carry clipboard + paper.
- Look for ONE place where they think an element is too much or missing.

### Draw + share (12 min)

- Back in the classroom.
- Each child draws what they saw and writes ONE sentence (teacher helps): *"There is too much / too little ___________ at __________."*

- Quick share: 4 children show their drawings.

### Show + chant (3 min)

Close with chant.

## Homework

- Tomorrow we start projects. Think: which ONE element do you want to work on?

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Think of ONE small thing your class could do to help (pick up a piece of trash, plant a seed, save water at the tap).
- **Tier down:** Pick any element. The "imbalance" can be simply "I saw a lot of ____ today" without judgement.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Don't let this become depressing. Every child should also notice ONE place where the elements are in good balance (a healthy tree, clean water, a quiet corner).
- For schools in a tough environment, focus the walk on the school grounds and ONE specific issue (e.g. litter). Don't try to cover everything.
