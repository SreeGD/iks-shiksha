# Day 3 — Fire and Air

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children explore *tejas* and *vāyu* through warmth, breeze, and drawing.

## Materials

- Sunlight through a window (if possible) OR a tube light / desk lamp
- One or two balloons
- A small handheld paper fan (or each child folds one)
- Drawing paper

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Daily chant.

### Story — recall (3 min)

*"Who remembers our five friends?"* — chorus all five.
*"Yesterday we met Pṛthvī and Ap. Today: Tejas and Vāyu."*

### Feel station: Fire/warmth (7 min)

- Take children to a sunny window or under a warm tube light.
- *"Put your hand up. What do you feel?"* (Warm.)
- *"That warmth is Tejas. The sun gives us Tejas. A candle gives us Tejas. Your body makes a little Tejas — touch your hand to your cheek."*
- *"Tejas is also what cooks our food. Tejas is what helps us digest. Tejas is everywhere there is heat or light."*

(No actual flame in class. Children's bring-from-home objects — torches, candle pictures — can be shared.)

### Feel station: Air (7 min)

- Show a balloon. Inflate and release; watch it fly.
- *"What was inside the balloon?"* (Air.)
- Give each child a fan (or make one from paper).
- Fan their own face. *"That's Vāyu. When the wind blows your hair, that's Vāyu. When you breathe in, that's Vāyu coming inside you."*
- *"Everything that flows and is invisible — that's Vāyu."*

### Draw (10 min)

Each child draws:
- TOP: one Tejas thing they like.
- BOTTOM: one Vāyu thing they like.

### Show + chant (3 min)

2 shares. Daily chant to close.

## Homework / take-home

- Tomorrow: notice the **empty space** in your bedroom. How big is it? Can you fit your arms inside without touching anything?

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Why is the sun TOO MUCH Tejas in summer? Why is wind TOO MUCH Vāyu during a storm?
- **Tier down:** Just remember: Tejas = warmth/light, Vāyu = air/wind.

## Teacher's quick notes

- For the fire station: NO actual flames. Children's torches/lamps are safe. Body warmth (touching one's own hand) is the most reliable demo.
- Some children will giggle when they feel a breeze on their face. Let them giggle — surprise is good for memory.
