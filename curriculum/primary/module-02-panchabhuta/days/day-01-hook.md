# Day 1 — Five Friends Story

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children meet the five elements through a story and a touch-station, and can name at least three by end of class.

## Materials

- 5 sample objects: rock, water in a clear jar, candle (unlit), balloon, empty box
- Drawing paper + crayons

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Sit on the mat. Teacher: *"We're starting something new. For the next two weeks we'll meet **five friends**."*

Teach the chant (3 times: soft / normal / loud, clap on each name):

> *pṛthvī, ap, tejas, vāyu, ākāśa*
> Earth, water, fire, air, space!

### Story (10 min)

> **The Five Friends**
>
> Once there were five friends. Each one was made of something different.
>
> The first friend was **Pṛthvī**. She was made of **earth**. She was solid and steady. You could stand on her, build a house on her, plant a seed in her. (Hold up the rock.)
>
> The second friend was **Ap**. He was made of **water**. He flowed and changed shape. He could fill any cup, any bottle, any pond. (Hold up the water jar.)
>
> The third friend was **Tejas**. He was made of **fire**. He gave warmth and light. The sun is his big brother. (Hold up the candle — *"this would be Tejas if we lit it"*.)
>
> The fourth friend was **Vāyu**. She was made of **air**. She moved invisibly. When she ran past you, you felt a breeze. (Hold up the balloon.)
>
> The fifth friend was **Ākāśa**. She was made of **space**. She didn't have a shape or colour. She was where all the other friends lived. (Hold up the empty box — *"look — Ākāśa is INSIDE the box, where there's nothing."*)
>
> All five friends were everywhere — in this room, in your home, even inside your body. Today we'll go meet them.

### Touch + see (10 min)

Set the 5 objects out. Children go in pairs to each object, look and touch (where safe), and chorus the name as they touch.

### Draw (8 min)

Each child draws ONE element (their favourite) on paper. Write the Sanskrit name at the top (teacher helps spell).

### Show + chant (2 min)

3 children hold up their drawings. Class chants the name of that element.

End with the daily chant once.

## Homework / take-home

- At home tonight: find ONE thing for each of the five elements. Bring one of them to class tomorrow (small enough to fit in your pocket or a small bag).

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Draw all five elements on one page.
- **Tier down:** Just remember three names — *pṛthvī, ap, vāyu*. The other two can come later this week.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Sing the chant slowly the first time. Speed up over the week.
- Some children will laugh at "Ākāśa is empty space." That's fine. The laugh helps it stick.
