# Day 5 — Five-Element Song

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children memorise all five names (Sanskrit + English) through a song they can sing in chorus.

## Materials

- (Optional) A simple drum or clapper for rhythm
- Five large picture cards (one per element)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant — by now flying.

### Song (10 min)

Teach a simple tune to the chant. Suggested tune: *"Twinkle Twinkle Little Star"* (any familiar nursery tune works).

Verse 1:
> *Pṛthvī, ap, tejas, vāyu, ākāśa*
> *Five great friends are everywhere*
> *Earth and water, fire and air,*
> *And the space that's everywhere.*

Verse 2 (optional, slightly older children):
> *Pṛthvī holds us, ap flows free,*
> *Tejas warms us, vāyu's breeze,*
> *Ākāśa makes the room for all,*
> *Five friends standing tall.*

Sing 3 times. Add claps on the Sanskrit names.

### Recall game (10 min)

Hold up each picture card. Class chants the Sanskrit name.

Then turn the card face-down. Class chants the Sanskrit name from memory.

Then mix the order. Class chants whichever name you point to.

### Drawing / quiet time (10 min)

Each child completes a drawing book entry:
- 5 small boxes on one page.
- One element name in each box (teacher helps).
- One small picture in each box.

### Show + chant (2 min)

Hold up the best drawings (without ranking). Close with the song.

## Homework

- Tomorrow we sort! Bring 2 small objects from home — anything safe and small.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Make up a Verse 3 of the song with your own examples.
- **Tier down:** Sing along — don't worry if the words aren't perfect yet.

## Teacher's quick notes

- The song is *the* memory tool of this module. Sing it at every opportunity for the rest of the year.
- If a child won't sing, let them clap the rhythm instead.
