# Day 8 — Project Day 1

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Each child picks ONE element and starts a project (drawing, 3D model, or story).

## Materials

- Drawing paper, crayons, scissors, glue, recycled materials (newspaper, cardboard, fabric scraps)
- Clay or play-dough for 3D models
- Audio recorder (optional) for story-tellers

## The flow

### Settle + chant + song (5 min)

### Project choice (5 min)

- Each child picks ONE element.
- Each child picks ONE format:
  - **Drawing** — a big picture of the element with one example from home
  - **3D model** — clay, recycled junk, etc.
  - **Story** — a 30-second spoken story about the element

- Quick check: every child has decided.

### Project work (22 min)

- Children work individually or in twos at desks.
- Teacher rotates: 30 seconds per child, asking ONE question: *"What part of this is your favourite?"*

### Show + chant (3 min)

2 children share progress. Close with the song.

## Homework

- Finish any drawing/model at home if you didn't finish in class. Story-tellers practise your story 3 times.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Show two elements interacting (rain falling on soil = Ap + Pṛthvī).
- **Tier down:** Drawing is the easiest format. Just pick one element and one picture.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Some children will get stuck on "what to draw." Offer a one-sentence prompt: *"Draw your favourite Pṛthvī thing at home."*
- Watch for materials disputes — keep clay and glue stations supervised.
