# Day 2 — Earth and Water

**Module:** Panchabhūta (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children explore *pṛthvī* and *ap* through touching, sorting, and drawing.

## Materials

- Bring-from-home objects (each child has 1)
- A bowl of soil, a bowl of small pebbles, a jug of water, a sponge
- Drawing paper

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Daily chant: *pṛthvī, ap, tejas, vāyu, ākāśa* — 3 times, clapping.

### Story — recall (3 min)

*"Yesterday we met five friends. Who can name them?"* — chorus.

*"Today we meet two of them more closely: Pṛthvī and Ap."*

### Touch station: Earth (7 min)

- Show the bowl of soil and the bowl of pebbles. Pass them around.
- Question: *"What does Pṛthvī feel like?"* (Hard. Soft. Cold. Rough. Smooth.)
- Together: *"All solid things are part of Pṛthvī. The floor is Pṛthvī. Your desk is Pṛthvī. Your bones are Pṛthvī."*

### Touch station: Water (7 min)

- Show the jug. Pour a little into a bowl. Show the sponge soaking it up.
- Question: *"What does Ap feel like?"* (Wet. Cold. Flowing. Soft.)
- Together: *"All liquid things are part of Ap. Your tears are Ap. The river is Ap. Milk is Ap."*

### Draw (10 min)

Each child folds a paper in half. Left side: 3 things that are mostly Pṛthvī. Right side: 3 things that are mostly Ap.

### Show + chant (3 min)

2 children hold up their drawings. Class names one Pṛthvī thing and one Ap thing.

Close with the chant.

## Homework / take-home

- Tomorrow bring: ONE thing that gives warmth or light (a candle? a torch? a picture of the sun?).

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find one thing that is BOTH Pṛthvī AND Ap (a sponge with water? a wet rock?). Draw it on the back.
- **Tier down:** Just sort the bring-from-home objects into 2 piles — solid or liquid. Drawing optional.

## Teacher's quick notes

- The sponge demonstration ("water inside something solid") is great for showing that real things often have *more than one* element.
- A child may say "my bones are not in the floor!" — agree, explain "Pṛthvī isn't just the *floor* — it's all solid stuff, including your bones, your hair, your fingernails."
