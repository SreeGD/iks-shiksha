# Parent Guide — Module 2: The Five Friends (Panchabhūta)

**Your child's age:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 30 min over ~2 weeks

## What your child is learning

The *pañca-mahābhūta* — five Sanskrit names for everyday things:
- **Pṛthvī** (earth) — solid things
- **Ap** (water) — liquid things
- **Tejas** (fire) — warmth, light
- **Vāyu** (air) — wind, breath
- **Ākāśa** (space) — empty room

This is a 2000+ year old framework from Indian philosophy for organising the everyday world. We teach it alongside what your child knows from school science (solid, liquid, gas) — not instead of it.

## What to expect this fortnight

**A daily chant.** Your child will come home singing:
> *Pṛthvī, ap, tejas, vāyu, ākāśa*
> Earth, water, fire, air, space!

That repetition is the whole point. By Day 5 your child should remember all five.

**Homework you'll see:**
- Bring 3 small objects from home (Day 1).
- Find one thing that's "all five qualities" (Day 2 — e.g. soil, jaggery).
- A 20-min walk to find a place where one element is "too much" or "missing" (Day 7 — pollution, etc.).
- A drawing / model / 30-second story project (Days 8–10).

**Day 10 = "Show + Tell."** Your child will stand up and show their project for 30 seconds. They get a sticker.

## How to help — and how NOT to help

**Helpful:**
- Ask "which of the five friends did you meet today?" at dinner.
- For the home walk, accompany your child — Grade 3–4 students shouldn't walk alone.
- For their project: provide materials (paper, crayons, recycled items) and let *them* decide what to make.
- Sing the chant with them. Repetition embeds.

**Not helpful:**
- Quizzing them on Sanskrit pronunciation. Approximate is fine.
- Doing the project for them. A wobbly drawing they did themselves beats a perfect one you made.
- Worrying about "are they doing it right?" — at this age the goal is *noticing*, not getting answers correct.

## Common questions

- **"Why Sanskrit?"** Because the framework comes from a tradition that used Sanskrit. We give the English meaning every time. It's no different from learning that "H₂O" is water — a label that opens a door to a body of knowledge.
- **"Is this religious?"** No — we teach it as Indian intellectual tradition (the framework comes from Sāṅkhya philosophy). Devotional aspects are honoured but not centred.
- **"Will this be tested?"** Not with a paper quiz at this age. Oral recall + a sticker for the Day 10 show-and-tell.

## What success looks like

By the end of the module, your child can:
- Name all five elements in Sanskrit and English.
- Point to one example of each in your home.
- Tell you, in their own words, a story about one of them.

That's it. Anything beyond this is a bonus.
