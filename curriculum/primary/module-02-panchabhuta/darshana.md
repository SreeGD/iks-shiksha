# Module 2 — Panchabhūta — The Five Elements (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Pañcabhūta* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

The classical Indian classification of matter.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 2's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

Hold a glass of water. *"What can water do?"* (Flow, splash, freeze, evaporate.) Now an ice cube. *"Still water?"* (Yes — different form.) *"In India long ago, people said matter has FIVE basic forms — like five friends. Today we meet them."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**Meet the five friends:**
- **Pṛthvī (पृथ्वी)** — Earth. *Solid stuff.* Rocks, mountains, bones.
- **Ap (अप्)** — Water. *Liquid stuff.* Rivers, blood, juice.
- **Tejas (तेजस्)** — Fire. *Warm stuff.* Sun, candles, body warmth.
- **Vāyu (वायु)** — Air. *Moving stuff.* Wind, breath, the smell of food.
- **Ākāśa (आकाश)** — Space. *Where everything fits.* The room, the sky, the gap between hands.

Make a hand sign for each friend. Practice 3 times.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

**Element walk.** Stand in a line. The teacher names one element; children mime it:
- Pṛthvī → stand strong like a mountain
- Ap → wave arms like water
- Tejas → flicker like a flame
- Vāyu → blow softly
- Ākāśa → spread arms wide and breathe deep

Repeat 3 times, faster each time. Cheer at the end.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Tell the story of *Pañcabhūta* as five family members of Earth. *"They each have a job. Pṛthvī gives us food. Ap gives us drinks. Tejas warms us. Vāyu brings us smells and breath. Ākāśa makes room for everyone."*

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Five-friends drawing.** Each child folds a paper into 5 sections. In each section, draw something for that element (sun for tejas, balloon for vāyu, river for ap, mountain for pṛthvī, sky for ākāśa). Decorate. Share.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child names their favourite element and why. Group hug at the end (representing ākāśa connecting everyone).
