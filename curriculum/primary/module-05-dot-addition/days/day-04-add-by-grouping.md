# Day 4 — Add by Grouping

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children add a column of 4–6 single-digit numbers by first grouping into 10s, then adding leftovers.

## Materials

- Dice (one per pair)
- Worksheet with 5 small columns
- Counters

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Daily chant.

### Demonstration (8 min)

Write on the board:
```
6
3
7
5
4
2
```

*"Today we add a column. But not the slow way. The smart way."*

Walk through:
- Look for pairs first. 6+4 = 10. (Cross out the 6 and the 4.)
- 3+7 = 10. (Cross out the 3 and the 7.)
- That leaves 5 and 2 = 7.
- So total = 10 + 10 + 7 = 27.

Compare: the slow way would be 6+3=9, +7=16, +5=21, +4=25, +2=27. Same answer, more effort.

### Dice game (15 min)

- Pairs play.
- Roll the die 5 times. Write the 5 numbers in a column.
- Both players race to find the answer using the grouping trick.
- First with correct answer = 1 point.
- 10 rounds. Most points wins.

### Worksheet (5 min, individual)

5 small columns. Find groups, write the answer.

### Show + chant (2 min)

Close.

## Homework

- 5 more dice rolls at home. Practise the trick with family.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Columns of 7–8 numbers; find as many 10-groups as possible.
- **Tier down:** Columns of 3–4 numbers; just find ONE pair.

## Teacher's quick notes

- The dice game introduces healthy competition with low stakes.
- Watch for children who add without looking for pairs first — gently steer them back to "look first."
