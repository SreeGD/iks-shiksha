# Day 7 — Make-a-Game Day

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

In pairs, children design (paper sketch + first prototype) their own "Make 10" game.

## Materials

- Drawing paper, markers
- Spare cards, dice, counters

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

### Inspiration (5 min)

- *"You've played lots of games this week. Now YOU design one. Your game must include the 'Make 10' idea — somehow."*
- Quick brainstorm. Take 5 student ideas. Write on board.

Examples:
- A card game where you collect pairs
- A board game with "Make 10" squares
- A jumping game where you jump to the number that makes 10
- A hide-and-seek game with number cards

### Design + sketch (15 min)

- Pairs decide on ONE game.
- Sketch the game on paper: rules, what you need, how to win.
- Make a quick prototype if possible.

### Test it (8 min)

- Each pair tries to play their own game once. Notice what works and what doesn't.

### Show + chant (2 min)

- 2 pairs share their game idea aloud.
- Daily chant.

## Homework

- Finish your game design at home tonight. Bring all materials tomorrow!

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add a "twist" — what makes your game extra fun or tricky?
- **Tier down:** Adapt an existing game (snakes and ladders, but every step you say a 10-pair).

## Teacher's quick notes

- This is creative day. Don't over-direct. Wild ideas welcome.
- If a pair is totally stuck, suggest: "modify Snakes and Ladders so every ladder requires a Make 10 answer."
