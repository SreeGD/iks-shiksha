# Day 2 — Bonds of Ten Chant

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children memorise the five 10-pairs through chant, hand games, and repeated quick-recall.

## Materials

- Wall poster of the 5 pairs (with pictures)
- Number cards 0–9

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Daily chant: *"1 and 9 make 10!..."*

### The hand game (10 min)

- Children pair up.
- Partner A holds up some fingers (1–9).
- Partner B holds up the rest to make 10.
- Check: do their fingers together = 10?
- Swap, repeat.

Play for 5 min. Then teacher leads class:
- Teacher holds up 6 fingers. Class chorus: "FOUR!"
- Teacher holds up 8. Class: "TWO!"
- Mix it up. Faster.

### Walk the pairs (10 min)

- 5 stations around the room, one per pair (1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5).
- Each station has a picture + the equation.
- Children walk to each station and say the pair aloud.

### Quick-fire recall (8 min)

Teacher holds up a number card. Class shouts the partner.

- 5 → "FIVE!" (5+5)
- 7 → "THREE!"
- 9 → "ONE!"
- etc.

Do 20 cards in 60 seconds. Then do it again to beat the time.

### Show + chant (2 min)

Close with the daily chant — bigger than ever.

## Homework

- At home tonight: parent or sibling holds up some fingers. You give the rest to make 10. Win 10 in a row!

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find pairs that make 11 too (5+6, 4+7).
- **Tier down:** Use a "make-10 chart" if recall is slow. Withdraw the chart by Day 5.

## Teacher's quick notes

- This is a memorisation day. Repetition over depth.
- The hand-game pairings often surface friendship dynamics — be flexible.
