# Day 5 — The Add-9 Trick

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children add 9 to any 2-digit number using the trick "add 10, take 1 away."

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- "Add 9" drill sheet
- Quick-fire flash cards

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

### Story / trick (10 min)

- Write on the board:
  ```
  33 + 9 = ?
  ```
- *"Anyone want to try?"* Take 2 answers. (Usually 42 with some scratch-out work.)
- *"What if I told you there's a trick? Watch:"*

```
9 is the same as 10 - 1.
So 33 + 9 is the same as 33 + 10 - 1.
33 + 10 = 43.
43 - 1 = 42.
Done!
```

*"Add 10, take 1 away. Easy."*

More examples:
- 47 + 9 = 47 + 10 - 1 = 56
- 64 + 9 = 64 + 10 - 1 = 73
- 28 + 9 = 28 + 10 - 1 = 37

### Drill (15 min)

Worksheet of 20 "+9" problems. Or quick-fire flash cards.

After 5 min of individual: paired race — who can do 5 in 30 seconds?

### Speed round (3 min)

Teacher calls "27 plus 9" — class shouts "36!" Mix it up.

### Show + chant (2 min)

Close.

## Homework

- Find ANY 5 2-digit numbers (look at your house number, car plate, telephone digits). Add 9 to each in your head.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try the trick for adding 19 (add 20, take 1 away). 47 + 19 = 66.
- **Tier down:** Stick to +9 only. Build up.

## Teacher's quick notes

- The trick generalises to +99, +999 too — but at this band stay with +9.
- Some children will resist: "but I just add!" Honour that — say "you have two tools now, use whichever's faster."
