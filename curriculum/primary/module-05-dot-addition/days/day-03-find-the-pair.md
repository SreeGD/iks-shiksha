# Day 3 — Find the Pair (Scanning Drill)

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children spot 10-pairs in a list of 6–8 single-digit numbers within 10 seconds.

## Materials

- Worksheet with 10 rows, each a list of 6–8 digits (printable)
- Pencils

## The flow

### Settle + chant (5 min)

Daily chant.

### Demonstration (5 min)

- Teacher writes on the board:
  ```
  3  7  2  5  8  1  4  6
  ```
- *"Find ALL the pairs that make 10. Circle them."*
- Walk through together: 3+7, 2+8, 4+6. Three pairs. Leftover: 5, 1.

### Scanning drill (20 min)

- Hand out worksheet.
- Individual work: circle every 10-pair in each row. Note the leftover digits.
- 10 rows. About 1 minute per row.

After 10 min, **paired check**: partner reads a row aloud; you find pairs in under 10 seconds.

### Speed round (5 min)

Teacher projects one row at a time on the board:
```
4 6 2 8 3 7
1 9 5 5 3 7
8 2 6 4 7 3
```

Class shouts pairs in chorus. 20 seconds per row.

### Show + chant (close)

Daily chant.

## Homework

- 5 more scanning rows on the back of the worksheet.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find pairs that make 10 AND pairs that make 11.
- **Tier down:** Work with shorter rows (4 digits each).

## Teacher's quick notes

- The pattern of "scan and group" is the key skill — more important than getting every pair right.
- If a child circles 5+5 as one pair, that's correct (both 5s used together).
