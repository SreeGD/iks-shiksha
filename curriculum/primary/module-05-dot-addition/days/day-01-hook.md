# Day 1 — Make Ten Game

**Module:** Make Ten / Bindu Paddhati (Primary) · **Time:** 35 min

## Goal

Children play a card game that requires finding pairs that sum to 10.

## Materials

- A deck of number cards 0–9 (4 of each = 40 cards) per pair
- Counters (optional)

## The flow

### Settle + introduce the chant (5 min)

Teach the chant — clap on each pair:

> "1 and 9 make 10! 2 and 8 make 10! 3 and 7 make 10! 4 and 6 make 10! 5 and 5 make 10!"

Say it 3 times.

### Story (5 min)

*"Today we play a game called Make Ten. The rule is simple: find two cards that add up to 10. The player with the most 10-pairs wins."*

### Game (20 min)

**How to play:**

1. Shuffle the deck. Deal 5 cards to each player. Place 5 cards face-up in the middle.
2. On your turn:
   - Look at your hand and the middle cards.
   - Find a pair that makes 10 (one from your hand + one from the middle).
   - Take the pair. Put it in your "pile."
   - Draw a new card from the deck.
3. If you can't make 10, place one card in the middle and end your turn.
4. When the deck runs out, count pairs. Most pairs wins!

Play 2–3 rounds.

### Show + chant (5 min)

- "Who won a round?" — winner shares ONE 10-pair they made.
- Class chants the pair: *"6 and 4 make 10!"*
- Close with the daily chant.

## Homework

- At home, practise the chant with a sibling or parent. Win a round of Make Ten with family!

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Play with 0–9 cards including 0+10 pairs.
- **Tier down:** Use only cards 1–6 to keep the pairs simple.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Some children won't find pairs and will give up. Pair them with a stronger player as a "team."
- The game IS the lesson at this band. Don't add a worksheet.
