# Module 5 (Primary) — 10-Day Lesson Plan

## At a glance (35-min sessions)

| Day | Title | Activity | Output |
|-----|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Make Ten Game | Pair card game | First-day fun |
| 2 | Bonds of Ten chant | Memorise pairs | Chant + recall |
| 3 | Find the Pair | Scanning drill | Worksheet |
| 4 | Add by Grouping | Column-addition game | First column wins |
| 5 | Add-9 Trick | Add 10, take 1 | Drill sheet |
| 6 | Meet the Dot | Teacher demo of Bindu Paddhati | Awe |
| 7 | Make-a-Game Day | Design own game | Sketch + first prototype |
| 8 | Project Day 1 | Build the game | Game ready to play |
| 9 | Project Day 2 | Finish + test | Play once |
| 10 | Game Showcase | Play each other's games | Stickers |

## Daily structure (35 min)

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 5 min | Settle + the "Make 10!" chant |
| 8 min | Story / new game introduction |
| 17 min | Game / activity |
| 5 min | Sharing / reset |

## The daily chant

> "1 and 9 make 10! 2 and 8 make 10! 3 and 7 make 10! 4 and 6 make 10! 5 and 5 make 10!"

Sing it / chorus it with claps. By Day 3 it should be effortless.

## Pacing notes

- Games > worksheets at this band. If a worksheet feels slow, drop it and add another game round.
- Day 6 (dot method demo) is a "wow" moment — keep it short and mysterious.
- Days 8–10 build to the game showcase. Confirm groups by Day 6.

## What children leave with

- Instant recall of all 5 ten-pairs.
- A mental habit of looking for "easy groups" when adding.
- The Add-9 trick.
- Curiosity about the dot method.
- One game they made and one they played.
