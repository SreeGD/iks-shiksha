# Activity 01 — Make Ten Card Game

**Module:** Make Ten (Primary) · **Day:** 1 (and reusable) · **Time:** 20 min within Day 1

## Goal

Children play a paired card game finding pairs that sum to 10.

## Materials per pair

- One deck of 40 number cards (0–9, 4 of each)

## Set-up

- One deck per pair on the desk.

## Rules (child-facing)

> **Make Ten** — for 2 players.
>
> 1. Shuffle. Deal 5 cards to each player. Put 5 face-up in the middle.
> 2. On your turn:
>    - Look at your hand + the middle.
>    - Find 2 cards that add to 10 (one from your hand + one from middle).
>    - Take both. Put in your pile.
>    - Draw 1 from the deck.
> 3. If you can't make 10, put 1 card in the middle. End your turn.
> 4. When the deck is empty, count your pairs. Most pairs wins!

## Going well

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Working | Both children scanning cards, trading positions, claiming pairs | "I got 6 and 4!" "Your turn!" |
| Stuck | Long silences, one player taking all turns | "I don't know" / no eye contact |

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Use 0–9; pairs of 10 only.
- **Tier down:** Use 1–6; pair sums of 10 are rarer, allow pair sums of 7 too.

## Clean-up

- Cards back in the deck. Stack on the teacher's desk.
