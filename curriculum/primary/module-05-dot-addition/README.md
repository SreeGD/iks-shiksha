# Module 5 — Make Ten! (Towards Bindu Paddhati)

**Age band:** Primary · **Grades:** 3–5 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 35 min

## What children will learn

1. The five 10-pairs: 1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5. Instantly.
2. Spot 10-pairs in any list of digits.
3. Add a small column of single digits (4–6 numbers) by grouping into 10s.
4. Mental shortcut: "add a 9" = "add 10 and take 1 away."
5. A peek at the dot method (*Bindu Paddhati*) as a "trick the older kids will learn."

## How this module sounds

- Lots of games. Cards. Dice. Chanting.
- No long worksheets. Children move, talk, count out loud.
- Sanskrit term *Bindu Paddhati* introduced once on Day 6 as "the dot method name in Sanskrit."

## Materials

- A deck of number cards 0–9 (4 sets per pair)
- Dice (one per pair)
- "Make 10" wall poster
- Counters (beads, buttons)
- Drawing paper

## Day-by-day

| Day | Title | What we do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Make Ten Game | Card game with pairs |
| 2 | Bonds of Ten chant | Memorise the 5 pairs |
| 3 | Find the Pair (in lists) | Scan + circle |
| 4 | Add by Grouping | Small column drill via game |
| 5 | The Add-9 Trick | Add 10, take 1 |
| 6 | Meet the Dot (Bindu Paddhati) | Brief demo by teacher |
| 7 | Make-a-Game Day | Children design their own card game |
| 8 | Project Day 1 | Start their own "make 10" card or board game |
| 9 | Project Day 2 | Finish + practise |
| 10 | Game Showcase | Play each other's games |

## Project

Children design their own "make 10" card or board game in pairs. They play each other's on Day 10.

See `assessments/project-brief.md`.

## Sources

The "make 10" pattern is foundational arithmetic — every culture's basic teaching includes it. The Indian "dot method" name (*Bindu Paddhati*) comes from 20th-century Indian math pedagogy. For older grades' deeper treatment, see the Middle-band version of this module.
