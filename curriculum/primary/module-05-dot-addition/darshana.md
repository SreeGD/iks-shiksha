# Module 5 — Bindu Paddhati — Dot Addition (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Bindu Paddhati* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

A 20th-century mental-arithmetic technique for column addition.

## Opening hook · 5 min

Write 8 + 7 on the board. Solve traditionally. Now do it with dots: 8 dots ●●●●●●●● + 7 dots ●●●●●●● = 15 dots ●●●●●●●●●●●●●●●. *"That's the idea. Today we use this trick for much bigger sums."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**The dot method:** when you add and get 10 or more, put a DOT next to the number above.

Example: 6 + 7 in a column.
6
7 → 6 + 7 = 13. Write 3, put a DOT.

Each dot = 10. So at the end: count dots × 10, add the remainder.

Practice: 8 + 9 in a column.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

On the board, do 7 + 8 with the dot. Then 9 + 6. Then 8 + 4 + 5. Children call out the dots together. Cheer for each correct answer.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Story of an Indian teacher in the 1960s who saw children struggling with long column addition and said: *"What if we used a dot to mean ten?"* The trick spread to schools all over India.

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

Practice worksheet: 8 column-addition problems using the dot method. Pairs check each other.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child shows one problem they solved with the dot method. Round of applause.
