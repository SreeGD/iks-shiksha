# Parent Guide — Module 8: Yoga for Little Ones

**Your child's age:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 30 min (~12 min movement each)

## What your child is learning

Five safe yoga poses + one breathing practice:
- **Tāḍāsana** (mountain) — standing tall
- **Vṛkṣāsana** (tree, like a flamingo) — balance
- **Balāsana** (child's pose, like a sleeping puppy) — rest
- **Cat-Cow** (mārjārī-bidalāsana) — spine wave
- **Bhrāmarī** (bumblebee breath) — humming exhale

Plus the Sanskrit words *yoga* (to join), *āsana* (posture), *prāṇa* (breath).

## Safety — what we DO NOT do at this age

- **No** headstands or shoulder stands (inversions).
- **No** big backbends.
- **No** holding the breath.
- **No** competitive holding ("see who can stay longest").
- **Always** an opt-out — your child can sit out any pose, no questions.

If your child has asthma, a recent injury, or any other condition, the school has a medical opt-out form. Please complete it before Day 1.

## What to expect this fortnight

**Movement and animal sounds.** Day 4 is "Cat-Cow" with optional meows and moos. Day 5 is "Bumblebee Breath" with audible humming. Giggles are part of the practice.

**At home:**
- Stand like a mountain for 60 seconds before brushing teeth.
- Tree pose for 10 seconds per side.
- Bumblebee breath: 5 hums before bed.

**Creative project (Days 7–10):** Your child invents their OWN yoga pose, gives it a name, and teaches the class for 1 minute on Day 10. Sticker for everyone.

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Do the poses *with* your child. Mountain pose for 60 seconds before bed = great way to wind down.
- For balance poses (tree), don't worry about wobble. Wobble is what balance learning looks like.
- For the breathing: model slow exhale. "Breathe out slowly, like you're cooling soup."
- For their invented pose: support, don't direct. Whatever they create is the right answer.

**Not helpful:**
- Pushing them deeper into a pose.
- Adjusting their alignment with your hands (let the teacher do that).
- Filming and posting to social media — yoga is a personal practice, not a performance.

## A note on yoga and religion

Yoga has religious roots in India. Many families practise it devotionally. **In school we teach it as physical practice with attention to breath** — not as religion. We honour the religious dimensions but don't centre them.

If your family doesn't want your child to participate, the school will arrange a written observation alternative.

## What success looks like

By the end of the module, your child can:
- Do 5 safe yoga poses at home without help.
- Hum bumblebee breath when they need to settle.
- Teach you their invented pose.
