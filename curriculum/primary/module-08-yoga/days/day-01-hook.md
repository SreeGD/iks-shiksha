# Day 1 — What is Yoga? (The Mountain)

**Module:** Yoga (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children meet yoga through a story and stand in *tāḍāsana* (mountain pose).

## Materials

- Mats / floor space
- A picture of a mountain

## The flow

### Settle (3 min)

Sit on mat, hands on knees. 5 natural breaths.

### Story (5 min)

*"Yoga is a very old practice from India. It uses our bodies, our breath, and our mind — all together. The Sanskrit word **yoga** means 'to join.' We join body, breath, and mind."*

*"Today our pose is **tāḍāsana** — mountain pose. We stand still and tall, like a mountain."*

### Tāḍāsana practice (12 min)

- Show the picture of a mountain. *"What's a mountain like?"* (Tall. Strong. Still.)
- *"Now BE a mountain."*
- Stand at top of mat. Feet hip-width.
- *"Press your feet into the ground like roots."*
- *"Stand TALL. Pretend a string is gently pulling the top of your head up."*
- *"Take 3 deep breaths. Breathe like a calm mountain."*

Hold for 30 sec. Release. Repeat 3 times.

Between rounds, ask:
- *"Did anyone feel taller?"*
- *"Did your feet feel heavier or lighter?"*

### Reflection (5 min)

- *"Why might mountains be the first pose we learn?"* (Steady. Strong. Doesn't move.)
- *"You're a mountain. You can come back to mountain anytime you need to feel steady."*

### Close (5 min)

Tāḍāsana one more time. Then sit. Hands together at heart. *"Namaste"* — *"I bow to you."*

## Homework

- Stand like a mountain for 30 seconds before brushing your teeth tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Hold for 60 seconds. Add "soft eyes looking ahead."
- **Tier down:** Hold for 15 seconds. Lean on a wall if needed.

## Teacher's quick notes

- This is the foundational pose for the whole module. Use it to open and close every class.
- Some children will fidget. Allow gentle sway — mountains in nature aren't perfectly still either.
