# Day 5 — Bumblebee Breath (bhrāmarī)

**Module:** Yoga (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children practise *bhrāmarī* — the humming breath — and notice the buzzing sensation.

## Materials

- Mats
- (No props needed — just bodies and voices)

## The flow

### Settle + opening tāḍāsana (3 min)

### Story (5 min)

*"Today's special practice is a **breath**, not a pose. It's called **bhrāmarī** — bumblebee breath. We hum like bees."*

*"Why bees? Because the humming feels like a buzzing in your head — it helps the mind feel quiet."*

### Bhrāmarī practice (15 min)

- *"Sit cross-legged. Hands on your knees. Back tall."*
- *"Take a slow breath IN."*
- *"As you breathe OUT, hum. 'Mmmmmmmmm.' For as long as you can."*
- *"Then breathe in again. Then hum again."*

Do 5 rounds together.

Then try ONE round with hands gently covering the ears (just close the ear flaps with fingers). *"Now your hum sounds extra loud — like bees inside your head!"*

Do 3 rounds with ears covered.

### Reflection (5 min)

- *"How did your head feel after the buzzing?"*
- *"Was it easier or harder than counting breaths?"*

### Close (2 min)

Tāḍāsana. Namaste.

## Homework

- 5 bumblebee breaths before bedtime tonight. Notice how you feel.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try 10 rounds. Try a deeper hum.
- **Tier down:** Just normal slow breathing if humming feels uncomfortable.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Asthma alert — if a child has asthma, they do natural breath, no bhrāmarī.
- The "buzzing in the head" is a real sensation — vibration calms the nervous system.
- Some children won't want to make sound in front of others. They can hum very quietly or just exhale slowly.
