# Day 4 — Cat and Cow (mārjārī-bidalāsana)

**Module:** Yoga (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children do the cat-cow spine wave with breath co-ordination.

## Materials

- Mats
- Pictures of a cat (arched back) and a cow (sagging back)

## The flow

### Settle + opening tāḍāsana (3 min)

### Story (5 min)

*"Today we have TWO poses that go together — **mārjārī** (cat) and **bidalāsana** (cow). We flow between them like waves."*

Show pictures: cat with arched back, cow with belly down.

### Cat-cow practice (15 min)

- *"Come to your hands and knees on your mat. Hands under shoulders, knees under hips."*
- *"COW: as you BREATHE IN, drop your belly. Lift your head and tailbone. Look slightly up. Moooo if you like."*
- *"CAT: as you BREATHE OUT, round your back like a scared cat. Tuck your chin. Meow!"*
- *"COW (inhale). CAT (exhale). COW. CAT."*

Do 5 slow rounds. Then 5 more, focusing on the breath.

### Animal sound bonus (4 min)

*"Animal sounds are allowed. Cows say 'moo.' Cats say 'meow.' Try making the sound on each pose."*

### Reflection (3 min)

- *"Which felt better — cat or cow?"*
- *"Did the breath help you move?"*

### Close (close)

Tāḍāsana. Namaste.

## Homework

- 5 cat-cow waves before breakfast. Make the sounds!

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** 10 rounds with deeper breathing.
- **Tier down:** Skip the sounds if shy. Just the movement.

## Teacher's quick notes

- The breath-movement co-ordination is the actual skill. Cue: "breath leads, body follows."
- Watch wrists — students with wrist issues can do this on forearms.
- Some children will giggle uncontrollably at the animal sounds. Let them — it loosens the body.
