# Day 7 — Invent a Pose

**Module:** Yoga (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Each child invents ONE pose, gives it a name, and tries it.

## Materials

- Mats
- Drawing paper + crayons (to draw the pose)

## The flow

### Settle + opening tāḍāsana (3 min)

### Story (5 min)

*"Yoga has thousands of poses, and people are still inventing new ones today. Today YOU invent ONE."*

*"It can be inspired by an animal, an object, a feeling — anything. The rules:"*

- It must be SAFE (no jumping, no flips, no putting weight on your head).
- It must be HOLDABLE for 10 seconds.
- It must have a NAME (any name — Sanskrit-sounding, animal, made-up).

### Invent time (15 min)

- Children sit on their mats and think.
- 5 minutes: try poses quietly.
- 5 minutes: draw your favourite invented pose on paper, with the name above.
- 5 minutes: try the pose 3 times.

### Share (5 min)

- 2 brave volunteers demonstrate their invented pose for the class.
- Class tries it together for 5 seconds.

### Close (2 min)

Tāḍāsana. Namaste.

## Homework

- Practise your invented pose at home. Get a parent to try it!

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Invent TWO poses. Pick the better one for project day.
- **Tier down:** Modify an existing pose (e.g. tree pose but with hands at a different position).

## Teacher's quick notes

- All poses must be vetted for safety. Walk around during invent time.
- Encourage creativity over imitation — but if a child copies tree pose with a different arm position, that's a creative achievement at this age.
