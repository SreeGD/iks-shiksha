# Day 3 — Rest Like a Child (balāsana)

**Module:** Yoga (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children rest in *balāsana* (child's pose) and notice how it feels in the body.

## Materials

- Mats
- Optional: soft blanket for each child

## The flow

### Settle + opening tāḍāsana (3 min)

### Story (3 min)

*"Today's pose is **balāsana** — child's pose. The name literally means 'child posture.' It's the way babies sometimes sleep — curled up, safe, resting."*

*"This is a REST pose. Not a hard pose."*

### Child's pose practice (15 min)

- *"Kneel on your mat. Knees wide apart. Big toes touching."*
- *"Fold forward. Bring your forehead toward the mat. (If it doesn't reach — stack your fists and put your head on them.)"*
- *"Arms forward or alongside your body — whichever feels nice."*
- *"Breathe slowly. This is rest."*

Hold for 60 seconds. *"Notice your breath."*

Come up slowly. *"Did anything in your body feel different?"*

Repeat 2 more times (with brief activity between — try a different arm position each time).

### Bumblebee preview (5 min)

Sit cross-legged. *"Tomorrow we'll do a special breath called bumblebee. Just to start: hum like a bee for 3 seconds. Mmmmmm. Did you feel the buzz?"*

### Reflection (2 min)

- *"Was child's pose more like resting or more like working?"*
- *"What did you notice when you sat back up?"*

### Close (2 min)

Tāḍāsana. Namaste.

## Homework

- When you feel tired tonight, try child's pose for 1 minute before bed.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Stay in child's pose for 2 minutes. Count your breaths.
- **Tier down:** If knees hurt, put a folded blanket between calves and thighs.

## Teacher's quick notes

- Child's pose is restorative. Don't rush.
- If a child's forehead doesn't reach the mat (hip flexibility), stacked fists work fine.
- This is also THE pose to offer when a child is overwhelmed mid-class.
