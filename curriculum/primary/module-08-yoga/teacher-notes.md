# Teacher Notes — Module 8 Yoga (Primary)

## Tone

Movement is play. Yoga at this age should feel like fun, not like a class. Animal cues turn poses into games.

## Safety — please read before Day 1

- **NEVER** inversions (headstand, plough, downward-dog beyond a hint).
- **NEVER** backbends beyond gentle cat (mild arch).
- **NEVER** breath retention or forceful breathing.
- **NEVER** correct alignment with your hands without explicit child consent.
- **Opt-out always allowed.** Child can sit in sukhāsana (cross-legged seat) and watch.

## Medical opt-outs

Collect before Day 1. Common conditions:
- Asthma — restrict bhrāmarī (Day 5) — natural breathing only.
- Knee/ankle injury — modify standing balance poses (Day 2 — sit instead).
- Any back issue — skip cat-cow's arch (Day 4).

## On the Sanskrit names

- Use the names once a day, slowly. Don't quiz children on them.
- Pair each name with the animal cue — "tree pose, like a flamingo standing on one leg" — and the animal cue is what they'll remember.

## Common things to handle

1. **A child can't balance in vṛkṣāsana.** Use a wall. Or hold a friend's hand.
2. **A child won't try the cat sound in cat-cow.** No pressure. Movement only.
3. **A child is anxious to close eyes.** Eyes-lowered or eyes-open is fine.
4. **A child says "this is silly."** Validate. *"Yes, it's a little silly. That's part of the fun. You can sit out if you'd rather just watch."*

## Project oversight

- Days 8–10: each child invents ONE pose, gives it a name, and teaches it for 1 minute on Day 10.
- Pose must be SAFE (no inversions, no jumping). Teacher vets all invented poses on Day 8.
- Names can be anything — Sanskrit-sounding, animal names, made-up words.

## If you only have 6 days

- Day 1: Mountain + tree
- Day 2: Child's rest + cat-cow
- Day 3: Bumblebee breath
- Day 4: Animal walk
- Day 5: Invent a pose
- Day 6: Showcase
