# Day 4 — Sacred Groves

**Module:** Indic Ecology — Vasudhaiva Kuṭumbakam (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Identify devarakāḍu and similar groves as biodiversity reserves.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *devarakāḍu* (IAST: devarakāḍu; lit. "sacred grove (Karnataka, S. India)")

- Sacred groves are special little forests that people DON'T cut.
- Why? Because they believe a god, a sage, or an ancestor lives there.
- Animals and plants live safely in these groves.

Show a picture of a grove. *"This is a place where no one breaks a branch."*

Children draw a tiny forest with animals: birds, butterflies, deer. Add a small temple or shrine.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sacred Grove (or Local Ecology) Mapping* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
