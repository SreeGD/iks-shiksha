# Day 3 — Rivers as Sacred

**Module:** Indic Ecology — Vasudhaiva Kuṭumbakam (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Discuss the ecological function of river-worship traditions.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *ahiṁsā* (IAST: ahiṁsā; lit. "non-violence; extended to non-human life")

- Rivers are sacred in India! The Gaṅgā, Yamunā, Sarasvatī...
- Why? Because rivers give us water, food, life!

Show a picture of the Ganga. *"People say: don't pollute the river. The river is our mother."*

Children draw a river with fish, plants, and a small flower-offering. Discuss: *"What happens if rivers get dirty?"*

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sacred Grove (or Local Ecology) Mapping* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
