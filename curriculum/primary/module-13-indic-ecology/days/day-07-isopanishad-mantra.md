# Day 7 — Iṣopaniṣad — Non-Greed Ethic

**Module:** Indic Ecology — Vasudhaiva Kuṭumbakam (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Translate *īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvam* and discuss its ecological implication.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *yajña* (IAST: yajña; lit. "cycle of giving and receiving (BG 3.10–14)")

- An old wise verse says: *"Take only what you need!"*

The verse: *"īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvam..."* — "All this is one big home."

*"If you take TOO much, others don't have enough. If you waste, the Earth gets sad."*

Children think of one thing they sometimes take too much of (food, water, time on screens). They draw it with a small heart, promising: *"I'll take just what I need."*

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Sacred Grove (or Local Ecology) Mapping* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
