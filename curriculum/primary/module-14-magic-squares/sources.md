# Sources — Module 14 Magic Squares (Primary)

## For the teacher (not shown to children at this band)

### Primary historical source

**Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita, *Gaṇita-kaumudī*, Book 14 (*Bhadragaṇita*), 1356 CE.**

- The systematic Sanskrit treatment of magic squares we draw on indirectly. At Primary band the children meet the *result* (the 3×3 with 1–9 sums to 15) without meeting the systematic method.

### Secondary scholarly source

**Kim Plofker, *Mathematics in India* (Princeton University Press, 2009).**

- Background reading. Used for historical context only.

### Material artefacts referenced

- **Lo Shu (China).** Earliest documented in *Da Dai Liji* (Han dynasty, ~5th–1st c. BCE). Legend of Emperor Yu and the turtle from the Lo river: a *story*, not historical fact. We tell the story as a story.
- **Khajuraho 4×4 inscription (Madhya Pradesh, India).** Chandela period, ~10th–11th c. CE. We show the artefact and locate it on a map of India. At Primary band we acknowledge the existence; we do not work the 4×4.

## What to tell children about sources

At Primary band: nothing in formal citation form. The Khajuraho stone is "a real carving in a real temple in India" (not "an inscription dated c. 10th c. CE"). The Lo Shu is "a story from China about a turtle" (not "Da Dai Liji, Han dynasty").

The framing matters: stories are stories; carvings are real things; numbers are numbers. Keep the language age-appropriate without losing accuracy.

Per the style guide: **Primary band has no student-facing citations.** Citations belong in this teacher-facing sources document only.
