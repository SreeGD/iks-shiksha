# Module 14 — Magic Squares — Bhadragaṇita (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Bhadragaṇita* · **Band:** Primary (3-5) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita's 1356 CE magic-square algorithms.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 14's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

Show a 3×3 grid with numbers 1-9. *"All rows, all columns, both diagonals add to 15! Magic!"*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**A magic square: numbers in a grid where rows, columns, diagonals all add to the same.**

The 3×3 with 1-9:
```
2 7 6
9 5 1
4 3 8
```
All rows = 15. All columns = 15. Diagonals too!

The middle number is ALWAYS 5. (Try shifting numbers around.)

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Show the 3 × 3 magic square. Children find the rows that add to 15, the columns, the diagonals. Cheer for each find.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Tell the story of Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita, who in 1356 wrote a book full of number puzzles, called *Gaṇita-kaumudī* — "Moonlight of Numbers."

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Decorate the 3 × 3 magic square.** Each child draws the square with colours, frames, and animals around the numbers.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

Each child shows their decorated 3 × 3. Cheer.
