# Day 4 — Stair-Step Method (Nārāyaṇa)

**Module:** Magic Squares — Bhadragaṇita (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Apply Nārāyaṇa's algorithm to build a 5×5 square.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *bhadra-gaṇita* (IAST: bhadra-gaṇita; lit. ""auspicious arithmetic" — the Indian term for magic squares")

- Big-kid trick! Nārāyaṇa (an Indian mathematician 700 years ago) taught how to BUILD a magic square step-by-step.

Show on the board:
1. Put **1** in the top-middle.
2. From 1, jump UP-and-RIGHT to put 2... but you fall off the top!
3. Wrap to the bottom row (same column).
4. Keep going!

The pattern looks like a horse jumping. *"That's why he called it the horse-step!"*

Children watch the teacher build a 3×3 step-by-step.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Class 3×3 Magic-Square Tournament* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
