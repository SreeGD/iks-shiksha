# Module 14 — Magic Squares (Primary)

**Age band:** Primary · **Grades:** 3–5 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × ~30 min

## What this module is

Children meet the **3×3 magic square** as a puzzle: place the numbers 1 to 9 in a grid so that every row, every column, and the two diagonals all add up to the same number. They discover by play that the magic number is **15** and that the same arrangement keeps coming up (just rotated). Along the way they hear the story of the **Lo Shu turtle** (China) and meet a stone carving from **Khajuraho** (India) that looks like the same idea.

## What the children will be able to do

By the end of this module:
- Recognise a 3×3 magic square when they see one.
- Build a 3×3 magic square with 1–9 either from memory or with a hint.
- Add three single-digit numbers and check whether the sum is 15.
- Tell, in two or three sentences, where magic squares come from in stories (Lo Shu turtle, Khajuraho temple stone).
- Sing the "magic square song" — *"All to fifteen, all to fifteen"*.

## NEP 2020 alignment

- **§4.6** — Integration of Indian Knowledge Systems in age-appropriate, story-led form.
- **§4.23** — Experiential learning through hands-on tile placement and discovery.
- **§4.27** — Local context: the Khajuraho temple stone is a real Indian artefact children can locate on a map.

## What we won't do at this band

- No formula (*n*(*n*² + 1)/2 is Middle-band material).
- No 4×4 or 5×5 construction.
- No formal Sanskrit citations to students. Sources used only by teacher.
- No ritual or religious framing of the diagrams.

## Materials checklist

- A class set of paper tiles or wooden tokens numbered 1–9 (1 set per pair)
- Printed blank 3×3 grids (~5 per child across the module)
- Coloured pencils (4 colours per child)
- A simple picture of a turtle for Day 7 (for the Lo Shu story)
- A picture or photocopy of the Khajuraho stone-square panel for Day 8
- Big chart paper for the class wall: "OUR MAGIC SQUARE — sum 15"

## Day-by-day overview

| Day | Title | Focus |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 1 | The Magic Number 15 | Hook: warm-up addition game |
| 2 | Pairs that add to 10 | Foundation: making addition fluent |
| 3 | The First Try | Children try the 3×3 by hand |
| 4 | The Secret of the Centre | Always 5 in the middle |
| 5 | Our Class Magic Square | Build it together on a big chart |
| 6 | Magic Square Sing-along | A chant + tile game |
| 7 | The Lo Shu Turtle | Story from China |
| 8 | The Khajuraho Stone | A magic square from India |
| 9 | Make-and-Keep | Each child decorates their own magic square |
| 10 | Show and Tell | Children show their magic square + sticker assessment |

## Project (light)

Each child colours and decorates a magic square page they have built themselves. Stickers awarded based on:
- Sum-check stickers (verified each row, column, diagonal)
- Story-telling stickers (knows the Lo Shu story OR the Khajuraho story)
- Effort sticker
- Helping-a-friend sticker

## Sources used (teacher only)

- Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita, *Gaṇita-kaumudī*, Book 14 (*Bhadragaṇita*), 1356 CE (primary source — for the construction method shown to children).
- Plofker (2009), *Mathematics in India* (secondary teacher reference).

Children do not see these citations directly at Primary band.
