# Parent Guide — Module 9: Friends to Ten! (Towards *Nikhilam*)

**Your child's age:** Primary (Grades 3–5) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 35 min

## What your child is learning

A foundational mental-math skill called **"friends to ten"** — knowing instantly which digit completes another to make 10:

- 1 + **9** = 10
- 2 + **8** = 10
- 3 + **7** = 10
- 4 + **6** = 10
- 5 + **5** = 10

And then a step up: using "friends to ten" to instantly subtract from 100. (`100 − 7 = 93`, because 7's friend is 3, and we shift one over.)

At the end of the module they'll meet the Sanskrit name *Nikhilam* — pronounced "ni-khi-lam" — and a small magic phrase: "all from nine and the last from ten." They won't learn the full method until Grade 6, but they'll see it once and be impressed.

## What to expect this fortnight

**A daily chant:**

> "1 and 9 are friends! 2 and 8 are friends! 3 and 7 are friends! 4 and 6 are friends! 5 and 5 are friends! All make TEN!"

**Games at home:**
- A card-based "Friends to Ten" game (Day 1).
- Quick-fire friend-naming drills with a parent or sibling.
- A "coin game" (Day 5): your child plays shopkeeper; you hand them 100-rupee paper money for a small item; they give change.

**A creative project (Days 7–10):** Your child will design their own "Friends to Ten" game — cards, board, dice, jumping — and play classmates' games on Day 10.

## How to help — and how NOT to help

**Helpful:**
- 5-minute friend-drill before bed: call out digits; your child says the friend. Try to beat their time.
- Be the customer in the coin game: you hand a 100; for a 67-rupee item, your child gives change of 33. They say "67's friend is 33!"
- For their project: provide materials (cards, paper, dice) and let *them* design the rules.

**Not helpful:**
- Insisting that "the way I was taught" is the only right way. The "complete to 10" trick gives the same answer as standard borrowing.
- Designing their game for them.
- Anxiety about speed. Speed comes from practice, not pressure.

## A note on "Vedic mathematics"

You may have heard the term "Vedic mathematics." Here is the honest framing we use:

- The "friends to ten" rule with the Sanskrit name *Nikhilam* is a real, useful Indian mental-arithmetic technique.
- The full rule comes from a book by Bharati Krishna Tirthaji called *Vedic Mathematics* (1965).
- Tirthaji claimed the rule comes from a Vedic appendix-text. **No other Sanskritist has confirmed this.** The method is real; the Vedic attribution is unverified.
- We teach the method enthusiastically AND are honest about its history. By Senior band (Grade 9–12) your child will read the scholarly debate.

## What success looks like

By the end of the module, your child can:
- Recall all 5 friends-to-ten instantly.
- Compute `100 − N` mentally for small N (under 20).
- Pronounce and recognise the word *Nikhilam*.
- Show classmates a game they designed.
