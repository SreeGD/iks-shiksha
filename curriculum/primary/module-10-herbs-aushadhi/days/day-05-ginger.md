# Day 5 — Ginger — Warming Root

**Module:** Herbs — Auṣadhi (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Distinguish fresh vs dried; gingerol vs shogaol.

## Materials

- Notebook, pencils, drawing paper
- (See lesson-plan.md for any day-specific materials)

## The flow

### Settle + chant (3 min)

Daily chant or breathing exercise.

### Story recall (4 min)

Briefly recall yesterday's lesson by asking: *"What's one thing you remember from yesterday?"*

### Today's idea (10 min)

- *ārdraka* (IAST: ārdraka; lit. "ginger — *Zingiber officinale*")

- Today: meet Ginger (Adraka)! 🌿

Show fresh ginger root. *"It's bumpy, like a knobbly hand!"*

Smell it (mild). Then smell dried ginger powder (strong). *"Drying makes it stronger!"*

Ginger tea = ginger + warm water + a tiny bit of honey. People drink it when their tummy hurts.

Children draw ginger root with knobbly bumps.

### Activity (8 min)

A simplified version of *Blind Smell-Test Identification* (see `activities/`). Draw, sing, or act it out.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

Each child shares one word that describes today.
