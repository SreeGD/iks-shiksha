# Day 7 — What Friends Eat (Food)

**Module:** Three Friends (Primary) · **Time:** 30 min

## Goal

Children learn that the three friends have food preferences — and that variety matters.

## Materials

- Drawing paper
- Optional: pictures of varied foods (rice, lemon, salt, chili, bitter gourd, banana)

## Flow

### Settle + chant + song (5 min)

### Story (10 min)

*"Each friend likes different foods!"*

**Vāta loves:** warm food, smooth food, oily food.
*Examples:* warm rice with ghee, soup, milk.

**Pitta loves:** cool food, sweet food (in good measure), not too spicy.
*Examples:* fruit, milk, cucumber.

**Kapha loves:** light food, spicy food, less sweet.
*Examples:* warm spice tea, ginger, vegetables.

*"But we ALL have ALL THREE friends — so we need ALL kinds of food. A meal with VARIETY makes all three friends happy."*

**The six tastes** (introduce quickly):
- Sweet (sweet rice)
- Sour (lemon)
- Salty (salt)
- Spicy/pungent (ginger)
- Bitter (bitter gourd, neem)
- Astringent (banana skin, beans)

*"A traditional Indian thali has ALL six tastes — that's clever cooking!"*

### Activity (10 min)

Each child draws a "balanced plate" with examples of as many tastes as they can include.

### Show + chant (5 min)

## Homework

- At dinner tonight, count how many of the 6 tastes are on your plate. Tell us tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Make a "thali" drawing with 6 sections.
- **Tier down:** Just remember: variety on your plate makes the friends happy.
