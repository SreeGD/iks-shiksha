# Parent Guide — Module 8: Yoga (Senior)

**Your student's age:** Senior (Grades 9–12) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min (~15 min movement)

## What your student is learning

This is a serious comparative-and-research module:

1. **Patañjali's *Yoga Sūtras*** — primary-source reading of YS 1.2, 2.46–48, 2.49–50, in IAST with Bryant's commentary.
2. **The eight limbs (*aṣṭāṅga*)** in their classical Sāṅkhya-Yoga context — including the observation that Patañjali devotes only 3 of his ~196 sūtras directly to āsana (posture).
3. **Modern physiology** — polyvagal theory (Porges 2009), heart-rate variability, the vagus nerve and parasympathetic activation.
4. **Historical scholarship** — Singleton's *Yoga Body* (2010) on the 20th-century origins of modern postural yoga.
5. **A 5–6 page research-synthesis paper** integrating classical, historical, and modern-research sources.

## What's at stake

Your student will engage these honest framings:

- **Modern postural yoga is largely a 20th-century synthesis.** It draws on classical Indian texts AND on European physical-culture movements (gymnastics, Swedish drill) that arrived in India during the colonial period. Sūrya Namaskāra in its modern 12-pose form was codified by the Raja of Aundh in 1928. (Singleton 2010.)
- **Patañjali's classical yoga is much older.** ~2nd c. BCE – 4th c. CE. The eight-limbed framework is a major Indian intellectual achievement.
- **These two facts coexist.** Modern postural yoga is also a real Indian intellectual achievement — just a more recent one.

## Safety — Senior band caveats

Standard rules apply. At Senior band:
- **Inversions** (headstand, shoulder stand) allowed ONLY if the school has a qualified yoga teacher AND the student has demonstrated readiness over multiple sessions. **Default: no inversions.**
- **Breath retention (kumbhaka)** allowed only with explicit teacher screening for contraindications.
- Standard medical opt-out form required.

## Paper expectations

5–6 page research-synthesis paper. Required:
- Clear thesis.
- **One** classical primary source (YS, BG, or SB verse in IAST + translation).
- **One** historical scholarly source (Singleton, De Michelis, Sjoman).
- **One** peer-reviewed modern research paper (Porges, Streeter, Pascoe).
- Steel-manned counter-argument.
- MLA or Chicago citations.

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Listen to their thesis. Ask "what's the strongest objection?"
- Try the stress-recovery sequence with them. (4 in, 4 out for 5 minutes really does slow your heart rate. Notice together.)
- For the Day 7 discussion: ask which of three reactions to Singleton (diminishment / enrichment / Western-attack) matches theirs, and why.

**Not helpful:**
- Insisting yoga is "ancient and unbroken." Singleton's history is honest scholarship; engage it, don't dismiss it.
- Pushing them toward a religious interpretation OR away from one. The classroom frame is the academic study of yoga as Indian intellectual and physical tradition.
- Filming their practice for social media.

## On the politics

This module touches on terrain where some families have strong views (yoga's religious dimension, the "ancient vs modern" question, what counts as authentically Indian). The classroom answer:
- Both classical AND modern postural yoga are real Indian achievements.
- The 20th-century origins of postural yoga do NOT diminish its value.
- Religious and secular framings can coexist; the school teaches the secular framing.

Your student will form their own view through engagement. You may disagree with their conclusions. That's healthy — disagree by defending your view, not by dismissing theirs.

## Assessment

- Summative quiz: 35 marks.
- Paper: 65 marks.
- Total: 100.

## What success looks like

By end of module, your student can:
- Read YS 2.46 (*sthira-sukham āsanam*) in IAST and discuss what Patañjali's dual condition means.
- Explain the eight limbs and identify what proportion Patañjali devotes to each.
- Articulate polyvagal-theory's account of slow-breath effects on the body, including known critiques.
- Distinguish modern postural yoga from classical Patañjala yoga, fairly to both.
- Write a research paper that integrates classical, historical, and modern-empirical sources.
