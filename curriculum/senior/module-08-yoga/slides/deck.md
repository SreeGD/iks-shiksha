Grades 9–12 · 10 sessions

# Three Definitions of Yoga

> **Patañjali (YS 1.2):** *yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ*
> — "Yoga is the settling of the fluctuations of consciousness."

> **Bhagavad-gītā (2.50):** *yogaḥ karmasu kauśalam*
> — "Yoga is skill in action."

> **Modern postural yoga:** physical postures + breath for health and wellness.

Not the same. Not incompatible. Different scopes.

# Important proportions

Patañjali's *Yoga Sūtras* total ~196 sūtras.

Of these, only **3** are devoted to *āsana* (YS 2.46–48).

**Modern postural yoga focuses on what Patañjali treats as one small piece.**

---

# Āsana per Patañjali (YS 2.46)

> *sthira-sukham āsanam*
> "Posture should be steady AND comfortable."

3 words. Dual condition.

NOT "challenging." NOT "advanced." Steady-and-comfortable.

---

# YS 2.47 — how to achieve it

> *prayatna-śaithilyānanta-samāpattibhyām*

Two means:
- *prayatna-śaithilya* — relaxation of effort
- *ananta-samāpatti* — absorption into the infinite

---

# Prāṇāyāma per Patañjali (YS 2.49)

> *tasmin sati śvāsa-praśvāsayor gati-vicchedaḥ prāṇāyāmaḥ*

When āsana is established, prāṇāyāma is the regulation of inhalation and exhalation.

Note: *āsana first, then prāṇāyāma.*

---

# YS 2.50

Three modifications: outer (exhale), inner (inhale), held (retention).

Three measurements: place, time, number.

Result: *dīrgha-sūkṣma* — long and subtle.

---

# Sūrya Namaskāra — the surprise

The modern 12-pose form was codified by **the Raja of Aundh in the early 20th century** (1928).

Sanskrit names are old. The *sequence* is recent.

---

# What slow breath does — modern frame

The **vagus nerve** carries parasympathetic signals.

**Slow exhalation stimulates the vagus nerve.**

Polyvagal theory (Porges 2009) nuances this with a three-tier model:

- Ventral vagal: social engagement / calm
- Sympathetic: fight-flight
- Dorsal vagal: shutdown / freeze

---

# Limits of polyvagal theory

Not unchallenged. Grossman & Taylor (2007) dispute specific vagal-tone claims.

The cleanest claim that survives: slow exhale → parasympathetic activation.

(Use Porges as ONE framework, not as proof.)

---

# Singleton's (2010) thesis

Modern postural yoga is largely a 20th-century synthesis.

Components: classical Indian + 19th–20th c. European physical culture (gymnastics, bodybuilding).

Key figures: Krishnamacharya → Iyengar, Pattabhi Jois, Desikachar.

---

# What Singleton does NOT claim

- ❌ "Yoga is fake."
- ❌ "Patañjali is recent."
- ❌ "Indians didn't invent yoga."

He claims: *the modern postural FORM is recent, even though many techniques are old.*

---

# Three reactions

When students hear Singleton:

- **Diminishment:** "So it's not even real?"
- **Enrichment:** "Knowing the history makes the practice richer."
- **Resistance:** "This is Western academia attacking Indian culture."

All three reactions are legitimate. Engage them.
