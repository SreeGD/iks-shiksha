# Writing Prompts — Module 8 Yoga (Senior)

## Reflection

**1. (Day 1)** Before this module, what did "yoga" mean to you? Which of the three definitions (Patañjali / Gītā / modern) most matched your assumption?

**2. (Day 3)** Does YOUR yoga practice meet Patañjali's *sthira-sukham* criterion? Be honest.

## Explanatory

**3. (Day 5)** Explain what YS 2.49 says about the relationship between āsana and prāṇāyāma in 5 sentences.

**4. (Day 6)** Explain polyvagal theory to a Grade 9 reader in 1 page.

## Argumentative

**5. (Day 7)** Argue: "Singleton's (2010) historical thesis enriches rather than diminishes yoga practice." 1–2 pages.

**6. (Day 7)** Argue the OPPOSITE: "Singleton's framing is a Western-academic project that doesn't change what yoga IS for the practitioner."

## Cross-disciplinary

**7. (Stretch)** Compare Patañjali's *sthira-sukham* with a similar dual condition from another tradition (Japanese *seiza* meditation? Christian centering prayer?). What's the philosophical move both make?

**8. (Stretch)** Read a contemporary peer-reviewed study on yoga's effects (Pascoe 2017 meta-analysis recommended). Critique the methodology in 1 page.

Same as Module 2/5 Senior — thesis, citations, counter-argument, prose.
