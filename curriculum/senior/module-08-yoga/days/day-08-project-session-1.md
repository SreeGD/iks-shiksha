# Day 8 — Project Session 1: Topic + Outline

**Module:** Yoga (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students commit to a research-synthesis paper topic, draft a thesis paragraph, and produce an outline citing all 3 required source types.

## Materials

- Project brief + rubric
- Drafting materials

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

### Topic selection (15 min)

Suggested topics:
- "Does the counted-breath prāṇāyāma practice we learned have measurable effects on HRV? A literature review."
- "Patañjali defines āsana as 'steady and comfortable' (YS 2.46). Does modern postural yoga meet this criterion? Examine through 3 specific postures."
- "Singleton (2010) argues modern postural yoga is largely 20th century. Evaluate his evidence."
- "Compare Patañjali's prāṇāyāma framework (YS 2.49–53) with the polyvagal theory's account of breath effects."
- "Choose any one āsana and trace its history from classical text (if any) to modern practice. What does the history reveal?"

Each student commits to a topic. Discusses with teacher (60-sec rotation) — too broad? too narrow?

### Outline (20 min)

Standard 5-6 page outline:
- Intro with thesis
- 3 body sections
- Counter-argument
- Conclusion
- Works cited (3+ sources, must include 1 classical primary + 1 historical scholarly + 1 modern research)

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Outline submitted at door.

## Homework

- Begin drafting at home.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add a 4th source type (e.g. an empirical study of an unrelated culture's somatic practice).
- **Tier down:** Narrow your topic further; one specific verse + one specific modern study is enough depth.

## Teacher notes

- Strong topics are specific. Weak topics are broad. Push for specificity.
