# Moon Phases and Tithi

**Band:** Senior (Grades 9–12) · **Sessions:** 10 × 45 min · **Sanskrit:** Candra-pakṣa, Tithi

## Overview

The Moon's apparent change of shape across ~29.5 days is the synodic month. Indian astronomy divides this into 30 *tithi*s (lunar days), grouped into a waxing fortnight (*śukla pakṣa*) and waning fortnight (*kṛṣṇa pakṣa*). This module connects observation, lunar geometry, calendar construction, and the panchanga framework.

## Key terms (this module)

- *candra* — Moon
- *pakṣa* — fortnight (half-month)
- *śukla pakṣa* — waxing fortnight (new → full)
- *kṛṣṇa pakṣa* — waning fortnight (full → new)
- *tithi* — lunar day (1/30 of synodic month)
- *amāvasyā* — new moon
- *pūrṇimā* — full moon
- *pañcāṅga* — five-limbed Indian calendar (tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa)

## 10-day map

- **Day 1** — The Moon and Its Shapes: Observe and name the four basic phases (new, first quarter, full, last quarter).
- **Day 2** — Two Fortnights — Pakṣa: Distinguish śukla pakṣa from kṛṣṇa pakṣa and name today's.
- **Day 3** — Fifteen Tithis per Pakṣa: Recite the 15 tithi names (pratipadā, dvitīyā, ...) and explain why a tithi isn't exactly a day.
- **Day 4** — Building a Tithi Calendar: Construct a tithi calendar for the current month using observation and computation.
- **Day 5** — Lunar Months and Festivals: Map the 12 lunar months (caitra, vaiśākha, ...) to major festivals.
- **Day 6** — Panchanga — Five Limbs: Identify the five components: tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa.
- **Day 7** — Why Eclipses Happen: Explain solar/lunar eclipses using orbital plane geometry (5° tilt of Moon's orbit).
- **Day 8** — Modern Lunar Science: Phases as Sun's illumination of Moon; mention libration, tides.
- **Day 9** — Cross-cultural Calendars: Compare with Hijri (Islamic), Chinese, and Hebrew lunar calendars.
- **Day 10** — Synthesis and Quiz: Final quiz and 10-day moon-diary share.
