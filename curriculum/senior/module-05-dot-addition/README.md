# Module 5 — Bindu Paddhati, Tirthaji's Sūtras, and the Historicity Question

**Age band:** Senior · **Grades:** 9–12 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Execute** the dot method (*Bindu Paddhati*) on 5-digit × 5-addend column sums with sub-60-second speed.
2. **State** Bharati Krishna Tirthaji's 16 sūtras and 13 sub-sūtras and locate the dot method's pedagogical lineage in relation to them.
3. **Generalise** the dot method to base-*n* arithmetic (base 8, base 16) and articulate why it works.
4. **Analyse** the historicity question: did Tirthaji's claimed Vedic origin for his sūtras hold up to scholarly scrutiny? Use primary sources (Tirthaji 1965) and secondary scholarship (Plofker, Dani).
5. **Construct** a research paper (5–6 pages) on either the math (base-*n* generalisation) OR the history (provenance of Tirthaji's claims).

## Key vocabulary (Senior additions)

- *sūtra* — short aphorism / formula
- *Tirthaji* — Bharati Krishna Tirthaji (1884–1960), Śaṅkarācārya of Govardhana Maṭha (Puri); author of *Vedic Mathematics* (1965, posthumous)
- *Pariśiṣṭa* (or *Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa*) — appendix-text genre Tirthaji claimed his sūtras came from
- *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa, Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ, Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyāṁ, Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām, Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ* — sūtras directly relevant to the dot method's spirit
