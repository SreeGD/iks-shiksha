## What to tell students about provenance

Tirthaji claimed his sūtras came from an *Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa* (Vedic appendix-text). **No other scholar has ever produced this manuscript.** Dani (1993) and other historians of mathematics consider the Vedic-provenance claim unproven. The mathematics itself is genuine and useful. The question of *what to call it* is the open question. Honest options:
- "Tirthaji's 16 sūtras" (avoids the disputed Vedic claim)
- "Indian mental-arithmetic tradition" (broader, includes 19th–20th c. pedagogy)
- "Vedic mathematics" (Tirthaji's term, used in most schools, but contested)

Students should pick a term explicitly in their paper and justify the choice.
