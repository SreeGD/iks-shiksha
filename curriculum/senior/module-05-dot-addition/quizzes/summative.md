## Section A — Multiple choice (5 × 1 = 5 marks)

**1.** Tirthaji's 16 sūtras come from what text he claimed?
a) Ṛg Veda  b) Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa  c) Sūrya-siddhānta  d) Yoga Sūtras

**2.** *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* means:
a) "Vertically and crosswise"  b) "By addition and by subtraction"
c) "All from nine, last from ten"  d) "By one more than the previous"

**3.** S. G. Dani's 1993 critique of Tirthaji's claim argues:
a) The mathematics is wrong
b) The manuscript of the *Pariśiṣṭa* has never been produced
c) Tirthaji was not a Śaṅkarācārya
d) Indian mathematics is overrated

**4.** In base 8 dot method, a dot represents:
a) 8 used up = 1 carry  b) 10 used up = 1 carry
c) 1 fewer than the digit  d) Nothing meaningful

**5.** The dot method itself is:
a) Sūtra #4 of the 16  b) Sūtra #7 of the 16
c) A 20th-century pedagogical technique using the spirit of sūtras 7 and 8
d) From the Bakhshali manuscript
