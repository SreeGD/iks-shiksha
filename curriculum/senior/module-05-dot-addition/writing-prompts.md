# Writing Prompts — Module 5 Dot Addition (Senior)

## Reflection

**1. (Day 1)** Before this module, what did you assume about "Vedic mathematics"? Be specific about where the assumption came from.

**2. (Day 6)** After reading Dani — has your view shifted? In what specific way?

## Explanatory

**3. (Day 3 or 5)** Explain the dot method procedure to a college-level audience, then prove its correctness in 1 page. Use place-value language.

**4. (Day 5)** Explain why the dot method works in any positional base. Use one specific example in a non-decimal base.

## Argumentative

**5. (Day 6 or 7)** Argue: "The Vedic-provenance claim for Tirthaji's sūtras is not central to their value." 1–2 pages with citations.

**6. (Day 6)** Argue the OPPOSITE: "Calling the techniques 'Vedic' matters significantly — both for accuracy and for cultural recognition." 1–2 pages.

## Cross-disciplinary

**7. (Math track)** Compare Tirthaji's *Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyāṁ* multiplication with the standard long-multiplication algorithm. Complexity O(n²) for both — discuss the constant-factor differences.

**8. (History track)** Place Tirthaji's project (1925–1960 work, posthumous 1965 publication) in the context of the Indian independence movement and post-colonial scientific identity. 2 pages.
