# Day 2 — The 16 Sūtras

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students name all 16 Tirthaji sūtras + 13 sub-sūtras and gloss the meaning of at least 5.

## Materials

- Printed table: all 16 + 13 sūtras with English translations
- `Microsoft Word - sutras1.pdf` reference

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Recall from Day 1: how many sūtras? (16 + 13 sub-sūtras.)

### Core (25 min)

1. **Walk through the 16 sūtras with translations:**

   | # | Sanskrit | English | Domain |
   |---|----------|---------|--------|
   | 1 | Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa | "By one more than the previous" | Multiplication, division |
   | 2 | Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ | "All from 9, last from 10" | Subtraction, multiplication |
   | 3 | Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyāṁ | "Vertically and crosswise" | General multiplication |
   | 4 | Parāvartya Yojayet | "Transpose and apply" | Division |
   | 5 | Śūnyaṁ Sāmyasamuccaye | "When the sum is the same, that sum is zero" | Equations |
   | 6 | (Ānurūpye) Śūnyam Anyat | "If one is in ratio, the other is zero" | Equations |
   | 7 | Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām | "By addition and by subtraction" | Equations, mental math |
   | 8 | Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyām | "By completing or non-completing" | Equations, mental math |
   | 9 | Calana-Kalanābhyām | "By differentiation and integration" | Calculus (modern interpretation) |
   | 10 | Yāvadūnam | "Whatever the extent of its deficiency" | Squaring near a base |
   | 11 | Vyaṣṭi-Samaṣṭiḥ | "Specific and general" | — |
   | 12 | Śeṣānyaṅkena Caramena | "The remainders by the last digit" | Division |
   | 13 | Sopāntya-dvayam Antyam | "The penultimate and the last" | — |
   | 14 | Ekanyūnena Pūrveṇa | "By one less than the previous" | Multiplication by 9, 99, etc. |
   | 15 | Guṇitasamuccayaḥ | "The product of the sum is the sum of the products" | — |
   | 16 | Guṇaka Samuccayaḥ | "All the multipliers" | — |

2. **The two sūtras most relevant to addition:**
   - **#7 *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām*** — "by addition and by subtraction" — the basis of the "add 10, subtract 1" trick (Day 4).
   - **#8 *Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyām*** — "by completing or non-completing" — the basis of "scan a column for completions to 10" (the dot method's core insight).

3. **The dot method itself is NOT one of the 16 sūtras.** It is a pedagogical technique that *uses* the spirit of sūtras 7 and 8. (Important to be honest about this; it comes back on Day 6.)

### Sūtra-recall quiz (10 min)

- 8 single-mark questions on the back of the worksheet:
  - Match 6 sūtras to their English meanings.
  - "Which sūtra means 'vertically and crosswise'?"
  - "Which 2 sūtras are most relevant to addition?"

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 3: *"Tomorrow — full dot-method at Senior level. 5-digit × 5-addend, sub-60-second target."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Pick a sūtra (say, *Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām*) and look up its multiplication application. Bring a worked example on Day 4.
- **Tier down:** Focus on memorising the names of sūtras 1, 2, 3, 7, 8 — the most commonly used.

## Teacher notes

- The "calculus" mapping for sūtra 9 (*Calana-Kalanābhyām*) is Tirthaji's later interpretation — Senior students may notice this is anachronistic. Acknowledge: "Tirthaji extends his sūtras' interpretation; whether all these extensions are defensible is part of the Day 6 historicity debate."
