# Day 1 — Baseline + The Historicity Question

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students record a personal baseline for standard-algorithm column addition AND consider the question: was "Vedic mathematics" actually Vedic?

## Materials

- Baseline sheet: a single 5-addend × 5-digit column sum
- Stopwatch
- Printed extract: Tirthaji's preface to *Vedic Mathematics* (1965)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Provocation: *"You've all heard of 'Vedic Mathematics.' What does the 'Vedic' part mean?"*

Take 3 student responses. Don't correct. Note variety of views.

### Baseline timing (5 min)

- 5-addend × 5-digit sum (e.g. 57283 + 14916 + 62385 + 27548 + 31057).
- Solve using your standard method. Record time.

### Core (25 min) — The 16 sūtras and the provenance puzzle

1. **The 16 sūtras** (write on board, abbreviated):
   ```
   1. Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa     2. Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ
   3. Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyāṁ     4. Parāvartya Yojayet
   5. Śūnyaṁ Sāmyasamuccaye  6. (Ānurūpye) Śūnyam Anyat
   7. Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām (← we'll meet this one)
   8. Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyām (← and this one)
   9. Calana-Kalanābhyām     10. Yāvadūnam
   11. Vyaṣṭi-Samaṣṭiḥ        12. Śeṣānyaṅkena Caramena
   13. Sopāntya-dvayam Antyam 14. Ekanyūnena Pūrveṇa
   15. Guṇitasamuccayaḥ      16. Guṇaka Samuccayaḥ
   ```

2. **Tirthaji's claim.** Read aloud the relevant sentence from Tirthaji's preface (in handout):
   > "The sūtras (aphorisms) of *Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa* are an integral part of the Vedas..."

   *(Tirthaji claims these 16 sūtras come from a Vedic appendix-text called the* Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa*.)*

3. **The puzzle:** No other Sanskritist has ever produced the manuscript of this *Pariśiṣṭa*. Tirthaji died (1960) before producing it; his book was published posthumously (1965).

4. **What's at stake.** If the sūtras are Vedic, they're 3000+ years old — a major historical claim. If they're not, they're a brilliant 20th-century synthesis — *which would also be a real achievement, just a different one*.

5. **Preview Day 6.** *"Day 6 we read the scholarly critique by S. G. Dani (1993). For now, hold the question open."*

### Wrap-up (10 min)

- Quick survey on the board:
  - Hands up if you think the sūtras are likely Vedic.
  - Hands up if you think they're more likely 20th-century.
  - Hands up if you don't know yet.
- Discuss the distribution.
- Homework: read Tirthaji's preface (handout). Bring questions.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Plofker (2009), Ch. 1 — the broader history of Indian mathematics. What was happening in the 19th–early 20th c. that might explain Tirthaji's project?
- **Tier down:** Focus on memorising the 16 sūtra names this week. The provenance question deepens at Day 6.

## Teacher notes

- Most students will start sympathetic to the "Vedic = ancient" claim. That's a starting point, not a fault.
- Don't decide the question for the class. The point is that they read primary and secondary sources, then form their own position.

## Citation(s) used

- Tirthaji (1965), *Vedic Mathematics*, Preface.
- *Microsoft Word - sutras1.pdf* — the sūtra list.
