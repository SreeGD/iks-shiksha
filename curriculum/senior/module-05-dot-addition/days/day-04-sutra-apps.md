# Day 4 — Sūtra Applications

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students apply *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* and *Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ* to a range of mental-arithmetic problems beyond simple addition.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Drill sheet (mixed sūtra applications)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Quick recall of sūtras 7 and 8.

### Core (25 min) — Sūtra in action

1. **Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām** — "by addition and by subtraction."
   - Addition: 33 + 9 = 33 + 10 − 1 = 42
   - Subtraction: 53 − 9 = 53 − 10 + 1 = 44
   - Larger: 234 + 98 = 234 + 100 − 2 = 332
   - Generalisation: when one operand is near a base, replace it with `base ± deficiency` and apply.

2. **Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ** — "by completing or non-completing."
   - Completion: 47 + 53 = 100 (complete to 100, no carry mess).
   - Non-completion as a technique: when partial sums equal a base, you "complete" them mentally.

3. **Application to the dot method.** The dot itself IS a Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ application — every dot = "I completed to 10." Connect this to the dot method procedure explicitly.

4. **A 4-question drill (10 min):**
   - 67 + 38 = ?  (use Sankalana)
   - 234 − 99 = ?  (use Sankalana)
   - Compute 4+6+7+3+8+2 by Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ (find completions first).
   - 99 × 4 = ?  (apply Ekanyūnena Pūrveṇa — "one less than the previous"; 99 = 100 − 1, so 99×4 = 400−4 = 396)

### Formative Part A (10 min)

- `quizzes/formative.md` Part A: 5 sūtra-application questions.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 5: *"Tomorrow — does the dot method work in other bases? Let's find out."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Pick *Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām* (vertically-and-crosswise multiplication). Self-teach from `Vedic Mathematics.pdf` §3.
- **Tier down:** Focus on Sankalana additions only.

## Teacher notes

- Some students will love the named-sūtra framing; some will find it pretentious. Both responses are fine. The math is what matters.
