# Day 3 — Dot Method at Senior Level

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students perform the dot method on 5-addend × 5-digit sums in under 90 seconds.

## Materials

- Practice sheets (3 levels: warm-up 3×3, target 4×4, stretch 5×5)
- Stopwatch
- Whiteboard

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Quick make-10 chorus (should be automatic).
- Recall: dot procedure (scan, dot on completion to 10, count dots as carry).

### Core (15 min) — Procedure refinement at speed

1. Demo on board: a 5×5 problem. Solve at speed (45 sec target).
2. Identify three skills that distinguish speed-users from slow-users:
   - **Eye scan pattern** — top-to-bottom, then jump to next-in-column-that-completes (rather than always one-at-a-time).
   - **Mental running total** — keep it in your head; don't recompute.
   - **Carry insertion** — write the carry as a tiny digit AT THE TOP of the next column before starting that column.

3. Worked example with these three skills called out explicitly.

### Drill (20 min)

Three rounds, increasing difficulty:

- **Round 1 (5 min):** 5 × 3-addend × 3-digit sums. Self-timed.
- **Round 2 (8 min):** 5 × 4-addend × 4-digit sums. Self-timed.
- **Round 3 (7 min):** 3 × 5-addend × 5-digit sums. Self-timed.

Record times for Round 3 — compare to Day 5's retest target.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Show of hands by Round 3 average time: <60 sec / 60–90 sec / 90+ sec.
- Preview Day 4: *"Tomorrow — Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām and Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyām with worked examples beyond just addition."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try 6-addend × 6-digit. Time-target: under 120 sec.
- **Tier down:** Stay at Round 2 level until comfortable.

## Teacher notes

- The "eye scan pattern" is the speed multiplier. Most students serial-scan. Teach the jump-pattern: see one completion, mark it, scan rest of column for the NEXT one without restarting.
