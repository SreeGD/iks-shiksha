# Day 7 — Discussion: How Should We Frame "Vedic Mathematics"?

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students articulate and defend a labelling position with at least one citation and one steel-manned counter.

## Materials

- Day 6 reading response in hand
- Whiteboard

## Flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- Three positions on the board:

  | Position | One-line claim |
  |----------|----------------|
  | **A — Defend "Vedic"** | The label "Vedic mathematics" is appropriate; Tirthaji's attribution should be defended. |
  | **B — Drop "Vedic"** | The label is misleading; call it "Tirthaji's 16 sūtras" or "Indian mental arithmetic." |
  | **C — Keep "Vedic" with caveat** | Use Tirthaji's label but with a footnote noting the contested provenance. |

### Debate (30 min)

- 5 min: self-select into groups by position.
- 10 min: groups prep — 2 main arguments + 1 steel-manned counter to each opposing position + 1 citation to use.
- 12 min: structured debate (4 min per group).
- 3 min: open floor cross-questions.

### Oral assessment (during debate)

Teacher uses same rubric as Module 2 Senior Day 7 — positions, evidence, counter-argument, listening.

### Wrap-up (7 min)

- 3 student reflections: did anyone's position shift?
- Note: *"You don't have to commit to a position tonight. But by Day 8, you'll choose a paper topic, and the position you defend in the paper might be different from the one you defended today. That's OK."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Take a position you DON'T agree with. Defend it.
- **Tier down:** Use your Day 6 reading response as your speaking notes.

## Teacher notes

- This debate is more politically loaded than the Module 2 debate. Some students' families have strong feelings. Model respect.
- The "Position C" hybrid is often the strongest scholarly position — but defend it on its merits, not as a compromise.

## Citation(s) used

- Tirthaji (1965), Preface.
- Dani (1993).
- Plofker (2009) optional.
