# Day 6 — The Historicity Question

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students read Dani's "Myths and Reality: On Vedic Mathematics" and identify Dani's three main critiques of Tirthaji's provenance claim.

## Materials

- Printed copy of Dani (1993/2001) — full text or teacher-prepared synopsis
- Tirthaji's preface (handout from Day 1)
- Quiet reading time

## Flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- *"Today: a scholarly reading. S. G. Dani is a respected mathematician at TIFR/IIT Bombay. He published this critique in 1993."*

### Reading (20 min)

- 20 min silent reading of Dani (1993).
- As they read, students note in their workbook:
  - Dani's three main critiques (in their own words).
  - One sentence from the text that struck them.
  - One question they want to ask.

### Discussion (15 min)

1. **Class compiles Dani's three main critiques** on the board:
   - No manuscript of the *Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa* containing these 16 sūtras has ever been produced.
   - The Sanskrit style of the sūtras (as Tirthaji presents them) is more recent than Vedic.
   - The mathematical techniques have 19th–early-20th-century European parallels (Trachtenberg etc.); calling them "Vedic" overstates the cultural specificity.

2. **Discuss:**
   - Are these critiques *fair*? Does Dani steel-man Tirthaji?
   - Does the absence of a manuscript = absence of the tradition?
   - If the sūtras are 20th-century, does that diminish their value?

3. **Frame the path forward:**
   - "The sūtras might NOT be Vedic AND might still be a real Indian intellectual achievement. The two are not in opposition. Tirthaji's achievement is the *synthesis and codification*, even if the *attribution* doesn't survive scrutiny."

### Reading response (7 min)

- Each student writes 1 paragraph (4–5 sentences): *"What is your current best position on whether 'Vedic mathematics' is fairly named?"*

### Wrap-up

- Preview Day 7: *"Tomorrow — structured debate. Pick your position."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Plofker (2009) Ch. 1 too. Compare Plofker's broader history with Dani's specific critique.
- **Tier down:** Focus on Dani's three critiques as written; the synthesis reasoning is the goal.

## Teacher notes

- Some students will react defensively to Dani's critique. Honour the reaction; don't dismiss it. Ask: *"What is Dani's strongest claim? What's his weakest?"*
- Dani's tone is firm but not dismissive. Model that tone.

## Citation(s) used

- Dani, S. G. (1993). "Myths and Reality: On Vedic Mathematics." *Frontline*. Reprinted in *Resonance* (October 2001).
