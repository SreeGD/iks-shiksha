# Project — Module 5 (Senior)

**Type:** 5–6 page research paper · **Marks:** 65 · **Due:** End of Day 10

## Choose your track

### Math track

Develop a mathematical paper. Options:
- Generalise the dot method to base-*n* and prove correctness via place-value reasoning.
- Compare the algorithmic complexity (and constant factors) of the dot method vs. standard right-to-left addition.
- Extend the dot method idea to multiplication (use *Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyāṁ*) — show how the "spirit" of the technique generalises.
- Apply the dot method to non-positional bases (e.g. balanced ternary). Does it still work? Why or why not?

### History track

Develop a humanities-style paper. Options:
- Argue: "Vedic mathematics" should be relabelled / retained / retained-with-caveat. Defend.
- Place Tirthaji's project in its historical-political moment (Indian independence, post-colonial science, etc.).
- Compare Tirthaji's project with the parallel emergence of Trachtenberg's system (1930s Germany/Poland). Independent invention or convergent evolution?
- Reconstruct what an Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa containing such sūtras *might* have looked like — and discuss what scholarly evidence would be needed to support the existence claim.

## Required elements

- Clear thesis (one paragraph in introduction).
- For math track: at least one correctly-worked algorithm OR formal proof.
- For history track: at least one steel-manned counter-argument with response.
- Citations from at least 3 sources: Tirthaji preface + Dani 1993 + one additional (Plofker, *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf*, Hayashi, etc.).
- Works-cited page in MLA or Chicago format.

## Format

- 5–6 pages, double-spaced, 12pt.
- Title page with name, class, track, thesis.
