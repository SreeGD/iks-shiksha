# A persistent question

*"What categories of stuff (and not-stuff) does the world come in?"*

Every culture's natural philosophers asked this.

Today: three answers — Greek, Sāṅkhya, modern.

---

# Three frameworks

| Framework | Date | Categories |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Greek (Empedocles, Aristotle) | 5th–4th c. BCE | 4 + 1 (aither) |
| Sāṅkhya (India) | pre-5th c. BCE | 25 tattvas (5 bhūtas) |
| Modern periodic table | 1869 → | 118+ elements |

---

# The 25 Tattvas

```
25. puruṣa — consciousness
24. prakṛti — primordial nature
23. buddhi / mahat — intellect
22. ahaṅkāra — ego
21. manas — mind
20–16. indriyas (sense organs)
15–11. karmendriyas (action organs)
10–6. tanmātras (subtle essences)
5–1. mahābhūtas (gross elements)
```

The 5 bhūtas are the most concrete (bottom) layer.

---

# The Bhūta–Tanmātra–Indriya Symmetry

| Bhūta | Tanmātra | Indriya |
|-------|----------|---------|
| ākāśa | śabda (sound) | śrotra (ear) |
| vāyu | sparśa (touch) | tvac (skin) |
| tejas | rūpa (form) | cakṣus (eye) |
| ap | rasa (taste) | rasanā (tongue) |
| pṛthvī | gandha (smell) | ghrāṇa (nose) |

**One-to-one, symmetric, complete.**

---

# Quality-count rule

Each bhūta inherits prior qualities + adds its own.

| Element | # qualities |
|---------|-------------|
| ākāśa | 1 (sound) |
| vāyu | 2 |
| tejas | 3 |
| ap | 4 |
| pṛthvī | 5 (all) |

**Predicted consequence**, not arbitrary stipulation.

---

# Primary source: SB 3.26.36

> *mṛdutvaṁ kaṭhinatvaṁ ca śaityam uṣṇatvam eva ca*
> *etat sparśasya sparśatvaṁ tanmātratvaṁ nabhasvataḥ*

Word-by-word:
- mṛdutvam = softness
- kaṭhinatvam = hardness
- śaityam = cold
- uṣṇatvam = heat
- sparśasya sparśatvam = "touch-ness of touch"
- tanmātratvaṁ nabhasvataḥ = the subtle essence of air

---

# Note the move

**"Touch-ness of touch"** — the verse names the *essence* of a category.

This is sophisticated philosophical work.

Compare modern dictionary: "touch — to come into physical contact with."

---

# Greek comparison

| | Sāṅkhya | Greek (Empedocles + Aristotle) |
|---|---|---|
| Elements | 5 | 4 + 1 |
| Origin | Emerge from prakṛti | Eternal roots |
| What combines them | 3 guṇas | Love + Strife / natural motions |
| Fifth element | ākāśa (everywhere) | aither (only celestial) |
| Quality-count | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 | hot/cold + wet/dry pairs |
| Tanmātra-indriya mapping | Yes | No |

---

# Aristotle's aither vs Sāṅkhya's ākāśa

**Aither:**
- Substance of celestial spheres
- Incorruptible, perfect
- ONLY in the heavens

**Ākāśa:**
- Universal medium
- Has a defining quality (sound)
- Present terrestrial AND celestial

Not the same. Don't conflate.

---

# Modern science: what it adds

- Atomic structure
- Predictive chemistry
- Fields, forces, quantum descriptions
- Quantitative measurement

---

# Modern science: what it drops

- Ākāśa as a positive category (vacuum = absence, not a category)
- Tanmātra-indriya mapping (perception treated as neuroscience, not metaphysics)
- Consciousness in the scheme (default in chemistry: bracketed)

**Different scopes, not different qualities.**

---

# A balanced view

| Question | Best framework |
|----------|------------------|
| "What's the predictive chemistry of NaCl?" | Modern periodic table |
| "What categories does my everyday world come in?" | Pañcabhūta |
| "How does perception relate to reality?" | Sāṅkhya tanmātra–indriya |
| "Where does consciousness fit?" | Sāṅkhya (puruṣa); modern: contested |
