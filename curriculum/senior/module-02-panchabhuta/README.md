# Module 2 — Pañcabhūta: Sāṅkhya Cosmology and Cross-Cultural Comparison

**Age band:** Senior · **Grades:** 9–12 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Recall** the *pañca-mahābhūta* and locate them within the 25-*tattva* enumeration of Sāṅkhya philosophy.
2. **Quote** at least one primary-source verse (in IAST with translation) on the cosmological emergence of the elements.
3. **Compare and contrast** the Sāṅkhya five-element scheme with (a) Empedocles' four elements, (b) Aristotle's *quintessence*, and (c) the modern atomic/molecular framework — articulating what each captures that the others do not.
4. **Analyse** the relationship between *bhūta* and *tanmātra* using the quality-count rule, and explain why the framework is internally consistent independent of modern science.
5. **Construct** an original argument (essay or research paper) on whether classical Indian intellectual frameworks should be taught alongside modern science in school — citing primary sources, secondary scholarship, and at least one counter-argument.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with Middle-band Module 2 content (the five elements at conceptual level).
- Comfort reading short Sanskrit verses with provided transliteration and translation.
- Basic familiarity with the periodic table and states of matter.
- Ability to write a structured 1–2 page argumentative essay.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md` for base entries. Senior-band additions:

- *tattva* — "thatness, principle." The 24 (or 25) principles of Sāṅkhya philosophy. The *bhūtas* are the last five.
- *prakṛti* — "nature, primordial matter."
- *puruṣa* — "person, consciousness."
- *guṇa* (Sāṅkhya sense) — *sattva, rajas, tamas* — the three constituent qualities of *prakṛti*.
- *avyakta* / *vyakta* — unmanifest / manifest.
