# Day 7 — Discussion: Should IKS Be in Science Class?

**Module:** Pañcabhūta (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students articulate and defend a position on whether classical Indian intellectual frameworks should be taught in mainstream science class, with at least one citation and one steel-manned counter-argument.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Stopwatch
- Score sheet for the oral assessment (rubric below)

## Flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- Brief: today is structured debate. Three positions on the table. Pick one. Defend it. Acknowledge the other two fairly.

### The three positions (5 min — clarify, don't argue yet)

Write on board:

| Position | One-line claim |
|----------|----------------|
| **A — Integration** | IKS frameworks should be taught alongside modern science in mainstream science class. |
| **B — Separation** | IKS should be taught in cultural studies or philosophy, not in science class. |
| **C — Hybrid** | IKS should be referenced briefly in science class as historical context, but not taught as parallel content. |

### Debate (25 min)

- Divide class into 3 groups by position (let students self-select; balance roughly).
- 5 min: each group prepares — strongest 2 arguments, 1 steel-manned response to the other two positions.
- 12 min: structured debate. Each group has 4 min.
  - 2 min: main argument.
  - 1 min: response to the other 2 positions.
  - 1 min: closing.
- 8 min: open floor cross-questions.

### Oral assessment (during the debate)

Teacher uses this rubric to score 5 students chosen at random:

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|
| Position clarity | Crystal clear with 1-line summary | Clear | Mostly clear | Unclear |
| Use of evidence | Cites at least 1 specific source | Cites generally | Vague references | No evidence |
| Counter-argument | Steel-mans an opposing view | Acknowledges another view | Mentions another view | Ignores |
| Listening | Responds to specific points raised | Responds generally | Sticks to script | Doesn't engage |

### Wrap-up (10 min)

- Take 3 student reflections: *"Did anyone's mind change during the debate?"*
- Note: the goal is not to "win" but to *understand the strongest version of each position*.
- Preview Day 8: *"Tomorrow you start your paper. Use this debate to sharpen your own thesis."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Take a position you DISAGREE with and defend it. (The hardest form of argument.)
- **Tier down:** Pre-prepare your 2-minute argument the night before.

## Teacher notes

- This is where some students discover they don't really know what they think. That's the point.
- If the debate gets heated, slow it down: *"Steel-man the other side. What's their best argument?"*
- The Day 7 oral grade flows into the project rubric — not as a separate score.

## Citation(s) used

- All sources from `sources.md` are fair game during the debate.
