# Day 1 — Hook: A Persistent Question

**Module:** Pañcabhūta (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students articulate what "categorising matter" means and identify three distinct historical frameworks for doing it.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Periodic table poster
- Printed copy of SB 2.10.3 with IAST + translation (from cache)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Opening provocation on board: *"Categories are not in nature. Categories are imposed on nature. Discuss."*

Take 3 student responses.

### Core (25 min)

1. **Three frameworks, one question.** Write on board:

   | Framework | Approximate date | Categories |
   |-----------|------------------|------------|
   | Greek (Empedocles, Aristotle) | 5th c. BCE → 4th c. BCE | 4 + 1 (quintessence) |
   | Sāṅkhya (India) | likely pre-5th c. BCE, codified c. 4th c. CE | 25 tattvas (5 are bhūtas) |
   | Modern periodic table | 1869 (Mendeleev) → present | 118+ elements |

2. **The persistent question:** *"What categories of stuff (and not-stuff) does the world come in?"* Every culture's natural philosophers tried to answer this. The answers differ because the methods differ and the questions are framed differently.

3. **Today's anchor verse — SB 2.10.3.** Read in IAST. Then translation:
   > "*bhūta-mātrendṛya-dhiyāṁ janma sarga udāhṛtaḥ ...*"
   > "The manifestation of the gross elements, the senses, and the intellect is called creation (*sarga*)."

   **Note the move:** the Sāṅkhya verse names the bhūtas (elements), the indriyas (senses), and the dhī (intellect) as products of a single creation event. Modern science talks about atoms; Sāṅkhya talks about a unified scheme that includes the *perceiver*. This is a real structural difference, not just terminology.

### Discussion (10 min)

Open question: *"Is the modern periodic table 'more correct' than Empedocles' four elements? More correct in what way?"*

- Let students argue.
- Reinforce: a framework can be more *predictive* (chemistry, atomic structure) without being more *comprehensive* (the Sāṅkhya scheme includes consciousness; modern chemistry doesn't claim to).

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 2: *"Tomorrow we map out the full 25-tattva ladder of Sāṅkhya. The five elements are only the LAST five."*
- Homework: read the SB 2.10.3 cache entry in full. Note one question you have.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Kirk, Raven & Schofield's intro on Empedocles. Be ready to present on Day 4.
- **Tier down:** Stick to the framework-comparison; the primary-source reading deepens on Day 5.

## Teacher notes

- Don't let "modern science is just objectively right" be the resting position. Push: "what does objectively right *mean*? Predictive accuracy? Comprehensiveness? Explanatory power?"
- This module is about *intellectual honesty across frameworks*, not about advocacy for any one of them.
