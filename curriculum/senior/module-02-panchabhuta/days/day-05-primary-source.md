# Day 5 — Primary Source Reading + Mid-Module Check

**Module:** Pañcabhūta (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students read SB 3.26.36 in IAST with word-by-word gloss and submit the formative quiz.

## Materials

- Printed handout: SB 3.26.36 in Devanāgarī, IAST, word-by-word gloss, continuous translation
- Formative quiz (`quizzes/formative.md` Part B)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Hand out the printed verse. Quick recall: yesterday's Greek comparison.

### Reading (15 min)

1. **First read — sound.** Teacher reads the IAST aloud once. Students listen.

   > *mṛdutvaṁ kaṭhinatvaṁ ca śaityam uṣṇatvam eva ca*
   > *etat sparśasya sparśatvaṁ tanmātratvaṁ nabhasvataḥ*

2. **Second read — meaning.** Students follow the word-by-word gloss:

   | Sanskrit | Gloss |
   |----------|-------|
   | mṛdutvam | softness (nominative neuter) |
   | kaṭhinatvam | hardness |
   | ca | and |
   | śaityam | cold |
   | uṣṇatvam | heat |
   | eva ca | as well |
   | etat | these |
   | sparśasya | of touch (genitive) |
   | sparśatvam | the touch-ness (= the defining quality of touch) |
   | tanmātratvam | the subtle-essence-ness |
   | nabhasvataḥ | of air / wind (genitive; nabhasvat = wind) |

   Continuous translation:
   > "Softness, hardness, cold and heat — these are the touch-ness of touch, the subtle essence of air."

3. **Class discussion (8 min):**
   - What surprises you about the categories included under "touch"?
   - Note the philosophical move: *sparśasya sparśatvam* (touch-ness of touch) — the verse identifies the defining property of a category by giving its essence a name.
   - How is this different from a modern dictionary definition of "touch"?

### Formative quiz (15 min)

- `quizzes/formative.md` Part B.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 6: *"Tomorrow — the modern lens. What modern science adds, and what it explicitly drops."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Write a 1-paragraph response to "what is gained when philosophy gives an *essence* a name?"
- **Tier down:** Focus on the meaning. The IAST pronunciation is bonus.

## Teacher notes

- Don't require pronunciation perfection. The win is *engagement with the meaning at primary-source level*.
- This is many students' first time reading a Sanskrit verse. Honour the moment.
