# Day 9 — Project Session 2: Draft + Counter-Arguments

**Module:** Pañcabhūta (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Each student reaches at least 2 drafted pages including a fully-articulated counter-argument and response.

## Materials

- Drafts in progress
- Rubric

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Brief: today's focus is the counter-argument. The rubric rewards a *fair* presentation of the strongest opposing view.

### Core (30 min)

**Sprint 1 (15 min) — Draft body.** Quiet writing. Aim for 1.5–2 pages drafted.

**Sprint 2 (15 min) — Counter-argument workshop.**
- Pair with a buddy.
- Read each other's drafts so far.
- Each pair argues: *"Take my thesis. Give me the strongest counter-argument you can think of."*
- The student takes notes on the counter-argument and decides how to respond in their paper.

### Wrap-up (10 min)

- 3 students share their best-discovered counter-argument.
- Outline-to-date submitted at door (revised since Day 8).

## Homework

- Finish a complete draft at home tonight. Bring tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Construct TWO counter-arguments — one philosophical, one practical.
- **Tier down:** Submit a clear outline + 1 paragraph if a full draft is too much.

## Teacher notes

- Some students will skip the counter-argument because it feels like "weakening" their position. Reframe: a strong paper anticipates and answers objections.
