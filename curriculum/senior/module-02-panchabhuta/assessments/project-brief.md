# Project Brief — Module 2 Pañcabhūta (Senior)

**Type:** 5-page argumentative paper · **Marks:** 65 · **Due:** End of Day 10

## The question

Choose ONE of the following or propose your own (with teacher approval):

1. **Should classical Indian intellectual frameworks be taught alongside modern science in mainstream science class?**
2. **Is the Sāṅkhya tanmātra-indriya symmetry a philosophical achievement that modern psychophysics later rediscovered, or an unrelated coincidence?**
3. **Does the Sāṅkhya inclusion of ākāśa as a positive category (not "absence of matter") anticipate any question that modern field theory addresses?**
4. **Compare the role of guṇas (sattva, rajas, tamas) in Sāṅkhya with the role of Love-and-Strife in Empedocles. What does each scheme explain that the other doesn't?**

## Required elements

Your paper must include:

1. **Clear thesis statement** in the opening paragraph.
2. **At least one primary scriptural source** quoted in IAST + translation (from the RAG cache files in `.rag-cache/`).
3. **At least one secondary scholarly source** (Larson, Plofker, Bryant, or another academic source you locate).
4. **At least one cross-cultural or modern comparative source** (Empedocles, Aristotle, modern science).
5. **A steel-manned counter-argument** and your considered response to it.
6. **Works-cited page** with proper formatting (MLA or Chicago).

## Format

- 5 pages (4.5–5.5 acceptable)
- Double-spaced, 12pt
- Title page with name, class, thesis statement
- IAST diacritics used correctly
