## Pacing notes

- This is a reading-and-writing heavy module. Build in time for *quiet reading* and *quiet drafting*.
- The Day 5 primary-source reading is the most demanding session — students read a Sanskrit verse with transliteration and grapple with the meaning. Schedule it when energy is highest.
- Day 7 debate can run long. Cap at 35 min.
- Paper drafts (Day 9): students should leave class with at least 2 pages drafted.

## What students leave with

- Working vocabulary for Sāṅkhya philosophy (tattva, prakṛti, puruṣa, guṇa).
- Ability to read a short Sanskrit verse with translation and discuss it.
- Comparative analytical writing.
- One original argumentative paper.
- The habit of holding multiple frameworks simultaneously without collapsing them into one another.
