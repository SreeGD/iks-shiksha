# Module 2 (Senior) — Formative Quizzes

## Part A — Day 3 (8 min, 5 questions)

**1.** (2 marks) Write the 5 bhūtas in emergence order with their tanmātras.

**2.** (1 mark) What does *sparśa* include in SB 3.26.36 that modern English "touch" usually does NOT?

**3.** (1 mark) Why does *pṛthvī* have 5 qualities while *ākāśa* has only 1?

**4.** (1 mark) The Sāṅkhya scheme has how many total tattvas?

**5.** (1 mark) Where do the five bhūtas sit in the tattva ladder — at the top, middle, or bottom?

### Answer key

| Q | Answer | Marks |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | ākāśa-śabda, vāyu-sparśa, tejas-rūpa, ap-rasa, pṛthvī-gandha | 2 (1 if order correct, 1 if pairings correct) |
| 2 | Cold and heat (thermal sensations) | 1 |
| 3 | Earth emerges last, inheriting all prior qualities AND adding its own (gandha). Space emerges first, has no prior qualities to inherit, adds only śabda. | 1 |
| 4 | 25 | 1 |
| 5 | Bottom (most concrete, most manifest layer) | 1 |

**Total Part A:** 6 marks.

## Part B — Day 5 (20 min, 6 questions including primary source)

**1.** (3 marks) Read this verse (already provided in handout):

> *mṛdutvaṁ kaṭhinatvaṁ ca śaityam uṣṇatvam eva ca / etat sparśasya sparśatvaṁ tanmātratvaṁ nabhasvataḥ*

Identify (1 mark each):
   a) Which bhūta is named? _______________
   b) Which tanmātra is identified as belonging to it? _______________
   c) What four specific qualities are listed as examples? _______________

**2.** (2 marks) Translate *sparśasya sparśatvam* word-by-word. What philosophical move does this phrase make?

**3.** (2 marks) Compare: Aristotle's *aither* and Sāṅkhya's *ākāśa*. Name ONE crucial difference.

**4.** (2 marks) Why is the bhūta–tanmātra–indriya mapping called "one-to-one" and "symmetric"?

**5.** (2 marks) State one thing modern science ADDS that Sāṅkhya does not have, and one thing Sāṅkhya INCLUDES that modern science excludes.

**6.** (3 marks) Open: in 3–4 sentences, articulate your current best position on whether Sāṅkhya's tanmātra-indriya mapping is "philosophically interesting" or "empirically obsolete." Defend.

### Answer key

| Q | Answer | Marks |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1a | vāyu (air; nabhasvat) | 1 |
| 1b | sparśa (touch) | 1 |
| 1c | softness, hardness, cold, heat (mṛdutvam, kaṭhinatvam, śaityam, uṣṇatvam) | 1 |
| 2 | "touch-ness of touch" — the defining quality of touch. The move: naming the *essence* of a category so it can be philosophically tracked. | 2 |
| 3 | Aether: ONLY in heavens, incorruptible, celestial. Ākāśa: EVERYWHERE, the universal space-medium, terrestrial AND celestial. | 2 |
| 4 | 5 bhūtas, 5 tanmātras, 5 indriyas — each in one-to-one pairing. No bhūta has two tanmātras; no tanmātra has two perceivers. | 2 |
| 5 | Examples: modern adds atomic structure / predictive chemistry; Sāṅkhya includes empty space (ākāśa) as a category / consciousness (puruṣa) in the scheme | 2 |
| 6 | Personal answer. Marked: 1 for clear position, 1 for ≥1 specific reason, 1 for clarity. | 3 |

**Total Part B:** 14 marks.
