# Doshas — Vāta, Pitta, Kapha

**Band:** Senior (Grades 9–12) · **Sessions:** 10 × 45 min · **Sanskrit:** Tri-doṣa

## Overview

Three patterns of internal rhythm — *vāta* (motion), *pitta* (transformation), and *kapha* (structure) — that Āyurveda uses to describe individual constitutional differences. Mapped to *pañcabhūta* from Module 2 and framed as wellness lens, NOT medical diagnosis.

## Key terms (this module)

- *doṣa* — humour / functional principle
- *vāta* — motion principle (wind, ākāśa+vāyu)
- *pitta* — transformation principle (fire, tejas+ap)
- *kapha* — structure principle (cohesion, ap+pṛthvī)
- *prakṛti* — innate constitution
- *vikṛti* — current imbalance

## 10-day map

- **Day 1** — Three Doshas Introduction: Name vāta, pitta, kapha and recognise one observable trait of each.
- **Day 2** — Vāta — The Motion Principle: Identify vāta-style behaviours (quick, dry, cold, moving) in self and surroundings.
- **Day 3** — Pitta — The Transformation Principle: Identify pitta-style behaviours (warm, sharp, focused, transforming).
- **Day 4** — Kapha — The Structure Principle: Identify kapha-style behaviours (steady, cool, moist, structured).
- **Day 5** — Mapping to the Five Elements: Connect each doṣa to its two constituent elements (vāta=ākāśa+vāyu, pitta=tejas+ap, kapha=ap+pṛthvī).
- **Day 6** — Prakṛti Self-Assessment: Use a worksheet to estimate own dominant doṣa(s) — emphasising NOT diagnosis.
- **Day 7** — Doṣa Rhythms in a Day: Map the kapha-pitta-vāta cycle across morning/noon/evening hours.
- **Day 8** — Doṣa Rhythms Across Seasons: Map doṣa cycles to monsoon/summer/winter.
- **Day 9** — Balance and Imbalance: Distinguish prakṛti (innate) from vikṛti (current state); recognise simple imbalance signs.
- **Day 10** — Synthesis and Project Pitch: Pitch the 7-day self-observation project and answer assessment quiz.
