# Module 3 — Ayurveda: Classical Foundations and Modern Evidence

**Age band:** Senior · **Grades:** 9–12 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Locate** Ayurveda within the IKS map (Upaveda of the Atharva Veda) and identify the 8 branches (*aṣṭāṅga*) and 3 foundational texts (Caraka Saṃhitā, Suśruta Saṃhitā, Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya).
2. **Read** at least one primary source verse (Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna excerpt) and one BG verse (6.17) in IAST + translation.
3. **Articulate** the Tridosha theory at scholarly depth, including its dual nature (typology + medical framework) and contemporary scholarly engagement with it.
4. **Distinguish** the parts of Ayurveda that have strong modern evidence (turmeric anti-inflammatory action, time-restricted eating, sleep hygiene) from the parts that don't (e.g. dosha-as-strict-biomedical-classification).
5. **Engage** the contemporary debate about integrating Ayurveda into modern healthcare (AYUSH ministry, NHS pilot programmes, AIIMS integration units), articulating multiple defensible positions.
6. **Construct** a 5–6 page research-synthesis paper on a specific Ayurvedic claim, integrating classical primary sources, modern empirical research, and one historical/scholarly source.

## Prerequisites

- Module 2 (Panchabhuta) recommended — Tridosha builds on the five elements.
- Basic biology of digestion, circadian rhythms.
- Familiarity with structured argumentative writing + IAST reading.

## Key vocabulary (Senior additions)

- *aṣṭāṅga Ayurveda* — the 8 branches
- *kāya-cikitsā* — internal medicine
- *śalya-tantra* — surgery (Sushruta's domain)
- *prakṛti / vikṛti* — constitution / current state
- *pramāṇa* — means of valid knowledge (epistemological framework)

## Materials checklist

- Patwardhan et al. (2015) extract on integrative approaches
- Sample modern RCT abstract on turmeric (Aggarwal & Harikumar 2009 or similar)
- BG 6.17 handout
