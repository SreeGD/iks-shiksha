# Day 1 — Ayurveda's Place in IKS

**Module:** Ayurveda (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students locate Ayurveda within the IKS map and name the 8 branches and 3 foundational texts.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Reference card with the 8 branches

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Provocation: *"In what way is Ayurveda 'science'? In what way is it not?"*

Take 3 student answers.

### Core (25 min)

1. **Ayurveda's place.** Ayurveda is an **Upaveda** — a "subordinate Veda." Specifically, it's traditionally an Upaveda of the **Atharva Veda** (which contained early medical material).

2. **The 8 branches (aṣṭāṅga Ayurveda):**

   | # | Sanskrit | Domain |
   |---|----------|--------|
   | 1 | Kāya-cikitsā | Internal medicine |
   | 2 | Bāla-cikitsā | Pediatrics |
   | 3 | Graha-cikitsā | "Psychiatry" / affliction medicine |
   | 4 | Ūrdhvāṅga-cikitsā | ENT, ophthalmology |
   | 5 | Śalya-tantra | Surgery |
   | 6 | Daṁṣṭra-cikitsā | Toxicology |
   | 7 | Jarā-cikitsā | Geriatrics, rejuvenation |
   | 8 | Vṛṣa-cikitsā | Reproductive medicine |

   Note: this is a *comprehensive* medical framework, not just herbalism.

3. **The bṛhattrayī ("great three") foundational texts:**

   - **Caraka Saṃhitā** (~2nd c. BCE) — internal medicine focus. Attributed to Caraka; redacted by Dṛḍhabala.
   - **Suśruta Saṃhitā** (~6th c. BCE or later) — surgery focus. Suśruta is the source of the cataract-surgery and rhinoplasty procedures Module 1 covered.
   - **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya** (~7th c. CE) — synthesis by Vāgbhaṭa; standard classical textbook.

4. **The honest scope:** Ayurveda was (and is) a comprehensive medical system covering internal medicine, surgery, pediatrics, psychiatry, ENT, toxicology. It is NOT just "herbs."

### Discussion (10 min)

- Which branch surprised you most?
- Where does Ayurveda overlap with modern medicine cleanly?

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 2: *"Tomorrow — we read Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna ch. 1 in IAST."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Wujastyk (2003) intro chapter.
- **Tier down:** Memorise the 3 foundational texts + 4 of the 8 branches.

## Teacher notes

- Many students think Ayurveda = herbal teas. The 8-branches list is a corrective.
- The Suśruta-surgery point ties to Module 1; reinforces continuity.

## Citation(s) used

- Wujastyk (2003), introduction.
- Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna ch. 1 — Sharma (1981).
