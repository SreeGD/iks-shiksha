# Day 6 — BG 6.17 + Lifestyle Medicine Convergence

**Module:** Ayurveda (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students read BG 6.17 in IAST + translation and articulate the convergence between traditional dinacharya and modern lifestyle medicine.

## Materials

- BG 6.17 handout (IAST + translation + Bryant commentary)
- Panda (2018) extract on time-restricted eating

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Recall: 5 dinacharya practices.

### Reading (15 min)

> *yuktāhāra-vihārasya yukta-ceṣṭasya karmasu*
> *yukta-svapnāvabodhasya yogo bhavati duḥkha-hā*
> — Bhagavad-gītā 6.17

Word-by-word + translation (as in Middle Day 7 — see also `curriculum/middle/module-03-ayurveda-good-health/days/day-07-primary-source.md`).

### The convergence (15 min)

Build comparison table:

| Traditional principle (Caraka, BG) | Modern lifestyle medicine equivalent | Evidence |
|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|----------|
| Wake before sunrise | Aligning with circadian rhythm | Nobel 2017 — circadian biology |
| Largest meal midday (Pitta peak) | Time-restricted eating; metabolic window | Panda et al. (2018) |
| Sleep by ~10pm | Aligning with melatonin curve | Sleep science (Walker 2017) |
| Regular exercise to 50% capacity | Moderate-intensity training | Standard exercise physiology |
| Six tastes per meal | Macronutrient diversity, polyphenol intake | Nutrition science |

**Note the convergence is striking but not identical.** The tradition observed; modern science measures. They overlap in many places; they're not the same project.

### Discussion (8 min)

- *"Is the convergence evidence that the tradition was 'right'? Or that human physiology hasn't changed?"*
- Both. Don't force a binary.

### Wrap-up (2 min)

- Preview Day 7: *"Tomorrow — the contemporary integration debate."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Walker (2017) *Why We Sleep* chapter on circadian.
- **Tier down:** Focus on the convergence table.

## Teacher notes

- Convergence is a powerful pedagogical moment but DON'T oversell ("Ayurveda predicted everything").
- The honest framing: tradition + science arrived at overlapping conclusions by different methods.
