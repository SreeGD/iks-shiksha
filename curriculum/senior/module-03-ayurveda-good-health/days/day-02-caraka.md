# Day 2 — Caraka Saṃhitā: Primary Source Reading

**Module:** Ayurveda (Senior) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students read selected verses from Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna in IAST + translation, identifying Caraka's definition of *āyu* (life) and his epistemological framework.

## Materials

- Printed handout: Caraka Sūtra-sthāna 1.41–45 (definition of āyu) in IAST + translation
- Optional: Caraka Sūtra-sthāna 11 (pramāṇa — means of valid knowledge)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Recall: 3 foundational texts; 4 of the 8 branches.

### Reading (25 min)

1. **Caraka's definition of *āyu*** (Sūtra-sthāna 1.42):

   > *śarīrendṛyasattvātmasaṁyogo dhāri jīvitam*
   > *nityagaścānubandhaśca paryāyair āyur ucyate*

   "The conjunction of body, senses, mind, and self — which sustains [life] — is called *jīvita* (life). Persistent and connected — these are synonyms for *āyu*."

   **Word-by-word:**
   - *śarīra* = body
   - *indṛya* = senses
   - *sattva* = mind
   - *ātma* = self
   - *saṁyogo* = conjunction, union
   - *dhāri* = sustainer
   - *jīvitam* = life

   **Note the move:** āyu = body + senses + mind + self. **A four-component definition of life.** Not just biology — includes psychology and the witnessing self.

2. **Caraka's epistemology (Sūtra-sthāna 11).** Three pramāṇas (means of valid knowledge):

   - *Āpta-upadeśa* — authoritative teaching (testimony)
   - *Pratyakṣa* — direct perception
   - *Anumāna* — inference

   Note: this is essentially the same epistemological framework that the Nyāya school formalised (with one additional pramāṇa — comparison). Caraka was working within an articulated philosophy of knowledge.

3. **Discussion:**
   - How does Caraka's 4-component āyu compare with the modern biomedical view of "life"?
   - The pramāṇa framework predates Aristotle's *Posterior Analytics* in important ways. Compare.

### Activity (10 min)

Quiet reading. Students annotate the verse handout. Note one question.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 3: *"Tomorrow — Tridosha theory in classical depth + modern engagement."*

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Sūtra-sthāna ch. 11 in full (Sharma 1981 translation).
- **Tier down:** Focus on the *āyu* definition; the pramāṇa material is bonus.

## Teacher notes

- Caraka's text is dense. Don't try to cover everything. The *āyu* definition + the pramāṇa naming are enough.
- The "mind + self" inclusion in life surprises students raised on a body-only definition. Honour the surprise.
