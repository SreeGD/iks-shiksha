# Caraka's definition of life

> *śarīrendṛyasattvātmasaṁyogo dhāri jīvitam*

Body + senses + mind + self.

A 4-component definition. Not just biology.

---

# Caraka's epistemology

3 pramāṇas (means of valid knowledge):

- **Āpta-upadeśa** — authoritative teaching
- **Pratyakṣa** — direct perception
- **Anumāna** — inference

(Compare with Aristotle's *Posterior Analytics*.)

---

# Tridosha in depth

Each dosha has FIVE sub-types with anatomical locations.

E.g. Pitta sub-types: Pācaka (digestive), Rañjaka (coloring), Sādhaka (intellect), Ālocaka (vision), Bhrājaka (skin).

The framework is more granular than "fire dosha."

---

# Modern engagement with Tridosha

- Prasher et al. (2008) — some gene-expression differences by prakṛti type.
- Patwardhan (2014) — "Ayurgenomics."
- Critical: dosha-as-biomarker has mixed evidence; dosha-as-clinical-heuristic has utility.

**Distinguish the claims.**

# Reading an RCT abstract

Hedge words matter:
- "may"
- "possibly"
- "limited data"
- "further research needed"

These are the signals of honest science.

# BG 6.17 — the convergence

> *yuktāhāra-vihārasya yukta-ceṣṭasya karmasu*
> *yukta-svapnāvabodhasya yogo bhavati duḥkha-hā*

5 activities to regulate: eating, recreation, work, sleep, waking.

Modern lifestyle medicine arrives at the same answers — by different methods.
