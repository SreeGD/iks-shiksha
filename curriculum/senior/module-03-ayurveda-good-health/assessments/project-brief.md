# Project Brief — Module 3 (Senior)

**Type:** 5–6 page research-synthesis paper · **Marks:** 65

## Topics

Choose one or propose:

1. **A specific intervention** — e.g. "Curcumin's anti-inflammatory action — what does the evidence say?" or "Triphala for digestive function — review."
2. **A specific framework claim** — e.g. "Is the Tridosha typology validated by modern biomarker studies?"
3. **A specific convergence** — e.g. "Dinacharya and circadian biology — same insight or convergent inquiry?"
4. **A specific policy question** — e.g. "Should AYUSH practitioners be allowed to prescribe biomedical drugs?"
5. **A specific historical question** — e.g. "Sushruta's surgical legacy — what specifically did European surgeons learn?"

## Required elements

- Clear thesis in opening.
- ≥1 classical primary source (Caraka, Suśruta, Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya, BG) — IAST + translation.
- ≥1 peer-reviewed modern empirical study.
- ≥1 historical / scholarly source (Wujastyk, Patwardhan).
- Steel-manned counter-argument.
- MLA or Chicago citations.

## Format

5–6 pages, double-spaced, 12pt.

## Schedule

- Day 5: topic + thesis.
- Day 8: outline.
- Day 9: draft.
- Day 10: paper submitted.
