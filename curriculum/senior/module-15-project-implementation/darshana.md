# Module 15 — Project Implementation — Capstone (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Pañcagavya (and other capstones)* · **Band:** Senior (9-12) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

10-day applied project drawing on Modules 1-14.

> **Darśana note.** This workshop condenses Module 15's full 10-day arc into one hour. For the deeper version — daily lesson plans, quizzes, assessments, slide decks — switch to **[Adhyayana](../)** (the Full Course path).

---

## Opening hook · 5 min

Project: "Knowledge without application is incomplete." *"You've seen 14 modules. The capstone asks: where will you take this beyond the classroom? Today: project menu, scoping, and the safety floor."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**Capstone menu — 10 options + 1 open-design:**

1-9 as above. Plus:
- **Critical-history essay** (any module) — 1000 words on the honest dating + historiography of one IKS claim.
- **Open-design** — propose your own project that draws on at least 2 modules. Pitch to teacher for approval.

**Project requirements:**
- Daily journal (10 days, minimum 100 words/day)
- Source citations (minimum 5 sources, verifiable)
- Reflection (500 words at end: what you did, what worked, what you'd change)
- Class presentation (5 minutes)

**Safety floor (repeated):**
- NO cow-urine ingestion for pañcagavya — use fermented-foods comparative study
- NO unsupervised herbal medicine experiments
- NO extreme yoga poses without instructor

**Assessment:** teacher rubric (60%) + self-assessment (20%) + peer feedback (20%).

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

**Project-scoping workshop.** Pairs swap project ideas. Each pair gives the other (a) one strength, (b) one concern, (c) one suggested narrowing or expansion. Then each student writes their final scope.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

**Direct verse.** Bhagavad Gītā 2.47:

> *karmaṇy-evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana /*
> *mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo 'stv akarmaṇi //*

"You have a right to action, never to its fruits. Let not the fruits of action be your motive; nor be attached to inaction."

A capstone project is an exercise in *karma-yoga* — focused, disciplined work without obsessing over the outcome.

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Project scoping + critique.** 40 minutes individual + 10 minutes pair-critique. Submit plan for teacher approval.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"How does THIS capstone challenge your assumptions about IKS? What's the riskiest part of your project — and why is the risk worth taking?"*
