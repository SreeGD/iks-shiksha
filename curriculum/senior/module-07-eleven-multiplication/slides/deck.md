# ×11 Multiplication — Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa
## Mental multiplication by 11 from Tirthaji's Vedic Mathematics
### Senior band — Grades 9–12


---
## What this module is about


A mental-arithmetic method for multiplying any number by 11 instantly: split the digits, add adjacent pairs, place sums in between. Tirthaji's *Vedic Mathematics* (1965) labelled this an application of the *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* sūtra. Honest history is taught alongside: this is a 20th-century mental-arithmetic system, not strictly ancient mathematics.

## Key Sanskrit terms


- *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* — "by one more than the previous" — sūtra 1 in Tirthaji

- *sūtra* — aphorism / formula

- *Anurūpyena* — "proportionately" — sūtra used for ×12, ×13 extensions

---
## Day 1 — The Hook — What Is the Trick?

Compute 23×11, 35×11 mentally using the split-and-sum method.


---
## Day 2 — The Carry Case

Compute 87×11, 65×11 correctly using the carry rule.


---
## Day 3 — Why It Works — The Algebra

Derive (10a+b)×11 = 100a + 10(a+b) + b and explain.


---
## Day 4 — 3-Digit × 11

Compute 234×11 = 2_5_7_4 using the extended split method.


---
## Day 5 — Speed Day

Complete a 50-problem mental-math sprint in 5 minutes.


---
## Day 6 — Anurūpyena — ×12 to ×19

Extend the method to ×12 and ×13 using one-more-digit adjustments.


---
## Day 7 — Limits of the Method

Recognise when traditional long multiplication is faster than the sūtra method.

## Day 9 — Mixed Practice

## Activity


**Mental Math Sprint Cards**


In pairs: one student holds up a card, the other answers within 3 seconds using the ×11 method. Swap. Track personal best time.

## Project


**Method Comparison Poster**


Choose 5 multiplication problems and solve them using both the ×11 method and traditional long multiplication. Time yourself. Make a poster showing when each method is faster and why.
