# Parent Guide — Module 1 (Senior): What is IKS?

**Your student's age:** Senior (Grades 9–12) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## What your student is learning

This is an introductory but rigorous module on Indian intellectual history:

- The canonical structure of Indian Knowledge Systems — 4 Vedas, 4 layers within each, 6 Vedāṅgas, 4 Upavedas, 6 darśanas (philosophical schools).
- **Primary-source reading** — Ṛg Veda 1.1, Īśa Upaniṣad, one Aryabhata verse — in IAST with translation.
- **Specific intellectual achievements** — Pāṇini's grammar, Mādhava's infinite series, Sushruta's surgery, Aryabhata's earth-rotation, the Yavana (Greek-Indian) exchange.
- **The contemporary policy debate** — NEP 2020 §4.6 on integrating IKS into mainstream education. Three positions taken seriously: integration, separation, qualified integration.
- **A 5–6 page research paper** on a specific IKS claim, with primary, scholarly, and comparative sources.

## What's at stake

Your student will engage these honest framings:

- **"IKS" as a single category is recent (post-2000).** The intellectual traditions it groups are ancient. The label is newer. This matters for clear thinking.
- **Real achievements deserve specific credit.** Pāṇini's grammar (~4th c. BCE), Mādhava's π series (~14th c. CE), Sushruta's surgery (~6th c. BCE) — named, dated, defensible.
- **Some popular claims overreach.** "Vedic mathematics" (Module 5) is 20th-century, not Vedic. "Indians knew everything first" is triumphalism, not history. Your student learns to push back fairly.

## Policy debate (Day 7)

Three positions, taken seriously:

1. **Integration:** IKS should be taught alongside modern science. (NEP §4.6.)
2. **Separation:** IKS belongs in humanities/history class; modern science class should focus on the scientific method.
3. **Qualified integration:** Historical context yes; equivalent scientific validity no.

All three have scholarly defenders. Your student will steel-man all three before forming their own view.

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Read NEP §4.6 yourself; discuss with your student.
- Recommend a scholarly book: Plofker, Wujastyk, Pollock.
- For the paper: be a thoughtful skeptic.

**Not helpful:**
- Push them toward a "correct" position on the policy debate. The point is informed engagement.
- Insist that all claims about IKS are equally valid OR equally false.

## What success looks like

By end of module, your student can:
- Read a Vedic verse in IAST with translation and discuss it.
- Name 3+ specific Indian intellectual achievements with dates and authors.
- Steel-man all three policy positions.
- Write a research paper that holds historical context and modern scientific standards together.
