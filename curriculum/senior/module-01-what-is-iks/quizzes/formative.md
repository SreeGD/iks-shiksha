## Part A — Day 3 (8 min)

1. (2) When did the term "IKS" gain currency as a unified category?
2. (2) Name the 6 darśanas.
3. (1) Which darśana provides the metaphysics underlying Yoga?
4. (1) What distinguishes *āstika* from *nāstika* schools?
5. (2) Name two Indian intellectual traditions excluded from a narrow "Brahmanical IKS" definition.

1. Post-2000; codified by NEP 2020. 2. Nyāya, Vaiśeṣika, Sāṅkhya, Yoga, Mīmāṁsā, Vedānta. 3. Sāṅkhya. 4. Acceptance vs non-acceptance of Vedic authority. 5. Buddhism, Jainism (or Cārvāka).

## Part B — Day 5 (15 min, 8 Q)

1. (2) Translate word-by-word: *agnim īḷe purohitam* (Ṛg 1.1 opening).
2. (2) What does the Īśa Upaniṣad opening verse say about ownership?
3. (1) The Bakhshali manuscript dates from approximately when?
4. (1) Who computed an infinite series for π in the 14th c. CE in Kerala?
5. (1) What did Aryabhata propose about the earth?
6. (1) What did he NOT propose?
7. (2) The Yavana exchange in astronomy — what does it show about "pure Indian" framings?
8. (3) Briefly state the three positions on the NEP IKS integration debate.

### Key
1. agnim (Agni, acc.) īḷe (I praise) purohitam (household priest). 2. Do not covet what is not yours; whatever moves in this world is enveloped by the Lord. 3. ~7th c. CE. 4. Mādhava of Saṅgamagrāma. 5. The earth rotates on its axis. 6. Heliocentrism (sun-centred). 7. Indian astronomers actively absorbed Greek methods (Varāhamihira's Pañca-siddhāntikā compares 5 systems including Greek-derived). "Pure Indian" overstates; the tradition was cosmopolitan. 8. (A) Integration — IKS in mainstream science class. (B) Separation — IKS in humanities/history. (C) Qualified integration — historical context yes, equivalent validity no.
