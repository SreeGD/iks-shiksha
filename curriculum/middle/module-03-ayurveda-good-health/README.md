# Module 3 — Ayurveda and Good Health

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Define** *Āyurveda* (literally "science of life") and *Swasthya* (wellbeing, including physical, mental, and social dimensions).
2. **Name** the three doshas (*Vāta, Pitta, Kapha*) with their elemental basis, qualities, and observable signs.
3. **Recall** the components of *Dinacharya* (daily routine) — waking, oral hygiene, oil massage, exercise, meals, sleep — and articulate the principle behind each.
4. **Quote** at least one primary source verse (Bhagavad-gītā 6.17 on regulated lifestyle) in IAST with translation.
5. **Apply** the framework: design a 1-week dinacharya for themselves with rationale.
6. **Distinguish** Ayurveda's domain (lifestyle, prevention, chronic care, herbal pharmacology) from modern allopathic medicine's domain (acute care, surgery, infectious disease), articulating where they're complementary and where they're not equivalent.

## Key vocabulary

- *Āyurveda* — "science of life"
- *Swasthya* — wellbeing (literally "self-established")
- *Dosha* — humour, biological energy
- *Vāta, Pitta, Kapha* — the three doshas
- *Dinacharya* — daily routine
- *Ritucharya* — seasonal routine
- *Ahāra* — food
- *Vihāra* — recreation, lifestyle

## Project brief

Each student designs a 7-day dinacharya (daily routine) for themselves, with one line per item explaining the Ayurvedic principle behind it. Presented Day 10. Full brief in `assessments/project-brief.md`.

## Safety note

Ayurveda is not magic. Students with medical conditions (asthma, allergies, diabetes, etc.) should NOT change their existing care based on this module. The module teaches the framework; medical decisions belong with a qualified physician.
