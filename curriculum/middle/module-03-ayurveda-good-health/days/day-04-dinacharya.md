# Day 4 — Dinacharya (Daily Routine)

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students recall the components of *dinacharya* (waking, oral hygiene, oil massage, exercise, meals, sleep) and articulate the principle behind each.

## Materials

- Wall chart of dinacharya timeline
- Whiteboard

## Vocabulary

- *Dinacharya* — daily routine (literally "day's conduct")
- *Brāhma muhūrta* — "Brahma's hour," ~96 min before sunrise (recommended wake time, traditionally)
- *Abhyanga* — oil self-massage
- *Vyāyāma* — exercise

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: dosha balance principle from yesterday.

### Core (25 min)

1. **The dinacharya timeline** (build on the board, in order):

   | Time | Practice | Why |
   |------|----------|-----|
   | Brāhma muhūrta (before sunrise) | Wake | Body's natural alertness peak |
   | Just after waking | Drink warm water | Stimulates digestion, aids elimination |
   | Morning | Oral hygiene (tongue scraping, oil pulling) | Removes overnight buildup |
   | Pre-bath | Abhyanga (warm oil self-massage) | Calms Vāta; nourishes skin |
   | Morning | Exercise (vyāyāma) — up to 50% capacity | Builds Pitta; doesn't deplete |
   | Mid-morning | Largest meal of the day | Digestive fire (Pitta) is strongest |
   | Evening | Light dinner, before sunset (ideally) | Aligns with digestive rhythm |
   | Night | Sleep by 10pm | Aligns with Kapha→Pitta night cycle |

2. **The principle:** the day is divided into Kapha (6am–10am), Pitta (10am–2pm), Vāta (2pm–6pm), then repeating. Each time block has dominant dosha character. Activities aligned with the block work with the body, not against it.

3. **Modern science overlap:**
   - **Circadian biology** (Aschoff 1965, Nobel Prize 2017 to Hall, Rosbash, Young) — confirms ~24-hour rhythms in metabolism, hormones, sleep.
   - **Time-restricted eating** (Satchidananda Panda's work) — confirms larger meal earlier is metabolically better.
   - **Sleep science** — confirms sleeping by ~10pm aligns with melatonin curve.
   - **Oil massage** — emerging evidence on skin barrier, lymphatic drainage.

4. **The honest framing:** Many dinacharya practices align with modern lifestyle medicine. The framework was figured out from observation centuries before modern measurement.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read about Panda's time-restricted eating research. Note overlap with dinacharya meal timing.
- **Tier down:** Focus on 3 practices: wake with sun, eat largest meal mid-morning, sleep by 10pm.

## Teacher notes

- The "brāhma muhūrta wake time" is *traditionally* recommended but unrealistic for many students. Don't push.
- The science overlap is real; don't oversell ("Ayurveda predicted everything"). The honest framing: "tradition observed; science measured; they agree on much."
