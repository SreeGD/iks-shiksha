# Day 7 — Primary Source: Bhagavad-gītā 6.17

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students read BG 6.17 in IAST + translation, identify the five activities Krishna names as needing regulation, and connect each to a dinacharya practice.

## Materials

- Printed handout: BG 6.17 (Sanskrit, IAST, word-by-word, translation, brief commentary)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: dinacharya practice they tried.

### Reading (20 min)

> *yuktāhāra-vihārasya yukta-ceṣṭasya karmasu*
> *yukta-svapnāvabodhasya yogo bhavati duḥkha-hā*
> — Bhagavad-gītā 6.17

**Word-by-word:**

| Sanskrit | Gloss |
|----------|-------|
| yukta | regulated, balanced, joined |
| āhāra | food |
| vihārasya | of recreation, lifestyle (gen.) |
| yukta-ceṣṭasya | of regulated effort/activity (gen.) |
| karmasu | in work, actions (loc.) |
| yukta-svapna | regulated sleep |
| avabodhasya | of waking (gen.) |
| yogo | yoga, discipline |
| bhavati | becomes |
| duḥkha-hā | sorrow-destroying |

**Continuous translation:**
> "He who is regulated in eating and recreation, in his effort in work, in his sleep and waking — for him, yoga becomes the destroyer of sorrow."

### The five activities (10 min)

Krishna names FIVE activities that need regulation:
1. *Āhāra* — eating
2. *Vihāra* — recreation, lifestyle
3. *Ceṣṭā in karma* — effort in work
4. *Svapna* — sleep
5. *Avabodha* — waking

Now connect to dinacharya:
- *Āhāra* → eating timing + six tastes (Day 6)
- *Vihāra* → exercise (vyāyāma) + leisure
- *Ceṣṭā in karma* → balanced effort, neither lazy nor over-exerted
- *Svapna* → sleep by 10pm
- *Avabodha* → waking before sunrise (brāhma muhūrta)

**Note:** the verse is from the *Bhagavad-gītā*, a philosophical-spiritual text. It frames regulated living as the foundation for *yoga* (the discipline of mind). Ayurveda picks up this same framework on the body-health side.

### Discussion (8 min)

- Question: *"Which of the five activities do you find HARDEST to regulate in your own life?"*
- Take 4 student answers.

### Wrap-up (2 min)

- Preview Day 8: *"Tomorrow — start designing your own 7-day dinacharya."*

## Homework

- Pick ONE of the five activities. Notice it tomorrow. Are you "yukta" (regulated) or out of balance?

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Bryant (2009) commentary on BG 6.17.
- **Tier down:** Memorise the 5 activities only.

## Teacher notes

- BG is a religious text for many traditions. Honour that. Here we use it as a primary source for the lifestyle-regulation principle.
- The "yoga destroys sorrow" line is profound; let it land without over-interpretation.
