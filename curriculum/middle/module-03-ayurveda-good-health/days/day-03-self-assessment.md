# Day 3 — Self-Assessment (with Caveats)

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students apply the dosha framework to themselves via a self-assessment worksheet, while understanding firmly that it is for fun and learning, not medical diagnosis.

## Materials

- Self-assessment worksheet (12 questions)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: 3 doshas with one quality each.

### The disclaimer (5 min) — TEACHER FRAMING IS CRITICAL

Write on board:

> **TODAY'S QUIZ IS NOT A DIAGNOSIS.**
>
> It's a way to practise the framework.
>
> If you have any health concerns, see a doctor.
>
> Everyone has all three doshas.

Make the class chorus this last line out loud.

### Self-assessment (15 min)

Hand out worksheet (sample below). Students answer honestly. Tally V / P / K.

#### Worksheet (12 questions, 3 each per dosha)

For each, circle the option closest to you:

| # | Question | V | P | K |
|---|----------|---|---|---|
| 1 | Build | thin, light | medium | sturdy, heavier |
| 2 | Skin | dry, cool | warm, sensitive | smooth, oily |
| 3 | Energy | bursts and lulls | strong and focused | steady, slow |
| 4 | Hunger | irregular | strong, can't skip | mild, can skip |
| 5 | Sleep | light, easily disturbed | sound, normal | deep, long |
| 6 | Speech | fast, talkative | sharp, persuasive | slow, calm |
| 7 | Memory | quick to learn, quick to forget | sharp, decisive | slow, lasting |
| 8 | Stress | anxious, worried | irritable, frustrated | withdrawn, slow |
| 9 | Weather preferred | warm | cool | warm and dry |
| 10 | Walking | fast | medium | slow |
| 11 | Hands & feet | cold | warm | cool, moist |
| 12 | Spending | impulsive | calculated | careful, saver |

Total: V = ___, P = ___, K = ___

The largest score = your *current* dosha tilt. Note: this is a snapshot, not your identity.

### Discussion (15 min)

- Share-of-hands: how many are predominantly Vāta? Pitta? Kapha? Mixed?
- Reinforce: *"This is a tendency observation, not a verdict."*
- Discuss: did anyone get a result that surprised them?

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 4: *"Tomorrow — Dinacharya. The daily routine that tradition recommends for keeping all three doshas in balance."*

## Homework

- Look up ONE Ayurvedic recommendation for your dominant dosha (food, sleep timing, type of exercise). Bring tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Notice your result; do you AGREE? If not, why might the framework miss?
- **Tier down:** Focus on Q1–6; skip Q7–12.

## Teacher notes

- Some students will be deeply invested in "their type." Gently redirect: *"It's a tool, not a label."*
- The framework correlates loosely with body-type psychology (Sheldon's somatotypes, for example). Senior students engage this comparison.

## Citation(s) used

- Self-assessment adapted from standard Ayurveda introductory texts (Wujastyk 2003; various modern Ayurveda primers).
