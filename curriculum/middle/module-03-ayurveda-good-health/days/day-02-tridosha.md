# Day 2 — The Tridosha

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students name the three doshas, their elemental basis, characteristic qualities, and one observable sign of each.

## Materials

- Wall chart of the 3 doshas
- Whiteboard

## Vocabulary

- *dosha* — humour, biological energy
- *Vāta* — wind/space dosha
- *Pitta* — fire/water dosha
- *Kapha* — water/earth dosha

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: define Āyurveda and Swasthya.

### Core (25 min)

1. **Build the dosha table on the board:**

   | Dosha | Elements | Qualities | Function | When out of balance |
   |-------|----------|-----------|----------|----------------------|
   | **Vāta** | air + space | mobile, dry, light, cold, rough | Movement, breath, nervous system | Anxiety, insomnia, dry skin, constipation |
   | **Pitta** | fire + water | hot, sharp, light, oily, slightly liquid | Digestion, metabolism, vision | Heartburn, irritability, skin inflammation |
   | **Kapha** | water + earth | heavy, slow, cool, oily, smooth | Structure, immunity, lubrication | Lethargy, congestion, weight gain |

2. **Note the connection to Module 2.** The doshas are built from the *pañcabhūta* (five elements). Each dosha is a particular combination of elements + qualities.

3. **Important framing:** **Everyone has all three doshas.** Your *prakṛti* (constitution) is the proportion you're born with. Your *vikṛti* (current state) is your present balance.

4. **Where modern medicine agrees, where it doesn't:**
   - Modern lifestyle medicine: agrees on regular sleep, balanced meals, moderate exercise.
   - Modern circadian biology: agrees on aligning daily activities with body rhythms.
   - Modern dosha-as-biomedical-category: **mixed evidence**. Some studies show correlates with body type and metabolism; others don't.

### Activity (10 min) — Sign spotting

Students think of one person they know (not themselves). Write 3 observable traits. Try to match to a dosha tilt.

(Stress: NOT diagnostic. Just practice with the framework.)

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 3: *"Tomorrow — your own self-assessment. With caveats."*

## Homework

- Notice tonight: what did you eat? When? How did you feel after? Take notes.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read the Caraka Saṃhitā chapter on dosha (in Wujastyk 2003).
- **Tier down:** Memorise just: Vāta (air+space, movement), Pitta (fire+water, digestion), Kapha (water+earth, structure).

## Teacher notes

- Don't push students to "find their type." The framework is interesting; over-typing is not.
- The "everyone has all three" point is critical. Resist the pop-Ayurveda "you ARE Vāta" framing.
