# Day 8 — Project Session 1: Design Your Dinacharya

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Each student drafts a 7-day dinacharya for themselves, with each practice paired to its Ayurvedic principle.

## Materials

- Project brief + rubric
- Worksheet template

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Brief: today is design. Show rubric.

### Core (35 min) — Design sprints

**Sprint 1 (10 min) — Wake-to-school routine.**
- Wake time?
- First glass of water?
- Oral hygiene?
- Exercise?
- Breakfast?

Each item paired with its Ayurvedic principle.

**Sprint 2 (10 min) — Midday routine.**
- Largest meal timing?
- Activities during Pitta hours (10am–2pm)?

**Sprint 3 (10 min) — Evening + sleep.**
- Dinner timing?
- Pre-bed wind-down?
- Sleep target?

**Sprint 4 (5 min) — Realism check.**
- Are these times achievable for your actual school week?
- If not, what's the MINIMUM viable change?

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Plan submitted at door.

## Homework

- Try ONE day of your dinacharya tonight + tomorrow morning. Note what worked and what didn't.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Also design a "weekend" version + a "school holiday" version.
- **Tier down:** Pick 3 specific changes (not a full overhaul).

## Teacher notes

- Realism is the #1 thing. A dinacharya that requires getting up at 4am won't be followed.
- Some students may push back on family meal times. Don't override the family; suggest small adjustments only.
