# Day 1 — What is Ayurveda? + Swasthya

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students define *Āyurveda* and *Swasthya* and articulate the scope of the framework (lifestyle, prevention, herbal medicine — not surgery or acute care).

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- A few sample herbs (turmeric, tulsi if available — check allergies)

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Provocation: *"If someone said to you 'what is Ayurveda?', what would you say in one sentence?"*

Take 3 student answers.

### Core (25 min)

1. **Etymology:** *āyu* (life, vitality) + *veda* (knowledge, science) = **"the science of life."** Not just illness — the entire life.

2. **Swasthya** — *swa* (self) + *stha* (established) = **"self-established / situated in oneself."** A working definition of wellbeing: physical + mental + social balance.

3. **What Ayurveda covers:**
   - Daily routine (*dinacharya*)
   - Seasonal routine (*ritucharya*)
   - Food (*ahāra*) — what, when, how to eat
   - Recreation (*vihāra*) — exercise, sleep, social life
   - Herbal medicines (*aushadhi*)
   - Surgery (*śalya-tantra*) — historically, e.g. Sushruta
   - 8 branches total (the *aṣṭāṅga* of Ayurveda)

4. **What Ayurveda does NOT cover well:**
   - Acute infections requiring antibiotics
   - Emergency surgery
   - Genetic conditions requiring modern diagnostics
   - Mental health requiring specialised psychiatric care

5. **The honest framing:** Ayurveda + modern medicine are *partners*, not rivals. Each has a domain. Don't substitute one for the other.

### Activity (10 min) — Map a day

Students fold paper in 4. Label each quadrant: *Sleep, Food, Exercise, Mood*. In each, write what they did yesterday.

Note: *"This is your raw data. Over the next 2 weeks we'll learn the Ayurvedic framework to make sense of it."*

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 2: *"Tomorrow — the Tridosha. Three biological energies."*

## Homework

- Notice tonight: what time do you go to sleep? What did you eat for dinner? How did you feel after?

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read Wujastyk's intro chapter.
- **Tier down:** Just memorise *Āyurveda* (science of life) and *Swasthya* (wellbeing).

## Teacher notes

- The "8 branches" detail is for students who want depth. At Middle band, the 6 mentioned items are enough.
- Watch for students with health conditions — don't single them out or imply Ayurveda is for them.
