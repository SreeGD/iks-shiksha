# Day 6 — Ahāra (Food)

**Module:** Ayurveda (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students name the six tastes (*rasa*) in Ayurveda, the recommended meal-timing principle, and one food pairing tradition recommends avoiding.

## Materials

- 6 taste samples if available (salt for salty, lemon for sour, jaggery for sweet, mint for bitter, ginger for pungent, raw banana or persimmon for astringent)
- Whiteboard

## Vocabulary

- *Ahāra* — food
- *Rasa* — taste (also: essence)
- *Madhura, amla, lavaṇa, kaṭu, tikta, kaṣāya* — sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, astringent

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: dinacharya principle.

### Core (25 min)

1. **The Six Tastes** (rasa):

   | Sanskrit | English | Examples | Dosha effect |
   |----------|---------|----------|---------------|
   | Madhura | Sweet | Rice, milk, sugar, fruit | ↓ Vāta, ↓ Pitta, ↑ Kapha |
   | Amla | Sour | Citrus, yogurt, vinegar | ↓ Vāta, ↑ Pitta, ↑ Kapha |
   | Lavaṇa | Salty | Salt, sea vegetables | ↓ Vāta, ↑ Pitta, ↑ Kapha |
   | Kaṭu | Pungent | Chili, ginger, garlic | ↑ Vāta, ↑ Pitta, ↓ Kapha |
   | Tikta | Bitter | Bitter gourd, turmeric, neem | ↑ Vāta, ↓ Pitta, ↓ Kapha |
   | Kaṣāya | Astringent | Banana (unripe), pomegranate, beans | ↑ Vāta, ↓ Pitta, ↓ Kapha |

2. **The principle:** a balanced meal includes ALL six tastes. Traditional Indian thalis are designed this way — you'll notice rice (sweet), pickle (sour/salty), chili (pungent), bitter gourd or fenugreek (bitter), and a pulse-based dish (astringent).

3. **Meal timing:**
   - Largest meal at midday (Pitta peak — digestion strongest).
   - Light dinner before sunset (ideally).
   - No heavy meal close to bedtime.

4. **One food rule:** *Virrudha-ahāra* — incompatible food combinations. Classical examples:
   - Milk + fish (heating + cooling combination).
   - Milk + sour fruit (digestive disturbance).
   - Honey heated above body temperature (changes its qualities).

   Note: some virrudha-ahāra rules have modern evidence (e.g. some lactose-fruit-acid interactions); others are traditional with limited validation.

### Activity (10 min) — Taste tour

If samples available: tiny sample of each taste, one at a time. Note the sensation. Note what dosha it would affect.

If no samples: students list 3 foods they ate in the last 24 hours and identify which tastes were present + missing.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Preview Day 7: *"Tomorrow — primary source. BG 6.17."*

## Homework

- For tomorrow's meals, count how many of the six tastes you include. Aim for at least 4.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read about the six-taste theory in Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna chapter 26.
- **Tier down:** Memorise just 4 tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter).

## Teacher notes

- Skip the taste samples if allergies are a concern.
- The "milk + fish" rule is famous; honest framing: some virrudha-ahāra has biochemical basis, some is traditional. Don't overstate either way.

## Citation(s) used

- Caraka Saṃhitā Sūtra-sthāna ch. 26 — Sharma (1981) translation.
- Wujastyk (2003) on the six tastes.
