# Sources — Module 3 (Middle)

## Primary sources (from RAG)

- **Bhagavad-gītā 6.17** — *yuktāhāra-vihārasya yukta-ceṣṭasya karmasu yukta-svapnāvabodhasya yogo bhavati duḥkha-hā* — "He who is regulated in eating, recreation, work, sleep, and waking can mitigate sorrow."

This verse is the philosophical anchor for the dinacharya framework. Cached in `.rag-cache/day-04-dinacharya.json`.

- Supporting Bhāgavatam and Prabhupāda commentary chunks in `.rag-cache/day-01-intro.json`.

## Scholarly secondary

- **Wujastyk, D. (2003).** *The Roots of Ayurveda*. Penguin. — Accessible primary-source translations of Caraka, Suśruta, Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya excerpts.
- **Smith, F. M. (2006).** *The Self Possessed: Deity and Spirit Possession in South Asian Literature and Civilization*. Columbia. — Broader cultural-historical context.

## Classical primary (for teacher reference)

- **Caraka Saṃhitā** (~2nd c. BCE) — Sharma (1981) translation.
- **Suśruta Saṃhitā** — Sushruta's surgical procedures.
- **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya** by Vāgbhaṭa (~7th c. CE) — standard classical Ayurvedic textbook.

## Modern medical perspective

- Patwardhan, B., Mutalik, G., & Tillu, G. (2015). *Integrative Approaches for Health: Biomedical Research, Ayurveda and Yoga*. Academic Press. — Engages the modern-evidence question seriously.

## Citation discipline

A strong Middle-band project cites at least one source. Acceptable: the BG 6.17 verse from the RAG cache, OR a citation from Wujastyk, OR an NCERT Health & Physical Education chapter.
