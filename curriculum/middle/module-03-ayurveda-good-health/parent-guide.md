# Parent Guide — Module 3: Ayurveda and Good Health

**Your child's age:** Middle (Grades 6–8) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## What your child is learning

The Ayurvedic framework for daily health:

- **Āyurveda** — "the science of life." A 2000-year-old Indian framework for wellbeing.
- **Swasthya** — wellbeing, defined as physical + mental + social balance.
- **Tridosha** — the three biological energies: **Vāta** (movement; air + space), **Pitta** (transformation; fire + water), **Kapha** (structure; water + earth).
- **Dinacharya** — daily routine: when to wake, eat, exercise, sleep.
- **Ahāra** — food: the six tastes (sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, astringent).

Plus a primary-source verse — **Bhagavad-gītā 6.17** on regulated lifestyle.

## How we teach it

The framing is honest:
- Ayurveda is a real, ancient, sophisticated framework — especially good for prevention, daily wellbeing, lifestyle.
- It is NOT a substitute for modern medicine when acute care is needed.
- The Day 3 self-assessment is FOR FUN, not diagnosis.

## Important safety note

**Nothing in this module is medical advice.** If your child has any condition (asthma, allergies, diabetes, mental health, etc.), their existing care continues unchanged. The school will state this clearly. If you have any concern about your child making lifestyle changes, contact us.

## What your child will do

- Learn the Tridosha (Vāta, Pitta, Kapha) with examples.
- Try a self-assessment (for fun).
- Design a 7-day dinacharya — their own personal daily routine, with rationale.
- Read BG 6.17 in IAST with translation.
- Present their dinacharya on Day 10.

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Discuss your family's existing wellness habits (regular meal times, sleep schedule, traditional food choices). Many are aligned with dinacharya principles.
- For their project: help with realistic scheduling. A dinacharya that fits the actual school day works; one that requires waking at 4am doesn't.
- If your family uses any traditional remedies (turmeric milk, etc.), share the family knowledge.

**Not helpful:**
- Pushing dramatic lifestyle changes based on the self-assessment.
- Dismissing Ayurveda entirely; some practices ARE supported by modern lifestyle medicine.
- Pushing Ayurveda as a substitute for any medication or treatment your child is on.

## What success looks like

- Your child can name the three doshas with their elemental basis.
- They can describe dinacharya as a coherent framework, not a random list.
- They have a personal 7-day routine that's realistic for their actual life.
- They can articulate where Ayurveda helps and where modern medicine is essential.
