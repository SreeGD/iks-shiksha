# Module 5 — Bindu Paddhati (Dot Method of Addition)

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Recall** the Bindu Paddhati ("dot method") procedure for column addition: scanning each column for groups that sum to 10, marking dots, and counting dots as the carry.
2. **Execute** column addition on 3- to 4-digit numbers with at least three addends using the dot method, in under half the time their standard-algorithm baseline would take.
3. **Explain** *why* the dot method works — that a dot represents a multiple of 10 carried to the next column.
4. **Apply** the method to mental addition of 2-digit numbers using the "completing the ten" pattern (e.g. 19 + 8 = 19 + 1 + 7 = 27).
5. **Compare** the dot method with the standard right-to-left carry algorithm and articulate when each is faster.

## Prerequisites

- Confident with addition of two single-digit numbers up to 18.
- Comfortable with carrying in standard column addition.
- Can write numbers in columns aligned by place value.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md`.

- *bindu* — dot
- *paddhati* — method
- *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* — "by addition and by subtraction" (7th of Tirthaji's 16 sūtras); related but not the dot method per se
- *Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ* — "by completing or non-completing" (8th sūtra); names the "complete the ten" idea

## Materials checklist

- A4 sheets with pre-printed columns of numbers (one per student, varied difficulty)
- Stopwatches or phone timers for paired drills (1 per pair is enough)
- A class wall-poster of "pairs that make 10" (1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5)
- Whiteboards or scrap paper for live demos
