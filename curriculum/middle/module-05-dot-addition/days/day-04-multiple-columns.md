# Day 4 — Multiple Columns

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will add a 4-addend × 4-digit column-sum using the dot method, correctly propagating carries between columns.

## Materials

- Day 3 formative quiz returned with marks.
- Worksheet: 8 multi-column problems (varying from 3×3-digit to 5×4-digit).
- Whiteboard / large grid paper.
- Stopwatch.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Make-10 lightning round.
- Hand back yesterday's formative. Don't review — students see their marks privately.

### Core (10 min) — The carry handoff

- Demonstrate on the board:

  ```
  5728
  3417
  6892
  4536+
  ```

  **Units column:** 8 + 7 + 2 + 6.
  - 0 → 8 → 15 (dot on 7, total 5) → 7 → 13 (dot on 6, total 3) → 3.
  - **Write 3. Carry 2.**

  ```
        · ·
  5728
  3417
  6892
  4536+
     3 [carry 2]
  ```

  **Tens column:** 2 + 1 + 9 + 3 + carry-2.
  - Include the carry IN the column (write it as a small digit at the top of the column, or hold it in your head).
  - 0 → 2 → 3 → 12 (dot on 9, total 2) → 5 → 7 (the carry).
  - **Write 7. Carry 1.**

  Continue.

  Final result: **20573**

- Verify with calculator or standard algorithm.

### Activity (25 min) — Drill

Follow `activities/activity-01-column-drill.md`. Brief:

- 8 multi-column problems, individual work first (8 min).
- Then paired check (10 min): partners trade sheets; one reads problem aloud while the other re-solves on a fresh sheet; compare results.
- Final 7 min: any 3 problems the student got wrong, redo silently.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Show of hands: who finished all 8 in 8 minutes? Who needs more time? (Don't shame either group — Day 5 is the retest.)
- Preview Day 5: timed re-test of Day 1's baseline problem.

## Student-facing content

> **The dot method with carries**
>
> When you have multiple columns, the dots in column N become the *carry* added to column N+1.
>
> Add the carry IN the column as you scan — either by including it as a tiny digit at the top of the column, or by adding it to your starting running total.
>
> **Practice this until** you can do a 4×4-digit sum in under 60 seconds.

## Homework

- Find a column-addition problem in any textbook. Solve it BOTH ways (standard + dot method). Time yourself for each. Bring tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** 5×5-digit sums; aim for under 90 seconds.
- **Tier down:** 3×3-digit sums until comfortable; scale up.

## Teacher notes

- The carry handoff is where most errors happen. Two common patterns:
  1. Forgetting to include the carry in the next column.
  2. Including it twice (once as the carry, once as part of the running total).
- For each, slow down with the student and have them write the carry IN the column as a small digit before they start scanning.
