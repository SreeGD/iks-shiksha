# Day 5 — Mid-Module Check (Speed Retest)

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will retest their Day 1 baseline using the dot method and submit a project plan paragraph.

## Materials

- Each student's Day 1 baseline sheet (returned to them).
- Fresh copy of the SAME problem (5728 + 3417 + 6892 + 4536).
- Formative quiz (`quizzes/formative.md` Part B) — 5 mixed problems.
- Project brief handouts (`assessments/project-brief.md`).

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- Make-10 lightning round (30 sec; should be effortless by now).
- Brief: "Today is your retest. The same problem you did on Day 1, with the new method. We'll see how much you've sped up."

### Speed retest (5 min)

- Hand out the fresh sheet.
- On "Go," solve using dot method. Record time.
- Compare to Day 1 baseline. Average gain: target 50% reduction in time.

### Formative quiz (15 min) — `quizzes/formative.md` Part B

- 5 mixed problems:
  - 2 single-column adds
  - 2 multi-column 3×3 adds
  - 1 multi-column 4×4 add (the retest doesn't count)
- Closed notebook.
- 12 min to solve + 3 min for class review.

### Project commitment (15 min)

- Hand out project brief.
- Read aloud the three project formats:
  1. **60-second teaching video** — teach a younger student (Grade 4) the dot method.
  2. **Printed workbook** — 20 problems with worked solutions using the dot method, designed for self-study.
  3. **Comparative speed-test data project** — collect Day-1-style baseline + Day-5-style retest data from 5–10 family members or schoolmates. Plot the results.

- Pairs (or individuals) choose a format AND a partner by end of class.
- Each pair writes a 1-sentence "what we'll do" on a sticky note, posts to the board under their format.

### Wrap-up (7 min)

- Show of hands by gain category: <0% (slower), 0–25% faster, 25–50% faster, 50%+ faster.
- Note: *"Whatever your gain, you've learned a real technique that will keep speeding up with use."*
- Preview Day 6: *"Tomorrow we ask: *why* does this work? You'll write a paragraph explaining it in your own words."*

## Homework

- Submit project plan paragraph (one paragraph, 4–6 sentences) by tomorrow morning.
- Begin gathering materials.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** If you got 60%+ speedup, your project should challenge you. Pick the speed-test data project — it requires statistical thinking.
- **Tier down:** Pick the teaching video — the act of *teaching* the method reinforces it for you.

## Teacher notes

- Be honest with students whose times slowed: the method has a learning curve, and steady drill (Day 7's mental patterns will help) will catch them up.
- Mark the formative quiz tonight.
- Speed-test data projects need ethics: pairs should ask permission before timing family members.
