# Day 8 — Project Session 1: Plan and Begin

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, every pair will have a written project plan and at least one tangible artefact (script outline, first 5 workbook problems drafted, or first 3 data points collected) for their final project.

## Materials

- All project materials brought by students.
- A printed rubric (`assessments/rubric.md`) per pair.
- Stopwatch.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Make-10 round.
- Hand out the rubric. 60-second walk-through.

### Core (35 min) — Workshop sprints

Same three-sprint structure as Panchabhuta Day 8:

**Sprint 1 (10 min) — Plan completion.** Every pair finalises:
- Format (video / workbook / data project)
- One-sentence audience takeaway
- Three-part structure of the artefact
- Division of labour
- The PDF section they'll cite (e.g. "Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf, §5.4")

**Sprint 2 (10 min) — First artefact.**
- **Video projects:** draft script in 10 min. Rough storyboard. No filming yet.
- **Workbook projects:** draft 5 problems with worked dot-method solutions.
- **Data projects:** collect baseline times from 3 family members or schoolmates (in school: use classmates). Record in a table.

**Sprint 3 (10 min) — Build / iterate.** Continue.

Teacher's role:
- 60-sec rotation per pair in Sprint 2.
- Force a "stop planning, start producing" intervention for any pair still drafting their plan in Sprint 2.
- For video projects: confirm by Sprint 3 that they have a clear shot list, not just a script.
- For data projects: confirm by Sprint 3 that they have at least 3 real data points.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- 30-sec share-out from 4 pairs (you call them).
- Plan paragraph submitted at the door.
- Preview Day 9: refine and rehearse.

## Student-facing content

> **Project workshop — Session 1**
>
> By end of class, your pair should have:
>
> 1. A written project plan paragraph.
> 2. The start of your artefact: a script draft, 5 worked problems, OR 3 data points.
>
> Plan template:
>
> ```
> Format: __________ (video / workbook / data project)
> Audience walks away thinking: __________
> Structure (3 parts):
>   1. __________
>   2. __________
>   3. __________
> Division of labour (Partner A): __________
> Division of labour (Partner B): __________
> Source we'll cite: __________ (PDF + section)
> ```

## Homework

- Whatever didn't get done in class — finish at home tonight.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Stretch goal — add a comparison element. (E.g. video: include a side-by-side speed comparison; workbook: include 5 standard-algorithm problems for contrast; data: also test mental "near-base" additions.)
- **Tier down:** Allow simpler format mid-stream. A clean workbook beats a half-built video.

## Teacher notes

- For video projects: students often shoot poor-quality video at home. Encourage them to rehearse and shoot in one or two takes — the *teaching content* matters more than production value.
- For data projects: be wary of small-sample misinterpretation. If a pair has only 3 data points and one shows the dot method is slower, that's a real finding — let them report it. Statistical thinking is part of the project.

## Citation(s) used in this lesson

- Pair-chosen source from `sources.md`, verified during Sprint 1.
