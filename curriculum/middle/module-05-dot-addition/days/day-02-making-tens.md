# Day 2 — Making Tens

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will instantly recall the 10-pair of any single digit (0+10, 1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5) AND identify all 10-pairs in a list of 6–8 single-digit numbers in under 10 seconds.

## Materials

- A class wall-poster of the 5 pairs (1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5). Display before class.
- Pre-printed pair-drill sheets (lists of 8 digits each — students circle 10-pairs).
- Stopwatch per pair.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- **Make-10 lightning round.** Teacher calls 10 single digits in 30 seconds; students chorus the partner. (8 → 2. 3 → 7. 6 → 4. ...)
- Repeat with a different sequence.

### Core (15 min) — The 10-pair pattern

1. **Reveal the wall poster.** Walk through the 5 pairs.
2. **Note (write on board):** "Every single digit from 1–9 has exactly ONE partner that makes 10. Knowing all 5 pairs gives you the whole table."
3. **Mental check:** call out digits randomly for 60 seconds; class chorus the partner.
4. **The "extra" patterns:** if the digit pair sums to 11 instead of 10, write down 1 and "the extra" goes into running total. If sum is 9 (no 10-pair available), just count.

   ```
   Example column: 7, 5, 3, 8
   - Scan: 7 + 3 = 10. Mark dot above 3. Running = 0.
   - 5. Running = 5.
   - 8. Running = 13 → 10-pair found (3 already used). Mark dot above 8. Running = 3.
   - Write 3 under column. Carry 2 dots.
   ```

### Activity (20 min) — Pair drills

- Hand out drill sheets. Each sheet has 10 rows, each row a list of 6–8 digits.
- Working in pairs:
  - Partner A reads a row aloud.
  - Partner B identifies all 10-pairs in the row in under 10 sec.
  - Swap roles for the next row.

Suggested rows:
```
Row 1: 3 7 2 5 8 1 4 6
Row 2: 9 4 1 2 8 5 3 7
Row 3: 5 5 6 4 1 9 2 8
Row 4: 7 3 6 1 9 8 2 4
...
```

After 5 minutes of paired drills, do a class-level **timing test**: project a single row; students individually find all 10-pairs and write count + leftover. 20 seconds per row. Do 5 rows.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Quick check: *"Without thinking, what makes 10 with: 6?"* (chorus 4). *"With 8?"* (chorus 2).
- Preview Day 3: "Now we'll add a *single column* of numbers using this."

## Student-facing content

> **The Five 10-Pairs**
>
> | 1 + 9 | 2 + 8 | 3 + 7 | 4 + 6 | 5 + 5 |
> |---|---|---|---|---|
>
> Practice until you can name the partner of any single digit *instantly* — no thinking. This is the foundational skill of the dot method.
>
> **Drill:** in a row of 6–8 digits, scan and circle every 10-pair you can find. Write what's "left over."
>
> Example: in `3 7 2 5 8 1 4 6`, you can find: 3+7, 2+8, 4+6 — three pairs, leftover is `5 1`.

## Homework

- Practice the 10-pair recall. Have a parent or sibling call out single digits; you answer the partner. Beat 20 digits in 30 seconds.
- Continue: in any list of 6 digits, find all 10-pairs in under 10 sec.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find pairs that sum to 9 OR 10 OR 11. (For future Day 7 — numbers near a base.)
- **Tier down:** Stick to recall-only practice today. The column work can wait one day.

## Teacher notes

- This is the most important fluency lesson of the module. Do NOT skip the lightning round.
- If a student can't recall pairs by end of class, assign extra drill at home and provide a "pairs cheat sheet" for the rest of the module — withdraw it on Day 7.
