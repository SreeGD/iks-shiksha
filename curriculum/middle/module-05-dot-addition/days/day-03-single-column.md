# Day 3 — Single Column with Dots

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will add a single column of 4–6 single-digit numbers using the dot method, correctly identifying both the units digit and the number of dots (the carry).

## Materials

- Worksheet: 10 single columns of 4–6 digits each (no carrying yet — just write the result + count dots).
- Whiteboard.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Make-10 lightning round (30 sec).
- Recall: *"In any list of digits, how do I find the units digit if I'm using the dot method?"* (Take a student answer. Reinforce: scan, mark dots, the unwritten leftover is the units.)

### Core (15 min) — The procedure formalised

Write on the board, large:

```
THE DOT METHOD FOR ONE COLUMN

1. Start with running total = 0.
2. Scan top to bottom.
3. When adding a digit makes total ≥ 10, mark a DOT
   above that digit. Subtract 10 from the running total.
4. Continue.
5. At the end:
   - Final running total is the UNITS DIGIT (write below).
   - Number of dots = CARRY (to next column).
```

Worked example on the board:
```
6
3
7  ← total 6 → 9 → 16 ≥ 10, dot ·, total = 6
5
8  ← total 6 → 11 ≥ 10, dot ·, total = 1
4
2
__
1   ← running total at end
··· wait, let me recount
```

(Be honest with yourself — recount aloud. Models the self-checking habit.)

Correct walk-through:
- Start: 0
- + 6 → 6
- + 3 → 9
- + 7 → 16. Total ≥ 10: mark **·** above 7. Total = 6.
- + 5 → 11. Total ≥ 10: mark **·** above 5. Total = 1.
- + 8 → 9.
- + 4 → 13. Total ≥ 10: mark **·** above 4. Total = 3.
- + 2 → 5.
- **Units: 5. Dots: 3. Carry 3 to next column.**

Verify by standard addition: 6+3+7+5+8+4+2 = 35. Units = 5. Carry = 3. ✓

### Activity (20 min) — Single-column drill

- Hand out worksheet of 10 columns.
- 5 minutes individual work.
- 10 minutes paired correction: partner-A solves column 1 aloud while partner-B watches and corrects. Swap. Continue alternating.
- Final 5 minutes: silent retry on any column students got wrong.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Hand out the **formative quiz** (`quizzes/formative.md` Part A) — 4 quick column problems, 3 min.
- Collect; mark overnight.
- Preview Day 4: *"Now we put multiple columns together — with the carries flowing from one to the next."*

## Student-facing content

> **Dot method, one column:**
>
> 1. Running total = 0.
> 2. Scan top to bottom.
> 3. When total ≥ 10: mark a dot, subtract 10.
> 4. Final running total = units digit. Dot count = carry.
>
> **Example:** column `6, 3, 7, 5, 8, 4, 2`
>
> ```
>   6
>   3
>   7  ·
>   5  ·
>   8
>   4  ·
>   2
>   __
>   5      ← units
>   3 dots = carry 3 to the next column
> ```

## Homework

- Solve the back of the worksheet (10 more single-column problems). Self-check answers against standard addition.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Solve columns of 8–10 numbers each (challenge sheet).
- **Tier down:** Stick to columns of 4 numbers each. Build up.

## Teacher notes

- Common error: students forget that the dot count IS the carry — they recount the dots wrongly. Reinforce: 1 dot = +1 carry, 2 dots = +2 carry, etc.
- A second common error: students mark a dot but forget to subtract 10 from the running total — so they keep counting up. Watch for total > 20 mid-column; if so, they missed a subtraction.
