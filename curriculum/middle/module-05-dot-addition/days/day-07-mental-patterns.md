# Day 7 — Mental Math Patterns: Numbers Near a Base

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will add 2-digit numbers ending in 9, 19, 29, ... (or 8, 18, 28, ...) mentally, by adding the round base and subtracting the deficiency.

## Materials

- Whiteboard.
- Drill sheet for Activity 02 (`activities/activity-02-near-base.md`).
- Stopwatch per pair.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Make-10 round.
- Quick recall: *"In yesterday's writing, what was the most surprising place where you 'complete to 10' without thinking?"* — 2 student shares.

### Core (15 min) — The "near-base" pattern

1. Write on board:
   ```
   33 + 9 = ?
   ```
   Most students will scratch out 33+9 = 42 in their head, taking 2–3 seconds.

2. Show the trick:
   ```
   9 = 10 − 1
   So 33 + 9 = 33 + 10 − 1 = 43 − 1 = 42
   ```
   Now do it in your head: **add 10, subtract 1**. ~0.5 seconds.

3. **The general pattern (write on board):**

   > To add a number that ends in 9, 8, 7 (close to a multiple of 10):
   > 1. ROUND UP to the multiple of 10.
   > 2. ADD that.
   > 3. SUBTRACT the deficiency.

4. Worked examples (class chorus):
   - 47 + 9 = 47 + 10 − 1 = 56
   - 28 + 19 = 28 + 20 − 1 = 47
   - 64 + 29 = 64 + 30 − 1 = 93
   - 55 + 48 = 55 + 50 − 2 = 103
   - 36 + 38 = 36 + 40 − 2 = 74

5. **Why?** Same "completion to a base" idea — and now the base can be 10, 20, 30, 100. This is exactly Tirthaji's sūtra #7 (*Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* — "by addition and by subtraction"). Write the name on the board.

### Activity (20 min) — Near-base drills

Follow `activities/activity-02-near-base.md`. Brief:

- 5 min individual: 20 problems on a sheet, do as many as you can.
- 5 min paired: partner-A reads a problem aloud, partner-B answers mentally in <3 seconds, swap.
- 5 min mixed: 10 problems on the board; class shouts answer in chorus.
- 5 min quick-fire: teacher calls a 2-digit number ending in 9 or 8, then any other 2-digit number; students whisper-answer to their neighbour.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Show of hands: who can now add `+ 9` to any 2-digit number in under 1 second? (Should be most of the class.)
- Preview Day 8: project work begins.

## Student-facing content

> **Add a number near a base (10, 20, 30, 100)**
>
> 1. Round it UP to the base.
> 2. Add that.
> 3. Subtract the deficiency.
>
> **Examples:**
> - 33 + 9 = 33 + 10 − 1 = 42
> - 47 + 19 = 47 + 20 − 1 = 66
> - 55 + 48 = 55 + 50 − 2 = 103
> - 234 + 99 = 234 + 100 − 1 = 333
>
> This pattern is named in Tirthaji's 7th sūtra: ***Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām*** — "by addition and by subtraction."

## Homework

- 20 mental additions. Do them in under 60 seconds. List on the back of today's worksheet.
- Bring all project materials tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Apply the same idea to numbers near 100 (e.g. 234 + 98 = 234 + 100 − 2). Also try 3-digit additions in your head.
- **Tier down:** Stick to "+9" first. Once that's automatic, add "+19, +29, +39." Build up.

## Teacher notes

- Some students will object: "But subtracting is HARDER than adding." Not really — subtracting 1 or 2 is trivial once you've done the bigger jump. Push through the initial resistance with the drills.
- This lesson is also a preview of Module 9 (Subtraction by Complements). Don't elaborate now; just note that *Nikhilam* (sūtra #2) and other complement techniques are coming.
