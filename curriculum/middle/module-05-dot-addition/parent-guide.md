# Parent Guide — Module 5: Bindu Paddhati (Dot Method)

**Your child's age:** Middle (Grades 6–8) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## What your child is learning

**The dot method** — a mental-arithmetic technique for adding long columns of numbers fast. Instead of adding digit-by-digit, your child learns to *scan* a column for pairs summing to 10 and mark each "completion" with a dot. The dot count becomes the carry.

**Baseline → retest:** On Day 1, your child times themselves adding a standard 5-addend × 5-digit sum using their normal method. On Day 5, they retest the same problem using the dot method. The visible speedup (50%+ for most students) is the motivation.

**Mental shortcuts (Day 7):** Adding 9 to anything = add 10, subtract 1. (33 + 9 = 33 + 10 − 1 = 42.) This is Tirthaji's 7th sūtra, *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* — "by addition and by subtraction."

## A note on "Vedic mathematics"

Important honest framing:
- The dot method is a real, useful Indian pedagogical technique.
- It is **not directly from any Vedic text**. It is a 20th-century teaching tool that draws on the spirit of Bharati Krishna Tirthaji's 16 sūtras (published 1965, posthumous).
- The math is real. Tirthaji's sūtras are real and culturally significant. The dot method itself is more recent than its name suggests.

Your child will explore this honest framing on Day 6 ("Why it works"). At Senior band they'll read the scholarly debate (Dani 1993) about Tirthaji's claims.

## What to expect this fortnight

**Daily warm-up:** 1 minute of "make-10 fluency" — instant recall of 10-pairs.

**Speed drills:** paired practice, self-timed. No public leaderboards — your child races themselves, not classmates.

**Project (Days 8–10):** Your child chooses one of:
- A 60-second teaching video (Grade 4 audience).
- A printed 20-problem workbook with worked solutions.
- A comparative speed-test data project (collect times from 5–10 family/friends).

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- For the speed-test data project: be a willing test subject. Let your child time your standard-algorithm addition vs. their dot-method addition.
- 5-minute home drill: call out single digits, your child answers the 10-partner instantly. Then 2-digit add-9 problems.
- Try the dot method yourself on a 4×4-digit sum. Notice the scanning skill.

**Not helpful:**
- Insisting their teacher's "old way" is wrong. Both methods give the same answer; the dot method is faster on multi-addend columns.
- Doing their project. Even the workbook has to be in their handwriting / their drawings.

## What success looks like

By end of module, your child can:
- Add a 4-addend × 4-digit column in under 60 seconds using the dot method.
- Do mental "+9, +19, +29" instantly.
- Explain *why* the dot method works (a dot = 10 = 1 carried).
- Distinguish the dot method (technique) from Tirthaji's sūtras (named formulas).
