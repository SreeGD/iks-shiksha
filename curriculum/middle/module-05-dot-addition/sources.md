# Sources — Module 5 Dot Addition (Middle)

The **vidya-karana RAG** does not cover Vedic-math sources, so this module draws directly from SutraGanita's source corpus (`/Users/sree/Projects/SutraGanita/content/`). All PDFs cited below are accessible in that directory.

## Primary source (used most heavily)

**Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf** — *Vedic Cultural Center (ISKCON), 1420 228th Ave SE, Sammamish WA 98075*

- **§5.2 — Mental Addition** (page 16). Pattern recognition: "if you need 6+7+4, see that 6 and 4 make 10, then add 7 to get 17."
- **§5.3 — Completing the Whole** (page 17). Extends to bigger numbers: "for 19+8+1, see 19+1 makes 20, then add 8."
- **§5.4 — Columns of Figures** (page 18). The dot/scanning method: scan a column for pairs summing to 10, put the units digit down, carry 1 (the dot count).
- **§5.5 — By Addition and By Subtraction** (page 19). Numbers near a base (9, 19, 99): add the base, take away the difference. E.g. 33+9 = 33+10−1 = 42.

> Cite as: *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf*, §5.X.

## Historical context

- **Tirthaji, Bharati Krishna.** *Vedic Mathematics* (1965), Motilal Banarsidass. — The 16-sūtra system. Note: the dot-column method described in §5.4 of the source above is *not* one of Tirthaji's sūtras; it is a pedagogical technique often taught alongside the sūtras. Be honest about this distinction with Senior students; at Middle band a one-line note is enough.
- **Plofker, Kim.** *Mathematics in India* (2009), Princeton University Press. — Historical context of pre-Tirthaji Indian mathematics; mentions Bakhshali manuscript addition methods (~7th century CE).

## What to tell students about the citation chain

The "dot method" is not directly from any single classical Sanskrit source — it is a teaching technique developed in 20th-century Indian mathematics pedagogy, drawing on the same pattern-recognition spirit as Tirthaji's sūtras *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* and *Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ*. At Middle band, we'll cite the modern source (Vedic Cultural Center course material). At Senior band, the historical question itself becomes part of the lesson.
