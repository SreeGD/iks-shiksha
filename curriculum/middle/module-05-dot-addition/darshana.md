# Module 5 — Bindu Paddhati — Dot Addition (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Bindu Paddhati* · **Band:** Middle (6-8) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

A 20th-century mental-arithmetic technique for column addition.

## Opening hook · 5 min

Show the column 9 + 8 + 7 + 6 = ?. Most students will sum top-to-bottom. *"What if there's a faster way? A 20th-century Indian arithmetic technique called Bindu Paddhati (dot method) gives one. Today we learn it."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**Bindu Paddhati for column addition.**

For each column (units, tens, hundreds):
1. Add running total downwards.
2. Whenever you reach 10 or more, mark a DOT (•) next to that digit and continue with the remainder.
3. At the end of the column, write the remainder. Count the dots — that's your carry.

Example: 9 + 8 + 6 + 4 = ?
- 9 + 8 = 17 → mark • by 8, remainder 7
- 7 + 6 = 13 → mark • by 6, remainder 3
- 3 + 4 = 7 → final
- Total: 2 dots + 7 = 27. ✓

**Speed:** much faster than mental running-total for long columns.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Live calculation race. Same column (47 + 38 + 26 + 91 + 53), two methods:
- Traditional: ~25 seconds for most students
- Bindu Paddhati: with practice, ~12-15 seconds

Class observes the time difference.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Paraphrase Tirthaji 1965: *"The Bindu Paddhati removes the need to carry, by using a dot as a visual marker of 10."* Modern: dot-counting in computer arithmetic uses the same principle (binary carry-saving adders).

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Method race.** Each student does 10 column-addition problems using Bindu Paddhati. Time themselves. Then do another 10 using traditional method. Compare times — celebrate any improvement.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"You now know two methods for column addition. When would you use which? Why?"*
