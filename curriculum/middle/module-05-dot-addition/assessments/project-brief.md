# Project Brief — Module 5 Dot Addition (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Day 8 + Day 9 · **Presentation:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 40 (project 30 + presentation 10)

## The project

Each pair (or individual, with teacher approval) chooses ONE of three formats:

### Format 1: 60-second teaching video
Make a short video that teaches the dot method to a Grade 4 student. Constraints:
- Total length: 60 seconds (±10 sec)
- Must show: dot placement, units digit identification, carry handoff for at least one example.
- Cite the source (PDF + section) in the title slide or closing credit.

### Format 2: Printed workbook
Produce a 20-problem workbook with the dot method, designed for a Grade 4 self-learner. Constraints:
- 20 problems organised in 4 difficulty levels (5 problems each).
- Each level includes ONE fully worked example.
- Answer key on the back page.
- Title page with cited source.

### Format 3: Comparative speed-test data project
Collect baseline times (standard algorithm) and post-training times (dot method) from 5–10 people (family, schoolmates). Constraints:
- A table of names (or codes), problem solved, time using standard, time using dot method.
- A plot or visual comparison.
- A 1-paragraph interpretation of the data — including any surprising findings.
- Cite the source PDF.

## Why this matters

You're moving from *learner* to *teacher* (or *researcher*). Either role is harder than just doing the problems. The discipline of explaining or measuring forces you to understand more deeply.

## What success looks like

A strong project will:
- Demonstrate the dot method method accurately (no arithmetic errors).
- Be pitched for the intended audience (a 9-year-old for video and workbook; a peer reader for data).
- Show the pair's own thinking.
- Be clearly communicated.

## Working in pairs

- Pair work strongly encouraged.
- Individual allowed for the workbook or video format.
- Groups of 3 acceptable — each member must have a distinct role.

## Citation rules

- At minimum **one** citation must appear in your project — visible.
- Format: `<PDF filename>, §<section>` (e.g. *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf*, §5.4).
- See `sources.md` for the list of available PDFs.
