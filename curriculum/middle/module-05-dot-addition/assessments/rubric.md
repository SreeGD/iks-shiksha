# Project Rubric — Module 5 Dot Addition (Middle)

**Project marks:** 30 (out of 40 with presentation 10).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Method demonstrated correctly** | Method shown completely (dot placement, units digit, carry); at least 3 worked examples with no errors. | Method shown completely; minor errors on 1 example. | Method shown but with conceptual gap (e.g. missing carry handoff). | Method incorrect or absent. | × 2 |
| **Use of sources** | Cites at least one PDF section verbatim (e.g. "*Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf*, §5.4"); cites it correctly. | Cites a PDF source. | Mentions a source vaguely. | No source cited. | × 1.5 |
| **Audience suitability** | Pitched precisely to the target audience (Grade 4 for video; self-study for workbook; readable for data project). | Mostly suitable; minor tonal issues. | Suitable in places, off in others. | Inaccessible for target audience. | × 1.5 |
| **Originality** | The project shows the pair's own thinking — a unique example, a custom narrative, a real data set. | Clearly the students' own work. | Largely repeated from class examples. | Copied verbatim from a source. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals, layout, narration are clear, deliberate, polished. No errors in arithmetic shown. | Communicates clearly. 1–2 craft issues. | Message comes through with effort. | Hard to follow. | × 1 |

Maximum: 28. Project score = (sum × weights) × 30/28.

## Presentation (10 marks)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (3 min)** | 2:45–3:00 | 2:30–3:15 | 2:15–3:30 | Outside | × 1 |
| **Both partners contribute** | Equal | Both speak unequally | One dominates | One silent | × 1 |
| **Clarity** | Confident, audible | Audible, mostly clear | Audible only in front | Mostly inaudible | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 10.

## Format-specific notes

- **Video projects:** the 60-second video itself is the artefact; the 3-min presentation explains the design choices ("we used this example because ...").
- **Workbook projects:** the workbook is the artefact; the 3-min presentation walks through the cleverest 1–2 problems.
- **Speed-test data projects:** the table/chart is the artefact; the 3-min presentation interprets the data ("our 5 participants showed an average X% improvement").
