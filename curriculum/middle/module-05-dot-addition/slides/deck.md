---

# What you'll learn

- Scan a column for **pairs that make 10**.
- Mark a **dot** above each "completion."
- Read off: **units digit** + **carry** (= number of dots).

By Day 5, you'll re-do this problem 50%+ faster.

---

# The foundational skill

The Five 10-Pairs:

| 1 + 9 | 2 + 8 | 3 + 7 | 4 + 6 | 5 + 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Recall any partner in under 1 second.

---

# The procedure

```
1. Running total = 0.
2. Scan top to bottom.
3. When total ≥ 10: mark a dot, subtract 10.
4. End:
   - Final total = units digit.
   - Dot count = carry.
```

---

# Worked example: one column

```
6
3
7  ·   (+7 makes 16, dot, total = 6)
5
8  ·   (+8 makes 13, dot, total = 3)
4
2
__
5      ← units
2 dots = carry 2
```

Direct check: 6+3+7+5+8+4+2 = 35. ✓

---

# Multi-column with carries

```
       · ·
5728
3417
6892
4536 +
   3
```

The **dots become the carry** to the next column.

---

# Why it works

> A dot represents **10 used up**.
>
> 10 in this column = 1 in the next.
>
> So the dot count IS the carry.

Not magic. Just good notation.

---

# A historical note

The dot method is *not* one of Tirthaji's 16 sūtras.

It is a **20th-century pedagogical technique** inspired by:

- Sūtra 7: *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* — "by addition and by subtraction"
- Sūtra 8: *Pūraṇāpūraṇābhyāṁ* — "by completing or non-completing"

We'll be honest about the difference.

---

# Mental addition: numbers near a base

To add a number close to 10, 20, 30, ...:

1. Round UP to the base.
2. Add.
3. Subtract the deficiency.

```
33 + 9 = 33 + 10 − 1 = 42
55 + 48 = 55 + 50 − 2 = 103
```

---

# Sūtra #7 named

***Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām***

"by addition and by subtraction"

The mental near-base technique is *exactly* this sūtra in action.
