# Activity 02 — Near-Base Mental Addition

**Module:** Bindu Paddhati · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 7 · **Time:** 20 min

## Type

Quick-fire mental math drill, individual + paired

## Goal

Students add 2-digit numbers ending in 9, 19, 29, 49, 99 mentally using the "add the base, subtract the deficiency" pattern, in under 3 seconds per problem.

## Materials per pair

- 1 printed drill sheet (20 problems below)
- 1 stopwatch

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> **Near-Base Mental Drill** — work in pairs.
>
> **Round 1 (5 min, individual):** Solve as many of the 20 problems as you can on the sheet. NO writing intermediate steps — write only the answer.
>
> **Round 2 (5 min, paired):** Partner-A reads a problem aloud. Partner-B answers in under 3 seconds. Swap. Continue alternating through all 20.
>
> **Round 3 (5 min, mixed):** Teacher displays 10 new problems on the board. Class shouts the answer in chorus.
>
> **Round 4 (5 min, quick-fire):** Teacher calls a 2-digit number ending in 9 or 8, then any other 2-digit number; students whisper-answer to their neighbour.

### Drill sheet (20 problems)

```
1.  33 + 9         11. 27 + 19
2.  47 + 9         12. 56 + 29
3.  64 + 19        13. 35 + 39
4.  28 + 19        14. 73 + 49
5.  55 + 48        15. 81 + 19
6.  36 + 38        16. 42 + 28
7.  19 + 19        17. 65 + 99
8.  72 + 28        18. 88 + 12
9.  46 + 39        19. 54 + 47
10. 91 + 9         20. 33 + 88
```

### Answer key (teacher copy)

```
1. 42   2. 56   3. 83   4. 47   5. 103   6. 74   7. 38   8. 100   9. 85   10. 100
11. 46  12. 85  13. 74  14. 122 15. 100  16. 70  17. 164 18. 100  19. 101 20. 121
```

## Discussion prompts (after Round 4)

- Which type of problem was easiest? (Most students say +9 or +19 — single digit deficiency.)
- Which type took longer mental processing? (Often +48, +99 — bigger numbers requiring base-100 thinking.)
- Anyone discover a *new* shortcut while doing this?

## Safety / sensitivity

- Same as Activity 01 — no public competition.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** 3-digit near-base additions (234 + 99, 567 + 199, etc.).
- **Tier down:** Stick to +9 and +19 problems for today. Build up.

## Clean-up

- Worksheets kept by students for revision.
