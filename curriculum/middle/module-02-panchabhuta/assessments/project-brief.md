# Project Brief — Module 2 Panchabhūta (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Day 8 + Day 9 (in class) · **Presentation + summative:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 40

## The project

Each pair (or individual, with teacher approval) chooses **one** of the five elements and produces a small project that shows:

1. **What the *pañcabhūta* tradition says** about this element (its tanmātra, its position in the creation sequence, the quality-count rule, what it "does"). Must cite at least one source — a verse from the RAG cache, or a reference from `sources.md`.
2. **One modern-science parallel** — a concept from chemistry, biology, physics, or ecology that overlaps with this element. Explain where the parallel works and where it breaks down.
3. **One local imbalance** — a real example in your neighbourhood where this element is in excess or depletion, with one doable action.

## Why this matters

You're learning to hold two intellectual frameworks side-by-side without forcing one to win. This is a core skill — not just for *pañcabhūta*, but for any time you encounter a way of seeing the world that's different from your own.

## What success looks like

A strong project will:
- Demonstrate the module's central concepts accurately (tanmātra, creation sequence, quality-count rule).
- Connect the Indian tradition to a modern scientific concept fairly — without saying "ancient Indians knew this already."
- Show your own thinking, not just summary.
- Be clearly communicated in your chosen medium.

## Format options

| Format | Best for elements | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Poster (A2 or larger) | Any | Lots of visual space; works for nervous presenters |
| Live demonstration (2 min hands-on) | *tejas* (fire), *vāyu* (air), *ap* (water) | Needs safety check |
| Short written story (1 page) | *ākāśa*, any | Read aloud or hand out |
| 90-second video | Any | Pre-made and played in class |
| Infographic | *pṛthvī* (data-heavy), *ap* | Combine drawing + facts |
| Original song or poem | *vāyu*, *ākāśa* | 90 sec performed live |

Other formats with teacher approval. Whatever the format, the 3-minute time limit on Day 10 applies.

## Working in pairs (or individually)

- **Pair work is encouraged**, especially for video/song/demo formats.
- **Individual is allowed**, especially if the topic is *ākāśa* (often suits a solo reflective piece).
- Groups of 3 are acceptable but each member must have a distinct sub-role (visuals / script / presenter, or research / draft / final).

## Citation rules

- At minimum **one** citation must appear in your project — visible on the poster, named in the video, or printed in the story.
- The citation must be from a source you can point to: a verse from one of the day-NN.json cache files in `.rag-cache/`, OR a reference from `sources.md`.
- If you invent a citation or pull one off the internet without verification, you'll score 0 on both **Use of sources** and **Originality**.

## Self-reflection (submitted on Day 10 with project)

In 4–6 sentences, answer:

1. What did you set out to learn or make, and what did you actually end up with?
2. What is the most surprising thing you discovered while making this?
3. If you had three more days, what would you do differently?
