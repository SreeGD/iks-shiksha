# Project Rubric — Module 2 Panchabhūta (Middle)

**Project marks:** 30 (out of 40 total when combined with presentation 10).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Conceptual accuracy** | Names the element AND its tanmātra AND its position in the creation sequence correctly. Connects to the quality-count rule. | Names the element, tanmātra, and one accurate fact about its place in the framework. Minor imprecision OK. | Names the element correctly but mixes up tanmātra OR creation order. | Names the element only; gets the tanmātra or creation order wrong. | × 2 |
| **Use of sources** | Cites at least one verse from the RAG cache verbatim, with reference (e.g. "SB 3.26.36"); cites it correctly. Bonus: weaves a paraphrase in addition to the direct cite. | Cites a source by reference; the source matches a real entry in the cache or sources.md. | Mentions a source vaguely ("an old Sanskrit text says ...") without a specific reference. | No source cited. | × 2 |
| **Modern-science parallel** | Names a specific modern-science concept and explains the parallel AND where it falls short. | Names a modern-science concept and explains the parallel. | Names a modern-science concept but doesn't explain the parallel. | No parallel attempted. | × 1 |
| **Local-imbalance connection** | Names a specific local example with date/place/observation + names a doable action. | Names a local example and an observation. | Names a generic example ("pollution is bad"). | No local connection. | × 1 |
| **Originality of expression** | The project shows the pair's own thinking — a turn of phrase, a fresh comparison, a surprising example. | Clearly the students' own work; not lifted from any single source. | Largely paraphrased from a single source; little independent thinking visible. | Clearly copied from a single source. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals, layout, or narrative are clean, deliberate, and serve the message. Spelling and IAST are correct. | Communicates clearly. One or two craft issues (a typo, a misaligned heading). | Message comes through despite some craft issues. | Hard to follow; multiple craft issues impede understanding. | × 1 |

## Score calculation

`Project score = (sum of criterion scores × weights) × 0.9375`

Maximum raw score: 32. Multiply by 30/32 = 0.9375 to get out of 30.

## Presentation marks (10, on Day 10)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (3 min)** | Within 2:45–3:00 | Within 2:30–3:15 | Within 2:15–3:30 | Outside that range | × 1 |
| **Both partners contribute (or solo presents whole)** | Equal contribution; smooth handoffs | Both partners speak but unequally | One partner dominates | One partner silent | × 1 |
| **Clarity and audibility** | Confident, audible to the back of the room, clear pace | Audible, mostly clear | Audible only in front rows; some mumbling | Mostly inaudible | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 10.

## Self-reflection mark (folded into project score)

The 3 reflection questions are not separately scored — but if the reflection is missing or perfunctory, the **Originality** mark drops by 1.

## Common decision points

- **Group of 3 vs pair**: Acceptable, but the third member must have a clearly distinct sub-role (e.g. visuals + script + presenter). Marks are equal across the group unless one member visibly didn't contribute.
- **Late submission**: -2 marks per school day, capped at -6.
- **Originality dispute**: If a project closely matches an online source not cited, the project goes to 0 on **Use of sources** AND on **Originality**. A 1-day extension is offered for a redo.

## Marking process

- Mark in real-time during presentation (5 minutes per pair: 3 present + 1 Q&A + 1 marking).
- Write 1-sentence written feedback after each presentation finishes.
- Return marked rubrics within 2 school days.
