# Module 2

## Panchabhūta — The Five Elements

Grades 6–8 · 10 sessions

---

# What is around you right now?

- Solid things (your desk, the floor)
- Liquid things (water bottle)
- Heat and light (tube light, sunlight)
- Air (breath, breeze)
- ... and the *space* between everything

---

# An old framework, still useful

Around 1500 BCE, thinkers in the Indian subcontinent organised all of this into **five categories**.

They called them the ***pañca-mahābhūta*** — the **five great elements**.

---

# The Five

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English |
|-----------------|---------|
| *pṛthvī* | earth (solid) |
| *ap* | water (liquid) |
| *tejas* | fire (heat / light) |
| *vāyu* | air (gas / movement) |
| *ākāśa* | space (room / ether) |

---

# Where do they come from?

The Sāṅkhya tradition says the elements emerge in a sequence:

> *ākāśa* → *vāyu* → *tejas* → *ap* → *pṛthvī*

Space first. Earth last.

---

# The quality-count rule

Each new element **inherits** the qualities of the elements before it,
and **adds one new quality of its own**.

So:
- *ākāśa* has 1 quality
- *vāyu* has 2
- *tejas* has 3
- *ap* has 4
- *pṛthvī* has 5 — all the qualities

> "Earth has five qualities, water four, fire three, air two, and sky one — sound."
> — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 3.26.50

---

# The five qualities (*tanmātras*)

| Element | Tanmātra | Sense |
|---------|----------|-------|
| *ākāśa* | *śabda* (sound) | ear |
| *vāyu* | *sparśa* (touch) | skin |
| *tejas* | *rūpa* (form) | eye |
| *ap* | *rasa* (taste) | tongue |
| *pṛthvī* | *gandha* (smell) | nose |

---

# Why does earth have all five?

You can:
- **Hear** soil shift
- **Touch** its texture
- **See** its colour
- **Taste** it (you have, as a child)
- **Smell** it after rain

Solid stuff is perceivable in every way.

# What does this have to do with science class?

Two frameworks:

- ***Pañcabhūta*** organises **stuff and space** (sensory categories).
- **Modern periodic table** organises **atoms** (118 of them).

Both still in use. Both useful for different questions.

---

# Example: a candle flame

**Pañcabhūta lens:**
Dominant *tejas* (heat + light + transformation), touching *vāyu* (air feeds it), in *ākāśa* (space around it).

**Modern chemistry lens:**
Combustion: hydrocarbon (wax) + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + light + heat. The flame is incandescent gas.

Same object, two complete descriptions.

---

# A balanced environment

A healthy place has all five elements in balance.

When one is **overloaded** or **depleted**, the others suffer.

---

# Imbalance examples

| Imbalance | What you might observe |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Too much *tejas* | Urban heat-island, power cuts |
| Too little *ap* | Drought, failed crops |
| Polluted *vāyu* | Smog, breathing trouble |
| Polluted *pṛthvī* | Plastic waste, dead soil |
| Noisy *ākāśa* | Sound pollution, poor sleep |
