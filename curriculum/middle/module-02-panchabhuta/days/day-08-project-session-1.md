# Day 8 — Project Session 1: Plan and Begin

**Module:** Panchabhūta · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, every pair / individual will have a written project plan and at least one tangible artefact (draft poster outline, demo prop, story opening paragraph, etc.) for their final project.

## Materials

- Project materials brought by each pair (as per their plan)
- Spare poster paper, markers, scissors, glue
- A printed copy of the rubric (`assessments/rubric.md`) per pair
- Stopwatch / classroom timer

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Daily prompt — 2 students.
- Hand out the rubric. Walk through it in 60 seconds: *"You'll be graded on accuracy, use of sources, originality, communication, collaboration, and presentation. Highest mark in each row is 4."*

### Core (35 min) — Workshop

**Structure:** Three 10-minute sprints with a 1-minute "stand and stretch" between each.

- **Sprint 1 (10 min) — Plan completion.** Every pair finalises:
  - Element + project title
  - The single sentence the audience should walk away thinking
  - The structure (3 parts max: e.g. "what tradition says / one science parallel / one local imbalance")
  - The division of labour (who does what)
  - The source(s) they will cite (must include one RAG citation from Day 1–3 caches, OR a citation from `sources.md`)
- **Sprint 2 (10 min) — First artefact.** Pairs start building. Teacher does rapid-rotation: 90 seconds per pair, asking one focused question.
- **Sprint 3 (10 min) — Build / iterate.** Continue.

Teacher's role during sprints:
- 30-second visit to each pair in Sprint 2.
- Watch for pairs who are *still planning* during Sprint 2. Pull them aside, force a "start drawing / start writing / start building NOW" intervention.
- For projects on *ākāśa* (often the trickiest), check that they've found a concrete way to show emptiness — silence, white-space, a pause, a gap.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- 30-second share-out from any 4 pairs (you call them) — they hold up their progress.
- Submit project plan paragraph at the door on the way out.
- Preview Day 9: refine and rehearse.

## Student-facing content

> **Project workshop — Session 1**
>
> Today you'll plan and start your project. By end of class, your pair should have:
>
> 1. A written plan (the paragraph you turn in today).
> 2. Your first concrete artefact in progress — even if rough.
>
> **Plan template:**
>
> ```
> Element: __________
> Title: __________
> Audience walks away thinking: __________
> Structure (3 parts):
>   1. __________
>   2. __________
>   3. __________
> Division of labour (Partner A): __________
> Division of labour (Partner B): __________
> Sources we'll cite:
>   - __________ (from class cache or sources.md)
> ```

## Homework

- Whatever your pair didn't finish today — finish at home tonight. Tomorrow is for *refining*, not for *starting*.

## Differentiation

- **One tier up:** Stretch goal — add an interactive element (a question for the audience, a small experiment they can join). Adds depth.
- **One tier down:** Allow choosing a *simpler* format mid-stream if the original plan is collapsing. A clear poster beats a half-built video.

## Teacher notes

- The pacing of three sprints is deliberate — most students will resist starting to build. The 10-minute timer forces them past planning paralysis.
- If a pair seems stuck, ask: *"If you had to present right now, in 30 seconds, what would you say?"* That often unlocks the structure.

## Citation(s) used in this lesson

- The pair's chosen source(s) — verified during Sprint 1.
