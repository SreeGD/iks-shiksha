# Day 9 — Project Session 2: Refine and Rehearse

**Module:** Panchabhūta · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, every pair will have a presentation-ready project artefact and will have rehearsed their 3-minute presentation at least once with timed feedback.

## Materials

- Pairs bring all in-progress materials.
- Stopwatch.
- Printed feedback form: 1 strength, 1 fix, in 30 seconds.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Daily prompt — 2 students.
- Brief: "Today is **refine + rehearse**. By end of class, you'll have presented to one other pair and gotten feedback."

### Core (30 min)

**Structure:** Two 15-minute halves.

- **Half 1 (15 min) — Refine.**
  - Continue building / drafting.
  - Teacher does rapid rotation: 60 seconds per pair, focused question: *"Show me your strongest 30 seconds."*
  - Look for projects that:
    - Are missing the citation — flag immediately.
    - Are missing the modern-science parallel — required for accuracy mark.
    - Are missing the local imbalance — required for the "originality / connection" mark.
  - Pairs that look ready early start to rehearse.

- **Half 2 (15 min) — Rehearse with a buddy pair.**
  - Match each pair to one other pair. (Vary partners — match a strong pair with one that's behind, etc.)
  - Pair A presents to Pair B for 3 minutes (use the timer).
  - Pair B gives feedback for 60 seconds — one strength, one fix.
  - Swap. (Total: 8 min per rotation, ×2 = 16 min — runs slightly over, that's OK.)

### Activity in parallel

- Teacher with the timer; intervene if a pair tries to rehearse for more than 3 min.
- Listen for projects that are *all summary, no specifics*. Intervene: "Give me one concrete example or detail — what does the audience *see*?"

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- One-sentence share: *"What's the one thing I'm changing before tomorrow?"* — every pair contributes.
- Tomorrow: full presentations + summative quiz.

## Student-facing content

> **Project workshop — Session 2: Refine + Rehearse**
>
> Today's checklist:
>
> - [ ] My artefact is presentation-ready (no half-finished sections).
> - [ ] I've cited at least one source (verse, sūtra, or PDF).
> - [ ] I've named at least one modern-science parallel.
> - [ ] I've named one local example of imbalance.
> - [ ] I've timed my presentation — it fits in 3 minutes.
> - [ ] I know what my partner says and what I say.
> - [ ] I've heard feedback from one other pair and decided what to change.
>
> Tomorrow you present.

## Homework

- Final touches at home tonight. Bring everything tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **One tier up:** Add a Q&A-ready note: anticipate one tricky question from the audience and prepare a 30-second answer.
- **One tier down:** Allow reading from a written script for the presentation. The goal is communication, not memorisation.

## Teacher notes

- The "buddy pair" rehearsal often surfaces problems the teacher missed. Encourage students to be *kind and specific* in their feedback — "the part about water was clear, the part about pollution was rushed" beats "good job."
- Pairs who consistently can't fit in 3 minutes are usually trying to say too much. Help them cut.
