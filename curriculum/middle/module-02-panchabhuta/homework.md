# Homework — Module 2 Panchabhūta (Middle)

Homework tasks per day. None should take more than 25 minutes for a Middle-band student. Skip a day if it conflicts with another subject's deadline.

## Day 1 → Day 2

**Task:** Bring **three interesting objects from home** to class on Day 4. They should be small enough to fit on your desk.

**Bonus:** Which element does each object belong to?

Time: 5 min (deciding) + carrying the objects.

---

## Day 2 → Day 3

**Task:** Find ONE thing in your home that perfectly demonstrates the "all five qualities" of *pṛthvī*. It should be something you can hear, touch, see, taste (or imagine the taste), and smell. Write 2 sentences naming the object and what each quality feels like.

*Examples your classmates might pick: a sliced lemon, a piece of jaggery, a freshly cut carrot, a hand-made clay pot.*

Time: 10 min.

---

## Day 3 → Day 4

**Task:** For each of your three home-objects, figure out the **dominant element** and the **dominant tanmātra**. Write one line per object.

*Format:* `Object → dominant element → dominant tanmātra`

*Example:* `Lemon → pṛthvī → gandha (smell) is strongest, but rasa (taste) is close behind.`

Time: 10 min.

---

## Day 4 → Day 5

**No new homework.** Use the time to finalise your project pair / individual decision and to start thinking about which element you want to focus on.

---

## Day 5 → Day 6

**Task:** Submit your **project plan paragraph** in writing.

*Format:* one paragraph, 5–8 sentences, stating: which element, which format (poster / demo / story / video / infographic / song), and what audience will see/hear/feel.

Time: 15 min.

---

## Day 6 → Day 7

**Task:** Open your school science textbook to the chapter on "Matter in Our Surroundings" (Class 9, Chapter 1) — or whatever your closest equivalent is. Read one section. Write a 1-paragraph note: *what would the pañcabhūta framework get right about this topic, and what would it miss?*

Time: 20 min.

---

## Day 7 → Day 8

**Task:** **Bring ALL project materials** to tomorrow's class. The build session starts immediately. Anything you forget cannot be retrieved during class time.

**Bonus:** Pick one of the five elements (not your project element) and identify one "imbalance" example near your home — a polluted drain (*ap*), a dusty lot (*pṛthvī*), a noisy intersection (*ākāśa*+*śabda*), etc. Be ready to share in Day 7's group session.

Time: 10 min.

---

## Day 8 → Day 9

**Task:** **Finish at home what you couldn't finish in class today.** Tomorrow is for *refining*, not for *starting*.

Time: variable — but cap at 30 min, then stop and rest. Done-is-better-than-perfect.

---

## Day 9 → Day 10

**Task:** Final touches. Make sure you have:
- [ ] The artefact (poster / video file / printed story / etc.)
- [ ] Your script or notes
- [ ] A working version of your 3-minute presentation in your head

Time: 15 min.

---

## After Day 10

**No homework.** Module is complete. Take a breath. (If your next module begins immediately, check that module's README for prerequisite reading.)
