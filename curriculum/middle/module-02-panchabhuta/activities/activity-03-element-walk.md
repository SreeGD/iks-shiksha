# Activity 03 — Element Walk (Field Observation)

**Module:** Panchabhūta · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 7 (debriefed in class; observation done at home overnight)

## Type

Independent field observation, 20 min, completed at home or on the school grounds

## Goal

Each student finds ONE example of an element imbalance in their neighbourhood and documents it.

## Materials per student

- Notebook + pen, or a smartphone for photos
- The element-walk worksheet (below)

## Instructions (student-facing — printable handout)

> **Element Walk**
>
> Spend 20 minutes walking around your neighbourhood (or the school grounds). Look for ONE place where one of the five elements is clearly out of balance — too much, or too little.
>
> Examples of what to look for:
>
> | Element | Excess | Depletion |
> |---------|--------|-----------|
> | *pṛthvī* (earth) | Pile of plastic waste; bald soil from no plants | Eroded soil; bare slope |
> | *ap* (water) | Stagnant water pool (mosquitoes); flooded drain | Dry tap; cracked dry earth |
> | *tejas* (fire/heat) | Heat-island parking lot at noon; smoke from burning garbage | Cold dark corner where nothing grows |
> | *vāyu* (air) | Vehicle exhaust at a signal; dust storm | Stagnant indoor air; no breeze in a closed alley |
> | *ākāśa* (space/sound) | Constant honking; loudspeakers | An eerily silent abandoned spot |
>
> **Document on the worksheet:**

## Discussion prompts (Day 7 in class)

- Whose observation was the most specific (named a real street, a real time)?
- Did any two students choose the same location? How did their observations differ?
- Which "small actions" feel most doable? Which feel hardest?

## Safety / sensitivity

- **No solo walks for younger students (Grade 6).** Pair with a sibling, parent, or do the walk on school grounds during break.
- **Avoid actually inhaling smoke, drinking unsafe water, etc.** Observation is the goal — not exposure.
- **Be aware** of sensitivity around naming specific neighbourhoods as polluted (some students live there). Frame the observation as "everyone's neighbourhood has imbalances, including mine."

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Find TWO related imbalances (e.g. polluted water leads to depleted plant life). Document the connection.
- **Tier down:** Just describe what you saw. The "one action" can be one of the suggestions from the in-class examples.

## Clean-up

- Bring the worksheet to Day 7 class.
- Photos optional — only if devices are allowed in school per school policy.
