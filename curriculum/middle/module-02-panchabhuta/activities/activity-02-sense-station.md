# Activity 02 — Sense Station Rotation

**Module:** Panchabhūta · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 3 · **Time:** 15 min (within Day 3's 45 min)

## Type

Hands-on sensory perception, paired

## Goal

Students experience each *tanmātra* directly and notice when more than one perception is happening simultaneously.

## Materials per group

5 stations (one per element), each set up with:

- **Station *ākāśa* (sound)** — a small bell or two metal spoons. A printed label: *"Perceive sound. Use your ears."*
- **Station *vāyu* (touch)** — a piece of cotton wool, a small ice pack (or cold water in a sealed bag), a piece of sandpaper. Label: *"Perceive touch. Use your skin."*
- **Station *tejas* (form / colour)** — a small mirror, a coloured plastic sheet, an unlit torch turned on. Label: *"Perceive form. Use your eyes."*
- **Station *ap* (taste)** — a tiny pinch of salt, a tiny pinch of sugar (or jaggery), on individual paper plates. (Or a jar of mint leaves to chew if no food allowed.) Label: *"Perceive taste. Use your tongue."*
- **Station *pṛthvī* (smell)** — a sprig of mint, a slice of orange peel, a small jar of soil after wetting. Label: *"Perceive smell. Use your nose."*

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Arrange 5 stations around the room.
- Each pair is assigned a starting station (rotate clockwise every 2 min).
- 5 stations × 2 min each = 10 min for rotation + 5 min discussion.

## Instructions (student-facing — printable handout)

> **Sense Station Rotation** — work in pairs.
>
> At each station, spend 2 minutes:
>
> 1. Use the sense organ named on the label to perceive the sample.
> 2. On your worksheet, write one sentence describing **what you perceived**.
> 3. Then ask yourself: **what OTHER sense could perceive something here at the same time?** Write that too.
>
> Rotate when the teacher rings the bell.

### Worksheet (one per pair)

| Station | Tanmātra | What I perceived | Other element / tanmātra I noticed |
|---------|----------|---------------------|--------------------------------------|
| ākāśa | *śabda* (sound) | | |
| vāyu | *sparśa* (touch) | | |
| tejas | *rūpa* (form) | | |
| ap | *rasa* (taste) | | |
| pṛthvī | *gandha* (smell) | | |

## Discussion prompts (last 3 min)

- At which station did you notice *more than one* element at once?
- Example: at the bell — did you HEAR the sound (*ākāśa*) and also FEEL the vibration in your fingers if you touched the bell (*vāyu*)?
- What does this tell us about how the elements actually appear in the world?

## Safety / sensitivity

- **Diabetic check** before using sugar / jaggery — substitute with salt or a non-edible smell-only sample.
- **Allergy check** for peel, mint, soil.
- No sharing of food samples between students.
- Cold pack: don't let students hold it for more than 30 seconds.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** At each station, name ONE everyday object that demonstrates the same tanmātra better. (E.g. ākāśa station — "I hear better with a singing bowl than this bell.")
- **Tier down:** Just write what you perceived. Skip the "other element" column.

## Clean-up

- Teacher collects all materials; students do not eat from leftover samples.
