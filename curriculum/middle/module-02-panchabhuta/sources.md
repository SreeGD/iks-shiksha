# Sources — Module 2 Panchabhūta (Middle)

All scriptural citations were retrieved via the **vidya-karana-kg** RAG and cached under `.rag-cache/`. The verses below are the primary anchors used across the lesson plan.

## Primary scriptural sources (from RAG)

| Citation | Used in | Content |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **SB 2.10.3** | Day 1 hook, glossary | "*bhūta-mātrendṛya-dhiyāṁ janma sarga udāhṛtaḥ ...*" — defines *sarga* as the manifestation of the five gross elements (sky, air, fire, water, earth), their qualities, the senses, and the intellect. |
| **SB 3.5.36** | Day 2 creation sequence | The earth element emerges from water, carrying the quality of smell (*gandha-guṇām*), under the impulse of time. |
| **SB 3.26.36** | Day 3 qualities | "*mṛdutvaṁ kaṭhinatvaṁ ca śaityam uṣṇatvam eva ca ...*" — softness, hardness, cold, heat are the attributes of touch (*sparśa*), the subtle element of air. |
| **SB 3.26.44** | Day 3 qualities | Taste (*rasa*) transforms into smell (*gandha*); water gives rise to earth, and smell is perceived by the nose. |
| **SB 3.26.50** (paraphrase in cached chunk) | Day 2 creation, Day 3 qualities | Earth has five qualities, water four, fire three, air two, sky (space) only one — sound. |

These are not exhaustive — the cache files under `.rag-cache/` contain additional supporting chunks from Canto 4, Canto 6, Canto 11, and Caitanya-caritāmṛta.

## Modern science references (Day 6 cross-disciplinary)

These are *not* in either RAG; standard middle-school science textbooks suffice:

- **States of matter** — NCERT Class 9 Science, Chapter 1 ("Matter in Our Surroundings"). Solid / liquid / gas; plasma and Bose-Einstein condensate as further states.
- **Periodic table** — NCERT Class 9 Science, Chapter 5 ("The Fundamental Unit of Life"). For the distinction: in modern physics "element" = atom of a single proton-number; in *pañcabhūta* "element" = an observable category of matter/space.
- **Ecology and ecosystem balance** — NCERT Class 8 Science, Chapter 18 ("Pollution of Air and Water").

## Historical context references (Day 6 reading list, optional)

- Larson, G. J. (1979). *Classical Sāṁkhya: An Interpretation of its History and Meaning*. Motilal Banarsidass. — On the *tattva* enumeration in Sāṅkhya, of which the *pañcabhūta* are the last five.
- Plofker, K. (2009). *Mathematics in India*. Princeton University Press. — Sets the historical context of classical Indian intellectual frameworks.
