# Parent Guide — Module 2: Panchabhūta (Five Elements)

**Your child's age:** Middle (Grades 6–8) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min over ~2 weeks

## What your child is learning

The Sāṅkhya framework of *pañca-mahābhūta* — five categories Indian thinkers used to organise everyday matter and space:

| Sanskrit | English |
|----------|---------|
| pṛthvī | earth / solid |
| ap | water / liquid |
| tejas | fire / heat / light |
| vāyu | air / gas |
| ākāśa | space / room |

Plus a deeper layer:
- The **creation sequence** — how the five elements emerge one after another (space first, earth last).
- The **quality-count rule** — each element inherits all qualities of the elements before it.
- The **tanmātra mapping** — each element pairs with a perceptual quality (sound, touch, form, taste, smell).

## How we teach it

The framing is **secular and scientific first**. We lead with what your child can observe, then situate the Sanskrit framework as one historical Indian way of organising that observation. We do NOT teach this instead of modern chemistry — we teach it as an additional intellectual framework.

NEP 2020 §4.6 (Integration of Indian Knowledge Systems) supports this approach.

## Homework you'll see

- Bring 3 home objects to sort by element (Days 1, 4).
- Find a single thing that has "all five qualities" — e.g. soil, lemon, jaggery (Day 2).
- A 20-min walk in your neighbourhood to find an element "imbalance" (Day 7).
- A project — poster, demo, story, video, infographic, or song (Days 8–10).
- 6–10 writing prompts across the module (reflection, explanatory, argumentative, creative).

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Quiz your child on the five Sanskrit names *gently*. They should know them by Day 5.
- For the home walk, walk with them. Ask what they notice — don't tell them what to write.
- For their project: provide materials and access to a source verse from the cache (see below) if they want to cite.
- Discuss the Day 6 cross-disciplinary lesson: how does the modern periodic table relate to the five elements? Both are real. Both are useful. They answer different questions.

**Not helpful:**
- Insisting "ancient Indians knew everything before science discovered it." This framing isn't what we teach.
- Doing the project. The struggle is the learning.
- Pushing your child toward a "correct" position on Day 7's discussion. The point is engagement, not agreement.

## Citation standard

By Middle band, projects must cite at least one verse from a primary source. The verses we use are from the Bhāgavata Purāṇa (specifically Cantos 2, 3, 4). The retrieved verses with translations are cached in `.rag-cache/` folders within the module pack — your child can show you the source they used.

This trains the habit: real citations from real texts, not invented references.

## What success looks like

By end of module, your child can:
- Name all 5 elements + 5 tanmātras + 5 senses in Sanskrit with English meaning.
- Diagram the creation sequence.
- Explain why earth has 5 qualities and space only 1.
- Compare pañcabhūta with the modern states-of-matter framework fairly.
- Present a 3-minute project on one element with a source citation.

## On respect across traditions

Some students' families read these verses devotionally. The classroom frame is *both/and*: the verses are real scripture AND we use them here as historical primary sources. We avoid framing that diminishes or inflates either dimension.
