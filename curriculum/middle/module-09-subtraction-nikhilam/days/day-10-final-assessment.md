# Day 10 — Final Assessment + Project Share

**Module:** Nikhilam Subtraction · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will complete the summative quiz, present their project artefact in 3 minutes, and submit a brief written self-reflection.

## Materials

- `quizzes/summative.md` (printed, one per student)
- Stopwatch for project presentations
- Camera (phone) for documenting projects, if permitted by school policy
- Stickers for completion (optional, but motivating)

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- Final sūtra recitation, in unison, from memory — *Nikhilaṁ Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ.*
- One sentence each, going around the room: *"The one thing I'll remember from this module is..."*

### Summative quiz (20 min)

- Closed notebook.
- 25 marks. Sections: complement recall, subtraction practice, general subtraction, conceptual short answer, open response on "why it works." See `quizzes/summative.md`.
- Strict 15-min time limit on the multiple-choice / short-answer parts; remaining time for the open response.
- Collect.

### Project presentations (18 min)

- Each pair presents for 3 minutes (set the timer visibly).
- Format depends on artefact:
  - **Shopkeeper chart:** Pair demos using pretend currency on a real scenario.
  - **Teaching video:** Pair plays the 60-sec video, then takes 90 sec of Q&A.
  - **Two's-complement poster:** Pair walks through the poster's argument.
- Rest of class fills in a quick "feedback card" — one strength, one question.

### Wrap-up (4 min)

- Quick poll: was Module 9 useful? Surprising? Hard?
- Honest history note one more time: *"You learned a real, useful method. The sūtra is from Tirthaji's 1965 book. The deeper history is more recent than the name 'Vedic' suggests. You can hold both: the method is real; the historical claim is unverified."*
- Self-reflection slip (1 minute): on the back of the quiz, complete:
  - One thing I learned: __________
  - One thing I'm still confused about: __________
  - My favourite Sanskrit term from the module: __________

## Student-facing content

> **Today's tasks**
>
> 1. Summative quiz (20 min, closed notebook).
> 2. Project presentation (3 min, with your partner).
> 3. Self-reflection (1 min, on the back of the quiz).
>
> You've come a long way. Day 1's `10 000 − 4567` took most of you about 30 seconds. By now you can do it in 5.

## Homework

- None. Module ends.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** If finished early on the quiz, write the algebraic identity for the Nikhilam method in full generality (for any base b).
- **Tier down:** Extra 5 min on the quiz allowed for slow processors; quiet pull-aside support.

## Teacher notes

- The summative is the most marked single assessment of the module. Be strict on closed-notebook BUT generous on the open-response question — student paraphrases of the algebraic identity are valid even if not perfectly phrased.
- Project presentations: keep the energy up. Use stickers / acknowledgment for every pair. The presentation is partly a performance; affirm the work.
- If a pair didn't finish their artefact, still let them present. The presentation itself is graded; the artefact is graded separately on the rubric.
- File the quiz results AND the project rubric scores to inform pacing for next year's cohort.
