# Module 9 — Subtraction by Complements (*Nikhilam Sūtra*)

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min (≈ 2 weeks)

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Recite and translate** the Nikhilam sūtra — *Nikhilaṁ Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ*, "all from nine and the last from ten."
2. **Find the complement (*pūraka*)** of any number to the next power of ten — 10, 100, 1000, 10 000 — without paper computation.
3. **Subtract from a power of ten** (e.g. 1000 − 567) by reading off the digit-wise complement, with no borrowing.
4. **Apply** the same idea to general subtractions (e.g. 8243 − 567) by combining a complement with a place-value adjustment.
5. **Explain algebraically** why the rule works — that 10^n − A = (10^n − 1) − A + 1, and that "(10^n − 1) − A" is exactly the digit-wise subtraction from nines.
6. **Compare** the speed and error-pattern of Nikhilam subtraction with standard borrowing.

## Prerequisites

- Confident standard-algorithm subtraction with borrowing across at least two columns.
- Place-value fluency for 3- and 4-digit numbers.
- "Make 10" reflex from Module 5 helps — the Nikhilam rule reuses it heavily.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md` for full entries.

- *Nikhilaṁ Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ* — "all from nine and the last from ten"
- *pūraka* — complement (the number that completes another to a base)
- *sūtra* — short formula or aphorism
- *vyavakalana* — subtraction
- *Tirthaji* — Bharati Krishna Tirthaji (1884–1960), compiler of the 16 sūtras

## Materials checklist (whole module)

- Whiteboards / scrap paper for live drills
- Stopwatch or wall clock (one per pair is enough)
- A class wall-poster of the Nikhilam sūtra in IAST + English
- Pretend currency (paper notes denominated in 10, 100, 500, 1000) for the shopkeeper activity
- Printed practice sheets — complement drills, subtraction drills, mixed sets
- Optional: a calculator (used *only* for self-checking)
