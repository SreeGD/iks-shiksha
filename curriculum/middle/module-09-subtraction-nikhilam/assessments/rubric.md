# Project Rubric — Module 9 Nikhilam Subtraction (Middle)

**Project marks:** 30 (out of 40 with presentation 10).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Method demonstrated correctly** | Method shown completely (digit-wise complement, padding, verification); at least 3 worked examples with no errors. | Method shown completely; minor errors on 1 example. | Method shown but with a conceptual gap (e.g. forgetting the "last from 10" rule). | Method incorrect or absent. | × 2 |
| **Use of sources** | Cites Tirthaji (1965) AND one supporting source (PDF / textbook) correctly. | Cites at least one source correctly. | Mentions a source vaguely. | No source cited. | × 1.5 |
| **Audience suitability** | Pitched precisely to the target audience (Grade 4 for video; real shopkeeper for chart; CS-curious peer for poster). | Mostly suitable; minor tonal issues. | Suitable in places, off in others. | Inaccessible for target audience. | × 1.5 |
| **Originality** | The project shows the pair's own thinking — a unique scenario, a custom narrative, or a new framing. | Clearly the pair's own work. | Largely repeated from class examples. | Copied verbatim from a source. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals, layout, narration are clear, deliberate, polished. No arithmetic errors shown. | Communicates clearly. 1–2 craft issues. | Message comes through with effort. | Hard to follow. | × 1 |

Maximum: 28. Project score = (sum × weights) × 30 / 28.

## Presentation (10 marks)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (3 min)** | 2:45–3:00 | 2:30–3:15 | 2:15–3:30 | Outside | × 1 |
| **Both partners contribute** | Equal | Both speak unequally | One dominates | One silent | × 1 |
| **Clarity** | Confident, audible | Audible, mostly clear | Audible only in front | Mostly inaudible | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 10.

## Format-specific notes

- **Shopkeeper's quick-change chart:** the chart itself is the artefact; the 3-min presentation walks through one real scenario, end-to-end, with pretend currency. The chart should be usable WITHOUT the presenter present.
- **60-second teaching video:** the video itself is the artefact; the 3-min presentation explains design choices ("we chose this example because..."). The video must show at least one full Nikhilam subtraction with verification.
- **Two's-complement explainer poster:** the poster is the artefact; the 3-min presentation walks the audience through the binary-base 2 parallel. Should reference both Tirthaji and a CS source.

## Honesty bonus (+2)

Up to 2 bonus marks for projects that explicitly address the historicity question — i.e. correctly state that the method is in Tirthaji (1965) and that the deeper Vedic provenance is unverified. This is rewarded because it models the intellectual stance we want students to take.

(Bonus capped: total project + presentation cannot exceed 40.)
