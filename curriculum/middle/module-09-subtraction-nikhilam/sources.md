# Sources — Module 9 Nikhilam Subtraction (Middle)

The **vidya-karana RAG** does not cover Vedic-math sources. This module draws directly from SutraGanita's source corpus (`/Users/sree/Projects/SutraGanita/content/`) and from the published Tirthaji corpus.

## Primary source

**Tirthaji, Bharati Krishna.** *Vedic Mathematics* (1965), Motilal Banarsidass.

- Sūtra #2 of the 16: ***Nikhilaṁ Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ*** — "all from nine and the last from ten."
- The sūtra is used in Tirthaji's book for both subtraction (the focus of this module) and a multiplication trick (out of scope here; covered in a future module).
- Cite as: *Tirthaji, Vedic Mathematics (1965), sūtra Nikhilam (#2).*

## Historical and critical context (teacher reference)

- **Plofker, Kim.** *Mathematics in India* (2009), Princeton University Press. Background on classical Indian arithmetic and the broader history into which Tirthaji's 20th-century synthesis fits. No verse from Plofker is quoted to students at Middle band.
- **Dani, S. G.** "Myths and Reality: On Vedic Mathematics," *Frontline* (1993), reprinted in *Resonance* (October 2001). The scholarly critique of Tirthaji's "Vedic" provenance claim. Senior-band reading; Middle-band teachers should know it exists.

## Honest provenance note (for the teacher)

The Nikhilam *sūtra itself* is a real entry in Tirthaji's 1965 list. Tirthaji presents it as Vedic; no other Sanskritist has produced a manuscript of the *Atharva-veda Pariśiṣṭa* he claims to be drawing from. Treat the sūtra and its method as a **20th-century synthesis** that may well draw on older Indian mental-arithmetic traditions but whose direct Vedic attribution is unverified.

What is *definitely* true:

- The complement-based subtraction technique (in some form) has parallels in earlier Indian arithmetic and in 19th–20th-century European systems (e.g. Trachtenberg).
- The Sanskrit phrasing and the systematic packaging into 16 sūtras is Tirthaji's contribution.
- The method works. The history is just younger than the name suggests.

At Middle band we teach the method enthusiastically, name the sūtra accurately, and don't oversell its antiquity. The Senior band module unpacks the historicity question in detail.

## What to tell students about the citation chain

When students cite a fact about the Nikhilam method in their project, the citation looks like:

> *Tirthaji, Vedic Mathematics (1965), sūtra Nikhilam.*

When they cite a worked example from a workbook PDF, the citation looks like:

> *FUNDAMENTAL AND VEDIC MATHEMATICS .pdf*, Nikhilam chapter.

When they cite the binary two's-complement parallel (Day 7), they may cite any introductory computer-science text; we suggest:

> *Patterson & Hennessy, Computer Organization and Design (any edition)*, two's-complement section.

This citation chain is the standard for math modules in this curriculum.
