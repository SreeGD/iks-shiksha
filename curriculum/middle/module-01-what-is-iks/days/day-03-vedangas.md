# Day 3 — The Six Vedangas

**Module:** What is IKS? (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students name the six Vedāṅgas (auxiliary disciplines) and match each to its function in preserving and applying Vedic knowledge.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Matching-game cards (6 names + 6 functions, shuffled)

## Vocabulary

- *Vedāṅga* — "limb of the Veda"
- *vyākaraṇa* — "grammar; analysis"
- *jyotiṣa* — "luminary; astronomy/astrology"

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Daily prompt + recall: 2 students name the 4 Vedas + 4 layers from yesterday.

### Core (20 min)

1. **The Six Vedāṅgas** (write on board, then build each row):

   | # | Sanskrit | Function | Modern equivalent |
   |---|----------|----------|---------------------|
   | 1 | *Śikṣā* | Phonetics, pronunciation | Linguistics / phonology |
   | 2 | *Kalpa* | Ritual procedure | Liturgy |
   | 3 | *Vyākaraṇa* | Grammar (peaks in Pāṇini ~4th c. BCE) | Formal grammar / parsing |
   | 4 | *Nirukta* | Etymology | Lexicography |
   | 5 | *Chandas* | Prosody (poetic metre) | Poetics |
   | 6 | *Jyotiṣa* | Astronomy / time-keeping | Astronomy + astrology |

2. **Why they exist.** The Vedas had to be transmitted PRECISELY, orally, across millennia. Six disciplines grew up around the texts to ensure correct pronunciation, ritual use, grammatical understanding, etymological clarity, metrical recitation, and astronomical timing.

3. **One stand-out: Pāṇini's *Aṣṭādhyāyī* (~4th c. BCE)**.
   - ~4000 sūtras describing Sanskrit grammar.
   - Considered the first known formal grammar of any language.
   - Influenced modern formal-language theory (Chomsky's generative grammar acknowledges Pāṇinian inspiration).
   - A single Vedāṅga (vyākaraṇa) produced one of the most important works in the history of linguistics.

4. **One more stand-out: Jyotiṣa.**
   - The astronomy/astrology pair. Distinguish: jyotiṣa contains BOTH calculation (astronomy) and interpretation (astrology). Modern science separates these; the Indian tradition kept them together.

### Activity (15 min) — Matching game

Pairs. 12 cards (6 Sanskrit names + 6 function descriptions) shuffled face-down. Memory-match game. First pair done writes their match on the board.

Then class discusses: *"Which Vedāṅga surprised you most? Why?"*

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Exit ticket: name 3 of the 6 Vedāṅgas with their functions.
- Preview Day 4: *"Tomorrow — the Upavedas. Four APPLIED sciences."*

## Homework

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read about the Chomsky-Pāṇini connection — find 1 specific example of Pāṇinian influence on modern linguistics.
- **Tier down:** Memorise just 3 Vedāṅgas: Vyākaraṇa, Jyotiṣa, Kalpa.

## Teacher notes

- Students often confuse Jyotiṣa with horoscope-astrology. Distinguish: the discipline contained BOTH planetary calculation AND interpretive use. Modern science separates these.
- The "first formal grammar" claim about Pāṇini is mainstream scholarly view (Cardona, Kiparsky, etc.). Defensible.
