# Day 8 — Project Session 1: Research and Outline

**Module:** What is IKS? (Middle) · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Each student commits to a specific topic, drafts a thesis sentence, and outlines a 5-minute presentation.

## Materials

- Library books / curated reading list
- Project brief + rubric

## Flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

Brief: today's project work. Show the rubric on the wall. Cover the key criteria.

### Core sprints (35 min)

**Sprint 1 (10 min) — Thesis.** Each student writes:
- Working title
- One-sentence thesis (specific claim about their topic)
- 2 sources

Teacher rotates: 60 sec per student, asking *"What's the specific claim? Not 'X is interesting' — what specifically are you arguing?"*

**Sprint 2 (10 min) — Outline.** 5-slide / 5-section outline:
1. The topic + named practitioner
2. What it claimed / did
3. One specific source (primary verse, theorem, or text)
4. Modern view / parallel
5. Honest limitation (what the tradition didn't do)

**Sprint 3 (15 min) — Begin slides / notes.** Each student starts drafting their actual presentation content.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Plan submitted at door.
- Preview Day 9: *"Tomorrow — rehearse and refine."*

## Homework

- Continue drafting at home. Tomorrow is for *refining*, not starting.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add a counter-argument: "Some scholars dispute X" with citation.
- **Tier down:** Use the 5-section outline as your full structure.

## Teacher notes

- The "specific claim" filter is the most useful intervention. Many students start with "Indian math is interesting" — push to "Mādhava's series converges to π but converges slowly; here's why that mattered."
