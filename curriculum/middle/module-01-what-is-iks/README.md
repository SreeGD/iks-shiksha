# Module 1 — What is IKS?

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Define** what "Indian Knowledge Systems" (IKS) refers to and identify three distinct strands within it.
2. **Recall** the four Vedas, the six Vedangas, and the four Upavedas with one-line descriptions.
3. **Name** at least 3 Indian contributions to mathematics (zero, decimal place-value, infinite series), 3 to astronomy (Aryabhata's heliocentric hint, calculation of pi, Sūrya-siddhānta), and 3 to medicine (Ayurveda, Sushruta's surgery, herbal pharmacopoeia).
4. **Distinguish** what IKS includes (intellectual traditions) from what it does NOT include (religious doctrine alone, supernatural claims as science).
5. **Construct** a 5-minute presentation on one strand of IKS that includes a primary-source reference, a modern parallel, and a fair statement of what the tradition does AND does not claim.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with general world history at Grade 6 level.
- Basic Sanskrit term reading (transliteration acceptable, IAST taught here).

## Key vocabulary

- *Veda* — "knowledge"; four main collections (Ṛg, Yajur, Sāma, Atharva)
- *Vedānga* — "limb of the Veda"; six auxiliary disciplines
- *Upaveda* — "subordinate Veda"; four applied sciences (Ayurveda, Dhanurveda, Gandharvaveda, Arthaśāstra)
- *darśana* — "view"; the six classical philosophical systems
- *śāstra* — "treatise"; a systematic body of knowledge
