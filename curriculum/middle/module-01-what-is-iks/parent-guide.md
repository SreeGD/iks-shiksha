# Parent Guide — Module 1: What is IKS?

**Your child's age:** Middle (Grades 6–8) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## What your child is learning

The map of Indian Knowledge Systems — what's actually in there, beyond yoga and curry:

- **Vedas** — the oldest layer; ritual hymns + early linguistic and astronomical observation.
- **Vedangas** — six auxiliary disciplines (phonetics, ritual, grammar, etymology, prosody, astronomy).
- **Upavedas** — four applied sciences: **Ayurveda** (medicine), **Dhanurveda** (martial arts), **Gandharvaveda** (music), **Arthaśāstra** (governance/economics).
- Specific Indian contributions: **zero**, decimal place-value, infinite series (Mādhava, 14th c.), planetary calculations (Sūrya-siddhānta), Sushruta's surgical procedures (~6th c. BCE), Pāṇini's formal grammar (~4th c. BCE).

## What this module is — and isn't

**Is:** An honest, specific introduction to the *intellectual traditions* developed on the Indian subcontinent. With dates, names, and primary-source references.

**Isn't:** Religious instruction. Nor a competition with modern science. Nor uncritical promotion of "everything Indian came first."

The framing we model:
- Real achievements, specifically named, with what they got right AND what they got wrong.
- Tradition presented as historical context for ongoing inquiry.
- Modern science included as a partner, not a rival.

## Project (Days 8–10)

Your child picks ONE strand (math, astronomy, medicine, music, architecture, etc.) and gives a 5-minute presentation. Required:
- A named practitioner (Aryabhata, Sushruta, Pāṇini, etc.).
- One primary or scholarly source citation.
- A fair statement of what the tradition does AND does not claim.

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Ask your child to teach you ONE strand. The act of teaching cements the learning.
- Pull a relevant book from your shelf if you have one — *Mathematics in India* (Plofker), *The Roots of Ayurveda* (Wujastyk), *Light on Yoga* (Iyengar).
- For the project: be a thoughtful skeptic. Ask "where is the evidence for that?"

**Not helpful:**
- Saying "ancient Indians invented everything." This isn't what we teach.
- Saying "all that is just myth." This also isn't what we teach.
- Doing the project. The research IS the learning.

## What success looks like

- Your child can list the four Vedas, the six Vedangas, and the four Upavedas with one-line descriptions.
- They can name at least one specific Indian contribution in each of: mathematics, astronomy, medicine.
- They can articulate what IKS includes and what it doesn't.
- They give a confident 5-minute presentation with citations.
