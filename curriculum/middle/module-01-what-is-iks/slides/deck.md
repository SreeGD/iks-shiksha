# What does "Indian Knowledge" mean?

Not just religion.
Not "Indians knew everything first."
Not pseudoscience.

It IS the intellectual traditions of the Indian subcontinent over ~3000 years.

---

# The map

```
Vedas (4) → Vedangas (6) → Upavedas (4)
```

Plus: 6 darśanas (philosophical systems), classical sciences, modern syntheses.

---

# The Four Vedas

| Veda | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Ṛg | Hymns (~1500–1200 BCE) |
| Yajur | Ritual formulas |
| Sāma | Chants & melody |
| Atharva | Spells; early medicine |

Each has 4 layers: Saṃhitā → Brāhmaṇa → Āraṇyaka → Upaniṣad.

---

# The Six Vedangas

1. Śikṣā — phonetics
2. Kalpa — ritual
3. **Vyākaraṇa — grammar (Pāṇini, ~4th c. BCE)**
4. Nirukta — etymology
5. Chandas — prosody
6. Jyotiṣa — astronomy

---

# Pāṇini's *Aṣṭādhyāyī*

~4000 sūtras describing Sanskrit grammar.

Widely considered the **first formal grammar of any language**.

Influenced 20th-century formal-language theory (Chomsky).

---

# Indian Mathematics — three contributions

1. **Zero as a number** — Bakhshali manuscript, ~7th c. CE.
2. **Decimal place-value** — 6th c. CE; reached Europe ~12th c.
3. **Infinite series for π** — Mādhava, ~14th c. CE.

Mādhava: π = 4(1 − 1/3 + 1/5 − 1/7 + ...)

300 years before "Leibniz series."

---

# Indian Astronomy — three contributions

1. **Aryabhata (~500 CE)** — earth rotates on its axis; π to 4 decimals.
2. **Sūrya-siddhānta** — planetary calculations; earth's circumference to ~1%.
3. **Varāhamihira** — compared 5 astronomical systems including Greek.

---

# What Aryabhata did NOT do

Did not propose heliocentrism (that's Copernicus, 1500s).

He proposed *the earth rotates on its axis* — a different (and earlier) insight.

Distinguish.

---

# What Indian math/astronomy did NOT do

- No telescope (no one had one until 17th c. anywhere).
- No formal calculus (though Mādhava came very close).
- No heliocentrism in any sustained form.

Both lists matter.
