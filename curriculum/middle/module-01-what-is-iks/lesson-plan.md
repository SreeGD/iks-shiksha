## Pacing

- Days 1–4 are survey-heavy. Don't go deep — give the map.
- Days 6–7 zoom in on math & astronomy specifically (depth on demand).
- Day 10 needs ~6 min per student (5 talk + 1 Q&A). 12+ students = book a second period.

## Daily warm-up

Open with the question: *"What's one Indian-origin idea you encountered yesterday outside this class?"* (Yoga, zero in math homework, an Ayurvedic ingredient in family cooking, an Indian-named star, etc.) Two students share.

## What students leave with

- A vocabulary for talking about IKS as *intellectual traditions* (not religion alone).
- The tree of Vedas → Vedangas → Upavedas as a mental map.
- Specific named achievements (zero, Aryabhata's earth's rotation, Sushruta's surgery) with dates.
- One well-researched 5-minute presentation.
