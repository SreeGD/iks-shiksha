# Project Brief — Module 1 (Middle)

**Type:** 5-minute presentation · **Marks:** 20 · **Due:** Day 10

## What you'll do

Pick ONE strand of Indian Knowledge Systems. Give a 5-minute presentation that includes:

1. **Named practitioner** — a real person (Aryabhata, Pāṇini, Suśruta, Mādhava, Bhāskara II, Kauṭilya, Caraka, Bharata, ...).
2. **Specific claim** — not "X is great" but "X did Y in year Z."
3. **One source** — primary (verse, theorem, text) or scholarly (Plofker, Wujastyk, Pollock).
4. **Modern parallel or use** — what does this connect to today?
5. **Honest limitation** — what did the tradition NOT do?

## Format

- 5 min spoken + 1 min Q&A.
- Slides optional; if used, 3–5 slides max.
- Handout welcome.

## Rubric (see `rubric.md`)

Conceptual accuracy (×2) · Source quality (×1.5) · Specific claim (×1.5) · Honest limitation (×1) · Communication (×1).

## Self-reflection (submit on Day 10)

In 4–5 sentences:
1. What did you set out to research?
2. What surprised you?
3. With 3 more days, what would you research deeper?
