# Teacher Notes — Module 1 (Middle)

## Posture

This is the introductory module of the IKS curriculum. The student's first exposure shapes everything that follows. The framing matters:

> **IKS = the intellectual traditions developed on the Indian subcontinent over ~3000 years.**
>
> It includes science, mathematics, astronomy, linguistics, philosophy, medicine, music, architecture.
>
> It is NOT only religion. It is NOT magical thinking dressed up as science. It is NOT a competitor to modern science — it is a body of work that *predates* and *coexists with* modern science.

Push back gently against two failure modes:
- **Triumphalism:** "Indians knew everything before everyone else." Reframe: real, specific achievements documented carefully; not a competition with other traditions.
- **Dismissal:** "It's all religion / outdated / pseudoscience." Reframe: distinct intellectual traditions with rigorous methods (e.g., Pāṇini's grammar, classical Sanskrit poetics, Sushruta's surgical procedures).

## Common misconceptions

1. **"The Vedas are religious texts."** Partly true — the Saṃhitā portions contain ritual hymns. But the Vedas also contain the earliest layer of Indian linguistic and astronomical observation. Distinguish the layers.
2. **"Vedic mathematics is from the Vedas."** No — Tirthaji's 1965 system is named for the Vedas but isn't from them. (Module 5 covers this in detail.)
3. **"Indian astronomy is just astrology."** Different. Sūrya-siddhānta (early CE) is a serious astronomical text with planetary calculations. Astrology (jyotiṣa, the Vedanga) and astronomy share roots but diverge.
4. **"Aryabhata was wrong about the sun."** Aryabhata proposed the earth rotates on its axis (correct) and computed orbital periods (mostly correct). He didn't propose heliocentrism (Copernicus did, ~1100 years later) — distinguish carefully.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Read selections from Bhaskara II's *Līlāvatī* in translation. Compare with Newton-era European mathematics.
- **Tier down:** Focus on Day 1–4 (the survey). The deep math/astronomy can be skimmed.

## Sensitivity

- Some students will come from families that read these texts devotionally. The classroom frame is *both/and* — religious reading is valid AND we study them here as historical intellectual sources.
- Some students will be skeptical that "Indian" is a meaningful category given internal diversity. Acknowledge: the syncretic, multi-traditional reality is real. "IKS" is a convenient label for a fuzzy set, not a sharply-bounded category.

## Project oversight

- Strong topics are SPECIFIC: "Aryabhata's claim that the earth rotates — what evidence did he have, what did he get wrong?"
- Weak topics: "Indian mathematics is great." Push for specificity.
