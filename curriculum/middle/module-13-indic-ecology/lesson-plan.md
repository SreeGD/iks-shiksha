## Pacing notes

- Days 1–4 are introductory and observation-heavy. If a class is unfamiliar with Module 2, spend 5 extra minutes on Day 2 reviewing the *pañcabhūta*.
- Day 5 (Gītā 3.10–14) and Day 7 (Īśa Up. mantra 1) involve student reading of short translated verses. Print them in advance; do not ask students to read Sanskrit cold.
- Day 8 (climate change) is content-heavy. If your class needs more, split it across Day 7's final 10 min and a full Day 8.
- Day 9's walk depends on weather and your campus context. If the walk is impossible, run a "virtual walk" using photos from the past month — outcome is the same.

## Suggested running theme

Open each class with the same question on the board: **"Who in nature gave you something today, and did you give anything back?"** Two students share for 30 seconds each. This is the *yajña* habit in plain words.

## Formative vs. summative weight

- Formative quizzes (Days 3 and 5) — diagnostic, not graded. Hand back within 24 hours.
- Summative quiz (Day 10) — see rubric in `assessments/rubric.md`. Combined with project, total 40 marks.

## What students leave the module with

- Three Sanskrit phrases in their own handwriting, with meaning: *vasudhaiva kuṭumbakam*, *yajña*, *īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvam*.
- A drawn or written record of one local environmental observation.
- One specific small action — planted, signed, repaired, repeated, or refused.
- The habit of noticing what they take from the environment and what they give back.
