## Primary scriptural sources

| Citation | Used in | Content |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Mahā Upaniṣad 6.71–73** | Day 1, sources page | The *vasudhaiva kuṭumbakam* verse. The full half-line reads "*ayaṁ nijaḥ paro veti gaṇanā laghu-cetasām / udāra-caritānāṁ tu vasudhaiva kuṭumbakam*" — "this is mine, that is theirs — such reckoning is for the small-minded. For those whose conduct is large, the whole earth is one family." Variants of the second half also appear in the *Hitopadeśa* prologue (1.3.71) and the *Pañcatantra*. The Upaniṣadic source is the earliest. |
| **Bhagavad Gītā 3.10–14** | Day 5 *yajña* lesson | The *yajña-cakra* (cycle of sacrifice) verses. BG 3.14 in particular: "*annād bhavanti bhūtāni parjanyād anna-sambhavaḥ / yajñād bhavati parjanyo yajñaḥ karma-samudbhavaḥ*" — "Beings come from food, food from rain, rain from sacrifice, sacrifice from action." A four-stage circulation. |
| **Atharva Veda 12.1** — *Pṛthivī Sūkta* | Day 6 lesson | 63-verse hymn to Earth as mother. Key verse used: AV 12.1.12 — "*mātā bhūmiḥ putro ahaṁ pṛthivyāḥ*" — "Earth is the mother; I am the son of Earth." Also AV 12.1.1 (on Earth as supporter of *satya*, *ṛta*, *dīkṣā*, *tapas*) and AV 12.1.35 (the *kṣatra* / soil verse). |
| **Īśa Upaniṣad mantra 1** | Day 7 lesson | "*īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvaṁ yat kiñca jagatyāṁ jagat / tena tyaktena bhuñjīthā mā gṛdhaḥ kasya svid dhanam*" — "All this, whatever moves in this moving world, is to be regarded as enveloped by the Lord. By that renunciation, enjoy. Do not covet the wealth of anyone." Used as the non-greed ethic. |

## Secondary scholarly references

- **Gadgil, M. & Guha, R.** (1992). *This Fissured Land: An Ecological History of India*. Delhi: Oxford University Press. — Landmark scholarly work. Argues that India's ecological history is more complex than either the "ancient Indians lived in harmony with nature" narrative or its reverse. Used for the historical caveat in Day 4 and teacher notes.
- **Gadgil, M. & Vartak, V. D.** (1976). "The Sacred Groves of Western Ghats in India." *Economic Botany* 30(2): 152–160. — Foundational survey of *devarakāḍu* groves as community biodiversity reserves.
- **Chapple, C. K. & Tucker, M. E. (eds.)** (2000). *Hinduism and Ecology: The Intersection of Earth, Sky, and Water*. Harvard University Press. — Anthology; useful for teacher background.
- **IPCC** (2023). *AR6 Synthesis Report: Climate Change 2023*. — Source for Day 8 figures (global warming since 1850; 1.5°C threshold; carbon-budget framing).
- **United Nations** (2015). *Transforming Our World: the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development* (Sustainable Development Goals). — Mentioned in Day 8 as the modern policy framework.

## Editions used for the primary sources

- *Mahā Upaniṣad* — text per the Adyar Library edition (one of the 108 Upaniṣads enumerated in the *Muktikā* canon). The phrase is the same across all printed editions.
- *Bhagavad Gītā* — text per the critical edition used in standard Sanskrit pedagogy. BG 3.14 is uncontroversial across all editions.
- *Atharva Veda 12.1* — text per the Vulgate (Śaunaka) recension; verse numbers per Whitney's translation (Harvard Oriental Series, 1905).
- *Īśa Upaniṣad* — text per the Mādhyandina recension; identical to the version embedded in Śukla Yajur Veda 40.

## What we did NOT cite

We deliberately did not cite popular but contested attributions:
- The "Vasudhaiva Kuṭumbakam" line is sometimes attributed to the *Pañcatantra* prologue alone; the earliest *scriptural* source is the Mahā Upaniṣad, and we lead with that.
- Several internet sources cite a "Vedic environmental ethics" passage that does not exist as a single verse. Day 6 covers the *Pṛthivī Sūkta* (which is real and 63 verses long) instead.
