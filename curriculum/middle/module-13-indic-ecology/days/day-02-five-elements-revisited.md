# Day 2 — Five Elements Revisited

**Module:** Indic Ecology · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min

## Learning objective

Students re-state the five elements from Module 2, and re-frame each one as a "family member" of the ecological family — so the *vasudhaiva kuṭumbakam* ethic gets concrete, element by element.

## Materials

- Whiteboard
- Each student's Module 2 notebook (if available); otherwise a five-row table on the board
- Five posters or sheets, one per element, labelled in Sanskrit + English
- Sticky notes from Day 1

## Vocabulary

(All from Module 2 — review, do not introduce as new.)

- *pṛthvī* (earth / solid)
- *ap* / *jala* (water / liquid)
- *tejas* / *agni* (fire / heat / light)
- *vāyu* (air / gas / movement)
- *ākāśa* (space)

Add today:
- *bhūta-saṁskāra* (IAST: bhūta-saṁskāra; lit. "element-care") — a working term we use for "the care we owe each element." This is not a classical compound; we use it as a teaching shorthand.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min)

- Read 3–4 anonymous exit slips from yesterday — students' "extended family" examples.
- Quick chorus: *"What were the five elements from Module 2?"* — class names them in order.

### Core (20 min)

1. **Re-draw the five-element chart on the board:**

   | Element | English | One thing it does for us | One way we pollute it |
   |---------|---------|--------------------------|------------------------|
   | *pṛthvī* | earth / solid | Soil for food, ground to stand on | Plastic waste, chemical fertiliser |
   | *ap* | water | Drink, wash, irrigate | Sewage, industrial effluent |
   | *tejas* | fire / heat / light | Cook, warm, illuminate | Burning fossil fuels (CO₂) |
   | *vāyu* | air | Breath, wind, weather | Vehicle exhaust, factory smoke |
   | *ākāśa* | space | Room to live, transmit sound | Noise, light pollution, satellite debris |

2. **The reframe.** *"In Module 2 we learned the five elements as categories of matter. Today we look at the same five as members of the family."* Each element gives us something; we owe each one something back.

3. **Why this is an ecological lens.** Modern environmental science uses categories like "atmosphere," "hydrosphere," "lithosphere," "biosphere," etc. The five-element framework is a sensory, everyday version of the same idea. It says: *the place you live is made of these five. Notice them. Care for each.*

   > "Earth, water, fire, air, and sky — these five great elements (*pañca-mahābhūta*) sustain all beings."
   > — paraphrase from Bhāgavata Purāṇa Canto 3.

   (Cited as paraphrase to satisfy Middle-band citation rule; the verse cluster is SB 3.26.40–51 covered in Module 2.)

4. **The "give back" idea.** *"What does a family member do for another? They give, they take, and they don't pollute each other's spaces. We'll come back to this on Day 5 when we read the Bhagavad Gītā on the *yajña* cycle."*

### Activity (15 min) — Element-as-family poster

- Group of 4. Each group gets one element-poster.
- They fill in:
  - **One concrete way this element gives to us, near our school.** (E.g. *vāyu*: the breeze through the open windows.)
  - **One concrete way we take from this element, near our school.** (E.g. *vāyu*: the parents' cars idling outside the gate.)
  - **One concrete way to give back to this element, near our school.** (E.g. *vāyu*: ask parents to switch off engines during pick-up.)
- Posters go up on the wall. They stay up for the rest of the module.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- **Exit ticket.** Each student writes on a slip: *"The element I will pay closest attention to this week is _______, because _______."*
- Preview Day 3: *"Tomorrow we look at the element that has had the most religious attention in India — water, especially rivers."*

## Student-facing content

> **The five elements as family members.**
>
> | Element | Gives us | We owe it |
> |---------|----------|-----------|
> | *pṛthvī* (earth) | Food, ground, minerals | Less waste, less concrete, more compost |
> | *ap* (water) | Drink, growth, cleanness | Less waste, less pollution |
> | *tejas* (fire / energy) | Warmth, light, cooking | Less fossil fuel, more efficiency |
> | *vāyu* (air) | Breath, weather | Less burning, fewer engines |
> | *ākāśa* (space) | Room to live, sound to travel | Less noise, less light at night, less stuff |
>
> The five elements are not five things "out there." They are five members of the family the verse calls *kuṭumba*.

## Homework

- Walk for 5 minutes anywhere near your home. Notice ONE specific way one element is being mistreated (the dump, the dust, the noise, the leaking tap). Write 3 sentences. Bring tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **One tier up:** Add a sixth row to the table for "biological diversity" (the *jīva-jantu* — living creatures). Is biodiversity a sixth element? Or is it already covered by *pṛthvī*? Defend either position.
- **One tier down:** Stick to two elements that feel obvious to you (e.g. water and air). The others will sink in over the week.

## Teacher notes

- Some students (correctly) will say "polluting space doesn't make sense — space is just space." Push: *"Have you ever been kept awake by a neighbour's loud TV? Have you been blinded by a glaring street light? That's polluting *ākāśa*."* The five-element frame's strength is that it forces us to think about subtle pollutions too.
- Don't let "fire" mean "we should stop using electricity." That's not the point. The point is that the *tejas* we use is currently mostly from burning fossil fuels; the family relationship is to use less or use cleaner.
- Some students will ask about climate change here. Hold them off — Day 8 is the proper place. Today is the framework, not the data.
