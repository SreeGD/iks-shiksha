## Opening hook · 5 min

Project the verse: *"vasudhaiva kuṭumbakam"* — *"the Earth itself is one family."* (Mahā Upaniṣad 6.71-73.) *"This is the framework. Today we look at the practices — sacred groves, yajña, ahiṁsā — that came from it."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**Indic ecology framework:**

1. **Vasudhaiva Kuṭumbakam** (Mahā Upaniṣad 6.71-73): the Earth is one family. Universal moral inclusion.

2. **Sacred groves (devarakāḍu, sarpa-kāvu):** community-protected forests, ~13,000 documented across India. Biodiversity hotspots, water-table protection.

3. **Yajña (Bhagavad Gītā 3.10-14):** the ecological cycle — every taking is part of a give-and-take with the larger system.

4. **Ahiṁsā:** non-harming, extended to non-human life.

5. **Five elements (pañcabhūta):** ecological substrate — air, water, fire, earth, space. Pollution = imbalance.

**Climate context:** these are frameworks. Practice was uneven. Don't romanticise.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

**Local sacred-grove research.** Pairs research one named sacred grove (e.g., Mawphlang in Meghalaya, Sarguja in Chhattisgarh, Karnataka coast devarakāḍu). 5 minutes. Quick presentation.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Paraphrase Mahā Upaniṣad 6.71-73: *"The narrow-minded ask 'is this mine or yours?' For the noble-minded, the whole Earth is one family."*

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Local environmental project pitch.** Pairs design one local environmental action: tree planting, river cleanup, school waste reduction. Present.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"Which Indic framework most surprises you? Which would you adapt to your daily life?"*
