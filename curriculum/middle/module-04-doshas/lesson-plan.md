## Pacing notes

- Days 1–3 are content-heavy. Day 4's sort activity is the consolidation. If the class needs more time on Days 1–2, swap Day 4 with a recap day and push the sort to Day 5.
- Day 6 (six tastes) is the lightest day; can be compressed if needed to make room for slower learners on Day 3 self-observation.
- Day 7 (safety + scope) is **not optional**. If you only have 6 days, fold it into Day 5.

## Suggested running theme

Open each class with: **"What did your body notice yesterday?"** — 2 students share for 30 seconds each. Students need not use *doṣa* vocabulary at first; by Day 5 they will.

## Formative vs. summative weight

- Formative quizzes (Days 3 and 5) — diagnostic only, not graded.
- Summative quiz (Day 10) — 25 marks. Combined with project (30 marks) and presentation (5 marks) = 40 total.

## What students leave the module with

- A *tridoṣa* table they built themselves, in their notebook.
- An informal sense of their own *prakṛti* tendency — held lightly, not as identity.
- A 5-day "doṣa-aware week" plan they actually wrote.
- The skill to spot when "lifestyle observation" is being misused as "diagnosis."
