# Module 4 (Middle) — Formative Quizzes

Two parts: **Part A** on Day 3 (after the self-observation lesson), **Part B** on Day 5 (after the doṣa clock). Both are diagnostic — not graded. Return with comments within 24 hours.

---

## Part A — Day 3 (5 min, 6 questions)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** Write the three *doṣa* names in IAST.

a) ____________  b) ____________  c) ____________

**2.** Which element pair makes *pitta*?

a) *ākāśa* + *vāyu*
b) *tejas* + *ap*
c) *ap* + *pṛthvī*
d) *vāyu* + *pṛthvī*

**3.** Fill in the blank: "*Prakṛti* is your __________, while *vikṛti* is your current state."

**4.** True or false: A person who scores heavily on *vāta* in a self-observation worksheet is diagnosed as a *vāta* type.   (T / F)

**5.** Which *doṣa* is associated with the qualities *heavy, slow, cool*?

_______________________

**6.** (Short answer — one sentence) In your own words, why do most people score across more than one *doṣa* column in the self-observation worksheet?

___________________________________________________________

---

### Part A — answer key

| Q | Answer | Notes |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | *vāta, pitta, kapha* | All three required; any order. |
| 2 | b) *tejas* + *ap* | — |
| 3 | baseline / constitution (or equivalent) | Accept "your born nature," "your default." |
| 4 | F | The worksheet identifies a *tendency*, not a diagnosis. |
| 5 | *kapha* | — |
| 6 | Any answer conveying that the body uses all three *doṣa*s simultaneously and pure single-*doṣa* constitution is rare. | Award 1 mark for reasonable reasoning. |

## Part B — Day 5 (8 min, 6 questions, mixed format)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** (1 mark) Which *doṣa* dominates the window **10 am – 2 pm**?

_______________________

**2.** (2 marks) Complete the table.

| Window | Time | Dominant doṣa |
|--------|------|----------------|
| 1 | 6 am – 10 am | _____________ |
| 2 | 10 am – 2 pm | _____________ |
| 3 | 2 pm – 6 pm | _____________ |

**3.** (1 mark) True or false: According to the *doṣa* day-clock, each *doṣa* appears once in a 24-hour period.   (T / F)

**4.** (2 marks) Give ONE quality of *vāta* and ONE quality of *kapha*.

*Vāta* quality: ___________  *Kapha* quality: ___________

**5.** (1 mark) Name the modern science field that studies how the body cycles through the day.

_______________________

**6.** (1 mark — short answer) What is ONE difference between the *doṣa* day-clock and modern chronobiology?

___________________________________________________________

---

### Part B — answer key

| Q | Answer | Mark scheme |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | *pitta* | 1 |
| 2 | *kapha* / *pitta* / *vāta* (in that order) | 2 if all correct; 1 if two correct. |
| 3 | F (each *doṣa* appears twice — once day, once night) | 1 |
| 4 | *Vāta*: any of dry, cold, mobile, light, rough. *Kapha*: any of heavy, slow, cool, oily, stable. | 2 (1 each) |
| 5 | Chronobiology (or circadian biology) | 1 |
| 6 | Any reasonable difference: e.g., "the *doṣa* clock uses Ayurvedic categories; chronobiology uses measurable hormones like cortisol and melatonin" — or "the clock windows don't match precisely with laboratory-measured cycles." | 1 for a sensible contrast. |

Total Part B: 8 marks. Not added to summative.

---

## What to do with the results

- Class average below 5/8 on Part B = reteach the *doṣa* clock and *prakṛti / vikṛti* distinction before Day 6.
- Individual students below 3/8 = brief one-on-one on Day 7 before the safety activity begins.
- Q4 wrong across the majority = spend 5 minutes in Day 6 warm-up re-drilling the qualities table from Day 2.
