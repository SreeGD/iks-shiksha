# Activity 02 — Taste Station Rotation

**Module:** Doṣas · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 6 · **Time:** 20 min (within Day 6's 45 min)

## Type

Sensory rotation, paired

## Goal

Students sample all six *ṣaḍ-rasa* (six tastes), identify each in IAST + English, and predict which *doṣa* each calms.

## Materials per class

- Six labelled stations around the room. Each station has:
  - The sample (covered until needed)
  - A label: Sanskrit name + IAST + English
  - A worksheet question card
  - Toothpicks for sampling
  - A small bin for used toothpicks
- One glass of water + small cup per student for rinsing between stations
- Worksheet for each pair (template below)

## Suggested samples per station

| Station | Sanskrit | English | Sample | Backup if allergy |
|---------|----------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| 1 | *madhura* | sweet | a piece of jaggery / date / raisin | sugar cube |
| 2 | *amla* | sour | a wedge of lemon / a pinch of amla powder | a single grape |
| 3 | *lavaṇa* | salty | a few grains of rock salt | regular salt |
| 4 | *kaṭu* | pungent | a small piece of fresh ginger (let them smell) | a peppercorn (sniff only) |
| 5 | *tikta* | bitter | a methi leaf / a single neem leaf / dark chocolate (>70%) | bitter gourd slice |
| 6 | *kaṣāya* | astringent | a piece of unripe banana / a few sips of strong black tea | a pomegranate seed |

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Lay out 6 stations clearly spaced.
- Cover each sample with a small cloth or upside-down cup so students can't pre-judge.
- Pre-circulate a quick **allergy/dietary check** at least one day before. Note any students who must opt out of specific samples.
- Have water and rinse cups within easy reach of every station.

## Instructions (student-facing — printable handout)

> **Six Tastes — work in pairs. 2 minutes per station.**
>
> 1. Lift the cover. Look at the sample. Smell it.
> 2. **Either** taste a small bite (one toothpick-tip) **or** smell-only. No pressure to taste.
> 3. On the worksheet, write:
>    (a) the Sanskrit name in IAST + English
>    (b) one descriptor word (cooling, warming, sharp, mild, drying)
>    (c) which *doṣa* you'd predict it calms
>    (d) which *doṣa* you'd predict it aggravates
> 4. Rinse mouth between stations with the water.
> 5. Rotate clockwise to the next station.

## Answer key (for the debrief)

| Station | Calms | Aggravates | Note |
|---------|-------|------------|------|
| *madhura* (sweet) | vāta, pitta | kapha | grounding, heavy |
| *amla* (sour) | vāta | pitta, kapha | warming, slightly heating |
| *lavaṇa* (salty) | vāta | pitta, kapha | warming, water-retaining |
| *kaṭu* (pungent) | kapha | vāta, pitta | hot, sharp, drying |
| *tikta* (bitter) | pitta, kapha | vāta | cooling, light, drying |
| *kaṣāya* (astringent) | pitta, kapha | vāta | cool, dry, drawing-in |

## Discussion prompts (5 min after rotation)

- Which taste surprised you? Why?
- Which one tastes "Indian" most familiar? Why?
- Most plates from your home — how many of the six show up?
- The classical view: a *balanced meal* hits all six. What does your typical dinner miss?

## Safety / sensitivity

- **No forced tasting.** Smell-only is fine.
- **Allergy opt-out without question.** Especially for nuts (avoid), citrus (some are sensitive), neem (rare allergy possible).
- No comparison of students' choices. ("Why don't YOU taste it?" — disallow.)
- Diabetic students should not taste the sweet station — use a sugar-free alternative.
- Hand hygiene: hand-sanitiser before and after.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** For each taste, predict whether it's appropriate for hot summer noon vs cold winter morning. Defend.
- **Tier down:** Do three stations only — sweet, sour, salty. The other three can be smell-only.

## Clean-up

- Used toothpicks in the bin per station.
- Wipe stations.
- Recycle paper labels for next time.
