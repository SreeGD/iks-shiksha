# Activity 01 — Qualities Sort

**Module:** Doṣas · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 4 · **Time:** 25 min (within Day 4's 45 min)

## Type

Hands-on classification, paired

## Goal

Pairs sort 30 cards naming objects/qualities/activities into the three *doṣa* trays, with reasoned justification and visible disagreement on borderline cases.

## Materials per class

- 30 prepared cards (see card list below)
- 3 paper trays / chalk-circle on floor, each labelled with one *doṣa*
- A notebook per pair for *why* notes
- Sticky notes for disagreements

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Print or hand-write 30 cards.
- Lay out 3 trays in three corners of the room, each clearly labelled with a *doṣa* name in IAST + English.
- One pair starts at each tray with 10 random cards. Plan rotation so no two pairs hold the same card.

## Instructions (student-facing — printable handout)

> **Qualities Sort** — work in pairs.
>
> 1. Take 6 cards from the deck.
> 2. For each card, decide which *doṣa* it most affects (or *belongs to*). Place it on the matching tray.
> 3. In your notebook, write a 1-line *why* for each placement.
> 4. **Disagree with your partner?** Argue. Write both placements. Circle the one you finally vote for.
> 5. After 15 min, swap your full set with another pair. They check your placements.
> 6. **Gallery walk (last 5 min):** if you disagree with another pair's placement, write a sticky note explaining why (kindly).

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Both partners arguing kindly; pen on notebook; cards moving | "But it's heavy — it has to be kapha." / "Yes but it's also cold." / "Then it's mostly kapha with some vāta." |
| Needs intervention | One partner placing all the cards; rows filled with one word | Silence; "I don't know, write anything." |

## Safety / sensitivity

- No actual food handling (all cards). The food cards reference foods only by name.
- No comments on classmates' bodies, eating habits, or health.
- If a student feels singled out by a card ("heavy oily curry"), reframe immediately — we're sorting *qualities*, not people.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** After sorting, the pair picks 3 cards and writes a 1-paragraph defense for each — including *why the other two doṣas almost worked too*.
- **Tier down:** Sort just 10 cards across 3 doṣas. Skip the mixed ones.

## Clean-up

- Cards back in deck for next class.
- Worksheets stay in student notebooks for revision.
- Teacher takes a quick photograph of the 3 trays for the day-record.
