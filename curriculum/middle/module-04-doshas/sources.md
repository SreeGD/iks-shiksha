# Sources — Module 4 Doṣas (Middle)

## Primary classical sources

The two anchor texts for this module are the *Caraka Saṁhitā* and the *Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya*. Verse numbering below follows the editions in standard use in Indian Ayurvedic colleges.

| Citation | Used in | Content |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Caraka Saṁhitā, Sūtrasthāna 1.57** | Day 2, Day 7 | The three *doṣa*s — *vāta, pitta, śleṣman* (= *kapha*) — when in their natural state sustain the body; when vitiated, they cause harm. This is the source-of-record statement of the framework. |
| **Caraka Saṁhitā, Sūtrasthāna 1.58** | Day 2 (paraphrase) | Doṣa-dhātu-mala enumeration: three *doṣa*s, seven *dhātu*s (tissues), three *mala*s (wastes) constitute the *śarīra* (body). |
| **Caraka Saṁhitā, Sūtrasthāna 1.59** | Day 2 anchor paraphrase | *vāyuḥ pittaṁ kaphaścoktaḥ śārīro doṣasaṅgrahaḥ* — "Vāyu, pitta, and kapha — declared the summary of bodily *doṣa*s." Used as the Middle-band paraphrase citation. |
| **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya, Sūtrasthāna 1.6–7** | Day 5 | Vāgbhaṭa's compact formulation of the *doṣa-dhātu-mala* triad and the principle that the *doṣa*s have characteristic times of day, season, and life-stage. |
| **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya, Sūtrasthāna 1.8** | Day 7 | The principle that knowing the *doṣa*s in natural and vitiated states is the basis of medical science. |

## Modern science references (Day 5 and Day 7)

Standard middle-school biology and chronobiology references are sufficient. None of the references below are required student reading.

- **Chronobiology of digestion** — Panda, S. et al. (2012). "Time-restricted feeding without reducing caloric intake prevents metabolic diseases in mice fed a high-fat diet." *Cell Metabolism* 15. (For Day 5 — the modern observation that meal timing matters, independent of calories.)
- **Chronotypes (morningness/eveningness)** — Roenneberg, T. (2012). *Internal Time: Chronotypes, Social Jet Lag, and Why You're So Tired.* Harvard University Press. (Used as a modern parallel to *doṣa*-clock framing.)
- **Six tastes and modern dietetics** — Mooventhan, A. and Nivethitha, L. (2017). "Scientific evidence-based effects of hydrotherapy on various systems of the body." (Tangential — used only as a flag that some lifestyle claims are modestly supported by modern trials.)

## Why we don't quote certain commentators

This module avoids citation of the *Aṣṭāṅga Saṅgraha* (Vṛddha-Vāgbhaṭa) and Cakrapāṇi's commentary on Caraka because verse-numbering across editions varies more than the two anchor texts. The Middle band needs traceable citations.

## What students see vs what teachers see

- **Students** see the paraphrase *"the three doṣas — vāta, pitta, kapha — in their balanced state sustain the body; vitiated, they cause disease"* with the reference "Caraka Saṁhitā, Sūtrasthāna 1.57."
- **Teachers** have access to the full reference table and the `.rag-cache/README.md` build note.

## Note on transliteration

We use IAST throughout: *vāta* (not *vata*), *pitta*, *kapha*, *śleṣman* (the classical synonym for *kapha*), *prakṛti* (not *prakriti*), *vikṛti*. Where popular spellings differ, the glossary lists the common alternatives.
