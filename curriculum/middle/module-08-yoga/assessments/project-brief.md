# Project Brief — Module 8 Yoga (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Day 8 + Day 9 · **Showcase:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 40 (project 30 + presentation 10)

## The project

Each pair designs and leads a **5-minute yoga routine** for ONE chosen audience.

### Audience options (pick one)

| Audience | Suggested intent | Examples of fitting elements |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------------------|
| Peers | Energising — wake up the body before class | Standing poses, partial Sūrya Namaskāra |
| Parents (after a long workday) | Restorative — release back tension | Forward folds, child's pose, counted breath |
| Younger siblings (Grade 3–5) | Playful + engaging | Animal poses (cat, cobra, downward dog), narration |
| Classmates pre-exam | Calming — settle anxiety | Child's pose, seated breath, slow forward fold |

## Required elements

Every 5-minute routine must include:

1. **At least 3 āsanas** from the five foundational poses (or appropriate additions).
2. **One breath element** — either a counted-breath segment OR a clear breath cue woven through the asanas.
3. **One source citation** spoken aloud (verse, sūtra name, or PDF reference).
4. **An opening** (intention setting, 30 sec) and a **closing** (śavāsana or seated rest, 30 sec).

## Constraints

- **No inversions, advanced backbends, or breath retention.** Safety floor is non-negotiable.
- **5 min ± 30 seconds.** Practice with a timer.
- **Audience does the routine alongside you** — design for following, not for watching.

## Why this matters

You're moving from *learner* to *teacher*. The act of leading others through a practice forces you to understand it more deeply — to think about pace, cueing, audience experience, and your own breath.

## Working in pairs

- Pair work strongly encouraged.
- Individual allowed with teacher approval.
- Group of 3 only if the audience is "younger siblings" — three teachers can manage a small group of kids better than one.

## Citation rules

- One citation, spoken aloud.
- Acceptable formats:
  - "*This pose is mentioned in Bhagavad-gītā chapter 6, verse 13.*"
  - "*The eight limbs come from Patañjali's Yoga Sūtra, systematised around 2nd century BCE.*"
  - "*Our breath practice follows Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 4.8.44, which describes gradual purification through prāṇāyāma.*"
