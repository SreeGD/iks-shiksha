# Sources — Module 8 Yoga (Middle)

## Primary scriptural sources (from vidya-karana RAG)

| Citation | Used in | Content |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **SB 4.8.44** | Day 6 prāṇāyāma | "*prāṇāyāmena tṛ-vṛtā prāṇendṛya-mano-malam śanair vyudasyābhidhyāyen manasā guruṇā gurum*" — describes the use of regulated prāṇāyāma to gradually purify the senses and mind. |
| **SB 3 (purport)** | Day 2 eight limbs | Enumerates yoga's eight stages: *yama, niyama, āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra, dhāraṇā, dhyāna, samādhi*. |
| **BG Ch. 4** (verse on breath sacrifice, in cached chunks) | Day 6 prāṇāyāma | The Gītā mentions practitioners offering the outgoing breath into the incoming breath as a form of sacrifice (*apāne juhvati prāṇaṁ*, BG 4.29). |
| **BG Ch. 6** (dhyāna-yoga) | Day 1 hook, Day 7 physiology | Krishna instructs Arjuna on the seat, posture, gaze, and discipline of the meditator. Verses BG 6.11–13 specifically describe the seat and posture. |
| Veda, Secrets from the East (Prabhupāda) | Day 1, Day 2 | Discusses the Bhagavad-gītā's treatment of all eight limbs of rāja-yoga. |
| Journey of Self Discovery (Premnidhi Das) | Day 2 | Enumerates the eight-fold yoga process clearly. |

**Note on framing:** the vidya-karana corpus is predominantly devotional (Vaiṣṇava / Gauḍīya). The verses we cite are real and verifiable, but the *interpretation* in many of the chunked purports is devotional. In this curriculum we use the verses for the **historical-philosophical** anchor and present yoga to students through the **physiological-experiential** lens (this is the secular framing locked in by the style guide).

## Modern physiology references (Day 7)

- **Autonomic nervous system** — NCERT Class 11 Biology, Chapter 21 ("Neural Control and Coordination"); for Middle band, a simplified one-page reading is sufficient.
- **Vagal tone and breathing** — A simple resource: any introduction to "vagal tone" available in middle-school health curricula; the key point is that slow exhalations stimulate the parasympathetic ("rest-and-digest") response.
- **Posture and biomechanics** — Any introductory physical-education textbook on alignment, neutral spine, and balance.

## Practical yoga references (teacher background)

- Iyengar, B. K. S. (1966). *Light on Yoga*. — The reference text on āsana alignment. Use for teacher background on the five foundational āsanas; do NOT assign to students at Middle band.
- Saraswati, Swami Satyananda. (1969). *Āsana Prāṇāyāma Mudrā Bandha*. Bihar School of Yoga. — Comprehensive teacher reference; useful for safe modifications.

## Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras

Patañjali (c. 2nd century BCE – 4th century CE) is the systematiser of the eight-limbed yoga (*aṣṭāṅga-yoga*). The *Yoga Sūtras* are not in the vidya-karana corpus directly. For Middle band, the references via Bhagavad-gītā and the secondary sources above are sufficient. At Senior band, students read selected sūtras (especially YS 1.2 — *yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ*).

## What to tell students

The Indian yoga tradition is *many* traditions — Patañjali's eight-limbed (rāja yoga), the Gītā's three paths (karma, bhakti, jñāna), the modern postural-yoga lineage (Krishnamacharya → Iyengar / Pattabhi Jois). At Middle band we focus on the *physical* limbs (āsana, prāṇāyāma) as taught in modern schools, anchored in Patañjali's enumeration and supported by Bhagavad-gītā references. We are honest that this is one slice of a much broader tradition.
