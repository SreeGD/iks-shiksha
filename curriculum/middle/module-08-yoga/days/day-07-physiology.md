# Day 7 — Why It Works: Physiology Connection

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (10 min movement)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will explain at Middle-band level why slow breathing and forward folds shift the body toward "rest-and-digest" (parasympathetic) function, and why backbends and inversions can shift it toward "fight-or-flight" (sympathetic) activation.

## Materials

- Whiteboard.
- Optional: a simple diagram of the autonomic nervous system (sympathetic vs. parasympathetic).
- Worksheet: concept check at end.

## Vocabulary introduced today

- **Autonomic nervous system (ANS)** — the part of the nervous system that runs "automatically" (heartbeat, digestion, breathing).
- **Sympathetic** — "fight or flight" branch; raises heart rate, releases adrenaline.
- **Parasympathetic** — "rest and digest" branch; lowers heart rate, supports digestion.
- **Vagus nerve** — the main nerve carrying parasympathetic signals; long, complex, connected to heart, lungs, and digestive organs.

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (15 min) — Two modes of the body

1. **Two modes:** write on board, large.

   | "Fight or flight" (sympathetic) | "Rest and digest" (parasympathetic) |
   |----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
   | Heart rate up | Heart rate down |
   | Breath fast and shallow | Breath slow and deep |
   | Pupils dilate | Pupils contract |
   | Digestion stops | Digestion runs |
   | Muscles tense | Muscles soften |
   | Useful when | Useful when |
   | running from danger; exam panic | resting; eating; sleeping; healing |

2. **The breath is the lever.** Most ANS functions you can't consciously control — but you CAN control your breath. And breath signals back to the ANS.

3. **What slow exhalation does:** stimulates the vagus nerve. Vagus nerve signals "rest and digest" to the body. So slow exhales = parasympathetic activation = calm body.

4. **What this means for yoga:**
   - **Counted-breath prāṇāyāma** (Day 6) — directly down-regulates stress response.
   - **Forward folds and child's pose** — head below heart, parasympathetic-friendly. Calming.
   - **Backbends and inversions** — energising, more sympathetic activation. (We don't teach inversions at this band.)
   - **Sūrya Namaskāra** — mixed: opening (energising) + folding (calming). A balanced sequence.

### Practice (10 min) — Stress-recovery sequence

Demonstrate a short calming sequence the class can do before a stressful event:

1. **Tāḍāsana**, 30 sec.
2. **Uttānāsana** (standing forward fold), 30 sec — let the head hang.
3. **Balāsana** (child's pose), 60 sec.
4. **Sukhāsana**, counted breath (4 in, 4 out), 60 sec.
5. **Tāḍāsana** to close, 30 sec.

Total: 3.5 min. Practice once together. *"Use this before exams. Use this when you're stressed. It works because of physiology, not magic."*

### Concept check (10 min)

- 5-question worksheet:
  1. Which branch of the ANS is "fight or flight"?
  2. Which is "rest and digest"?
  3. Which nerve carries most of the parasympathetic signals?
  4. Does a slow exhale stimulate sympathetic or parasympathetic?
  5. Why does *balāsana* feel calming?

### Wrap-up (3 min)

- 2 student shares on the worksheet answers.
- Preview Day 8: *"Project work begins. You'll design your own 5-minute routine for your chosen audience."*

## Student-facing content

> **Why Yoga "Works" — The Physiology**
>
> Your body has two modes:
>
> | Sympathetic | Parasympathetic |
> |-------------|------------------|
> | "Fight or flight" | "Rest and digest" |
> | Fast heart, fast breath | Slow heart, slow breath |
>
> The **vagus nerve** carries the "rest" signals.
> **Slow exhalation stimulates the vagus nerve.**
>
> So when you breathe slowly OR fold forward OR rest in child's pose — your body shifts toward calm. Not magic; physiology.
>
> **A short stress-recovery sequence:**
>
> 1. Tāḍāsana — 30 sec
> 2. Uttānāsana — 30 sec
> 3. Balāsana — 60 sec
> 4. Sukhāsana with counted breath (4-in, 4-out) — 60 sec
> 5. Tāḍāsana — 30 sec
>
> Use this before exams.

## Teacher notes

- This is the most accessible "why does this work?" answer at Middle band. Connect it explicitly to PE class and biology if those teachers are interested.
- Some students may ask whether yoga is "scientifically proven." Honest answer: many specific health claims for yoga are still under study; the physiological mechanism of slow-breath calming is well-established.
