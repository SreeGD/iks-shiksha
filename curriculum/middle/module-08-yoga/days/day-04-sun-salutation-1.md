# Day 4 — Sūrya Namaskāra, Part 1 (Learning the 12 Poses)

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (25 min movement)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will name and demonstrate the 12 poses of Sūrya Namaskāra in order, performed slowly without breath co-ordination yet.

## Materials

- Mats. Sūrya Namaskāra wall poster (12 poses with names).
- Stopwatch.

## Vocabulary introduced today

- *Sūrya Namaskāra* (IAST: sūrya-namaskāra; lit. "salutation to the sun") — the 12-pose flowing sequence.
- *praṇāmāsana* — prayer pose
- *hasta-uttānāsana* — raised hands
- *uttānāsana* — standing forward fold
- *aśva-sañcalanāsana* — lunge (literally "horse-stride pose")
- *daṇḍāsana* / *catur-aṅga* — plank / four-limbed staff
- *aṣṭāṅga-namaskāra* — eight-limbed salutation (knees-chest-chin)
- *bhujaṅgāsana* — cobra
- *adho-mukha-śvānāsana* — downward-facing dog

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (5 min) — Why Sūrya Namaskāra?

- Sūrya Namaskāra is a *flow* of 12 poses traditionally practised at sunrise, facing the rising sun.
- Modern usage: a complete movement warm-up that activates major muscle groups, mobilises the spine, and synchronises breath with movement.
- We'll learn it in two days: today we **memorise the poses**, tomorrow we **add the breath flow**.

### Practice (30 min) — Walk through all 12 poses

The 12 poses (one round):

1. **Praṇāmāsana** — stand at top of mat, palms together at heart centre.
2. **Hasta-uttānāsana** — sweep arms overhead, gentle back arch.
3. **Uttānāsana** — fold forward, hands toward the floor.
4. **Aśva-sañcalanāsana (right)** — step right foot back, lunge.
5. **Adho-mukha-śvānāsana** — step left back, hips up, downward dog.
6. **Aṣṭāṅga-namaskāra** — lower knees, chest, chin to mat (eight points down).
7. **Bhujaṅgāsana** — slide forward, low cobra, chest up.
8. **Adho-mukha-śvānāsana** — push back to downward dog.
9. **Aśva-sañcalanāsana (right)** — step right foot forward, lunge.
10. **Uttānāsana** — bring left foot forward, fold.
11. **Hasta-uttānāsana** — rise, sweep arms overhead.
12. **Praṇāmāsana** — palms together at heart centre. Pause.

*Note: the legs alternate across rounds. Round 1 uses right leg back first; Round 2 uses left.*

#### Teaching sequence

- Teacher demonstrates the full round once slowly, naming each pose (5 min).
- Class practises pose-by-pose, holding each for 5 seconds, with teacher walking around (15 min).
- Class attempts one full round together at slow pace, teacher calling each pose (5 min).
- One more attempt, slightly faster (5 min).

### Wrap-up (3 min)

- Quick recap: students chorus the 12 pose names in order (with teacher prompting).
- Preview Day 5: *"Tomorrow we add the breath — each pose has an inhale or exhale assigned. That's where it becomes a real flow."*

## Homework

- Practise the sequence at home — 1 round, slowly. Notice which transitions feel awkward.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Attempt 2 rounds (alternating leg) at home.
- **Tier down:** Practise only the first 6 poses today; complete the rest tomorrow.

## Teacher notes

- The hardest pose for beginners is usually #6 (*aṣṭāṅga-namaskāra* — knees, chest, chin to mat). Common error: chest plank-down instead of dropping knees first. Cue "knees down FIRST, then chest, then chin."
- In *bhujaṅgāsana*, keep the elbows bent and close to the body. Don't push to full backbend.
- For students with wrist issues, modify *adho-mukha-śvānāsana* by going to forearms (dolphin pose) or skipping.
