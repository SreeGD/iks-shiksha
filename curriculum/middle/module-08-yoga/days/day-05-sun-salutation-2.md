# Day 5 — Sūrya Namaskāra, Part 2 (Breath Flow) + Mid-Module Check

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (20 min movement)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will perform 2 rounds of Sūrya Namaskāra with breath co-ordination (inhale on opening poses, exhale on folding/closing poses), and submit a project plan.

## Materials

- Mats. Sūrya Namaskāra poster.
- Formative quiz (`quizzes/formative.md` Part B).
- Project brief handouts.

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (5 min) — The breath pattern

Write on the board:

| # | Pose | Breath |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | praṇāmāsana | neutral / exhale |
| 2 | hasta-uttānāsana | **inhale** |
| 3 | uttānāsana | **exhale** |
| 4 | aśva-sañcalanāsana | **inhale** |
| 5 | adho-mukha-śvānāsana | **exhale** |
| 6 | aṣṭāṅga-namaskāra | **hold** breath out (very brief) |
| 7 | bhujaṅgāsana | **inhale** |
| 8 | adho-mukha-śvānāsana | **exhale** |
| 9 | aśva-sañcalanāsana | **inhale** |
| 10 | uttānāsana | **exhale** |
| 11 | hasta-uttānāsana | **inhale** |
| 12 | praṇāmāsana | **exhale** |

**Pattern to notice:** *open the body = inhale; fold or close the body = exhale.*

### Practice (15 min) — 2 rounds with breath

- 5 min: walk through 1 round at slow pace, teacher calling each pose AND each breath.
- 5 min: students do 1 round at their own pace, focused on breath.
- 5 min: 1 more round, slightly faster, breath leading the movement.

Two rounds total. **Do not exceed 3 rounds at this band.** Quality over quantity.

After the rounds, students lie in *śavāsana* (corpse pose — flat on the back, arms slightly out, palms up) for 2 minutes to integrate.

### Mid-module formative quiz (15 min)

- Hand out `quizzes/formative.md` Part B.
- 8 questions: name the 8 limbs in order, name 3 of the 5 foundational āsanas, identify the breath assigned to 3 SN poses, short answer "what is yoga?"

### Project commitment (6 min)

- Hand out project brief.
- Pairs / individuals choose target audience for their 5-min routine: peers / parents / younger siblings / classmates before a test.
- Each pair writes audience + 1-sentence intent on sticky note. Posts on the board.

### Wrap-up (2 min)

- Quick share: each pair states aloud their audience.
- Preview Day 6: *"Tomorrow we slow down dramatically — natural-breath observation. Just sitting with the breath."*

## Student-facing content

> **Sūrya Namaskāra — with breath**
>
> Pattern: **open body = inhale, fold body = exhale.**
>
> Maximum 2–3 rounds today. After the rounds, *śavāsana* (lie down, rest) for 2 minutes.
>
> **Project (Days 8–10):**
> Design a 5-minute yoga routine for ONE audience. Pick today:
>
> | Audience | Examples of fitting āsanas |
> |----------|------------------------------|
> | Peers (your classmates) | Energising — standing poses, partial SN |
> | Parents | Relaxing — child's pose, seated stretches |
> | Younger siblings | Playful — animal poses (cat, cobra, downward dog) |
> | Pre-exam stress | Calming — child's pose, seated breath |

## Homework

- 1 round of Sūrya Namaskāra at home with breath co-ordination.
- Submit your project plan paragraph by tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** 3 rounds at home; experiment with holding each pose for 2 breaths instead of 1.
- **Tier down:** 1 round, half-speed; or use the modified short Sūrya Namaskāra (poses 1–5 + 11–12 only).

## Teacher notes

- The breath co-ordination is the hardest piece. Several students will hold their breath through 3–4 poses. Cue: "If you forget which breath, just keep breathing — that's better than holding."
- Śavāsana matters. Don't skip it even if you're short on time. Two minutes of supine rest after physical practice is essential.
- Mark the formative quiz overnight.
