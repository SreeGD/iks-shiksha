# Day 10 — Final Assessment + Routine Showcase

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 60 min (extended — book a double period if possible)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will demonstrate mastery via a summative quiz and a 5-minute routine they lead the class through.

## Materials

- Summative quiz (`quizzes/summative.md`).
- Mats for everyone (audience does the routine alongside the presenters).
- Stopwatch.
- Rubric per pair.

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Part 1: Summative Quiz (20 min)

- Distribute. Closed notebook.
- 25 marks: limb identification, āsana naming, breath co-ordination match, prāṇāyāma definition + one safety question, short answer on physiology, open response.

### Part 2: Routine Showcase (35 min)

- Each pair leads a 5-minute routine.
- AUDIENCE PARTICIPATES — does the routine on their own mat as the pair leads.
- 7 minutes per pair (5 routine + 2 rubric mark + brief Q&A).
- ~5 pairs fit in 35 min. **Book next day for remaining pairs.**
- Teacher marks rubric in real-time.

### Wrap-up (3 min)

- Closing namaste in tāḍāsana.
- Reflection slip submitted.

## Student-facing content

> **Day 10: quiz + routine showcase**
>
> 20-min quiz first (closed notebook).
>
> 5-minute routine showcase. The class will join you on their mats — you LEAD them through your routine.
>
> **Reflection (due at end of class):**
> 1. What did you set out to make/learn, and what did you actually end up with?
> 2. What's the most surprising thing you discovered while making this?
> 3. If you had three more days, what would you do differently?

## Homework

- None. Module complete.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Students who finish quiz early write a 1-paragraph "what should change for Grade 11 students?" — input for Senior band.
- **Tier down:** Open-notebook quiz for students with documented learning needs.

## Teacher notes

- Some pairs will get nervous leading the class — that's OK. Quiet leading is leading.
- If the routine drifts off-time, gently signal but let them finish.
- Mark quizzes within 48 hours.
