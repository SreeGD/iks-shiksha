# Day 6 — Prāṇāyāma (Natural Breath Observation)

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (15 min seated breath practice)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will complete a 5-minute natural-breath observation and a 5-minute counted-breath (4-in, 4-out) practice, and describe one observable effect of slow breathing.

## Materials

- Mats or comfortable seating.
- Stopwatch.
- Worksheet for breath observation (`activities/activity-02-breath-observation.md`).

## Vocabulary introduced today

- *prāṇa* (IAST: prāṇa; lit. "breath, vital energy") — breath as the bridge between body and mind.
- *prāṇāyāma* (IAST: prāṇa-āyāma; lit. "extension or control of prāṇa") — the fourth limb; breath regulation.

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (10 min) — What is prāṇāyāma?

1. **Frame the limb** (write on board):

   > Prāṇāyāma — the 4th of the 8 limbs.
   > Literally: *prāṇa* (breath) + *āyāma* (extension, regulation).
   > Practical meaning: *intentional breath observation and regulation, NOT forceful breath holding.*

2. **What we DO at this level:**
   - Observe natural breath without changing it.
   - Count breaths (in for 4, out for 4).
   - Notice the gap between inhale and exhale.

3. **What we DO NOT do at this level** (write on board, large):
   > **No breath retention. No forceful breathing. No bellows breath (bhastrikā). No skull shining (kapālabhāti).**
   >
   > These advanced techniques require a certified yoga teacher. We're not doing them here.

4. **Source** (read aloud, don't memorise):

   > "*prāṇāyāmena tṛ-vṛtā prāṇendṛya-mano-malam śanair vyudasyābhidhyāyen manasā guruṇā gurum*"
   > "Through the threefold prāṇāyāma practice, one gradually purifies the breath, senses, and mind, and then meditates."
   > — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 4.8.44

   *"Notice the word **gradually** (śanair). The tradition is unambiguous: this is slow work."*

### Practice (20 min) — Two breath practices

**Practice 1 (5 min) — Natural-breath observation.**
- Sukhāsana. Eyes closed (or lowered).
- Breathe completely naturally. Don't change anything.
- Observe: which nostril is more open? Is the breath deep or shallow? Even or uneven?
- Just NOTICE. Don't fix.
- Teacher rings a soft bell at 1 min, 3 min, 5 min.

**Practice 2 (5 min) — Counted breath.**
- Inhale slowly to count of 4 (count silently in your head).
- Exhale slowly to count of 4.
- No holding at the top or bottom. Continuous.
- 10 cycles = roughly 1 minute. Aim for 5 minutes (50 cycles).
- If you lose count, restart at 1.

### Reflection (8 min)

- Worksheet `activities/activity-02-breath-observation.md`.
- Students write briefly:
  - Before the practice, my mind felt: ____
  - After the practice, my mind felt: ____
  - One thing I noticed about my breath: ____

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- 3 student shares: one sentence each on "what I noticed."
- Note: *"A common report — the mind is busier than we thought. Noticing that IS the practice."*
- Preview Day 7: *"Tomorrow — why does this work? We'll look at the autonomic nervous system."*

## Student-facing content

> **Prāṇāyāma — the 4th limb**
>
> *prāṇa* = breath, vital energy. *āyāma* = extension, regulation.
>
> Today: **two practices**.
>
> 1. **Natural breath, 5 min.** Don't change anything. Just observe.
> 2. **Counted breath, 5 min.** Inhale 4 counts. Exhale 4 counts. Continuous, smooth.
>
> **What we DON'T do at this level:** breath retention, forceful breathing, bellows breath, skull shining. Those require trained guidance.
>
> > "*śanair* — gradually" — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 4.8.44

## Homework

- Practise the counted breath for 3 minutes before bed tonight. Note in your workbook: "I felt _____ before, _____ after."

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Extend the counted breath to 5-in, 5-out for the second practice.
- **Tier down:** Just do the natural-breath observation. Skip the counted breath today.

## Teacher notes

- Some students find sitting with the breath uncomfortable — minds race, anxiety rises. Honour that: "if it feels too much, open your eyes and just rest."
- The "natural-breath-first" frame matters. Forceful breathing can cause hyperventilation or dizziness in students; we sidestep that completely at this band.
- Reinforce: this is real prāṇāyāma. The simplest forms are not "lesser" — they are the foundation.
