# Day 2 — The Eight Limbs (Aṣṭāṅga)

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (10 min movement)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will name all eight limbs of Patañjali's yoga in order and identify which limbs are physical, which are mental, and which are about ethical conduct.

## Materials

- Wall chart (or board diagram) showing the 8 limbs as a vertical or circular arrangement.
- Worksheet: limb-matching exit ticket.

## Vocabulary introduced today

- *aṣṭāṅga* (IAST: aṣṭāṅga; lit. "eight-limbed") — Patañjali's eight-stage scheme.
- All eight limbs:
  - *yama* (ethical restraints)
  - *niyama* (personal observances)
  - *āsana* (posture)
  - *prāṇāyāma* (breath regulation)
  - *pratyāhāra* (sense-withdrawal)
  - *dhāraṇā* (focused attention)
  - *dhyāna* (sustained meditation)
  - *samādhi* (absorption)

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (15 min) — The eight limbs

1. **Reveal the eight-limb chart.** Walk through each limb with a one-line gloss:

   | # | Sanskrit | Meaning | Example |
   |---|----------|---------|---------|
   | 1 | *yama* | restraints | non-harming (*ahiṁsā*), truthfulness (*satya*) |
   | 2 | *niyama* | observances | cleanliness (*śauca*), self-study (*svādhyāya*) |
   | 3 | *āsana* | posture | tāḍāsana, vṛkṣāsana — what we're learning |
   | 4 | *prāṇāyāma* | breath regulation | natural-breath observation |
   | 5 | *pratyāhāra* | sense-withdrawal | turning attention away from external sights/sounds |
   | 6 | *dhāraṇā* | focused attention | holding the mind on one object for 12 seconds |
   | 7 | *dhyāna* | sustained meditation | unbroken attention for several minutes |
   | 8 | *samādhi* | absorption | total integration — the final stage |

2. **Source quote** (read aloud, don't memorise):

   > "Yoga is practised in eight different stages: yama, niyama, āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra, dhāraṇā, dhyāna, and samādhi."
   > — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, Canto 3 (purport)

   > "The Bhagavad-gītā addresses all eight limbs of rāja-yoga."
   > — *Veda, Secrets from the East* (Prabhupāda)

3. **Patterns to notice (write on board):**
   - Limbs 1–2 are about **conduct** (how you live).
   - Limbs 3–4 are about **the body** (what we practise this module).
   - Limbs 5–8 are about **the mind**.

4. *"Why does Patañjali put 'living ethically' BEFORE 'posture'? Why not start with the easy physical stuff?"*
   - Take 2 student answers.
   - Reinforce: the tradition says a turbulent inner life makes meditation impossible, so you have to settle the outer life first. (At Middle band that's a sufficient gloss.)

### Practice (15 min) — Tāḍāsana + introduction to vṛkṣāsana

- Tāḍāsana, 3 holds of 30 sec each.
- Now: **vṛkṣāsana (tree posture).**
  - Stand in tāḍāsana.
  - Shift weight onto the left foot.
  - Place the right foot's sole against the inside of the left calf (NOT the side of the knee).
  - Hands at heart centre, or raised above the head.
  - Gaze fixed on one spot on the wall ahead.
  - Hold for 20 seconds. Switch sides.
  - Wobbling is normal. Falling is information, not failure.

### Exit ticket (8 min)

- Worksheet: students match each of the 8 limbs to a description.
- See `quizzes/formative.md` Part A.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Three-question recap:
  - Which limb is posture? *(3rd — āsana.)*
  - Which limb is breath? *(4th — prāṇāyāma.)*
  - What comes LAST? *(8th — samādhi.)*
- Preview Day 3: *"Tomorrow — five foundational āsanas, including the tree we tried today."*

## Student-facing content

> **The Eight Limbs of Yoga** (Patañjali's *aṣṭāṅga*)
>
> | # | Sanskrit | Meaning |
> |---|----------|---------|
> | 1 | *yama* | restraints (don'ts) |
> | 2 | *niyama* | observances (dos) |
> | 3 | *āsana* | posture |
> | 4 | *prāṇāyāma* | breath regulation |
> | 5 | *pratyāhāra* | sense-withdrawal |
> | 6 | *dhāraṇā* | focused attention |
> | 7 | *dhyāna* | sustained meditation |
> | 8 | *samādhi* | absorption |
>
> Limbs 1–2: conduct. 3–4: body. 5–8: mind.
>
> Today's pose: **vṛkṣāsana** (tree). Stand on one leg. Balance. Wobble is allowed.

## Homework

- Memorise the 8 limbs in order. Bring to next class — pop quiz.
- Practice tāḍāsana + vṛkṣāsana for 5 min before breakfast.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add the meanings of *yama* and *niyama* with at least 2 examples each (ahiṁsā, satya, śauca, santoṣa, etc.).
- **Tier down:** Memorise just the 4 physical/body limbs (3, 4, 5) — āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra — and the 1 mental endpoint (8 — samādhi). Build up.

## Teacher notes

- The Sanskrit names will be slippery for some students. Daily chorus repetition helps; create a hand gesture per limb for the kinesthetic learners.
- Watch knee placement during vṛkṣāsana — the foot must NOT press into the side of the knee (medial cruciate strain risk). Foot above OR below the knee, never on it.
- If a student falls during balance, normalise it: "wobbling is what balance learning looks like."
