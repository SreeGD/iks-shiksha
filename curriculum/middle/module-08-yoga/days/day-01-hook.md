# Day 1 — What is Yoga?

**Module:** Yoga and the Body · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (with 12 min of physical practice)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will state at least two definitions of *yoga* (one from Patañjali, one from the Bhagavad-gītā), perform *tāḍāsana* with safe alignment, and complete a 60-second seated-breath observation.

## Materials

- One yoga mat per student (or clean floor)
- Comfortable clothing — heads-up given the previous day
- Wall poster (optional): the eight limbs (preview)

## Vocabulary introduced today

- *yoga* (IAST: yoga; lit. "yoke, union") — at Middle band, Patañjali's definition: "settling the fluctuations of the mind."
- *āsana* (IAST: āsana; lit. "seat, posture") — a held physical posture.
- *tāḍāsana* (IAST: tāḍāsana; lit. "mountain posture") — foundational standing pose.

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (5 min) — Settle

- Students sit cross-legged on their mats (*sukhāsana* — easy seat).
- Eyes closed. Count 5 natural breaths.
- Teacher: *"Today we begin a 10-day module on yoga. We'll move, we'll breathe, and we'll think about what yoga actually is."*

### Core (15 min) — What is yoga?

1. Question on the board: *"What does the word 'yoga' mean to you right now, before this module?"*
   - Take 4 student answers. (Common: "stretching," "exercise from India," "a religion," "what my mom does on YouTube.")
   - Don't judge. Write them all.

2. **Two definitions to remember** (write boxed on board):

   > **Patañjali (c. 2nd c. BCE – 4th c. CE):**
   > *"yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ"* — "Yoga is the settling of the fluctuations of the mind." (Yoga Sūtra 1.2)

   > **Bhagavad-gītā (Krishna to Arjuna, c. 4th c. BCE – 2nd c. CE):**
   > *"yogaḥ karmasu kauśalam"* — "Yoga is skill in action." (BG 2.50)

3. *"Neither of these is primarily about stretching."* Pause for that to land.
4. **The word itself** — *yoga* comes from the Sanskrit root *yuj* meaning "to yoke" — to join, to bind together. The yoga tradition uses it to mean the joining of body, breath, and attention — and ultimately the settling of the mind.

5. **What we'll do in this module** — work mainly with two of the *eight limbs* of yoga: *āsana* (posture) and *prāṇāyāma* (breath). The other six limbs are introduced briefly on Day 2.

### Practice (15 min) — Tāḍāsana

- Stand at the top of your mat. Feet hip-width apart.
- *"Press your big toes, little toes, and heels firmly into the mat. Lift your knee caps without locking your knees. Lengthen up through the crown of your head as if a string is gently pulling you upward."*
- Arms relaxed at the sides.
- Hold for 30 seconds. Breathe naturally.
- Release. Sit. Notice: *"Did anything feel different after that pose?"*
- Repeat twice more. Each hold a little longer.

### Seated breath observation (5 min)

- Sit back in sukhāsana.
- Eyes closed.
- **Just observe your breath for 60 seconds.** Don't try to change it. Just notice.
- After 60 sec: *"How many breaths did you count? Was your mind quiet, busy, somewhere else?"* — quick share, 2 students.

### Wrap-up (5 min)

- Three-question recap (chorus):
  - Who was Patañjali? *(The systematiser of yoga's eight limbs.)*
  - What is yoga? *("Settling the fluctuations of the mind." OR "Skill in action.")*
  - What is *tāḍāsana*? *(Mountain posture.)*
- Preview Day 2: *"Tomorrow — the eight limbs. We'll see why posture and breath are only TWO of them."*

## Student-facing content

> **Day 1 — What is Yoga?**
>
> *Yoga* (Sanskrit: *yuj* = "to yoke") is the joining of body, breath, and attention.
>
> Two foundational definitions:
>
> 1. **Patañjali:** *yoga = settling the fluctuations of the mind.*
> 2. **Bhagavad-gītā:** *yoga = skill in action.*
>
> Today's posture: **tāḍāsana** (mountain).
>
> 1. Stand at the top of your mat.
> 2. Feet hip-width apart, firm contact with the ground.
> 3. Lengthen up through the crown of your head.
> 4. Breathe naturally for 30 seconds. Release.

## Homework

- Practice *tāḍāsana* for 60 seconds tomorrow morning before brushing your teeth. Notice how your body feels.
- Bring comfortable clothing tomorrow.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Hold tāḍāsana for 90 sec; count your breaths during the hold.
- **Tier down:** Hold for 15 sec if 30 feels long; rest in between.

## Teacher notes

- This is the most important "framing" day of the module. Students who learn the two definitions can hold the philosophical frame for the rest of the module.
- Some students will resist closing their eyes during settle. Don't force it — "eyes lowered to the mat" is acceptable.
- Watch for students who hyperextend knees during tāḍāsana — common in young dancers. Cue "soft knees, lift knee caps."
