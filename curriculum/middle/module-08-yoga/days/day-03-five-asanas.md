# Day 3 — Five Foundational Āsanas

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (20 min movement)

## Learning objective

By the end of this lesson, students will perform five foundational āsanas — *tāḍāsana, vṛkṣāsana, baddha-koṇāsana, balāsana, sukhāsana* — with safe alignment cues, and identify which body parts each pose strengthens or stretches.

## Materials

- Mats. Poster of the five āsanas (optional).
- Formative quiz Part A (limb-matching) returned.

## Vocabulary introduced today

- *baddha-koṇāsana* (IAST: baddha-koṇa-āsana; lit. "bound-angle posture") — seated wide-knees pose, soles together.
- *balāsana* (IAST: bāla-āsana; lit. "child posture") — folded forward rest pose.
- *sukhāsana* (IAST: sukha-āsana; lit. "easy posture") — comfortable cross-legged seat.

## Lesson flow

### Settle (2 min)

- Sukhāsana. 5 breaths.

### Core (8 min) — The five āsanas, named

Write on the board:

| Āsana | Type | Strengthens / stretches |
|-------|------|--------------------------|
| 1. *tāḍāsana* (mountain) | standing | feet, legs, core; cultivates posture awareness |
| 2. *vṛkṣāsana* (tree) | standing balance | feet, calves, core, focus |
| 3. *baddha-koṇāsana* (bound-angle) | seated stretch | inner thighs, groin, hips |
| 4. *balāsana* (child) | seated rest | back, hips; calming |
| 5. *sukhāsana* (easy seat) | seated | spine, hips; foundation for breath practice |

*Note: none of these are inversions or advanced backbends. All five can be practised safely at home.*

### Practice (25 min) — Walk through all five

For each pose: 1 min demonstration with verbal cues, 1 min student practice with teacher observation, 1 min hold + breath.

1. **Tāḍāsana** — 30 sec.
   - Feet hip-width. Press into all four corners of each foot.
   - Lengthen up. Soft knees. Breathe.

2. **Vṛkṣāsana** — 20 sec per side.
   - From tāḍāsana, shift weight, foot on calf (NOT knee).
   - Hands at heart or overhead.
   - Wobble allowed. Switch sides.

3. **Baddha-koṇāsana** — 60 sec.
   - Sit. Bend knees, soles of feet together, drop knees out to the sides.
   - Hold the feet. Long spine. Don't force the knees down.
   - Gentle nodding of the knees is acceptable.

4. **Balāsana** — 90 sec rest pose.
   - From kneeling: knees apart, big toes touch.
   - Fold forward, forehead on the mat (or stacked fists if forehead doesn't reach).
   - Arms forward or alongside the body.
   - Breathe slowly. This is rest, not effort.

5. **Sukhāsana** — 60 sec.
   - Sit cross-legged. Long spine.
   - Hands on the knees, palms up or down.
   - Eyes closed. 10 natural breaths.

### Formative quiz (8 min)

- See `quizzes/formative.md` Part A — limb-matching from Day 2 + āsana-naming from Day 3.

### Wrap-up (2 min)

- Question: *"Which of the five did your body LIKE the most? Which felt hardest?"* — students share with a neighbour for 30 sec each.
- Preview Day 4: *"Tomorrow we begin Sūrya Namaskāra — the 12-pose sun salutation."*

## Homework

- Choose 3 of the 5 āsanas. Practise them for 5 minutes before bed tonight. Note in your workbook how you felt before vs. after.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add *adho-mukha-śvānāsana* (downward-facing dog) to your home practice — try 30 sec hold.
- **Tier down:** Use a wall for vṛkṣāsana support. Use a folded blanket under the knees for sukhāsana / baddha-koṇāsana.

## Teacher notes

- Walk around during baddha-koṇāsana. Many students try to force the knees down — this can strain the inner thigh. Cue "gentle, let gravity do the work."
- Balāsana is restorative. If a student's hips don't allow them to sit on their heels, place a folded blanket between thighs and calves.
- A handful of students will fidget through sukhāsana. Don't correct — let them feel the discomfort of stillness. That's part of the lesson.
