## Daily structure

Each class follows the same broad shape:

| | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Settle | 2 min | Sit on mat, eyes closed, 5 natural breaths |
| Theory | 8–12 min | Concept of the day |
| Practice | 15–20 min | Movement / breath work |
| Discussion | 5–8 min | What did you notice? |
| Close | 2 min | Tāḍāsana + namaste |

## Pacing notes

- This module is movement-heavy. **Confirm the space** the day before — yoga mats laid out, no chairs in the way.
- Days 4 and 5 (Sūrya Namaskāra) are the most demanding physically. Build the cumulative practice slowly. If your class is struggling, halve the rounds; quality of movement beats quantity.
- Day 6 (prāṇāyāma) is restricted to **natural-breath observation only** at this band. Do not teach forceful breath retention (kumbhaka), bhastrikā, or kapālabhāti.

## Daily warm-up

Every class opens with **2 minutes of seated awareness**: students sit in *sukhāsana* (easy cross-legged seat), close their eyes, and count 5 natural breaths. This is the foundational *settling* practice — by Day 10 students should do it without prompting.

## What students leave the module with

- Five safe foundational āsanas they can practise unsupervised at home.
- A complete Sūrya Namaskāra sequence with breath co-ordination.
- A natural-breath observation practice (no breath retention).
- The eight-limb framework as a "map" of the yoga tradition.
- One designed 5-minute routine they can share.
