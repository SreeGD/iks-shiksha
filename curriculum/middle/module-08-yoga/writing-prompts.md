# Writing Prompts — Module 8 Yoga (Middle)

Seven prompts. Useful as exit tickets, homework, or post-practice reflections.

## Reflection prompts

**1. (Day 1, end of class, 3 min)** Before this class, what did you think yoga was? In one sentence, how has that changed?

**2. (Day 6, end of class, 5 min)** During the 5-minute natural-breath observation, what did you notice about your mind? Was it quiet, busy, drifting, focused?

**3. (Day 7 or any later day, ~100 words)** Describe a moment outside class — maybe last night, maybe last week — when your body was clearly in "fight or flight" mode. What happened to your breath, your heart, your thoughts?

## Explanatory prompts

**4. (Day 3 or 4, homework, ~150 words)** Explain *Sūrya Namaskāra* to a friend who has never seen yoga. Name the 12 poses without using the words "yoga pose."

**5. (Day 7, homework, ~120 words)** In your own words, explain why slow exhalation makes the body feel calm. Use the words *vagus nerve* and *parasympathetic*.

## Argumentative prompt

**6. (Day 2 or 6, homework, 1 paragraph)** Some people say "yoga is just stretching." Some say "yoga is religious and shouldn't be in schools." Some say "yoga is exercise that comes from India." Pick the position you most disagree with and write 5–7 sentences explaining why you disagree, fairly.

## Creative prompt

**7. (Day 5 or 8, optional, 1 page)** Write a short scene (50–150 words) in which two students argue about whether to skip yoga class. One wants to. One doesn't. End with one of them noticing something they hadn't expected.

---

## Prompt-to-band notes

- **Primary** band: prompts 1, 2, 4, 7 work as drawings or oral responses.
- **Senior** band: prompt 6 deserves a 1–2 page essay with citations; prompt 3 can connect to specific scientific research.
