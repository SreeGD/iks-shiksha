# Module 8

## Yoga and the Body

Grades 6–8 · 10 sessions · ~15 min movement per class

---

# Two definitions of yoga

> **Patañjali (YS 1.2):**
> "Yoga is the settling of the fluctuations of the mind."

> **Bhagavad-gītā (2.50):**
> "Yoga is skill in action."

The root *yuj* means "to yoke" — to join body, breath, and attention.

---

# The Eight Limbs (Aṣṭāṅga)

| # | Sanskrit | Meaning |
|---|----------|---------|
| 1 | *yama* | restraints |
| 2 | *niyama* | observances |
| 3 | *āsana* | posture |
| 4 | *prāṇāyāma* | breath |
| 5 | *pratyāhāra* | sense-withdrawal |
| 6 | *dhāraṇā* | focus |
| 7 | *dhyāna* | meditation |
| 8 | *samādhi* | absorption |

Limbs 1–2: conduct. 3–4: body. 5–8: mind.

---

# Our focus

We work mainly with limbs **3** (āsana) and **4** (prāṇāyāma).

But knowing the other six is the map.

---

# Five Foundational Āsanas

1. **Tāḍāsana** — mountain
2. **Vṛkṣāsana** — tree
3. **Baddha-koṇāsana** — bound-angle
4. **Balāsana** — child
5. **Sukhāsana** — easy seat

All safe to practise daily at home.

---

# Vṛkṣāsana safety

The lifted foot goes **above** or **below** the standing knee.

**NEVER** on the side of the knee.

(Medial cruciate strain risk.)

# Sūrya Namaskāra — breath

**Pattern:** open body = inhale, fold body = exhale.

Max 2–3 rounds at this band.

Quality of movement over quantity of rounds.

---

# Prāṇāyāma at our level

**What we DO:**
- Natural-breath observation (5 min)
- Counted breath: 4 in, 4 out (5 min)
- Notice the gap between inhale and exhale

**What we DON'T do:**
- Breath retention (kumbhaka)
- Forceful breathing
- Bhastrikā, kapālabhāti

---

# A scripture anchor

> "*prāṇāyāmena tṛ-vṛtā prāṇendṛya-mano-malam **śanair** vyudasyābhidhyāyen ...*"

> "Through the threefold prāṇāyāma practice, one **gradually** purifies the breath, senses, and mind."
> — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 4.8.44

Note the word *śanair* — "gradually."

---

# Why slow breath calms us

Two modes of the body:

| Sympathetic | Parasympathetic |
|-------------|------------------|
| "fight or flight" | "rest and digest" |
| fast | slow |

The **vagus nerve** carries parasympathetic signals.

**Slow exhalation stimulates the vagus nerve.**

---

# Yoga in physiology terms

- **Slow counted breath** → vagus → calm body
- **Forward folds / child's pose** → head below heart → calming
- **Backbends / inversions** → energising (we don't teach inversions here)
- **Sūrya Namaskāra** → mixed = balanced

Not magic. Physiology.

---

# Your stress-recovery sequence

For before exams, after stress, any time:

1. Tāḍāsana — 30 sec
2. Uttānāsana — 30 sec
3. Balāsana — 60 sec
4. Sukhāsana + counted breath — 60 sec
5. Tāḍāsana — 30 sec

Total: 3.5 minutes. Works because of vagus, not magic.
