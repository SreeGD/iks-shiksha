# Parent Guide — Module 8: Yoga and the Body

**Your child's age:** Middle (Grades 6–8) · **Module length:** 10 sessions × 45 min (~15 min movement each)

## What your child is learning

The classical yoga framework + safe modern practice:

- **Patañjali's eight limbs** (*aṣṭāṅga*): yama, niyama, āsana, prāṇāyāma, pratyāhāra, dhāraṇā, dhyāna, samādhi.
- **Five foundational āsanas:** tāḍāsana, vṛkṣāsana, baddha-koṇāsana, balāsana, sukhāsana.
- **Sūrya Namaskāra** — the 12-pose sun salutation, with breath co-ordination.
- **Counted-breath prāṇāyāma** — natural breath observation and 4-in/4-out counted breath.
- **The physiology:** why slow exhale shifts the body toward calm (vagus nerve, parasympathetic activation).

## Safety — please read before Day 1

The school will send you a medical opt-out form. Please fill it in for:
- Asthma, recent injury, recent surgery, pregnancy, severe ADHD, any musculoskeletal issue.

**What we do NOT teach at this band:**
- No inversions (headstand, shoulder stand, plough).
- No advanced backbends.
- No breath retention (no bhastrikā, no kapālabhāti, no held breath).

**Opt-out always allowed.** Your child can sit out any movement, any time, no questions asked.

## What to expect this fortnight

**Daily settle:** 2 minutes of seated breath awareness to start every class.

**Movement:** ~15 min per class. Building toward 1–2 rounds of Sūrya Namaskāra by Day 5.

**Project (Days 8–10):** Your child designs and leads a 5-minute yoga routine for a chosen audience — peers, parents, younger siblings, or classmates before an exam. On Day 10 they lead the whole class through their routine.

**Reading:** verses from the Bhagavad-gītā and Bhāgavata Purāṇa with translation (cached in the module's `.rag-cache/` folder).

## How to help

**Helpful:**
- Try the stress-recovery sequence from Day 7 with your child: tāḍāsana 30s → uttānāsana 30s → balāsana 60s → counted breath 60s → tāḍāsana 30s. ~3.5 minutes. Use it before exams.
- Allow comfortable clothing on yoga class days.
- Be the "audience" for their project rehearsal at home.
- Read the brief on what the eight limbs are — your child will be glad you know.

**Not helpful:**
- Pushing depth in poses ("you should be able to touch your toes by now").
- Filming and sharing — yoga is a personal practice, not a performance.
- Religious framing or dismissal — the classroom frame is *yoga as physical and philosophical practice with Indian roots, taught secularly*. We respect families' devotional traditions; we don't centre them.

## A note on the religious dimension

Yoga has religious roots in India and is practised devotionally in many traditions. **In school we teach the physical and philosophical aspects in a secular frame.** Your child will read Patañjali's classical text as primary historical source, not as scripture.

If your family has religious concerns, the school will arrange a written observation alternative.

## A note on the modern-vs-classical distinction

Honest framing your child will encounter:
- Patañjali's eight-limb system is classical — c. 2nd century BCE – 4th century CE.
- Modern *postural* yoga (the asana-heavy form taught in studios worldwide) is largely a 20th-century synthesis — drawing on classical Indian texts AND on European physical-culture movements.
- Both are real. Both are Indian. The history is just more layered than most popular accounts suggest.

Senior band students read Singleton (2010) on this. Middle band gets a brief honest mention.

## What success looks like

By end of module, your child can:
- Name and perform 5 foundational āsanas safely.
- Lead a 1-round Sūrya Namaskāra with breath co-ordination.
- Do 5 minutes of counted-breath prāṇāyāma calmly.
- Explain *why* slow exhale calms the body (vagus nerve, parasympathetic).
- Teach a 5-minute routine to a chosen audience.
