# Activity 01 — Five Foundational Āsana Practice

**Module:** Yoga · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 3 · **Time:** 25 min (within Day 3's 45)

## Type

Guided physical practice, individual on mats

## Goal

Students perform all five foundational āsanas with safe alignment, holding each pose for the prescribed time.

## Materials per student

- One yoga mat
- Optional: folded blanket for seated poses

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Lay out mats in a grid with at least 1 metre between each.
- Wall poster of the 5 āsanas visible.
- Verify: no chairs/tables in falling distance.

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> **Five Āsana Practice**
>
> Work on your own mat. Follow the teacher's verbal cues. If anything causes pain, stop and rest.
>
> **1. Tāḍāsana (mountain) — 30 sec × 3 holds**
> - Feet hip-width apart, equal weight.
> - Lengthen up through the crown of the head.
> - Breathe naturally.
>
> **2. Vṛkṣāsana (tree) — 20 sec each side × 2**
> - From tāḍāsana, shift weight to left foot.
> - Right foot above OR below the standing knee (NEVER on it).
> - Hands at heart or overhead.
> - Switch sides.
>
> **3. Baddha-koṇāsana (bound-angle) — 60 sec hold**
> - Sit. Soles of feet together, knees fall out.
> - Hold the feet. Long spine.
> - Don't force the knees down. Gentle nodding allowed.
>
> **4. Balāsana (child) — 90 sec rest**
> - Kneel. Knees apart, big toes touch.
> - Fold forward. Forehead on mat (or fists).
> - Arms forward or along the body.
> - Breathe slowly. This is rest.
>
> **5. Sukhāsana (easy seat) — 60 sec**
> - Sit cross-legged.
> - Long spine. Hands on knees.
> - Eyes closed. 10 natural breaths.

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Steady breath, soft face, body responding to verbal cues | Quiet; "lengthen up," "soft knees" |
| Needs intervention | Holding breath; hyperextending knees; foot on side of knee in vṛkṣāsana | Grunting; "I can't" |

## Safety / sensitivity

- **Stop immediately** if a student reports sharp pain.
- **Watch for knee placement** in vṛkṣāsana — most common injury risk.
- **Hyperextension in tāḍāsana** — common in dancers. Cue "soft knees."
- **Opt-out** allowed any time, no questions. Student sits in sukhāsana.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add adho-mukha-śvānāsana (downward dog) for 30 sec.
- **Tier down:** Use a wall behind for vṛkṣāsana support; use a folded blanket under hips for seated poses.

## Clean-up

- Wipe down mats. Stack neatly.
