# Teacher Notes — Module 8 Yoga (Middle)

## Read this BEFORE Day 1

This module involves physical practice. The safety floor matters more than any other module's. If you have not taught yoga before, please:

1. Take the Day 4–5 Sūrya Namaskāra through yourself a few times before class. You should be able to demonstrate the 12 poses smoothly.
2. Know the **opt-out** procedure: a student may sit out any movement at any time, no questions asked. Write this on the board on Day 1.
3. Collect **medical opt-out forms** from any student with: asthma, recent injury (knee, ankle, back, neck, wrist), recent surgery, pregnancy, severe ADHD requiring physical accommodation. Modify or excuse accordingly.

## Hard safety rules (non-negotiable at this band)

- **NO inversions** — śīrṣāsana (headstand), halāsana (plough), pīncha mayūrāsana. Cobra and downward dog are fine.
- **NO advanced backbends** — cakrāsana (wheel), dhanurāsana (bow), uṣṭrāsana (camel). Gentle bhujaṅgāsana (cobra) only, knees down if discomfort.
- **NO breath retention** — kumbhaka (holding the breath in or out). No bhastrikā (bellows breath). No kapālabhāti (skull shining). Only **natural-breath observation** and gentle counted breathing (in for 4, out for 4).
- **NO partner stretching** — keep practice individual. Partner-assisted alignment is for trained teachers only.
- **NO music** — focus on breath. (Optional after Day 8 for project rehearsal.)

## Posture corrections

When walking around the room during āsana:
- **Verbal cues first**, hands-on adjustments only with explicit student consent.
- "Soft knee," "long spine," "ground through both feet" — language students can absorb without you touching them.
- If a student is at risk of injury (e.g. hyperextending a knee), say "lower your knee to the floor please" — direct, kind, clear.

## Common misconceptions

1. **"Yoga is religious."** Many students will have heard this from family or peers. Honest answer: yoga has religious roots and is practised devotionally in many traditions, AND it is also taught secularly in schools, hospitals, and offices worldwide for its physical and mental benefits. In this class we focus on the latter. (Acknowledge the former with respect.)
2. **"Yoga is just stretching."** Yoga includes physical practice but Patañjali defined yoga as *settling the fluctuations of the mind* (YS 1.2). The body work is *one limb* (āsana) of eight. Day 2 makes this clear.
3. **"Pranayama is dangerous."** Forceful pranayama can be dangerous if untrained. Natural-breath observation — which is what we teach — is safe for everyone and is supported by modern research on the parasympathetic nervous system.
4. **"Surya Namaskara has to be 108 rounds."** It doesn't. At this band, 1–3 rounds is the limit. The number 108 is symbolic in some traditions; we leave that aside.

## Differentiation hints

- **Tier up:** A student with prior yoga experience can hold poses longer, attempt Adho Mukha Śvānāsana (downward dog), or lead the Sūrya Namaskāra count for the class.
- **Tier down:** A student with limited mobility can practise the breath portions seated, do half-Sūrya-Namaskāra (modified short sequence), or use a chair for the standing portions.

## Sensitivity notes

- **Gender mixing during physical practice** — some families have concerns. If a student declines, accommodate (mat at the back, or sit out).
- **Religious objection from family** — extremely rare in India, but possible. If a parent objects, offer a written observation/reflection alternative (read about the eight limbs, write a 1-paragraph response per day instead of practising).
- **Body-image sensitivity** — some students are self-conscious about visible effort. Discourage mirrors. Encourage "look at your own mat, not the others."

## Project oversight

- The Day 8 project plan needs to specify the **target audience** clearly. A routine for "tired parents" looks different from a routine for "anxious classmates before a test."
- On Day 9, every pair must run through their routine at full speed at least once. No exceptions.
- Day 10 presentations include a teach-back element: the audience does the routine along with the presenters.

## On the devotional framing in the cached RAG chunks

The vidya-karana RAG returns Bhāgavatam and Bhagavad-gītā purports that are heavily devotional (Krishna-conscious). The verses themselves are factually accurate sources for the eight-limb framework. **Use the verses, paraphrase the framing.** Don't quote the purports' devotional language wholesale in the lesson — it conflicts with the secular framing of this curriculum.

## If you only have 6 days

- Day 2: Five āsanas (combined with brief eight-limb overview)
- Day 3: Sūrya Namaskāra full sequence
- Day 4: Prāṇāyāma + physiology connection (combined)
- Day 5: One project session
- Day 6: Final assessment + presentation

Skip the full eight-limb deep dive and the second SN day in compression.
