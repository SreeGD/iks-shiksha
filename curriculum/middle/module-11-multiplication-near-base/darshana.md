## Core idea · 15 min

**Nikhilam multiplication for numbers near a power of 10:**

For (B − a)(B − b), where B is a power of 10:
1. Compute deficits: a, b.
2. Cross-subtract: (B − a) − b OR (B − b) − a (same answer).
3. Multiply deficits: a × b.
4. Result: [cross-subtract] [multiply], with appropriate padding.

Example: 97 × 96.
- Deficits: 3, 4.
- Cross-subtract: 97 − 4 = 93. Or 96 − 3 = 93.
- Multiply deficits: 3 × 4 = 12.
- Answer: 9312.

**Numbers above base:** 103 × 104 — excesses 3, 4; cross-add → 107; multiply → 12; answer 10712.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Live speed: 5 problems near 100 (e.g., 96 × 93, 88 × 89). Each student solves mentally. Time it.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Paraphrase Tirthaji 1965, Sūtra 2 (same as Module 9, applied to multiplication): *"Nikhilaṁ — all from 9, last from 10 — gives the deficits, which then drive the multiplication."*

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Mental-math sprint.** 15 problems of near-100 multiplication. Each student records time + accuracy. Pair-share strategies.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"For what % of multiplication problems would Nikhilam help? When does it NOT help?"*
