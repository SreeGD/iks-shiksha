## Part A — Day 3 (3 min, 5 problems, both below 100)

## Part C — Day 6 (5 min, 4 problems mixed-bag + 1 short-answer)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** (1 mark) `998 × 997` = ____
**2.** (1 mark) `1003 × 1004` = ____
**3.** (1 mark, mixed-sign) `102 × 98` = ____
**4.** (1 mark, harder) `985 × 978` = ____
**5.** (2 marks, short answer) Why does the Nikhilam method work? In one sentence, use the word **base**.

### Part C answer key

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 1 | 995006 |
| 2 | 1007012 |
| 3 | 9996 |
| 4 | 963330 |
| 5 | Because `(B − a)(B − b) = B(B − a − b) + ab`, where `B` is the base — so cross-subtract gives `B − a − b` (the left), and the product of deficits gives `ab` (the right). 2 marks for full reference to algebra; 1 mark if it mentions "base" and "deficits" without the full identity. |

**6 marks total**.

## What to do with the results

- **Class average below 60%** (Part A or B) → re-teach the carry-overflow case before moving to next phase.
- **Individual below 40%** → 10-min reteaching block on Day 5 or Day 9.
- **Class average above 80% on Part B** → consider compressing Days 5+6 and using the saved time for Day 9 sprint.
