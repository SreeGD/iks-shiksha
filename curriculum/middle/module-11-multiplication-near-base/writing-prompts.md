# Writing Prompts — Module 11 Nikhilam Multiplication (Middle)

Six prompts. Useful as exit tickets, homework reflections, or project warm-ups.

## Reflection prompts

**1. (Day 1, end of class, 3 min)** When you saw the teacher solve `97 × 96` in three seconds, what did you feel? Curiosity, suspicion, "that can't be right"? Be honest — describe the feeling.

**2. (Day 9, end of class, 5 min)** Compare your Day 1 time on `97 × 96` (long multiplication) with your Day 9 time (Nikhilam). Has your *feeling* about mental math changed in the last two weeks? Be honest — even "no" is a valid answer.

## Explanatory prompts

**3. (Day 3 or Day 6, homework, ~120 words)** Explain the Nikhilam method to a Grade 4 student who has never seen it. No more than 6 sentences. Use one specific example. Don't say "magic" — say what's actually happening.

**4. (Day 7, homework, ~150 words)** Explain the algebraic identity `(B − a)(B − b) = B(B − a − b) + ab` in your own words. Use one numerical example. Show how each piece of the identity matches a step in the Nikhilam procedure.

## Argumentative prompt

**5. (Day 8 or homework, 1 paragraph)** Some say "Vedic mathematics" should be a mandatory subject in every Indian school. Others say it should be taught alongside the standard algorithm with honest historical framing. Pick a position and defend it in 5–7 sentences. Use what you've learned about (a) when Nikhilam is faster, (b) when long multiplication is faster, and (c) the honest history of Tirthaji's 1965 book.

## Creative prompt

**6. (Day 5 or Day 8, optional, 1 page)** Write a short script (50–150 words) in which a frustrated student and a confident Nikhilam user have a conversation. The frustrated student raises one good objection to the Nikhilam method ("but most multiplication problems aren't near 100!"). The confident user gives a clear, honest answer. (No "and then they magically agreed" endings — let the disagreement be real.)

---

## Prompt-to-band notes

- For **Primary** band, prompt 1 works as a drawing or oral response.
- For **Senior** band, prompt 5 deserves a full essay treatment with citations from `sources.md`.

## How to give feedback

- Prompts 1, 2: acknowledge with one sentence — no correction.
- Prompts 3, 4: respond with "the part that landed" and "the part that confused me."
- Prompt 5: use the rubric — does the writing acknowledge the other side, give a specific example, reach a clear position?
- Prompt 6: no grading. Read the best ones aloud (with permission).
