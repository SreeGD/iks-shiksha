# Sources — Module 11 Multiplication Near a Base (Middle)

The **vidya-karana RAG** does not cover Vedic-math sources, so this module draws directly from SutraGanita's source corpus (`/Users/sree/Projects/SutraGanita/content/`). All PDFs cited below are accessible in that directory.

## Primary source (used most heavily)

**Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf** — *Vedic Cultural Center (ISKCON), 1420 228th Ave SE, Sammamish WA 98075*

- Sections on Nikhilam-based multiplication near a base — both numbers below (the 7 × 8, 97 × 96 family), both numbers above (103 × 104), and the mixed case (102 × 98).
- The cross-subtraction / cross-addition for the left part.
- The deficit × deficit product for the right part and the "two-digit padding when base = 100" rule.

> Cite as: *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf* (Nikhilam multiplication section).

## Historical context

- **Tirthaji, Bharati Krishna.** *Vedic Mathematics: Sixteen Mathematical Formulae from the Vedas* (1965), Motilal Banarsidass. — Tirthaji's foundational text. The *Nikhilam* multiplication scheme is one of his showcase examples.
- **Plofker, Kim.** *Mathematics in India* (2009), Princeton University Press. — Historical context of Indian mathematics; useful for situating Tirthaji's work in the broader history.
- **Dani, S. G.** "Myths and Reality: On Vedic Mathematics" (1993, *Frontline*; reprinted in *Resonance* 2001). — Standard scholarly critique. At Middle band this is a teacher reference, not student reading; at Senior band it is required reading.

## Cross-references within the curriculum

- **Module 5 — Bindu Paddhati (Dot Addition).** Uses the *Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyām* "completion to a base" idea. Same family of techniques.
- **Module 7 — Multiplication by 11.** A special-case shortcut at a *different* multiplier. The contrast (×11 always works; Nikhilam only works near a base) is worth a 30-sec mention on Day 8.
- **Module 9 — Subtraction by Complements.** Uses *the same* sūtra (*Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ*) for finding complements. If students have done Module 9, the "deficit from base" idea is already familiar — skip the long explanation on Day 2 and double drills.

## What to tell students about the citation chain

At Middle band, the citation pattern is: "the *Nikhilam* method appears in Tirthaji's *Vedic Mathematics* (1965). The procedural details we use are from *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf* (Vedic Cultural Center, Sammamish)." Both the sūtra name (Sanskrit) AND the modern source (PDF) get cited.

A one-line honest framing for student-facing material: *"This method is published in Tirthaji's 1965 book. The Sanskrit name of the sūtra is real; the claim that the sūtra itself comes from an ancient Vedic text is debated by historians."* Senior-band students will engage with this debate in detail.
