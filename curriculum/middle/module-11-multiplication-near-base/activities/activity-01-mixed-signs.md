# Activity 01 — Mixed-Sign Drill

**Module:** Nikhilam Multiplication · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 5 · **Time:** 20 min

## Type

Paired practice with self-correction.

## Goal

Each student solves 8 mixed-sign Nikhilam problems (one factor above 100, one below), with the partner checking accuracy.

## Materials per pair

- 1 printed problem sheet (8 problems below).
- 1 pen each.
- 1 stopwatch (optional — for self-pacing).

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Print the problem sheet.
- Have the answer key on a separate slip for the pair to self-check after they finish.

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> **Mixed-Sign Drill** — work in pairs.
>
> 1. Solo (8 min): each partner solves all 8 problems individually. Don't peek.
> 2. Pair check (7 min): swap sheets. Partner A reads problem 1 aloud; partner B resolves it from scratch on a fresh strip. Compare answers. If they differ, work out which is right.
> 3. Last 5 min: each student redoes any 3 problems they got wrong, silently.

### Problem sheet

```
1.   102 × 98          5.   97 × 104
2.   104 × 97          6.   105 × 95
3.   103 × 96          7.   101 × 99
4.   106 × 95          8.   108 × 92
```

### Answer key (teacher copy — DON'T print on student sheet)

| Q | Signed parts | Left | Right (signed) | Answer |
|---|--------------|------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | +2, −2 | 100 | −4 | 9996 |
| 2 | +4, −3 | 101 | −12 | 10088 |
| 3 | +3, −4 | 99 | −12 | 9888 |
| 4 | +6, −5 | 101 | −30 | 10070 |
| 5 | −3, +4 | 101 | −12 | 10088 |
| 6 | +5, −5 | 100 | −25 | 9975 |
| 7 | +1, −1 | 100 | −1 | 9999 |
| 8 | +8, −8 | 100 | −64 | 9936 |

## Discussion prompts (last 2 min)

- Which problem took the longest? Why?
- Did you notice that Q7 (`101 × 99`) is exactly `(100+1)(100−1) = 100² − 1²` = 9999? That's the difference-of-squares identity. Nikhilam IS that identity for the special case.
- When excess equals deficit (Q1, Q6, Q7, Q8), the left is always exactly the base. Did you spot this shortcut?

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Partners writing deficits clearly, debating sign carefully | "Wait, +4 × −3 is −12, not +12." |
| Needs intervention | One partner just copies the other; or both ignore signs | "I got 10100, you got 10088 — which is it?" → great teachable moment |

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try `121 × 79` (large mixed gap; difference of squares — answer 9559). Try `1003 × 997` (mixed, near 1000 — answer 999991).
- **Tier down:** Stay with `102 × 99`, `103 × 98`, `101 × 99` (small differences) until the sign care is automatic.

## Safety / sensitivity

- No timed competition between pairs publicly. Pairs are self-pacing.

## Clean-up

- Worksheets collected (for the teacher to spot-check before Day 6).
