# Project Brief — Module 11 Nikhilam Multiplication (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Day 9 (planning) + home work · **Presentation:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 40 (project 30 + presentation 10)

## The project

Each pair (or individual, with teacher approval) chooses ONE of three formats:

### Format 1: 60-second teaching video

Make a short video that teaches Nikhilam multiplication to a Grade 4 or 5 student. Constraints:
- Total length: 60 seconds (±10 sec).
- Must show: **at least one** worked example clearly (deficits, cross-subtract, right-part with pad, final answer).
- Bonus: include both a both-below-100 example AND a both-above-100 example.
- Cite the source (PDF + sūtra name) in the title slide or closing credit.

### Format 2: Printed workbook

Produce a 20-problem workbook for a Grade 4 or 5 self-learner with Nikhilam multiplication. Constraints:
- 20 problems organised in 4 difficulty levels (5 problems each).
  - Level 1: single-digit × single-digit (base 10).
  - Level 2: both below 100 (no overflow).
  - Level 3: both below or both above 100 (mix).
  - Level 4: mixed-sign + near-1000.
- Each level includes ONE fully worked example.
- Answer key on the back page.
- Title page with cited source (PDF + sūtra name).
- Optional: a 1-page "when NOT to use this" note (from Day 8).

### Format 3: Comparative speed-test data project

Time 5–10 people (family, schoolmates) on a small set of multiplication problems, comparing long multiplication vs. Nikhilam. Constraints:
- 4–6 problems: a mix of Nikhilam-suitable (e.g. `97 × 96`, `103 × 104`, `998 × 997`) and Nikhilam-unsuitable (e.g. `47 × 53`).
- A table of names (or codes), problem solved, time using standard, time using Nikhilam (if applicable).
- A plot or visual comparison.
- A 1-paragraph interpretation of the data — including any surprising findings (e.g. *"Nikhilam was only faster when both numbers were near 100; for `47 × 53` it was slower"*).
- Cite the source PDF.

## Why this matters

You're moving from *learner* to *teacher* (or *researcher*). The discipline of explaining or measuring forces you to understand more deeply.

You'll also learn the meta-lesson: the Nikhilam method is fast in its niche and slow outside it. A good project shows both.

## What success looks like

A strong project will:
- Demonstrate the Nikhilam method accurately (no arithmetic errors).
- Be pitched for the intended audience (a 9-year-old for video and workbook; a peer reader for data).
- Show the pair's own thinking.
- Be clearly communicated.
- Acknowledge the *limit* of the method (when it's faster, when it's not).

## Working in pairs

- Pair work strongly encouraged.
- Individual allowed for the workbook or video format.
- Groups of 3 acceptable — each member must have a distinct role.

## Citation rules

- Format: `<PDF filename>` (e.g. *Vedic Mathematics Batch1.pdf*).
- Naming the sūtra (*Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ*) is encouraged.
