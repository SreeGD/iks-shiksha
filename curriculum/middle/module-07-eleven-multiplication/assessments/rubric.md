## Format-specific notes

- **Video projects:** the 60-second video itself is the artefact; the 2-min presentation explains the design choices ("we chose 87 × 11 because it's the dramatic carry case").
- **Workbook projects:** the workbook is the artefact; the 2-min presentation walks through the 2 hardest problems.
- **Proof poster projects:** the poster IS the artefact; the 2-min presentation IS the proof walked through aloud. This is the most algebra-heavy format.

## What counts as "the algebraic justification"

We're looking for the chain of derivation, not just the final identity. A complete answer shows:

```
(10a + b) × 11
    = (10a + b)(10 + 1)
    = 100a + 10b + 10a + b
    = 100a + 10(a+b) + b.
```

Three intermediate steps. Each can be a line of working in the project.

At least ONE citation must appear in your project — visible.

Format examples:

For the proof-poster format, the citation can be a single line at the bottom; for video, in the closing credit; for workbook, on the title page.
