# Project Brief — Module 7 Eleven Multiplication (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Day 9 · **Presentation:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 35 (project 25 + presentation 10)

## The project

Each pair (or individual, with teacher approval) chooses ONE of three formats:

### Format 1: 60-second teaching video

Make a short video that teaches the ×11 trick to a Grade 4 student. Constraints:
- Total length: 60 seconds (±10 sec).
- Must show: at least 3 worked examples — one no-carry 2-digit, one carry case, one 3-digit.
- Closing slide must cite Tirthaji (1965) AND a SutraGanita PDF.

### Format 2: Printed workbook

Produce a 25-problem workbook with the ×11 trick (plus 5 *Anurūpyeṇa* problems), designed for a Grade 4–5 self-learner. Constraints:
- 25 problems organised in 4 difficulty levels: 7 no-carry 2-digit, 7 carry 2-digit, 6 three-digit, 5 *Anurūpyeṇa* (×12–×19).
- Each level includes ONE fully worked example.
- Answer key on the back page.
- Title page with cited source.

### Format 3: Proof poster

Produce a one-page (A3) "proof poster" deriving (10*a* + *b*) × 11 = 100*a* + 10(*a* + *b*) + *b*. Constraints:
- The full derivation (at least 4 lines).
- Three worked examples illustrating: no-carry, carry, cascading carry.
- A 3–4 sentence "in plain English" explanation in your own words.
- Bottom-strip citation of Tirthaji (1965) and the SutraGanita sūtra list PDF.

## Why this matters

You're moving from *learner* to *teacher* (or *publisher*, or *prover*). Each role forces you to understand more deeply.

The proof poster is the format that requires you to *own* the algebra. We recommend it if you struggled on Day 3.

## What success looks like

A strong project will:
- Demonstrate the ×11 trick (or its scaled cousins) accurately, no arithmetic errors.
- Show the algebraic identity OR derive it in detail.
- Be pitched for the intended audience.
- Cite the sources properly.
- Show your own thinking, not just rephrase classroom examples.

## Working in pairs

- Pair work strongly encouraged.
- Individual allowed for the workbook or poster format.
- Groups of 3 acceptable — each member must have a distinct role.

## Citation rules

- At minimum **one** citation must appear in your project — visible.
- Format: `Tirthaji, *Vedic Mathematics* (1965), sūtra 1` OR `<PDF filename>, <page or section>` (e.g. *Microsoft Word - sutras1.pdf*).
- See `sources.md` for the list of available references.
