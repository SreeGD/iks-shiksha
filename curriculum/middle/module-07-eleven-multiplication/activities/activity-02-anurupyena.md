# Activity 02 — *Anurūpyeṇa* Drill (×12 to ×19)

**Module:** ×11 (*Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa*) · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 6 · **Time:** 20 min

## Type

Solo + paired drill, mixed multipliers

## Goal

Students mentally multiply 2-digit numbers by 12, 13, 14, 17, 19 using the *Anurūpyeṇa* pattern, with at least 80% accuracy.

## Materials per pair

- 1 printed drill sheet (16 problems below)
- 1 stopwatch
- Pen each

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> ***Anurūpyeṇa* Drill** — work in pairs.
>
> **Round 1 (5 min, solo):** Solve all 16 problems. No writing intermediate steps unless absolutely needed. Aim for the answer only.
>
> **Round 2 (5 min, paired):** Partner-A reads problem 1. Partner-B answers within 10 seconds. Swap. Continue alternating through all 16.
>
> **Round 3 (5 min, mixed-multiplier):** Teacher displays 8 new problems on the board, mixing all multipliers from ×11 to ×19. Class answers in chorus.
>
> **Round 4 (5 min, the hardest):** 3 problems with ×19 — the worst case. Show your working on paper this time.

### Drill sheet (16 problems)

```
×12 problems:
1.  23 × 12         5.  41 × 12         9.  56 × 12       13.  78 × 12

×13 problems:
2.  24 × 13         6.  37 × 13        10.  45 × 13       14.  62 × 13

×14 problems:
3.  21 × 14         7.  35 × 14        11.  47 × 14       15.  53 × 14

×17 + ×19 (harder):
4.  16 × 17         8.  23 × 17        12.  27 × 19       16.  31 × 19
```

### Answer key (teacher copy)

```
×12:  276, 492, 672, 936
×13:  312, 481, 585, 806
×14:  294, 490, 658, 742
×17 / ×19:  272, 391, 513, 589
```

## Worked sanity check (give to slow pairs)

```
56 × 12:
   units = 6·2 = 12 → 2, carry 1
   tens = 5·2 + 6 + 1 = 17 → 7, carry 1
   hundreds = 5 + 1 = 6
   → 672 ✓ (check: 56×12 = 56×10 + 56×2 = 560 + 112 = 672)

23 × 17:
   units = 3·7 = 21 → 1, carry 2
   tens = 2·7 + 3 + 2 = 19 → 9, carry 1
   hundreds = 2 + 1 = 3
   → 391 ✓
```

## Discussion prompts (last 2 min)

- Which multiplier was easiest? (Most students say ×12.) Why?
- Which was the hardest? (Most say ×19.) Why? (Bigger *k* means bigger carries.)
- How is the ×11 case the "easy" version of this same pattern?

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Pair calmly working through carries on paper | "Units 12, carry 1; tens 17 plus 6 plus 1 is 24, carry 2..." |
| Needs intervention | Pair guessing answers; no working shown | "I don't know, maybe 600?" — "Same." |

## Safety / sensitivity

- Same as Activity 01 — no public leaderboards.
- Some students will hit a wall at ×17/×19. That's expected — these are genuinely harder. Accept partial completion.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Try 3-digit × 13 problems (e.g. 234 × 13). Same pattern, more digits.
- **Tier down:** Stick to ×12 today. Build that fluency before adding ×13, ×14.

## Clean-up

- Worksheets collected (teacher spot-checks before Day 7).
