# Activity 01 — Mental ×11 Speed Race

**Module:** ×11 (*Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa*) · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 5 · **Time:** 20 min

## Type

Paired + group quick-fire drill

## Goal

Each student does at least 20 mental ×11 problems (mixed difficulty) in under 4 minutes, with peer-verified accuracy.

## Materials per pair

- 1 printed flashcard deck of 24 problems (cut into separate cards if possible)
- 1 stopwatch
- Pen + paper for tally

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Print and pre-cut the 24-problem flashcard deck. Mix the difficulty (8 no-carry, 8 carry, 8 three-digit).

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> **Speed Race** — work in pairs across 4 rounds.
>
> **Round 1 (5 min, solo):** Take 20 cards. Solve each silently in under 5 seconds. Self-check three random ones with long multiplication.
>
> **Round 2 (5 min, paired):** Partner-A reads card 1 aloud. Partner-B answers in under 5 seconds. Swap roles. Continue until you've each done 10 problems out loud.
>
> **Round 3 (5 min, group chorus):** Teacher displays a problem on the board. Class shouts the answer together. 10 problems total.
>
> **Round 4 (5 min, quick-fire):** Teacher calls a 2-digit number. First student to call back the ×11 product gets a tally mark. Light competition — rotate the "first responder" so no single student dominates.

### Flashcard deck (24 problems)

**Easy (no-carry, 2-digit, 8 cards):**

```
12 × 11    23 × 11    34 × 11    52 × 11
61 × 11    16 × 11    27 × 11    35 × 11
```

**Medium (carry, 2-digit, 8 cards):**

```

```

**Hard (3-digit, 8 cards):**

```

```

## Discussion prompts (last 2 min)

- Which type was hardest? (Most students say "carry" or "3-digit.")
- Did anyone discover a faster sub-trick?
- Are you noticeably faster than Day 1? (Yes — name a number.)

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Pair alternating, one card per ~5 sec | "47 × 11!" — "517!" — "58 × 11!" — "638!" |
| Needs intervention | One partner stuck > 30 sec | Long silences; partner doing the math TO them, not WITH them |

## Safety / sensitivity

- Round 4 quick-fire risks dominating the room. If one student gets >4 tally marks while others have 0, switch to "must wait if you've answered the last 2."
- No public leaderboards; tallies are private to each student.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Hard deck only. Try 4-digit × 11 (sample: 1234, 2345, 4567).
- **Tier down:** Easy deck only. Build no-carry fluency before adding carries.

## Clean-up

- Worksheets kept by students. Flashcards return to the bag for re-use next year.
