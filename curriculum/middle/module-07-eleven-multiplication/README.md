# Module 7 — Multiplication by 11 (*Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa*)

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Execute** the ×11 "split-and-add" trick on any 2-digit number, with and without the carry case, in under 3 seconds per problem.
2. **Extend** the trick to 3-digit numbers using the running "neighbour-sum" pattern (e.g. 234 × 11 → 2_5_7_4 → 2574).
3. **Prove** algebraically why the trick works: (10*a* + *b*) × 11 = 100*a* + 10(*a* + *b*) + *b*.
4. **Apply** the *Anurūpyeṇa* extension to multiply 2-digit numbers by 12, 13, …, 19.
5. **Compare** the ×11 shortcut against standard long multiplication and identify when each is faster.
6. **Locate** the trick honestly in the history of Indian mathematics — as Bharati Krishna Tirthaji's 1965 reformulation, not a verifiable Vedic teaching.

## Prerequisites

- Confident with addition of two single-digit numbers up to 18 (carry handling).
- Comfortable with standard long multiplication of 2-digit × 1-digit numbers.
- Knows what a variable is and can read expressions like 10*a* + *b*.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md`.

- *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* — "by one more than the previous" (1st of Tirthaji's 16 sūtras)
- *Anurūpyeṇa* — "proportionally" (sub-sūtra used to extend ×11 to ×12 … ×19)
- *sūtra* — short formula / aphorism
- *Tirthaji* — Bharati Krishna Tirthaji (1884–1960), author of *Vedic Mathematics* (1965)
