# Module 7 — ×11 Multiplication (Darśana)

**Sanskrit:** *Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa* · **Band:** Middle (6-8) · **Duration:** 60 min · **Path:** Darśana (Workshop)

Mental multiplication by 11 from 20th-century Indian arithmetic.

## Opening hook · 5 min

Show 87 × 11 mentally. Most students reach for long multiplication. *"Or — split, add, with carry. Today's lesson: ×11 mental method, with the carry case included."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**×11 method:**

For (10a + b) × 11 = 100a + 10(a+b) + b.

Procedure:
1. Split the 2 digits: a _ b
2. Add them: a+b
3. Put the sum in the middle: a (a+b) b

**Carry case:** if a+b ≥ 10, carry the tens digit to the first slot.
- 87 × 11 = 8_(8+7)_7 = 8_15_7 → carry: (8+1) 5 7 = 957.

**Extension:** 3-digit × 11: abc → a (a+b) (b+c) c.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Live speed race: every student solves 5 problems mentally. Time it. Average ~3 seconds per problem with practice. Compare with the long-multiplication time for the same problems.

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Paraphrase Tirthaji 1965: *"Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa — 'by one more than the previous.' The sūtra-name for the ×11 (and related) methods."*

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Mental-math sprint.** Each student does 20 ×11 problems in 3 minutes. Best score wins.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"When is ×11 mental method faster than the long method? When is it slower? Why?"*
