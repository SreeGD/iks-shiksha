# Project Rubric — Module 10 Auṣadhi (Middle)

**Project marks:** 15 (combined with the summative quiz's 25 for a module total of 40).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Conceptual accuracy (active compound, family, traditional use)** | All six cards have correct Sanskrit + botanical + family + active compound. Plus: traditional uses are *specific* (e.g. "ajwain water for indigestion"), not vague. | 5–6 cards fully accurate; minor errors on 1 card. | 3–4 cards fully accurate; rest have meaningful errors (wrong compound, wrong family). | Fewer than 3 cards accurate. | × 2 |
| **Safety framing** | Each card has a specific safety note — not vague ("be careful") but specific ("neem oil is hepatotoxic; do not ingest"). The neem card is clear and the essential-oil warning is explicit somewhere in the deck. | Each card has a safety note; most are specific. | Some cards have safety notes; some vague; the neem warning is present but not specific. | Safety notes vague or missing. | × 2 |
| **Identification features (visual + sensory)** | Each card has 3 *distinct* features — leaf shape, smell, family characteristic, etc. Sketches are accurate enough that a stranger could identify the herb. | Each card has 3 features; most sketches are accurate. | Features sometimes redundant ("green leaf, has a smell"); sketches simplified. | Features missing or vague; sketches unrecognisable. | × 1 |
| **Connection to doṣa framework** | At least one card explicitly notes the herb's *vīrya* (heating/cooling) AND links it to a doṣa (vāta/pitta/kapha) effect. | Some cards note heating/cooling; doṣa link present somewhere in the deck. | Heating/cooling mentioned vaguely. | No mention of vīrya or doṣa. | × 1 |
| **Source citation** | The deck references the *Auṣadhi-sūkta* (RV 10.97) or the *Caraka Saṁhitā* (Sūtrasthāna) at least once, accurately. Plus: at least one phytochemistry reference (named compound) per card. | Citation present but only at one level (scripture or compound, not both). | Citation vague ("ancient texts say..."). | No citation. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Cards are visually clean, IAST diacritics correct, italicisation correct for botanical names, handwriting legible. | Clear communication; one or two craft issues. | Some craft issues — multiple typos, missing italics, illegible patches. | Hard to read; multiple issues impede understanding. | × 1 |

## Score calculation

`Project score = (sum of criterion scores × weights) × (15 / 32)`

Maximum raw score: 32. Multiply by 15/32 = 0.46875 to get out of 15.

## Self-reflection (folded into project score)

The reflection slip (submitted with the deck) asks three questions. If the reflection is missing or perfunctory, the **Safety framing** mark drops by 1 (this is the most important takeaway of the module, and a perfunctory reflection suggests it didn't land).

## Common decision points

- **Group of 3 vs pair**: Acceptable. The third member must contribute at least 2 cards. Marks are equal across the group unless one member visibly didn't contribute.
- **Bonus 7th card**: Bonus card adds up to +1 mark to the **Conceptual accuracy** criterion (capped at 4). Doesn't change the rubric scale.
- **Late submission**: −1 mark per school day, capped at −3.
- **Plagiarism / web copying**: If a card is closely copied from a web source not cited, the deck goes to 0 on **Source citation** AND on **Conceptual accuracy**. A 1-day extension is offered for a redo.

## Marking process

- Mark in real-time during the Day 10 presentation (2 min present + 30 sec Q&A + 30 sec marking).
- Write one sentence of feedback per pair after their presentation.
- Return marked decks (or photos of them, if archived) within 2 school days.

## Note on the "Exceeds" tier

A deck reaches Exceeds when it's not just complete but **useful**. The clearest signal: another teacher could pick up the deck and use it to teach a 5-minute lesson on any of the six herbs. If you find yourself wanting to borrow the deck for the staff room, that's 4.
