# Activity 01 — Smell-Identification Challenge

**Module:** Auṣadhi · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** woven through Days 2–7 (mainly Day 7 for the assessment version) · **Time:** 15 min total

## Type

Sensory identification, individual then paired

## Goal

Each student can identify all six module herbs by smell alone, eyes closed.

## Materials per group

- 6 small opaque cups (paper cups work) per group of 4 students — one cup per herb
- Crushed/torn samples of each herb pressed into the bottom of each cup:
  - tulsi leaf
  - dried neem leaf (sealed cup — smell through small hole only)
  - turmeric powder
  - ginger powder OR a slice of fresh ginger
  - ajwain seeds (whole, lightly crushed)
  - mint leaf
- 1 numbered worksheet per student

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Label each cup with a number (1–6). Same numbering across groups, randomised so students can't compare with their neighbour.
- Keep a master key for grading.
- Cover cups with a piece of paper held by a rubber band, with a small hole pierced for smelling.

## Instructions (student-facing)

> **Smell-Identification Challenge** — individual round, then pair discussion.
>
> 1. Take the 6 cups assigned to your table.
> 2. Smell each cup through the hole in the cover. Do **not** open the cups. Do **not** taste anything.
> 3. On your worksheet, write the Sanskrit name and one feature you noticed for each numbered cup.
> 4. After 5 minutes — turn to your pair partner and compare answers. **Don't change yours.** Just compare.
> 5. Then the teacher reveals the answers. Self-score on the back of the worksheet.

## Discussion (last 3 min)

- Which herb was hardest to identify? Why?
- Was there a herb you smelled and immediately knew? Why was it easy?
- Did anyone confuse two herbs? Which pair? What do they have in common?

(Common confusion: tulsi and mint, both square-stem Lamiaceae; tulsi and clove, both eugenol-dominated; ajwain and dried thyme, both thymol-dominated.)

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Students smell each cup, look thoughtful, write a few words per herb | "This smells like clove — must be tulsi" / "Sharp! Like medicine. Ajwain?" |
| Needs intervention | Students rushing through; just writing "I don't know" | Silence; or "I'll just copy you" |

## Safety / sensitivity

- **No opening cups.** No tasting.
- **Neem cup smell only.** No touching the inside.
- **Allergy check before activity** — students with asthma or known plant allergies can opt out of smelling specific cups.
- **Don't put cups directly under anyone's nose** — let students choose their own distance.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** A pair gets 8 cups (the 6 plus 2 confusers — sweet basil, dried thyme). They must identify which are the 6 module herbs and which are imposters.
- **Tier down:** Only 4 cups (tulsi, ginger powder, mint, ajwain) — the most distinctive smells.

## Clean-up

- Seal all cups for next class (this activity can be reused for revision).
- Teacher checks if any cup has degraded (mint dries out fast — refresh as needed).
- Worksheets stored in the project folder.
