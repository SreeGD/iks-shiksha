# Activity 02 — Sort by *Vīrya*

**Module:** Auṣadhi · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 8 · **Time:** 15 min (within Day 8's 45 min)

## Type

Card-sort, paired, with defended reasoning

## Goal

Each pair correctly sorts 12 kitchen items into heating (*uṣṇa*) / cooling (*śīta*) / not-sure, and articulates the **reasoning** behind their classification — using both sensory observation and the Ayurvedic logic of taste-and-effect.

## Materials per pair

- 12 cards, each with one item name (no answer printed):
  - **6 module herbs:** tulasī, nimba, haridrā, adraka, yavānī, pudīnā
  - **6 kitchen-pantry items:** rice, ghee, lemon, ice cream, red chilli, coriander seed
- 1 sorting mat with three columns labelled *Uṣṇa* (heating) / *Śīta* (cooling) / *Not sure*
- 1 worksheet per pair (below)

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Pre-shuffle each pair's 12 cards.
- Have the sorting mats ready on each desk.

## Instructions (student-facing)

> **Sort by *Vīrya*** — work in pairs.
>
> 1. Take your 12 cards and your sorting mat.
> 2. For each item, decide together: heating (*uṣṇa*), cooling (*śīta*), or not sure.
> 3. Fill in the worksheet — name each item, your choice, and **one sentence explaining why**.
> 4. **Disagree with your partner?** Argue it out before you fill in the row. Use sensory evidence ("ginger feels warm when I eat it") or the doṣa logic ("bitterness reduces pitta, so neem is functionally cooling").
> 5. Last 3 min: gallery walk — visit two other pairs' mats. Note one card they placed differently from you.

## Teacher key (do not show to students until after sort)

| Item | Classification | Note |
|------|------------------|------|
| tulasī | *uṣṇa* (heating) | Eugenol-dominated aromatic warming |
| nimba | *śīta* (cooling) | Functional: bitterness reduces pitta |
| haridrā | mild *uṣṇa* (heating) | Curcumin; some texts call it neutral |
| adraka | *uṣṇa* (heating) | Gingerol activates TRPV1 (warm) |
| yavānī | *uṣṇa* (heating) | Thymol pungent stimulant |
| pudīnā | *śīta* (cooling) | Menthol activates TRPM8 (cool) |
| rice | *śīta* (cooling) | White rice in classical texts; basmati specifically *śīta* |
| ghee | *śīta* (cooling) | Classical Ayurvedic classification — interesting because it's warming sensation but cooling potency |
| lemon | *uṣṇa* (heating) | Sour and heating in classical texts (some debate; some texts call it neutral) |
| ice cream | *śīta* (cooling) | Both sensory and functional |
| red chilli | *uṣṇa* (heating) | Capsaicin activates TRPV1 |
| coriander seed | *śīta* (cooling) | One of the few *śīta* spices — used to balance heat |

**Note for the teacher:** the "interesting" ones are **ghee** (warming feeling but cooling potency in classical texts) and **lemon** (sour and somewhat heating despite cooling lemonade culture). These are where students will argue most productively. The argument *is* the learning.

## Discussion prompts (last 3 min, whole class)

- Which item caused the most disagreement in your pair?
- Did anyone classify ghee as cooling? Why might that be?
- What's the difference between "feels warm/cool" and "has a warming/cooling effect over time"? (This is the sensory vs functional point.)

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Two partners pointing at cards, both holding the sorting mat, sometimes laughing about ghee | "But ghee feels warm!" / "Yes but it cools pitta..." / "Maybe both? Let's go with cooling but write 'tricky'" |
| Needs intervention | One partner sorting silently; cards being placed without discussion | Silence or "I don't care, put it anywhere" |

## Safety / sensitivity

- No food handling in this activity (it's card-only).
- If a student raises dietary or religious restrictions about any item (e.g. ghee, ice cream), acknowledge and continue — the sort is a classification exercise, not a food choice.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Add 4 more cards — *aśvagandhā* (heating; reduces vāta), *brāhmī* (cooling; pitta-reducing), green tea (cooling), black pepper (heating). Pair must research one of the unfamiliar items before classifying.
- **Tier down:** Use only 8 cards (the 6 herbs + ice cream + chilli) — the most clearly classifiable.

## Clean-up

- Cards collected and stored in card bag for future use.
- Worksheets stapled into the student workbook.
