# Herbs — Auṣadhi
## Six common kitchen-and-garden healing plants
### Middle band — Grades 6–8


---
## What this module is about


Six widely-available herbs — Tulsī, Neem, Turmeric, Ginger, Ajwain, Mint — studied through botanical identification, traditional uses from Āyurveda, modern phytochemistry (active compounds), and safety. The module emphasises that kitchen amounts are safe, but therapeutic doses require a qualified practitioner.

## Key Sanskrit terms


- *auṣadhi* — medicinal plant / herb

- *tulasī* — holy basil — *Ocimum tenuiflorum*

- *nimba* — neem — *Azadirachta indica*

- *haridrā* — turmeric — *Curcuma longa*

- *ārdraka* — ginger — *Zingiber officinale*

- *yavānī* — ajwain — *Trachyspermum ammi*

- *pudīnā* — mint — *Mentha* spp.

## 10-day arc


---
## Day 1 — What is Auṣadhi?

Define auṣadhi and recognise the six herbs of this module.


---
## Day 2 — Tulsī — Holy Basil

Identify tulasī by leaf and smell; name the active compound (eugenol).


---
## Day 3 — Neem — Bitter Healer

Identify nimba; learn about azadirachtin; understand high-dose safety warning.


---
## Day 4 — Turmeric — Golden Root

Distinguish fresh root vs powder; identify curcumin as active compound.


---
## Day 5 — Ginger — Warming Root

Distinguish fresh vs dried; gingerol vs shogaol.


---
## Day 6 — Ajwain — Carom Seed

Recognise seeds and leaves; identify thymol; traditional digestive use.


---
## Day 7 — Mint — Cooling Leaf

Recognise several Mentha varieties; identify menthol.


---
## Day 8 — Herbs and the Doṣas

Categorise the six herbs by heating/cooling effect (links to Module 4).


---
## Day 9 — Build the Class Deck

Each student contributes one identification card to the class library.


---
## Day 10 — Final Share and Assessment

Present the class herb deck; take summative quiz.

---
## Activity


**Blind Smell-Test Identification**


Students close their eyes and try to identify each herb by smell. They record their guess + observation, then check.

---
## Project


**Class Herb Identification Deck**


Each student creates one identification card for one of the six herbs: Sanskrit/botanical name, leaf drawing, smell description, three traditional uses, one active compound, one safety note. Cards combined into class library.
