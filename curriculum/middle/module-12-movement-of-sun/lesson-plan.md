## Pacing notes

- Day 1's shadow-stick observation needs a sunny mid-morning window. If your timetable lands the lesson in early morning or after a rain, move the outdoor part to a later day and use the time for the explanation.
- The torch-and-globe demo (Day 2) works best with the room blackout-curtained. If your room doesn't black out, demonstrate the day before in a darker space and take photos to project.
- Day 7 (*sāyana* / *nirayana*) is intellectually the densest. Don't squeeze it.
- Project work (Days 8–9) assumes pair vs. individual is decided by Day 5.

## Suggested running theme

Open each class with the same prompt on the board: **"Where was the Sun this morning when you left for school?"** (Direction: east-ish — but more north of east in summer, more south of east in winter.) Two students share for 30 seconds each. By Day 10 the class will have a felt sense that the Sun does not rise from the exact same spot every day.

## Formative vs. summative weight

- Formative quizzes (Days 3 and 5) are **not graded** — they are diagnostic. Use them to decide who needs scaffolding on Day 7.
- Summative quiz (Day 10) — see rubric in `assessments/rubric.md`. Combined with project, total 40 marks.

## What students leave the module with

- A completed paper *rāśi*-wheel with their own labelling.
- The 12 Sanskrit *rāśi* names with English / Greek-derived equivalents.
- A diagram in their notebook showing axial tilt and the four turning points of the year.
- A felt sense that "what day is *Makara Saṁkrānti*?" and "what day is the winter solstice?" are answers to two slightly different questions.
