# Module 12 — Movement of the Sun

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min (≈ 2 weeks)

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Describe** the Sun's apparent annual path along the ecliptic (*krānti-vṛtta*) and explain how Earth's ~23.5° axial tilt produces the seasons.
2. **Distinguish** *uttarāyaṇa* (northward course) from *dakṣiṇāyana* (southward course) and identify the two solstices and two equinoxes (*viṣuva*) on a calendar.
3. **Recite** the 12 *rāśis* (Meṣa through Mīna) in order and locate the Sun approximately in each *rāśi* during the calendar year.
4. **Define** *saṁkrānti* as the Sun's transit from one *rāśi* to the next, and explain why **Makara Saṁkrānti** (~14 January) and **Karka Saṁkrānti** (~16 July) are tied to the solstices in the traditional reckoning.
5. **Compare** *sāyana* (tropical) and *nirayana* (sidereal) reference frames, and state that the gap between them — the *ayanāṁśa* — is now ~24° because of precession.
6. **Map** the Indian framework to its cross-cultural cousins (Greek zodiac, Egyptian solar calendar, Gregorian calendar) without claiming priority either way.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the four cardinal directions (N, S, E, W).
- Awareness that the Earth rotates (day/night) and revolves around the Sun (year). NCERT Class 6 Geography Chapter 3 covers this.
- Optional: prior exposure to Module 6 (Moon Phases & Tithi). Useful but not required.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md` for full entries. New terms introduced this module:

- *Sūrya* — the Sun
- *krānti-vṛtta* — the ecliptic
- *ayana* — half-year course (northward / southward)
- *uttarāyaṇa* — northward course
- *dakṣiṇāyana* — southward course
- *viṣuva* — equinox
- *rāśi* — sign of the zodiac (12 of them)
- *saṁkrānti* — the Sun's crossing into a new *rāśi*
- *sāyana* / *nirayana* — tropical / sidereal reference frames
- *ayanāṁśa* — the offset between the two frames (~24° today)

## Materials checklist (whole module)

- Globe (any size) + a strong torch or table lamp
- A compass (magnetic or a phone app)
- A 30 cm vertical stick (gnomon) for the schoolyard
- A printable year-calendar with dates marked
- Chart paper / printable circle (for the *rāśi*-wheel)
- Worksheets in the activity folder
- Sunglasses for ANY observer who steps outside — **no direct looking at the Sun**

## Sources used

See `sources.md` for primary and modern references. Key anchors:

- **Sūrya-siddhānta** — Sanskrit astronomical treatise (compiled c. 5th–10th c. CE) describing solar motion, *ayana*, and *rāśi* divisions.
- **Āryabhaṭīya** of Āryabhaṭa (499 CE) — early Indian treatment of precession-related corrections and spherical astronomy.
- **NCERT Geography Class IX, Chapter 3** ("Drainage" / "Climate") and **NCERT Physics Class XI, Chapter 8** (Gravitation) — modern grade-appropriate references for axial tilt and orbital motion.

## Safety

- **Never look at the Sun directly.** Even a quick glance can cause permanent retinal damage. The shadow-stick observation in Day 1 is the ONLY safe way to record the Sun's position in this module. No solar viewing without certified solar filters.
- Outdoor activity (Day 1) requires a shaded standpoint for the observer; only the gnomon's shadow is studied.
