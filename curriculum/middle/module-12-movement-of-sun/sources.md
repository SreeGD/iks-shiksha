# Sources — Module 12 Movement of the Sun (Middle)

This module does not use RAG-retrieved citations (the vidya-karana corpus does not cover *jyotiṣa*). All references below are real published works; only paraphrased material is taken from them, no invented quotations.

## Primary Sanskrit sources (paraphrased only, not quoted)

| Source | Used in | What it contributes |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **Sūrya-siddhānta** (Sanskrit astronomical compendium; modern critical editions ~5th–10th c. CE in their current form) | Days 3, 5, 6, 7 | The classical Indian treatment of solar motion, the *rāśi* divisions, *uttarāyaṇa* / *dakṣiṇāyana*, and the framework for calculating *saṁkrānti* dates. |
| **Āryabhaṭīya** of Āryabhaṭa (499 CE) | Day 8 (Senior reference) | One of the earliest extant Indian astronomical treatises; treats the Earth as rotating, gives values for the length of the year, and contains the *gola* (sphere) chapter. |
| **Bṛhat Saṁhitā** of Varāhamihira (6th c. CE) | Day 6 | Encyclopedic compendium with chapters on the *saṁkrāntis* and their interpretation. |

These works are widely available in scholarly translation (Burgess 1860 for Sūrya-siddhānta; Shukla 1976 for Āryabhaṭīya; M. Ramakrishna Bhat 1981 for Bṛhat Saṁhitā). The classroom does not need student-facing direct quotes for Middle band — a paraphrase with the title and a translator's reference is sufficient.

**Citation rule reminder (Middle band):** at least ONE paraphrase with reference appears in `days/day-05-rashis.md` and `days/day-06-sankranti.md`. We do not quote any specific verse number we cannot personally verify; paraphrasing the text's overall content is fine.

## Modern science references

- **NCERT Class 6 Geography, Chapter 3** — "Motions of the Earth." Introductory treatment of rotation, revolution, axial tilt, and the seasons.
- **NCERT Class 9 Geography, Chapter 4** — "Climate." Touches the Tropic of Cancer / Tropic of Capricorn and seasonality across India.
- **NCERT Class 11 Physics, Chapter 8** — "Gravitation." Background on planetary orbital motion.
- **NASA's solar-system educational pages** ("Solstices and Equinoxes," "How Earth's Tilt Causes Seasons") — useful classroom diagrams. URLs change; search by topic name on the NASA Science Education domain.

## Historical context references (teacher reading, optional)

- Plofker, K. (2009). *Mathematics in India*. Princeton University Press. — Sets the historical context of Indian astronomy alongside other traditions; chapter on Āryabhaṭa, Brahmagupta, and the Sūrya-siddhānta.
- Pingree, D. (1981). *Jyotiḥśāstra: Astral and Mathematical Literature*. A History of Indian Literature vol. 6. Otto Harrassowitz. — The reference catalogue of Indian astronomical and astrological texts; describes the *rāśi* system in its historical context.
- Subbarayappa, B. V. & Sarma, K. V. (1985). *Indian Astronomy: A Source-Book*. Nehru Centre, Bombay. — Translations of key passages from Indian astronomical texts.

These are reference works for the teacher, not assigned to students.

## On the "sidereal vs tropical" question (Day 7)

The current *ayanāṁśa* (gap between *sāyana* and *nirayana* zodiacs) is approximately 24° (Lahiri *ayanāṁśa*, the value adopted by the Government of India in its civil almanac). This corresponds to the fact that ~1700 years have passed since the two coincided. The drift rate is ~50.3 seconds of arc per year (about 1° per 72 years) due to the precession of the Earth's rotation axis — a 25,800-year cycle.

These are standard astronomical values, available in any modern positional-astronomy textbook (e.g. Smart, W. M., *Textbook on Spherical Astronomy*, Cambridge UP).

## What we will NOT cite

- We do not cite specific verse numbers from the Sūrya-siddhānta or Āryabhaṭīya in student materials. The numbering varies across editions and translations; without RAG verification we paraphrase only.
- We do not cite any Purāṇic verse for *uttarāyaṇa* / *dakṣiṇāyana* in student materials. These terms appear in many texts; nothing student-facing needs a verse-by-verse anchor at Middle band.
