Solar year, solstices, equinoxes, and the 12 rāśis.

## Opening hook · 5 min

Project a sunrise-direction composite (12 photos, one per month, same location). *"The sunrise drifts north and south through the year. Why?"* (Earth's axial tilt.) *"Today: the Indian framework — uttarāyaṇa, dakṣiṇāyana, and the 12 rāśis."*

---

## Core idea · 15 min

**The Sun's annual journey:**

1. **Axial tilt** (23.5°) → Sun's apparent path drifts north/south.
2. **Uttarāyaṇa** (Dec 21 → June 21): Sun rises further north each day.
3. **Dakṣiṇāyana** (June 21 → Dec 21): Sun rises further south each day.
4. **Solstices:** June 21 (Sun farthest north), Dec 21 (farthest south).
5. **Equinoxes:** March 21, Sept 22 (Sun crosses the equator, day = night everywhere).
6. **12 rāśis:** the ecliptic divided into 12 × 30° segments. Sun travels through ~1 rāśi per month.
7. **Saṅkrānti:** the day Sun moves from one rāśi to the next. Makara Saṅkrānti (Sun enters Capricorn) ~ Jan 14.

---

## Demonstration · 10 min

Globe + torch demo: tilt the globe, walk torch around. Show how axial tilt creates seasons. Compute today's ayanāṁśa (use a panchanga app).

---

## Source moment · 5 min

Paraphrase Sūrya-siddhānta, ch. 14: *"The Sun travels along the ecliptic. In one year it completes one revolution through the 12 rāśis."*

---

## Hands-on activity · 15 min

**Personal solar calendar.** Mark current month, ayanāṁśa, current rāśi, next saṅkrānti. Predict your birthday's rāśi (use a panchanga). Compare with classmates.

---

## Reflection + take-home · 10 min

*"Why does Makara Saṅkrānti (Jan 14) not match the December solstice exactly? What does the gap tell us?"*
