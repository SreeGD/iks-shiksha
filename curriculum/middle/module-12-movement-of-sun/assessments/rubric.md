# Project Rubric — Module 12 Movement of the Sun (Middle)

**Project marks:** 30 (out of 40 total when combined with presentation 10).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Conceptual accuracy** | All 12 *rāśis* in correct order with both Sanskrit and English/Greek names. Both *sāyana* and *nirayana* dates shown for all 4 turning-points. Explanation paragraph correctly links axial tilt to seasons. | All 12 *rāśis* in order; English/Greek correct; 4 turning-points labelled with at least one date system. Minor imprecision OK. | 10–11 *rāśis* correct OR turning-points missing or mislabelled. | Fewer than 10 *rāśis*, OR major errors (e.g. wrong solstice direction). | × 2 |
| **Use of sources** | At least one citation (Sūrya-siddhānta, Bṛhat Saṁhitā, or an NCERT chapter) named in the project AND used correctly in context. Bonus: a paraphrased point from the cited source. | One citation named correctly somewhere on the project. | A source mentioned vaguely ("an old Indian text says...") without specific reference. | No source cited. | × 2 |
| **Sāyana / Nirayana distinction** | Both systems labelled on the wheel; the *ayanāṁśa* offset shown numerically; one sentence explaining why the two differ. | Both systems mentioned; the ~24-day gap shown for at least one turning-point (e.g. Dec 21 vs Jan 14). | One system shown; the other only mentioned in text. | Only one system shown; the other not mentioned. | × 1 |
| **Cross-cultural element** | Compares Sanskrit names to at least TWO other traditions (Greek + Babylonian, or Greek + Gregorian calendar). Shows awareness that the 12-zodiac is a shared inheritance, not exclusively Indian. | Compares Sanskrit names to one other tradition (typically Greek). | Mentions the comparison only in passing. | No cross-cultural element. | × 1 |
| **Originality of expression** | The wheel shows the pair's own design choices — a fresh layout, a surprising visual element, a turn of phrase in the explanation. | Clearly the pair's own work; not copied from a single online template. | Clearly based on a single online template; little independent thinking. | Clearly copied. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals, labelling, and explanation are clean, deliberate, and serve the message. Sanskrit IAST is correctly typed. Spelling correct. | Communicates clearly. One or two craft issues. | Message comes through despite some craft issues. | Hard to follow; multiple craft issues impede understanding. | × 1 |

## Score calculation

`Project score = (sum of criterion scores × weights) × (30 / 32) = sum × 0.9375`

Maximum raw weighted score: 32 (eight weighted points × max 4). Scale to 30.

## Presentation marks (10, on Day 10)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (3 min)** | Within 2:45–3:00 | Within 2:30–3:15 | Within 2:15–3:30 | Outside that range | × 1 |
| **Both partners contribute (or solo presents whole)** | Equal contribution; smooth handoffs | Both partners speak but unequally | One partner dominates | One partner silent | × 1 |
| **Clarity and audibility** | Confident, audible to the back of the room, clear pace | Audible, mostly clear | Audible only in front rows; some mumbling | Mostly inaudible | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 10.

## Self-reflection mark (folded into project score)

The 3 reflection questions on Day 10 are not separately scored — but if the reflection is missing or perfunctory, the **Originality** mark drops by 1.

## Common decision points

- **Group of 3 vs pair:** Acceptable, but the third member must have a clearly distinct sub-role (e.g. visuals + script + cross-cultural research). Marks are equal across the group unless one member visibly didn't contribute.
- **Late submission:** -2 marks per school day, capped at -6.
- **Originality dispute:** If a wheel is clearly copied from an online template (some templates are easily recognised), the project goes to 0 on **Use of sources** AND on **Originality**. A 1-day extension is offered for a redo.

## Marking process

- Mark in real-time during presentation (5 minutes per pair: 3 present + 1 Q&A + 1 marking).
- Write 1-sentence written feedback after each presentation finishes.
- Return marked rubrics within 2 school days.

## Examples — "Meets" vs "Exceeds"

- **Meets:** All 12 *rāśis* labelled; the Sanskrit-Greek correspondence shown; the four turning-points marked with *nirayana* dates; explanation paragraph mentions axial tilt; Sūrya-siddhānta cited; paper is neatly drawn.
- **Exceeds:** Same as above PLUS both *sāyana* and *nirayana* dates shown for all four turning-points; the *ayanāṁśa* (~24°) is shown visually as a small offset between two concentric rings; the explanation paragraph correctly mentions precession; the Babylonian column appears in a small inset comparing names.
