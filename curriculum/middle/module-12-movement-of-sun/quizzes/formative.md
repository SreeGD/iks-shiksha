# Module 12 (Middle) — Formative Quizzes

Two parts: Part A on Day 3 (after *ayana* lesson), Part B on Day 5 (after *rāśis* lesson). Both are diagnostic — not graded. Hand back within 24 hours.

---

## Part A — Day 3 (3 min, 5 questions)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** Write the Sanskrit name for the **northward course** of the Sun.

_______________________

**2.** Which date marks the start of *uttarāyaṇa*?

a) 21 March
b) 21 June
c) 23 September
d) **21 December** ←

**3.** Fill in the blank: "The Sun's noon altitude RISES during *_______________*." (Choose *uttarāyaṇa* or *dakṣiṇāyana*.)

**4.** True or false: India straddles the Tropic of Cancer.   (T / F)

**5.** Why are seasons caused by axial tilt, NOT by Earth's distance from the Sun? (One sentence.)

___________________________________________________________________

___________________________________________________________________

---

### Part A — answer key

| Q | Answer | Notes |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | *uttarāyaṇa* | Spelling tolerated if recognisable; accept "uttarayana" without diacritics. |
| 2 | d) 21 December | The winter solstice. |
| 3 | *uttarāyaṇa* | Northward course = Sun climbs higher each noon. |
| 4 | T | The Tropic of Cancer runs through Bhopal, Ranchi, Kolkata. |
| 5 | Tilt determines how directly sunlight hits us and how many hours of daylight we get. Earth's distance from the Sun barely changes through the year (Earth is actually CLOSER to the Sun in January). | Accept any answer that names tilt + concentrating angle / day length, NOT distance. |

## Part B — Day 5 (5 min, 5 questions, mixed format)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** (2 marks) Write the first FIVE *rāśis* in order:

___________ → ___________ → ___________ → ___________ → ___________

**2.** (3 marks) Match each *rāśi* to its English/Greek equivalent. Draw lines.

```
Meṣa            Capricorn
Karka           Aries
Tulā            Cancer
Makara          Scorpio
Vṛścika         Libra
```

**3.** (2 marks) About how many days does the Sun spend in each *rāśi*?

a) ~7 days
b) ~15 days
c) **~30 days** ←
d) ~60 days

**4.** (1 mark) Name the *rāśi* that the Sun is in during the month containing your birthday (using the *nirayana* table). Use Day 5's table.

____________________________________________________________________

**5.** (2 marks, short answer) The Sanskrit *rāśi* names look very similar to the Greek/English names (Bull, Twins, Crab, Lion, …). Why?

___________________________________________________________________

___________________________________________________________________

---

### Part B — answer key

| Q | Answer | Mark scheme |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | *Meṣa → Vṛṣabha → Mithuna → Karka → Siṁha* | 2 if all 5 in correct order; 1 if 3–4 correct; 0 otherwise. |
| 2 | Meṣa↔Aries, Karka↔Cancer, Tulā↔Libra, Makara↔Capricorn, Vṛścika↔Scorpio | 3 if all 5 matched; 2 if 3–4 matched; 1 if 1–2 matched. |
| 3 | c) ~30 days | 2 |
| 4 | Whichever rāśi matches the student's birth-month per the Day 5 table. | 1 if correct; allow ± 1 *rāśi* if the birthday is near a *saṁkrānti* boundary. |
| 5 | Because both the Indian and Greek zodiacs share a common origin in **Babylonian astronomy** (~700 BCE), with later cross-cultural contact during the Hellenistic period. The 12-fold ecliptic division and most of the animal symbolism is a shared inheritance. | 2 if "shared origin / cultural exchange" is named with any further detail; 1 if just "they look similar because". |

Total Part B: 10 marks. Not added to summative.

---

## What to do with the results

- A class average below 6/10 on Part B = significant gaps. Reteach the *rāśi* order during Day 6 warm-up (or assign quick recall homework).
- Individual students below 3/10 on Part B = pull aside before Day 7's *sāyana*/*nirayana* lesson for a 5-minute one-on-one (otherwise Day 7 will not land).
