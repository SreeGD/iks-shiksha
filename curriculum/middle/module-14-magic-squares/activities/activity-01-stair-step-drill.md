# Activity 01 — Stair-Step Drill

**Module:** Magic Squares (*Bhadragaṇita*) · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 4 · **Time:** 15 min

## Type

Individual + paired practice

## Goal

Each student constructs three 3×3 magic squares using Nārāyaṇa's *turagagati* method, with the partner checking accuracy.

## Materials per student

- 3 printed blank 3×3 grids
- The 5-step rule sheet (from Day 4 student-facing content)
- Pencil

## Set-up (teacher, before class)

- Print blank 3×3 grids (3 per student).
- Have the 5-step rule visible on the board.

## Instructions (student-facing handout)

> **Stair-Step Drill** — work individually first, then in pairs.
>
> **Round 1 (5 min) — Slow build, rule sheet open.**
> Construct a 3×3 magic square using the *turagagati* method. Take your time. Speak the steps out loud quietly. Verify all three row sums equal 15 before stopping.
>
> **Round 2 (4 min) — Faster, rule sheet open.**
> Construct another 3×3 magic square from scratch. Time yourself.
>
> **Round 3 (3 min) — Rule sheet closed.**
> Construct one more 3×3, this time from memory. Time yourself.
>
> **Pair check (3 min)** — swap sheets with your partner. Did they get the same square? (They should — there is only one 3×3 magic square up to symmetry, and the algorithm always produces the same one.) Compare round-by-round times.

## What to expect

All three squares should be identical:

```
2 | 7 | 6
---------
9 | 5 | 1
---------
4 | 3 | 8
```

If a student's square is different, walk through their steps to find where the rule was misapplied — usually step 5 ("blocked → directly below").

## Discussion prompts (last 2 min)

- Did you get the same square all three times? If yes, why? If no, where did the difference come from?
- What did Round 3 (from memory) feel like compared to Round 1?

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Student narrates "1 in top middle, then up-right, off the top so wrap to bottom..." | "Where does 6 go? Up-right from 5 — that's outside the top, so wrap to..." |
| Needs intervention | Student stares at the blank grid; doesn't know where to begin | Silence; "I don't get it." |

## Safety / sensitivity

- No timed competition between students. Each student times *themselves*.

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Construct a 7×7 magic square using the same method. Constant should be 175. Verify two rows and the main diagonal.
- **Tier down:** Stay on the 3×3. The rule must be automatic before scaling up.

## Clean-up

- All 3 constructed squares stay in the student workbook for later reference.
