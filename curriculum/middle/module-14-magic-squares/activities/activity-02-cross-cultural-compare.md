# Activity 02 — Cross-cultural Comparison

**Module:** Magic Squares (*Bhadragaṇita*) · **Band:** Middle · **Day:** 8 · **Time:** 15 min

## Type

Paired worksheet with discussion

## Goal

Pairs compare three magic squares from three different cultures and identify the structural features they share or differ on.

## Materials per pair

- 1 comparison worksheet (template below)
- Printed images of the three squares (or projected on the board): Lo Shu, Khajuraho, Dürer

## Instructions (student-facing)

> **Cross-Cultural Comparison.**
>
> You will compare three magic squares:
> 1. **Lo Shu** (China, ~5th–1st c. BCE attested)
> 2. **Khajuraho** (India, ~10th–11th c. CE)
> 3. **Dürer** (Germany, 1514 CE)
>
> Complete the table together. For each cell that asks about a sum, verify the sum yourself (don't take the question's word for it).

## Comparison worksheet

| Question | Lo Shu (3×3) | Khajuraho (4×4) | Dürer (4×4) |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Order *n* | 3 | 4 | 4 |
| Magic constant | _____ | _____ | _____ |
| Numbers used | 1–____ | 1–____ | 1–____ |
| First row | _, _, _ | _, _, _, _ | _, _, _, _ |
| Centre number(s) | _ (one cell) | _, _, _, _ (four cells form the centre 2×2) | _, _, _, _ |
| Sum of corners | _ + _ + _ + _ = _ | _ + _ + _ + _ = _ | _ + _ + _ + _ = _ |
| Every 2×2 panel sums to constant? (panel-magic?) | (irrelevant — no 2×2 panels in 3×3) | Yes / No | Yes / No |
| Approximate date | ~5th–1st c. BCE | ~10th–11th c. CE | 1514 CE |

## Discussion (last 3 min)

- Which square is **most constrained** (i.e. satisfies the most rules)? Why?
- Which is **simplest** to construct? Which would you struggle with most?
- Do you see any reason the same idea appeared in three different places?

## Reference data

```

Khajuraho (India):

Dürer (Germany):
16 |  3 |  2 | 13

```

## Answer key (teacher)

- Lo Shu magic constant: 15. Numbers 1–9.
- Khajuraho: 34. Numbers 1–16. **Panel-magic: Yes** (every 2×2 sums to 34).
- Dürer: 34. Numbers 1–16. **Panel-magic: Yes** (every 2×2 sums to 34).
- Both Khajuraho and Dürer are panel-magic; this is unusual.

## Looks-like / sounds-like

| | Looks like | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Going well | Pair is summing cells in pencil, comparing answers | "Wait — let me check the top-left 2×2: 7+12+2+13 = 34 ✓" |
| Needs intervention | One partner fills in the worksheet; the other watches | Silence; "you do it" |

## Differentiation

- **Tier up:** Compare the four corners of each square. Do they sum to the magic constant? (Not in general, but yes for Dürer and Khajuraho — both are "associative" in this sense.)
- **Tier down:** Just verify the basic magic constant on one square. Skip the panel-magic question for now.
