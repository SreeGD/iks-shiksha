## Part A — Day 2 (10 min, 6 questions)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1. (1 mark)** What is the magic constant of a 3×3 magic square with the numbers 1 to 9? ____________

  Row 1: ____
  Row 2: ____
  Row 3: ____

  Is this a magic square? (Y / N) ____

**5. (1 mark)** A magic square is "magic" because:

  (a) it has special religious power
  (b) every row, column, and diagonal sums to the same total
  (c) it uses the numbers 1 to 9
  (d) it was discovered by Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita

  Circle one: ____

**6. (2 marks, short answer)** In your own words, explain why the magic constant for a 3×3 with 1–9 is 15. Use the number 45 somewhere in your answer.

### Part B answer key

| Q | Answer | Marks |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | 5 steps: (1) 1 in top-middle; (2) up-right diagonal for next; (3) off-top → wrap to bottom; (4) off-right → wrap to left; (5) blocked → directly below. | 2 (any reasonable rephrasing) |

| 4b | T | 1 |
| 5 | The formula's date matters because it shows Nārāyaṇa systematised the method 300+ years before the same method appeared in European mathematics (de la Loubère, 1687). ## What to do with the results

- Class average below 8/15 on Part B = repeat the stair-step demonstration in next warm-up; require additional practice in homework.
- Individual below 5/15 = pull aside for 10-minute reteaching of step 5 (the most common error point).
