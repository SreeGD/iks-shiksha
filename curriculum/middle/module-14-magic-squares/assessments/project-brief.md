# Project Brief — Module 14 Magic Squares (Middle)

**Work sessions:** Days 8 + 9 · **Presentation:** Day 10 · **Total marks:** 25 (project 20 + presentation 5)

## The project

Each pair (or individual, with teacher approval) designs and presents an **original magic square** of order 5×5 or larger. Choose ONE of three formats:

### Format 1: Labelled poster

Produce an A3-sized (or larger) poster that shows:
- The constructed magic square (at least 5×5)
- Step-by-step construction labels showing the *turagagati* placement order (number each cell 1, 2, 3, ... in the order the algorithm placed it)
- A verification panel showing row, column, and diagonal sums
- A 50-word caption stating the magic constant and citing Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita's *Gaṇita-kaumudī*

### Format 2: 60-second demo video

Make a short video that walks a Grade 5 viewer through constructing a 5×5 magic square. Constraints:
- Total length: 60 seconds (±10 sec)
- Must show: the placement of at least cells 1, 2, 3, and one "blocked → directly below" step
- Must include verbal verification of the magic constant
- Cite the source verbally in the closing line (e.g. "Method from Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita's *Gaṇita-kaumudī*, 1356 CE")

### Format 3: Written exposition

Produce a 2-page written piece that:
- Constructs an original magic square (5×5 or larger)
- Explains the construction in clear prose, step-by-step
- Shows the verification arithmetic
- Includes a paragraph on Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita and the *Gaṇita-kaumudī*
- Cites the source in a closing line

## Design choices

You are not expected to invent a new algorithm. Your design choices are:
- **Order (size).** Pick 5×5, 7×7, or 9×9.
- **Starting cell.** The classical method starts in the top-middle. What happens if you start elsewhere? (You may use any valid starting cell — bottom-middle, top-right, etc. — and your magic square will be a rotation/reflection of the classical one.)
- **Number range.** Default is 1 to *n*². For tier-up, try a square with consecutive numbers from a different starting number (e.g. 10 to 34 for *n* = 5). Compute the new magic constant.
- **Presentation format and style.** This is where most of your originality goes.

## Why this matters

You're moving from *using* an algorithm to *teaching* an algorithm. Teaching is the proof of understanding. The verification step is what makes your work mathematics rather than craft.

## What success looks like

A strong project will:
- Demonstrate the *turagagati* method accurately (no arithmetic errors).
- Be pitched for an audience (Grade 5 for the video, peer reader for poster/exposition).
- Show your own design choices.
- Include the verification panel — without exception.

## Working in pairs

- Pair work is the default.
- Individual work allowed for any format with teacher approval.
- Groups of 3 acceptable — each member must have a distinct role (e.g. designer, constructor, verifier).

## Citation rules

- At minimum **one** citation must appear visibly in the project.
- Format: "Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita, *Gaṇita-kaumudī*, Book 14 (*Bhadragaṇita*), 1356 CE."
- The source must be cited even if the artefact is a video — say it aloud at the end.
