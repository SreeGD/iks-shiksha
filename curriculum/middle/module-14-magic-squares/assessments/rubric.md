# Project Rubric — Module 14 Magic Squares (Middle)

**Project marks:** 20 (out of 25 with presentation 5).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Method demonstrated correctly** | Stair-step placement order shown clearly; algorithm applied without error; the resulting square is verifiably magic. | Method shown; one minor error in placement OR in verification. | Method partially shown; multiple errors or significant gaps. | Method incorrect, absent, or simply copied. | × 2 |
| **Magic constant verified** | All rows, columns, and both diagonals verified; arithmetic correct. | At least 3 rows, 3 columns, both diagonals verified. | Some verification present; incomplete. | No verification shown. | × 1.5 |
| **Use of sources** | Cites Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita's *Gaṇita-kaumudī* (Book 14, 1356 CE) in the correct format; the citation appears prominently. | Citation present and correct. | Citation present but vague or incomplete. | No citation, or wrong source. | × 1 |
| **Originality of design** | The pair makes a clear, defensible design choice (size, starting cell, number range, or presentation style) and explains it. | Clearly the pair's own work; one design choice made. | Largely a class example reproduced. | Copied verbatim. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals or narration are clear, deliberate, polished. Arithmetic shown is correct and legible. | Communicates clearly with minor craft issues. | Message comes through with effort. | Hard to follow. | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 24. Project score = (sum × weights) capped at 20.

## Presentation (5 marks)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (2 min)** | 1:50–2:10 | 1:30–2:30 | 1:15–2:45 | Outside | × 0.5 |
| **Both partners contribute** | Equal | Both speak unequally | One dominates | One silent | × 0.5 |
| **Clarity** | Confident, audible | Mostly clear | Audible in front rows only | Mostly inaudible | × 0.25 |

Maximum: 5.

## Format-specific notes

- **Poster projects:** the poster is the artefact; the 2-min presentation walks the class through the cleverest design choice.
- **Video projects:** the 60-second video itself is the artefact; the 2-min presentation explains the design choices ("we used this example because ...").
- **Written exposition:** the 2-page document is the artefact; the 2-min presentation summarises and reads the most important sentence aloud.
