# Homework — Module 14 Magic Squares (Middle)

## Day 1 → Day 2

**Task:** Copy the 3×3 magic square from class. Beside it, draw three rotations (90°, 180°, 270°). Verify one row of each.

Time: 10 min.

## Day 2 → Day 3

**Task:** Verify the magic constant formula *S = n(n² + 1)/2* for *n* = 8 and *n* = 9. Show your arithmetic. (Answers: 260 and 369.)

Time: 8 min.

## Day 3 → Day 4

**Task:** Take the 3×3 magic square and add 10 to every entry. The square becomes one with numbers 11–19 instead of 1–9. What is the new magic constant? Verify one row.

Time: 10 min.

## Day 4 → Day 5

**Task:** Construct three 5×5 magic squares from scratch using the *turagagati* method. Verify the magic constant of each (should be 65). Yes, they should all be the same square — that's part of the discovery.

Time: 20 min.

## Day 5 → Day 6

**Task:** Construct a 7×7 magic square using the stair-step method. Magic constant should be 175. Bring it tomorrow for spot-checks.

Time: 25 min.

## Day 6 → Day 7

Time: 5 min.
