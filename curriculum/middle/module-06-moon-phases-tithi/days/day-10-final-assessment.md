# Day 10 — Final Assessment + Showcase

**Module:** Moon Phases & Tithi · **Band:** Middle · **Time:** 45 min (split: 20 min quiz + 25 min showcase)

## Learning objective

Students demonstrate the module's full set of objectives through a summative quiz, and present their projects in 3 minutes per pair to the class.

## Materials

- Printed summative quiz (`quizzes/summative.md`) — one per student
- All projects ready to present
- A visible timer (phone, stopwatch)
- The rubric (`assessments/rubric.md`) for marking

## Lesson flow

### Warm-up (3 min)

- Daily prompt: *"Did you see the Moon last night? What tithi?"* — 2 students share. (Last time for the diary prompt.)
- *"Two things today — quiz first, then the showcase. Pencils ready."*

### Summative quiz (20 min)

- Distribute the quiz.
- 20 minutes, closed book, individual.
- Strict silence after the start signal.
- Collect at end. Mark over the weekend.

### Showcase (20 min)

- Pairs present in turn — 3 minutes each + 1 minute Q&A.
- Visible timer running. Strict cutoff at 3:30.
- Teacher marks on the rubric in real time.
- Audience: students take notes — what's one fact they didn't know, from each pair?

(For a class of 30 students = 15 pairs, this won't fit in 20 minutes. **Either split presentations across Day 9 and Day 10, or schedule a separate showcase session.** See the lesson-plan note.)

### Wrap-up (2 min)

- *"You've each kept a 10-night Moon diary. You've built a tithi calendar. You've explained eclipses. You've presented a project. Look up tonight — you'll see the Moon a little differently."*
- Reflection slips submitted with projects.
- Collect Moon diaries.

## Student-facing content

> **Module complete.**
>
> Today:
> 1. Summative quiz — 20 min, individual, closed book.
> 2. Showcase — 3 minutes per pair + 1-minute Q&A.
> 3. Reflection slip submitted.
>
> By end of class, you've shown what you learned. Take a breath.

## Homework

- **None.** Module complete.

## Differentiation

- **One tier up:** Volunteer to be the audience-facing rubric scorer (anonymously) — practice the discipline of assessing peers fairly.
- **One tier down:** Students who couldn't finish their project today have until next class to submit. Don't penalise on Day 10 if the artefact is 80% done.

## Teacher notes

- Mark the quiz over the weekend; return within 3 school days.
- Project rubric marks should be visible to students within 5 school days.
- Reflection slips are NOT graded but are read — write one-sentence response on each.
- Keep one or two strong projects (with student permission) as exemplars for next year.
