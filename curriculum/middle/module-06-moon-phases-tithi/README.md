# Module 6 — Moon Phases & Tithi

**Age band:** Middle · **Grades:** 6–8 · **Duration:** 10 sessions × 45 min (≈ 2 weeks)

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

1. **Describe** the synodic month (~29.5 days) and explain why the Moon appears to change shape each night using the Sun–Moon–Earth geometry.
2. **Define** *tithi* as one-thirtieth of a synodic month and explain why a tithi need not equal a calendar day.
3. **Name** the two *pakṣa*s (*śukla* and *kṛṣṇa*) and the 15 tithis of each, recognising the Sanskrit ordinal pattern from *pratipadā* to *caturdaśī* plus *pūrṇimā* and *amāvasyā*.
4. **Build** a tithi calendar for the current calendar month using a published *pañcāṅga* or astronomy app.
5. **Explain** why solar eclipses occur on *amāvasyā* and lunar eclipses on *pūrṇimā*, and why they do not happen every month.
6. **Compare** the lunisolar Indian calendar with three other lunar/lunisolar calendars (Islamic Hijri, Chinese, Hebrew) and identify what is shared and what differs.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the day-night cycle and the Earth's rotation (NCERT Class 6 Science).
- Comfort with degrees and basic geometry (a full circle is 360°).
- No Sanskrit knowledge assumed; all terms introduced with IAST + meaning.

## Key vocabulary

See `_shared/glossary.md` for full entries.

- *tithi* — lunar day, 1/30 of a synodic month
- *pakṣa* — fortnight; *śukla* (waxing) and *kṛṣṇa* (waning)
- *amāvasyā* — new moon; *pūrṇimā* — full moon
- *pañcāṅga* — five-limbed almanac: tithi, vāra, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa
- *nakṣatra* — one of 27 lunar mansions
- *adhika māsa* — intercalary "extra" month

## Materials checklist (whole module)

- A torch (flashlight) and a small white ball (table-tennis ball works) for the lighting demo
- A printed lunar phase calendar for the current month (NASA publishes monthly phase charts; any Indian *pañcāṅga* app also works)
- Chart paper for the class tithi calendar
- Index cards (~30) for the tithi sorting activity
- A globe and a smaller spherical object (for eclipse geometry)
- Project supplies (poster paper, optional camera or smartphone)

## Citation sources used

- **Modern astronomy:** NCERT Physics Class XI, Chapter 8 (Gravitation) for orbital motion; NASA's published lunar phase data for current-month verification.
- **Indian sources:** *Sūrya-siddhānta* (compiled c. 5th–10th c. CE) — chapter 2 (lunar motion) referenced for the tithi computation rule; paraphrased only at Middle band.
- See `sources.md` for the full list.
