# Project Rubric — Module 6 Moon Phases & Tithi (Middle)

**Project marks:** 30 (out of 40 total when combined with presentation 10).

## Criteria

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaches | 1 — Beginning | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Conceptual accuracy** | Names the lunar phenomenon AND its astronomical mechanism AND its position in the tithi/pakṣa system correctly. Connects to at least one other concept from the module (e.g. ties phases to the elongation rule). | Names the phenomenon and the mechanism, and one accurate fact about its place in the framework. Minor imprecision OK. | Names the phenomenon but mixes up the mechanism OR the position in the framework. | Names the phenomenon only; gets the mechanism or position wrong. | × 2 |
| **Use of sources** | Cites at least ONE real source: a paraphrase of the *Sūrya-siddhānta* (with chapter reference), or a specific reference to NCERT Physics XI, or the class *pañcāṅga*, or NASA's published phase data. Bonus: cites two sources. | Cites a real source by name. The source matches a reference in `sources.md`. | Mentions a source vaguely ("Indian astronomy texts say..." or "scientists have shown...") without a specific reference. | No source cited, or an invented citation. | × 2 |
| **Modern-traditional comparison** | Explicitly contrasts the traditional Indian account (tithi, pañcāṅga) with the modern astronomical account, AND names a way each one captures something the other does not. | Names both the traditional and the modern account; explains how they overlap. | Mentions both but doesn't explain the relationship. | Only one of the two accounts present. | × 1 |
| **Use of own observation** | Uses the 10-night Moon diary directly — for example, shows the diary as data, or refers to specific nights' sketches. | Mentions the diary or a personal observation. | The project is generic; no personal observation visible. | No observation; clearly from textbook/web only. | × 1 |
| **Originality of expression** | The project shows the pair's own thinking — a turn of phrase, a fresh comparison, a surprising example or angle. | Clearly the students' own work; not lifted from any single source. | Largely paraphrased from a single source; little independent thinking visible. | Clearly copied from a single source. | × 1 |
| **Communication / craft** | Visuals, layout, or narrative are clean, deliberate, and serve the message. Spelling and IAST are correct. | Communicates clearly. One or two craft issues (a typo, a misaligned heading). | Message comes through despite some craft issues. | Hard to follow; multiple craft issues impede understanding. | × 1 |

## Score calculation

`Project score = (sum of criterion scores × weights) × 0.9375`

Maximum raw score: 32. Multiply by 30/32 = 0.9375 to get out of 30.

## Presentation marks (10, on Day 10)

| Criterion | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 | Weight |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|--------|
| **Within time (3 min)** | Within 2:45–3:00 | Within 2:30–3:15 | Within 2:15–3:30 | Outside that range | × 1 |
| **Both partners contribute (or solo presents whole)** | Equal contribution; smooth handoffs | Both partners speak but unequally | One partner dominates | One partner silent | × 1 |
| **Clarity and audibility** | Confident, audible to the back of the room, clear pace | Audible, mostly clear | Audible only in front rows; some mumbling | Mostly inaudible | × 0.5 |

Maximum: 10.

## Self-reflection mark (folded into project score)

The 3 reflection questions are not separately scored — but if the reflection is missing or perfunctory, the **Originality** mark drops by 1.

## Common decision points

- **Group of 3 vs pair**: Acceptable, but the third member must have a clearly distinct sub-role (e.g. visuals + script + presenter). Marks are equal across the group unless one member visibly didn't contribute.
- **Late submission**: −2 marks per school day, capped at −6.
- **Invented citations**: If a project cites a source that doesn't exist (a fake *Sūrya-siddhānta* verse number, a fabricated NASA URL), the project goes to 0 on **Use of sources** AND on **Originality**. A 1-day extension is offered for a redo.
- **Diary incomplete**: If the Moon diary has fewer than 7 nights, the **Use of own observation** mark caps at 2. If under 4 nights, caps at 1.

## Marking process

- Mark in real-time during presentation (5 minutes per pair: 3 present + 1 Q&A + 1 marking).
- Write 1-sentence written feedback after each presentation finishes.
- Return marked rubrics within 2 school days.
