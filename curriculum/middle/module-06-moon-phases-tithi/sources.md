## Modern astronomy references

These are standard middle-school astronomy and are recommended for cross-checking:

- **NCERT Physics Class XI**, Chapter 8 ("Gravitation") — for the orbital motion of the Moon around Earth.
- **NCERT Science Class 6**, Chapter 17 ("Stars and the Solar System") — for the basic Sun–Moon–Earth geometry.
- **NASA's published lunar phase data** — visit the NASA Solar System Exploration site and search for "Moon phases [current year]" for an official phase calendar to compare against. (Do not invent specific URLs; tell students to search the official NASA site.)
- **In The Sky** astronomical reference (in-the-sky.org) — a verified secondary source for upcoming eclipses, used for Day 7.

## Cross-cultural calendar references (Day 9)

- **Islamic (Hijri) calendar** — strictly lunar; 12 months × ~29.5 days = ~354 days. No intercalation. Months drift through the seasons over ~33 years. See any standard reference; the *Encyclopaedia of Islam* entry on *taqwīm* is comprehensive.
- **Chinese calendar** — lunisolar with intercalary months, similar in principle to the Indian system. Lunar New Year falls between late January and mid-February.
- **Hebrew calendar** — lunisolar with the Metonic cycle (7 intercalary months in 19 years).
