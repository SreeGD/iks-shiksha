# Module 6 (Middle) — Formative Quizzes

Two parts: Part A on Day 3 (after tithi naming), Part B on Day 5 (mid-module). Both are diagnostic — not graded. Hand back within 24 hours.

---

## Part A — Day 3 (3 min, 5 questions)

**Name:** ______________________  **Class:** ______

**1.** A *tithi* is defined as the time during which the Moon gains _____ degrees of elongation on the Sun.

a) 12
b) 15
c) 24
d) 30

**2.** How many tithis are there in one synodic month?

a) 15
b) 27
c) 28
d) 30

**3.** True or false: A tithi always lasts exactly 24 hours.   (T / F)

**4.** Name the tithi that corresponds to the full moon.

_______________________

**5.** Translate: *aṣṭamī*.

_______________________

---

### Part A — answer key

| Q | Answer | Notes |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | a) 12 | 360° / 30 = 12°. |
| 2 | d) 30 | 15 per pakṣa × 2 pakṣas. |
| 3 | F | Average ~23h 37m; can range ~21.5–26h. |
| 4 | *pūrṇimā* | Spelling tolerated if recognisable. |
| 5 | Eighth | Sanskrit ordinal for 8. |

### Part B — answer key

| Q | Answer | Mark scheme |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | *śukla pakṣa* — Moon grows (waxes), from new to full; *kṛṣṇa pakṣa* — Moon shrinks (wanes), from full to new. | 2 if both names + both directions; 1 if either part incomplete. |
| 2 | New moon = *amāvasyā*, 15 (of *kṛṣṇa pakṣa*). Full moon = *pūrṇimā*, 15 (of *śukla pakṣa*). First-day-after = *pratipadā*. | 3 (1 per row); 0.5 for partial. |
| 3 | Caitra–Spring; Śrāvaṇa–Monsoon; Kārtika–Late autumn; Mārgaśīrṣa–Early winter. | 2 if all 4; 1 if 2–3. |
| 4 | Because it uses lunar months for naming (29.5-day months) but re-aligns periodically with the solar year using an *adhika māsa*. | 1 if both lunar and solar components named. |
| 5 | A tithi has an average length of ~23h 37m. The Moon doesn't move uniformly. So sometimes a tithi begins after one sunrise and ends before the next — its short duration means it never overlaps with a sunrise. | 2 if both "tithi length varies" AND "shorter than a day" reasoning appear. 1 if either part is present. |

Total Part B: 10 marks. Not added to summative.

## What to do with the results

- A class average below 6/10 on Part B = significant gaps. Reteach the pakṣa names and the tithi-vs-day distinction on Day 6 (before moving to the *pañcāṅga* tour).
- Individual students below 3/10 on Part B = pull aside on Day 7 for a 10-min one-on-one before group activity starts.
