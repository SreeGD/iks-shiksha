| 6 | The Sun lights up only half of the Moon. As the Moon orbits Earth, different fractions of the lit half face Earth. We see the changing fractions as phases. | 2 if all three of (Sun lights half) + (orbital motion) + (different fractions visible) are present. 1 if two of the three. |

| 8 | The Moon's orbit is tilted by ~5° relative to Earth's orbit around the Sun. Most months, the Moon's shadow misses Earth (or Earth's shadow misses the Moon) at the alignment. Eclipses happen only when the Moon is at one of its two orbital nodes near amāvasyā/pūrṇimā. | 2 if the tilted-orbit AND node ideas both appear; 1 if only one. |
| 9 | *Adhika māsa* is an intercalary "extra month" inserted approximately every 32.5 months in the Indian calendar. Without it, the lunar months (12 × 29.5 = 354 days) would drift 11 days behind the solar year (365.24) each year, and festivals would slowly leave their season. | 2 if both "intercalary month" AND "to re-align lunar with solar" are present. 1 if either alone. |
