## Inspired by

The structure of the 15-module syllabus — its sequencing, choice of topics (Panchabhūta, Bindu Paddhati / Dot Addition, Ayurveda, Yoga, etc.), and the pedagogical decision to combine Indian intellectual traditions with hands-on classroom activities — is inspired by the **Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) Sessions program** developed and delivered by the **Anaadi Foundation** as their Project Abhyudaya initiative.

The original program was delivered as 15 modules across 96 sessions to grades 6A–8B at **PM SHRI Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, Bangalore Rural, Karnataka 562163**, between **18 December 2025 and 3 January 2026**, by the faculty team of Gomathi Venkatachalam, Ankit Maurya, Amartya, and Sridhar.

## How this curriculum differs from the source

This is an **adaptation, not a reproduction**:

- The original 15 modules were delivered to a single age band (Middle / grades 6–8). We adapted to **three age bands** (Primary / grades 3–5, Middle / grades 6–8, Senior / grades 9–12) with band-specific tone, depth, assessment, and project formats.
- The content text in this curriculum is **original to this project** — written for this adaptation, not copied from any source program's materials.
- The framing is **secular and scientific first**, with traditional Indian context presented as historical and intellectual heritage. The Senior band engages contemporary scholarly debates honestly (Singleton on modern postural yoga; Dani on Vedic mathematics; integration debates around IKS in NEP 2020 §4.6).
- The pedagogical artefacts (10-day lesson plans, teacher notes, parent guides, student workbooks, rubrics, project briefs) are original.

If you are the original program team and notice anything that should be corrected, removed, or attributed differently — please open an issue or contact the maintainer of this repository.

## Policy framing

The integration of Indian Knowledge Systems into school curricula is informed by **NEP 2020 §4.6** (Government of India, 2020). The Senior band engages the policy debate around this directly, presenting all three defensible positions (full integration, separation, qualified integration).
